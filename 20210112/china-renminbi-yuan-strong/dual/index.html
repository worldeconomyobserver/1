中国经济走势强劲，人民币汇率水涨船高

China’s Economy Surges, and So Does Its Currency

艾莎
2021年1月12日
HONG KONG — China’s economy has come roaring back from the depths of the coronavirus pandemic, and its currency has joined the ride.
香港——中国经济已经迅速走出新冠病毒大流行的深渊，其货币也随之而起。
The currency, known variously as the yuan or the renminbi, has surged in strength in recent months against the American dollar and other major currencies. Through Monday, the U.S. dollar was worth 6.47 renminbi, compared with 7.16 renminbi in late May and close to its strongest level in two and half years.
近几个月来，人民币对美元和其他主要货币的汇率强劲上涨。截至周一，1美元价值6.47人民币，而在5月底为7.16人民币，接近两年半以来的最高水平。
Many currencies tend to jump around in value even more, but Beijing has long kept a leash on China’s, so the renminbi’s leap looks like a power move.
许多货币的涨幅往往会更大，但北京长期以来一直对中国的汇率施以限制，所以这次人民币的飞跃看起来似乎是一种力量展示。
The stronger renminbi has implications for companies that make stuff in China, which is a pretty big group. It could make Chinese-made goods more expensive for the world’s consumers, though the effect seems muted so far.
人民币升值对在中国制造商品的公司会产生影响，而这样的公司是一个很大的群体。但它可能会使中国制造的商品对全球消费者而言更加昂贵，尽管到目前为止，这种效应还未显现。
广告
The most immediate impact might be in Washington, where President-elect Joseph R. Biden Jr. is set to move into the White House next week. During past administrations, a weakening of China’s currency led to anger in Washington. The renminbi’s rise may not ease the tense relationship between the two countries, but it could remove one potential issue from Mr. Biden’s plate.
最直接的影响可能在华盛顿，候任总统小约瑟夫·R·拜登(Joseph R. Biden Jr.)将于下周入主白宫。在过去的政府中，人民币贬值会引发华盛顿的愤怒。人民币的升值也许不会缓解两国之间的紧张关系，但可以消除拜登手上的一个潜在问题。
Why Is the Renminbi Strengthening?
为什么人民币会升值？
The reason is simple: China is a rare bright spot in an otherwise ravaged global economy.
原因很简单：在全球其他地方经济遭到重创的情况下，中国是一个罕见的亮点。
The coronavirus has been tamed within its borders, at least for now. Its factories are charging ahead full steam. The world’s shoppers — many of them stuck at home or unable to buy plane or cruise ship tickets — are buying all the Chinese-made computers, televisions, selfie ring lights, swivel chairs, gardening tools and other accouterment of nesting that they can. China’s share of world exports rose to a record 14.3 percent in September, according to data compiled by Jefferies & Co.
至少到目前为止，新冠病毒在其境内已被控制住。工厂全负荷运转。世界各地的购物者——许多被困在家中或者无法买机票或船票——正在购买所有他们能买到的中国制造的电脑、电视、环形自拍照明灯、转椅、园艺工具和其他家居装备。根据杰富瑞投资银行(Jefferies & Co.)收集的数据显示，9月中国在世界出口中所占的份额升至创纪录的14.3％。
Investors are also keen to stash their money in China, or at least in investments tied to the renminbi. With the economy stronger, China’s central bank has room to leave interest rates higher than in Europe and the United States, where central bankers are keeping rates historically low to shore up growth.
投资者也热衷于把钱投在中国，或者至少在与人民币挂钩的投资中。随着经济的强劲发展，中国的中央银行有空间让利率高于欧洲和美国——那里的中央银行一直将利率保持在历史低位以支撑增长。
The renminbi looks particularly strong against the dollar right now because the U.S. currency has weakened. Investors are betting that the world economy will recover this year, so many are beginning to shift their money out of dollar-denominated safe havens, like U.S. Treasury bonds, and into riskier bets.
由于美元贬值，目前人民币兑美元走势看起来更加强劲。投资者押注全球经济将在今年复苏，因此许多人开始将资金从以美元为基准的安全港——如美国国债——转移到风险较高的押注上。
广告
All of that has sent money flooding into China, which tends to bolster a country’s currency.
所有这些都使资金涌入中国，这往往会助长一个国家的货币升值。
What Is Beijing Doing About It?
北京为此做了什么？
Not much, at least for now.
做的不多，至少目前是这样。
The Chinese government has long kept a firm hand on the value of its currency, in part by limiting how much of it can move past the country’s borders. With those tools, China’s leaders for years kept the renminbi weak against the dollar, even when the Chinese currency should have been strengthening. A weak renminbi helps China’s factories keep their prices down when they sell goods overseas.
长期以来，中国政府一直坚定地控制人民币汇率，部分是通过限制人民币出境的规模。借助这些工具，即使人民币本应升值，中国领导人多年来仍使人民币兑美元保持疲软。人民币贬值有助于中国工厂在向海外销售商品时保持低价。
Right now, it looks as if China’s factories don’t need that kind of help. China’s exports have continued to surge even as the renminbi has strengthened.
目前看来，中国的工厂不需要这种帮助了。即使人民币升值，中国的出口仍继续激增。
Many already price their business in dollars rather than renminbi because the United States is such a big share of their customer base, said Shaun Roache, chief economist for Asia-Pacific for S&P Global, the ratings firm. That means that while Chinese factory profit margins could take a hit, American shoppers won’t notice much of a price difference and will keep buying.
评级公司标普全球(S&P Global)亚太区首席经济学家肖恩·罗奇(Shaun Roache)表示，由于美国在其客户群中占有很大份额，许多人已经以美元而不是人民币来定价其业务。这意味着尽管中国工厂的利润率可能会受到打击，但美国购物者不会注意到价格差异，而会继续购买。
广告
A strong currency has benefits for China, too. Chinese consumers can more ably buy imported goods, helping Beijing nurture a new generation of shoppers. It looks good to economists and policymakers who have long been pressing China to loosen up its tight control of the country’s financial system.
强劲的货币对中国也有好处。中国消费者更有能力购买进口商品，从而帮助北京培育了新一代的购物者。对于长期以来一直在敦促中国放松对中国金融体系的严格控制的经济学家和政策制定者来说，这看起来不错。
The stronger renminbi could also help China to make its currency more attractive to companies and investors who like to do business in dollars. China has long sought to make its currency more global as a way to increase its international sway, though its desire to strictly control its use has often overshadowed those ambitions.
强劲的人民币还可以帮助中国提高其货币对那些喜欢以美元开展业务的公司和投资者的吸引力。长期以来，中国一直在寻求使其货币更具全球性，以增加其国际影响力，尽管对人民币的使用进行严格控制的愿望常常挫伤这方面的雄心。
“This is definitely a window of opportunity for China to push forward to internationalize its renminbi,” said Becky Liu, head of China macro strategy at Standard Chartered Bank.
渣打银行(Standard Chartered Bank)中国宏观策略部门负责人刘洁(Becky Liu)表示：“这绝对是中国推进人民币国际化的机会窗口。”
Still, if the currency strengthens too quickly, Chinese leaders could easily step in and end the trend.
不过，如果人民币升值太快，中国领导人很容易介入并结束这一趋势。
Why Does This Help Biden?
为什么这有助于拜登？
Critics of Beijing in Congress and within the government have long accused the Chinese government of unfairly manipulating the currency in ways that hurt American manufacturers.
长期以来，国会和政府内部的北京批评者指责中国政府不公平地操纵货币，伤害了美国制造商。
广告
At the height of the trade war with the United States, Beijing allowed the renminbi to weaken past a psychologically important point of 7 to the dollar. This led the Trump administration to label China a currency manipulator.
在中美贸易战最激烈的时候，北京方面允许人民币贬值到1美元兑7元人民币的重要心理关口。这导致特朗普政府将中国列为汇率操纵国。
Now, as a new administration prepares to move into the White House, experts are looking for signals that Beijing could soften. At the very least, a strong renminbi takes the issue off Mr. Biden’s plate for now.
现在，随着新一届政府准备入主白宫，专家们正在寻找北京缓和姿态的信号。至少，强劲的人民币可以消除拜登手上的一个难题。
Not everyone is optimistic that a stronger renminbi will be enough to patch relations between the world’s two biggest economies, however.
然而，并非所有人都乐观地认为，强劲的人民币足以修补世界两个最大经济体之间的关系。
“It will take a lot more than currency appreciation to get the China-U.S. relationship back on an even keel, although this certainly removes one of the potential flash points,” said Eswar Prasad, a former head of the International Monetary Fund’s China Division.
前国际货币基金组织(International Monetary Fund)中国区负责人埃斯瓦尔·普拉萨德(Eswar Prasad)说：“要使中美关系恢复平稳，需要的不仅仅是货币升值，尽管这无疑消除了潜在的引爆点之一。”

艾莎(Alexandra Stevenson)是时报驻香港商业记者，报道中国企业巨头、跨国公司以及中国在亚洲日益增长的经济和金融影响力。欢迎在Twitter和Facebook上关注她。
