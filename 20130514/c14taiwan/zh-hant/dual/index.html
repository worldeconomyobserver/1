台灣電子市場華碩一枝獨秀

In Taiwan, Lamenting a Lost Lead

KEITH BRADSHER
2013年5月14日
TAIPEI, Taiwan — Jonney Shih, the chairman of Asustek Computer, has epitomized the Taiwanese electronics engineer for a generation: a slender figure in rumpled, baggy trousers, he once helped Intel solve heat problems in its Pentium 4 microprocessors.
台北——華碩電腦股份有限公司(Asustek Computer)董事長施崇棠(Jonney Shih)在整整一代人的時間裡，都代表着台灣的電子工程產業：身材瘦削，穿着皺巴巴的松垮褲子，還曾幫助英特爾(Intel)解決了奔騰4(Pentium 4)微處理器的散熱問題。
So it has been a surprise over the last several years to see Mr. Shih, now 60, reinvent himself with snug-cut Italian suits, innovative designs for tablet and notebook computers and scathing criticisms of Taiwan’s test-obsessed, engineering-oriented educational system.
因此，在過去的幾年裡，目睹現年60歲的施崇棠改頭換面，穿上了合體的意大利西裝，給平板電腦和筆記本電腦帶來了創新性的設計，還對熱衷考試、以工科為導向的台灣教育體制提出了尖刻的批判，都讓人頗感驚訝。
“I don’t think the Taiwanese got very good training to drive the mentality of innovation,” he said during an interview at Asus’s headquarters here on the outskirts of Taipei. (Mr. Shih also demonstrated his flexibility in the interview, assuming the lotus position while wearing a dark blue Armani suit with a sky-blue Armani tie.)
他在位於台北郊區的華碩總部接受採訪時說，「我不認為台灣人得到了能激發創新精神的良好培訓。」採訪過程中，施崇棠還展現了他的柔韌性，他身着深藍色的阿瑪尼(Armani)西裝，打着一條天藍色的阿瑪尼領帶，盤腿而坐。
Fostering innovation has become a mantra among corporate leaders and government officials alike in Taiwan this year because the island’s huge consumer electronics industry has run into serious trouble.
今年，由於台灣龐大的消費電子產業遭遇了嚴重的問題，促進創新成了台灣企業領袖和政府官員的口頭禪。
Worldwide sales of PCs, for which Taiwanese companies control over 90 percent of the final design and manufacturing, are declining steadily. Sales of smartphones, for which Taiwanese companies control less than a fifth of the market, are rising briskly. Tablets based on the Android operating system, which most Taiwanese companies, with the exception of Asus, have been slow to embrace, are also on the same upward trajectory.
全球個人電腦(PC)銷量正在穩步下滑，而全球PC的最終設計和生產有超過90%由台灣企業控制。智能手機銷量正在迅猛上漲，但台灣企業只控制着不足五分之一市場份額。基於Android操作系統的平板電腦也正處在同樣的上升軌道，大多數台灣企業在接納Android系統方面都行動遲緩，只有華碩例外。
“Outside of Asus, all the others are struggling,” said Helen Chiang, a Taiwan electronics specialist at the IDC research firm.
IDC研究公司的台灣電子產業研究專家江芳韻(Helen Chiang)說，「除了華碩之外，其他企業都在苦苦掙扎。」
Foxconn and Acer have each reported that sales in the first quarter dropped 19 percent from a year ago. HTC’s sales plunged 37 percent, although that was partly because the company began shipping the annually improved version of its best-known smartphone in late March instead of February. At Quanta, a 70,000-employee contract designer and manufacturer of notebook computers, sales have shown double-digit percentage drops from year-earlier levels for 14 consecutive months.
富士康(Foxconn)和宏碁(Acer)都公布，今年一季度的銷售額比去年同期下滑了19%。HTC的銷售額下滑了37%，不過部分原因是該公司3月底才開始出售其最知名的智能手機的年度升級版本，而不是2月。有7萬名員工的筆記本電腦代工設計和製造商廣達電腦(Quanta)，銷售額同比下跌的百分比已經連續14個月超過兩位數。
Foreign rivals have proved more nimble. In South Korea, Samsung is expanding rapidly in smartphones, tablet computers and other sectors. After embracing the Android operating system early, the company has built on its huge economies of scale in the mass production of components, like display screens and microprocessors.
事實證明海外競爭對手反應更加敏捷。在韓國，三星電子(Samsung)正在智能手機、平板電腦和其他領域迅速擴張。三星很早就採用了Android操作系統，該公司還以大量生產顯示屏和微處理器等零部件為依託，實現了巨大的規模效益。
In China, Lenovo and many smaller manufacturers are relying on labor that, while no longer cheap, is still less expensive than in Taiwan. That helped make Lenovo the only one among the top five PC makers worldwide to eke out a gain in shipments in the first quarter — although by only a tenth of a percent.
在中國大陸，聯想集團(Lenovo)和許多規模較小的製造商，可以依賴儘管不再那麼廉價但仍然比台灣低廉的勞動力。這個優勢，讓聯想成為今年一季度全球五大PC製造商中，唯一一家艱難實現出貨量提高的企業，儘管增幅只有區區百分之零點一。
And in the United States, Apple, Google and Amazon have shown themselves adept at producing breakthrough consumer products, while pending legislation would allow them to import more foreign engineers at a lower cost than hiring and training domestic engineers.
在美國，蘋果(Apple)、谷歌(Google)和亞馬遜(Amazon)已經顯示了，它們擅長生產開創性的消費電子品。同時，正在審議中的議案將允許他們引進更多外國工程師，成本比僱傭和培訓國內工程師更低。
As notebooks and other Windows-based PCs have lost ground, first to Apple tablets and now to Android-based designs, even Microsoft has been indicating dissatisfaction with the pace of PC innovation in Taiwan. Despite a longtime aversion to hardware, Microsoft recently introduced its own Surface tablet.
隨着筆記本電腦和其他基於Windows操作系統的個人電腦，先是在蘋果平板電腦面前敗退，後來又在運行Android操作系統的產品面前敗退，就連微軟(Microsoft)都對台灣PC創新的步伐表達了不滿。儘管對硬件長期反感，微軟最近還是推出了自己的Surface平板電腦。
“The Surface tablet is a pretty strong signal to the whole Taiwan PC ecosystem that they’re not innovating enough,” said Bill Whyman, a senior managing director at the ISI research firm.
美國諮詢公司國際戰略與投資集團(ISI)高級董事總經理比爾·威曼(Bill Whyman)說，「對台灣的整個PC生態系統來說，Surface平板電腦是一個相當強烈的信號，表明他們的創新不足。」
One exception to Taiwan’s difficulties is Asus. Its many new Android-based tablets, including one that it has branded with Google, allowed it to surpass Amazon in the first quarter of this year to become the third-largest player in the global tablet computer market, behind Apple and Samsung, according to IDC.
台灣的困境有一個例外，就是華碩。根據IDC的數據，華碩旗下多款採用Android系統的新款平板電腦，包括以谷歌品牌生產的一款平板電腦，使它在今年一季度超越亞馬遜，成為全球平板電腦市場僅次於蘋果和三星的第三大生產商。
And some of its designs are downright clever. One new model, the PadFone, lets the user slide a cellphone into the back, turning the tablet into an oversize cellphone. Another tablet, the Transformer, features a detachable keyboard with a wireless connection and a two-sided display panel that can show a movie on one side to entertain children or guests while the other side is a regular computer display for the owner.
華碩的某些設計聰明絕頂。該公司的一個新型號產品，變形手機PadFone，允許消費者把手機滑入平板電腦的背面，從而把平板電腦轉換成一部超大號的手機。而另一款平板電腦Transformer則帶有一個可拆卸的鍵盤，配有無線連接和雙面的顯示屏，其中一面能播放電影，供孩子或客人娛樂，主人則可以用另一面當作常規的電腦顯示屏。
Those innovations have helped keep Asus’s sales growing, up 16 percent in the first quarter from a year ago. That was even as worldwide PC shipments fell 11.2 percent, according to Gartner, another research firm.
這些創新幫助華碩的銷售持續增長，其一季度銷售額同比增加16%。根據另一家研究公司高德納(Gartner)的數據，華碩業績上升時，全球PC出貨量下滑了11.2%。
Taiwan’s strengths and weaknesses in pursuing broader innovation echo the challenges of many countries that, for varying reasons, seek to foster the growth of industries based on creativity. The United States and Europe have long worried that their businesses and universities come up with many new ideas but then fail to commercialize them. Mainland China has fretted that its businesses and universities are too slow to come up with original ideas.
台灣在尋求促進創新過程中的優勢和劣勢，與許多國家面臨的挑戰相似。出於各式各樣的原因，這些國家在尋求促進創意產業的增長。長期以來，美國和歐洲一直擔憂，企業和大學產生出許多新創意，可是之後卻沒能把這些創意商業化。中國大陸焦慮的則是，企業和大學拿出新穎創意的步伐過於緩慢。
That Taiwan needs to look to 60-year-olds like Mr. Shih for innovation is a concern for government officials. As economic growth has stalled in Taiwan and new factories have shifted to mainland China, young people have become more interested in civil service jobs and academia instead of industry, government and industry officials say.
台灣需要指望像施崇棠這樣60歲的老人來開展創新，這種窘境引起了政府官員的擔憂。政府和行業官員表示，隨着台灣經濟增長停滯，新建工廠遷往中國大陸，年輕人對公務員和學術界的工作產生了更大的興趣，而非企業界。
One worry is that people with new ideas at universities and government laboratories are not commercializing them. “Our teams think the problem here is those inventors didn’t pursue it, to push their inventions into the market,” said C. Y. Cyrus Chu, the official who oversees the National Science Council. “Youngsters here are afraid to fail — we are not ready to face failure.”
其中一個擔憂是，大學和政府實驗室中，那些有新想法的人沒有並沒有將這些想法商業化。「我們的團隊認為，問題在於那些創造者沒有繼續追求，將自己的發明推向市場，」行政院國家科學委員會主任委員朱敬一(C. Y. Cyrus Chu)說。「這裡的年輕人很怕失敗，我們沒有做好面對失敗的準備。」
The government has started a program of grants and advice for inventors to help them develop business plans and seek venture capital.
政府已經啟動了一個項目，為發明人提供撥款和建議，幫助他們制定商業計劃，尋找風險投資。
Another big concern for industry and government is that the ability of Taiwan’s electronics business to innovate may be hamstrung by the very quality that has brought the sector so far over the last three decades: obsessive secrecy.
產業界和政府的另一大擔憂是，台灣電子企業的創新能力，或許受到其自身某種特點的制約，即過度的保密性。過去30年來，正是這一特點推動了台灣的電子企業取得如此巨大的發展。
American giants like Apple have long treasured the ability of Taiwanese companies like Foxconn to do the final engineering and then mass-produce new consumer electronics devices by the millions, with remarkably few leaks to competitors or to the ravenous technology news media.
富士康等台灣公司能夠進行最後的工程設計，然後數以百萬計地大批量生產新款電子消費品設備，同時還能做到幾乎不向競爭對手，或者貪婪的科技媒體泄漏任何信息，這一點長期以來受到了蘋果等美國大型企業的欣賞。
To win contracts to supply the likes of Hewlett-Packard and Dell, Taiwanese companies routinely divide their staffs and dedicate teams to each foreign customer. They even set procedures for making sure that engineers and buyers from different clients do not accidentally meet one another while having lunch at company cafeterias here.
為贏得合同，成為惠普(Hewlett-Packard)、戴爾(Dell)等公司的供應商，台灣公司通常會將員工分成不同的專項小組，然後讓這些小組為不同外國客戶工作。它們甚至會安排日程，確保針對不同客戶的工程師和採購人員，不會在公司餐廳吃午飯時碰巧遇見。
But that secrecy makes it harder for the Taiwanese industry to learn good ideas quickly from foreign and domestic rivals. “They keep secrets and don’t duplicate, so the customers are happy to work with them,” said Chan Wen-Hsin, a senior industrial technology specialist at the ministry of economic affairs.
但這種保密做法也讓台灣的行業更難快速從國內外競爭對手那裡學到好點子。「為了客戶樂意與他們合作，他們能做到保密，不複製，」台灣經濟部高級工業技術專家詹文鑫(Chan Wen-Hsin)說。
Taiwanese technology companies have decades of experience in accepting detailed computer performance benchmarks from American customers and then figuring out the least expensive way to meet those benchmarks. But that may also be an obstacle to innovation now.
台灣科技公司有着數十年的經驗，能用美國客戶給出的詳細的計算機性能基準值，找到滿足這些標準的最廉價方法。但是現在這可能也成為了創新的障礙。
The least expensive approach often involves barely exceeding the benchmarks, and not coming up with solutions that may cost more but dazzle consumers.
成本最低的方法可能往往意味着剛剛達標，而不是提供一個成本更高但能讓客戶驚艷的解決方案。
Mr. Chan cited a decision by Steven P. Jobs, then Apple’s chief, to choose glass screens for the iPhone instead of plastic ones, which could be scratched.
詹文鑫提到一個史蒂文·P·喬布斯(Steven P. Jobs)的例子。在任蘋果首席執行官時，喬布斯選擇給iPhone使用玻璃屏幕，而不是會出現劃痕的塑料屏幕。
In Taiwan, he said, “we do not pursue a perfect solution; we pursue a good enough solution.”
詹文鑫說，在台灣，「我們不會追求一個完美的解決方案，我們只是追求一個差不多夠好的方案。」
Yet another concern for Taiwan, much more than for the United States, lies in a brain drain of midcareer technology experts who speak fluent Mandarin Chinese. A tripling in the number of mainland Chinese college students over the last decade, to 30 million last year, has produced a severe shortage of professors who speak Mandarin Chinese.
但有一個問題是美國不需要太擔心，但對台灣格外要命的，那就是處於職業發展中期、能說流利普通話的科技專家人才在大量流失。過去10年，中國大陸的大學生數增至原來的三倍，去年達到3000萬人，這導致能說普通話的教授出現嚴重匱乏。
The result has been a series of departures from Taiwan’s universities and elite, government-backed research institutes. Even the vaunted Industrial Technology Research Institute, which gave birth to global semiconductor powerhouses like the Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company and the United Microelectronics Corporation, has been losing experienced researchers.
結果便持續出現了一系列人才從台灣的大學、政府支持的高級研究所流失的現象。連孕育了台積公司(Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company)和聯華電子(United Microelectronics Corporation)等舉世聞名的半導體重鎮的工業技術研究院(Industrial Technology Research Institute)，也在流失資深研究員。
To be sure, Taiwanese companies are now trying to increase the pace of innovation. Acer says that it plans to double research and development spending as a share of sales this year, by 1.2 to 1.5 percent. Gregory Bryant, the general manager of Intel’s Asia and Pacific operations, said, “There is a resurgence of innovation and investment in Taiwan.”
台灣公司的確是在努力加快創新步伐的。宏碁稱計劃將研發開支翻番，使其占銷售量的比例增加1.2%–1.5%。英特爾亞太區總裁宋義瀟(Gregory Bryant)說，「在台灣再度出現了創新和投資的浪潮。」
One lingering cost advantage for Taiwanese companies is that pay here for recent college graduates in electronics engineering and computer sciences is low and gradually declining. A rapid expansion of higher education over the last decade has produced a flood of fresh job applicants. Recent graduates typically earn $800 to $900 a month — more than in mainland China, where the pay is more like $500 to $600 a month, but still much less than in the United States.
台灣公司一直有個成本上的優勢，即當地電子工程及計算機科學行業給新近大學畢業生的薪水很低，而且這一薪水仍在不斷下降。過去10年，高等教育快速擴張產生了一大批應屆求職者。新近大學畢業生通常每月能掙800–900美元——這高於其在中國大陸能拿到的薪水，那裡的工資很可能是每月500–600美元，但和美國的薪水相比，仍低得多。
Japanese automakers opened design centers in the United States as pay in their home market began catching up with American levels, like the Toyota center in Ann Arbor, Mich., and the Nissan center in San Diego. But Taiwanese electronics companies have been wary of following suit. Asked why Asus does not open an engineering center in the United States so as to tap American innovation, Jonathan Tseng, the company’s vice chairman and president, gave a single-word answer: “Cost.”
隨着日本國內市場的薪資水平開始趕上美國，日本的汽車製造商在美國開設了設計中心，比如位於密歇根州安娜堡的豐田(Toyota)技術中心，以及位於聖迭戈的日產(Nissan)中心。但是，台灣電子產品公司一直都很謹慎，不願採取同樣做法。在被問到華碩為何不在美國開設一個設計中心來利用美國的創新資源時，該公司的副董事長兼副總裁曾鏘聲(Jonathan Tseng)只用了一個詞來回答：「成本。」
Asus, a 10,000-employee company, does have an office of 300 employees in Fremont, Calif., which includes sales staff and so-called program managers, who are responsible for pricing, marketing and consumer research.
擁有1萬名員工的華碩，確實在加利福尼亞州弗里蒙特有一個辦公室，那裡有300名員工，包括銷售人員和所謂的項目經理，這些人負責定價、市場營銷和消費者研究。
Analysts say that Asus’s innovations may not represent the same kind of big leaps forward as the iPad or iPhone, but may be what other Taiwan manufacturers need to retain their role in the global consumer electronics industry.
分析人士稱，華碩的創新可能不能和iPad或者iPhone所做出的重大飛躍同日而語，但它可能正是其他台灣製造商要保持其在全球電子消費業的地位所需的。
“It’s incremental, it’s true,” said Tracy Tsai, a Gartner analyst here. “But compared to other device manufacturers, they are doing better.”
「沒錯，這是個漸進的過程，」高德納駐台灣分析師特蕾西·蔡(Tracy Tsai)說。「但和其他設備生產商相比，他們做的算較好的。」
