索尼可能面临拆分

American Investor Targets Sony for a Breakup

ANDREW ROSS SORKIN, MICHAEL J. de la MERCED
2013年5月14日
An American hedge fund billionaire known for starting big fights is planning to call for a breakup of the entertainment and electronic colossus Sony, according to people briefed on the matter, possibly setting off a battle that could roil Japan’s famously staid corporate culture.
据知情人士透露，一名惯于引发巨大纷争的美国对冲基金富豪，正在计划对娱乐和电子业巨头索尼(Sony)发起拆分。此举可能会引发一场大战，撼动日本以古板著称的企业文化。
The call, expected to come Tuesday, will most likely be viewed by government officials and corporate leaders in Tokyo as a shot across the bow from Wall Street, just as Western investors begin piling into Japanese stocks.
这个预计将于周二提出的主张，很可能会被日本的政府官员和企业领袖视为华尔街发出的警告。这段时间，西方投资者正在开始大量买进日本股票。
The hedge fund manager, Daniel S. Loeb, plans to press Sony into spinning off part of its entertainment arm, which includes one of the biggest film studios in Hollywood and one of the largest music labels in the world, responsible for movies like “Skyfall” and artists like Taylor Swift.
对冲基金经理丹尼尔·S·勒布(Daniel S. Loeb)计划迫使索尼剥离其部分娱乐分支业务。索尼的娱乐部门拥有好莱坞最大的电影制片厂之一，以及全球最大的音乐厂牌之一，它们创作了《007：大破天幕杀机》(Skyfall)等电影，签约歌手则包括泰勒·斯威夫特(Taylor Swift)。
Mr. Loeb — known for ousting Yahoo’s former chief executive and poaching Marissa Mayer from Google to run the company — also signaled that he would accept a seat on Sony’s board. His hedge fund has quietly amassed a stake of about 6.5 percent in Sony, making it one of the biggest shareholders.
勒布最为人所知的一项事迹是，赶走雅虎(Yahoo)原有的首席执行官，并从谷歌(Google)挖来玛丽莎·梅耶尔(Marissa Mayer)掌管雅虎。勒布还表示，他会接受索尼董事会的一个席位。他的对冲基金已经悄悄地积攒起了索尼6.5%的股份，成为最大的股东之一。
Still, even big Japanese investors have often faced resistance in seeking changes at companies, a hurdle that may be significantly higher for a foreign hedge fund manager.
然而即使是日本的大投资者，在努力对一家公司实行变革时，也经常会遇到阻力。对于外国对冲基金经理来说，这个障碍可能会高出许多。
Mr. Loeb, 51, the founder of the hedge fund Third Point, flew to Tokyo last weekend for three days of meetings with government officials, regulators and senior Sony executives, according to people briefed on the matter. He is expected on Tuesday to hand-deliver a letter to the company’s chief executive, Kazuo Hirai, that praises a turnaround effort but asks for more.
今年51岁的勒布是对冲基金Third Point的创始人。对情况有所了解的人士透露，上周末他飞往东京，在三天时间里会晤了政府官员、监管者和索尼高管。预计他将于周二亲手向索尼首席执行官平井一夫(Kazuo Hirai)递交一封信函，赞扬他扭转业绩的努力，但要求采取更多举措。
“So while Third Point supports your agenda for change, we also believe that to succeed, Sony must focus,” Mr. Loeb wrote in the letter, which was reviewed by The New York Times.
在《世界经济观察报》阅读了的这封信件中，勒布写道，“尽管Third Point支持您实行变革的议程，但我们也认为，要想取得成功，索尼必须专注。”
The investor believes that spinning off a portion of the entertainment business to Sony shareholders could sharpen the company’s focus and lead to higher profit margins, while helping to revive the core electronics business. Mr. Loeb also plans to press for the spinoff of Sony’s insurance division, which accounted for much of the company’s profit last quarter.
勒布认为，索尼将一部分娱乐业务剥离给股东，可以让该公司更为专注，进而提高利润率，而且还能帮助索尼重振核心的电子产品业务。勒布还计划推动索尼剥离保险部门，该部门上季度为索尼贡献了很大一部分利润。
Mr. Loeb’s campaign is a bet that Japan will prove the next gold mine for global investors. Long hobbled by a lost decade of little economic growth, the country has come to life in recent months under the stewardship of Shinzo Abe, who as prime minister has promoted policies meant to attract private investment. Mr. Loeb is betting that Mr. Abe will expand deregulation.
勒布的行动是在押注日本实际上会成为国际投资者的下一座金矿。在失落的十年里，日本步履蹒跚，经济几乎没有增长。但近几个月，在首相安倍晋三(Shinzo Abe)的领导下，日本又重获生机。安倍晋三推行了意在吸引私人投资的政策，勒布现在赌的是，安倍会进一步放松监管。
“Under Prime Minister Abe’s leadership, Japan can regain its position as one of the world’s pre-eminent economic powerhouses and manufacturing engines,” the hedge fund manager wrote in his letter.
勒布在信中说：“在安倍首相的领导下，日本能够重新获得过去的地位，成为世界最主要的经济和制造业引擎之一。”
Despite its decade-long slump, Sony, the 67-year-old electronics pioneer, remains one of the most prominent companies in Japan, with a market value of roughly $18 billion.
尽管经历了长达十年的疲软时期，但索尼这个已有67年历史的电子产业先锋，仍然是日本最重要的公司之一，市值约有180亿美元。
Still, Mr. Loeb has plenty of ammunition. Shares of Sony have plunged nearly 85 percent over the last 13 years. The company long ago ceded its crown as the king of cool electronics to Apple, and its dominance in televisions was eroded by the emergence of Korean rivals like Samsung and LG.
勒布有着充分的拆分理由。索尼的股份在过去13年里缩水将近85%。该公司在很久之前就被苹果公司(Apple)夺走了时尚电子产品之王的桂冠，而随着三星(Samsung)、LG等韩国对手的出现，该公司在电视产品领域的主导地位也受到削弱。
Last week, Sony reported its first annual profit in five years. But it reached that milestone thanks largely to the weakening yen and some belt-tightening, including the consolidation of businesses and the sale of its American headquarters.
索尼在上周公布，五年来首次获得年度利润。但这一目标的实现在很大程度上依赖的是日渐疲软的日元及一些减支举措，比如合并业务，出售美国总部大楼。
Sony’s chief executive, Mr. Hirai, is scheduled to make a presentation about the company’s turnaround plan next week. He has argued that despite having come late to the era of digital media, the company that made the Walkman, the Trinitron television and the PlayStation can rebound.
索尼首席执行官平井一夫计划将于下周介绍该公司的重整计划。他曾表示，尽管索尼进入数字媒体领域的时间较晚，但那个曾经出产随身听(Walkman)、特丽珑(Trinitron)电视及PlayStation游戏机的公司能够再次创造辉煌。
To Mr. Loeb, more must be done, starting with the spinoff of Sony Entertainment. Though the division accounts for more than 40 percent of the company’s enterprise value, he said in his letter that it needed discipline to raise its profit margins. Mr. Loeb estimated that a partial spinoff of the entertainment business could bolster Sony’s share price by as much as 60 percent.
对于勒布来说，他有很多事情要做，首先是剥离索尼娱乐公司(Sony Entertainment)。虽然该部门贡献了超过40%的企业价值，他在信中表示，索尼需要磨练，以提高其利润率。据勒布估计，部分剥离娱乐业务会为索尼股价带来高达60%的增长。
The company should also consider selling off its 60 percent stake in Sony Financial, which largely sells life insurance policies and has real estate holdings and stakes in other companies, according to the people briefed on the matter. And Mr. Loeb is expected to argue that Sony’s electronics division must slash more costs, including by taking a cue from its protégé, Apple, in focusing on a few core products.
索尼还应该考虑出售索尼金融控股公司(Sony Financial)60%的股份，据知情人士透露，该公司主要出售人寿保险，持有房地产资产，并在其他公司拥有股份。预计勒布将会提出，索尼的电子部门必须大幅削减成本，比如效仿其门徒苹果公司，专攻一些核心产品。
Recently, Mr. Loeb has publicly expressed his interest in Japan. Referring to the changes by the Abe government, he called it “a huge game change” at an industry conference last week. “And there’s a lot more room to go,” he added.
最近，勒布曾公开表达了对日本的兴趣。在上周的行业会议上，勒布将安倍晋三内阁所做的变革称为“大变局”。“而且存在更多改变的空间，”他补充道。
Mr. Abe has called his revival effort a plan of “three arrows,” including aggressive monetary easing by the Bank of Japan and enormous stimulus spending by the government.
安倍晋三将其重振经济的举措称为“三箭”计划，其中包括日本银行(Bank of Japan)实施的激进货币宽松政策，以及政府推出的规模巨大的经济刺激计划。
So far, that effort appears to have drawn investor plaudits. The yen weakened in value last week, to 100 to the dollar, a level unseen in four years, helping local companies like Sony and Toyota. And the Nikkei 225-stock index has risen 43 percent so far this year. During the same time two years ago, the Nikkei was down 5.7 percent.
截至目前，这些举措似乎受到了投资者的欢迎。上周，日元已经贬值至100日元兑1美元，达到4年来最低水平，而这对索尼和丰田等本土公司有利。今年以来，日经225指数(Nikkei 225-stock index)已上涨43%。而两年前的同一段时间，该指数下跌了5.7%。
But it is the third arrow in that quiver that has Mr. Loeb’s attention. The Abe government hopes to shed Japan’s reputation as a land of strict hierarchy and bureaucracy. Business mistakes were often seen as shameful, and outright confrontation largely disdained.
但是勒布所关注的是安倍箭鞘里的第三支箭。日本向来被人们认为是有着严格的等级制度和官僚体制的国家，而安倍内阁想让日本摆脱这一印象。在日本，商业错误经常会被看作是丢脸的，而直接的对抗很多时候会遭鄙视。
“There’s an entrenched management culture there,” said Lawrence B. Lindsey, a former top economist in the administration of George W. Bush. “Activists aren’t particularly popular here among management, and they won’t be popular in Japan either.”
“那里有种根深蒂固的管理文化，”乔治·W·布什(George W. Bush)政府前首席经济学家劳伦斯·B·林赛(Lawrence B. Lindsey)说，“在这里的管理层内，‘活动人士’并不特别受欢迎，他们在日本也不会受欢迎。”
No less than Howard Stringer, Sony’s own chairman, has criticized the status quo.
对于目前的状况，索尼的董事长霍华德·斯特林格(Howard Stringer)同样颇有微词。
“Japan is a harmonious society which cherishes its social values, including full employment,” he said in a speech last year. “That leads to conflicts in a world where shareholder value calls for ever greater efficiency.”
“日本是一个和谐社会，它珍视自己的社会价值观，其中就包括充分就业，”霍华德在去年的一次演讲中说道，“而在当今世界，对股东来说重要的是不断提升的效率，这就出现了矛盾。”
Yet there have been changes. The percentage of foreign ownership in the Tokyo Stock Exchange’s companies nearly quintupled from 1990 to 2008, to 24 percent. And Japanese shareholders have increasingly adopted the aggressive tactics of Western fund managers.
然而，改变一直在发生。从1990年到2008年，东京证券交易所(Tokyo Stock Exchange)上市公司的外国所有权比例几乎上涨至原来水平的四倍，达到24%。而日本股东也开始越来越多地采用西方基金经理的积极策略。
Sony is the biggest bet yet for Mr. Loeb, an intense California native who built his name largely upon acidly written letters, berating targets for mismanagement and calling for change.
不过，对于勒布而言，索尼仍是他迄今为止最大的赌注。出生于加利福尼亚州的勒布为人严肃认真，主要以撰写言辞尖刻的信函而出名，他会抨击目标对象管理不善，并呼吁对方做出改变。
The strategy has proved profitable. Third Point’s returns are up 13.3 percent so far this year and up 2.6 percent for the first week of May. Forbes estimates Mr. Loeb’s net worth at about $1.5 billion.
事实证明，这种策略是能带来收益的。今年以来，Third Point的利润增长了13.3%，并在5月的第一周实现2.6%的增长。《福布斯》(Forbes)估计，勒布的净资产约为15亿美元。
Perhaps his most prominent victory has been Third Point’s investment in Yahoo, where Mr. Loeb pushed for the dismissal of a chief executive after exposing the executive for falsifying academic credentials.
或许勒布所取得的最受瞩目的胜利是Third Point对雅虎的投资，当时，在揭露一名首席执行官学历造假后，勒布促使该高管被解职。
Mindful of Japanese decorum, however, Mr. Loeb strikes a more conciliatory tone in his letter to Mr. Hirai of Sony. His calls are couched as suggestions aimed at improving the company, rather than aggressive demands.
然而，考虑到日本重视礼仪，在写给索尼首席执行官平井一夫的信中，勒布采用了较为和缓的语气。他通过提出旨在改善公司状况的建议，而不是咄咄逼人的要求，发出了委婉呼吁。
“Third Point would not have made this substantial investment if we did not believe in a bright future for Sony’s global brand, superior technology, and dedicated employees,” he wrote. “We are confident that by acting as partners, Sony will grow stronger.”
“如果我们不相信索尼作为全球品牌的光明未来、其先进的技术以及勤勉尽职的雇员，Third Point不会进行这一可观投资，”勒布写道，“我们相信通过合作，索尼将进一步发展壮大。”
