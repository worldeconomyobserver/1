電池測試獲批，波音787將試飛

F.A.A. Backs Boeing Plan for Battery Test

CHRISTOPHER DREW, JAD MOUAWAD
2013年3月13日
Boeing achieved a major milestone on Tuesday as the Federal Aviation Administration approved its plan to test fixes to the battery problems that have grounded its 787 jets since mid-January.
波音公司(Boeing)在周二迎來了一個重要的里程碑。聯邦航空管理局（Federl Aviation Administration，簡稱FAA）批准了波音對其電池問題的解決方案進行測試。自今年1月中旬以來，波音787客機因電池故障而停飛。
Boeing’s new battery design includes better protection in case a battery overheats. The F.A.A. could still demand changes if problems develop in the laboratory and flight tests. While Boeing hopes to begin fitting its redesigned batteries in the grounded 787 fleet by mid- to late April and resume commercial flights quickly after that, government officials are not sure that the process will move that fast.
波音提出的新的電池設計包括，安裝更好的保護設施，在電池過熱時提供保護。如果實驗室測試和飛行測試中仍發現問題，FAA可能還會要求波音再做修改。波音希望在四月中下旬前開始將重新設計的電池安裝到停飛的787客機上，之後迅速恢復商業航班，但政府官員並不確定整個過程能否如此迅速。
Still, the decision to start the tests is a big step in Boeing’s efforts to put the innovative jets back in the air. The 50 787s delivered to airlines have been grounded since mid-January after two planes developed battery-related problems — one battery caught fire while the plane was parked in Boston and another began to smoke and forced an emergency landing in Japan.
不過，開始測試的決定本身，就是波音公司努力讓這款新型飛機重返藍天的重要一步。1月中旬，兩架787客機出現了與電池有關的問題，一架停在波士頓的飛機發生電池起火事故，另一架飛機的電池開始冒煙，並在日本迫降。隨後，已經交付航空公司的50架787型客機全部停飛。
Since then, Boeing engineers have been scrambling to insulate the eight battery cells, build a sturdier battery case and create a smoke-venting system to quell concerns about the battery’s safety and persuade regulators to lift the grounding order. Federal and industry officials said the new plan includes fiberglass-like insulation between the battery’s cells to keep the plane’s vibrations from adding to the chance of a short-circuit in one of the cells cascading to the others.
隨後，波音的工程師緊急採取措施隔絕電池組的八個電池單元、製造更堅固的電池外殼、並加裝一個排煙系統，以期打消人們對電池安全的擔憂，並說服監管機構解除停飛令。聯邦官員和航空業官員表示，新計劃包括在電池單元之間添加類似玻璃纖維的隔絕裝置，降低由於飛機震動，致使一個電池單元短路蔓延到其他單元的風險。
They said the batteries would be enclosed in stainless steel boxes, better resistant to higher temperatures than the earlier aluminum ones. Boeing also plans to create tubes made out of titanium to vent any hazardous gases outside the plane.
他們表示，這些電池將被放進不鏽鋼箱子里，其抗高溫能力好於之前的鋁製箱。波音還計劃安裝一些鈦排氣管，將危險氣體排到飛機外。
Adding the insulation would space the cells further apart. Boeing would also add systems to monitor the temperature and activity in each cell.
添加隔絕裝置，會使電池間的距離擴大。波音還會添加一些系統，監測各電池單元內部的溫度和活動。
But the new plan, which calls for 20 tests that would place the battery under stress, could also sharpen a debate among some safety experts over how certain Boeing and the F.A.A. can be that the changes will eliminate the risk of smoke or fire in the batteries. Investigators in the United States and Japan have not been able to determine the precise cause of the battery problems. 
新計劃需要進行20次測試，測試電池在壓力下的表現。但它可能也會加劇一些安全專家的爭論：波音和FAA能在多大程度上確信這些改動能消除電池冒煙或起火的風險？美國和日本的調查人員尚未確定引發電池故障的確切原因。
“This comprehensive series of tests will show us whether the proposed battery improvements will work as designed,” said Ray LaHood, the transportation secretary. “We won’t allow the plane to return to service unless we’re satisfied that the new design ensures the safety of the aircraft and its passengers.”
“這一系列的全面測試，將讓我們看到，波音提交的電池改進方案能否像設計的那樣發揮作用，”美國交通部長雷·拉胡德(Ray LaHood)說。“只有新設計能夠確保飛機和乘客的安全，能夠讓我們滿意，我們才會允許飛機重新投入飛行。”
The National Transportation Safety Board has found that a short circuit in one cell caused a battery in the Boston incident to overheat and burst into flame on Jan. 7.
國家運輸安全委員會(National Transportation Safety Board)發現，在今年1月7日發生在波士頓的事故中，飛機上一個電池單元發生短路，導致一塊電池組過熱起火。
But investigators in Japan have raised the possibility that a battery on another 787 nine days later started smoldering because it might have been hit by a surge of electrical current from another part of the plane.
但日本的調查人員提出了這樣一種可能性：在九天後的另一架787客機事故中，機上電池開始冒煙，可能是受到了飛機上其他部件的電涌衝擊。
Jay Whitacre, an associate professor of materials science and engineering at Carnegie Mellon University, said Boeing’s approach was technically reasonable even if the cause of the battery fire is not known. Boeing’s system, he said, was designed to contain any failure of a single cell and prevent it from spreading to the rest of the plane.
卡內基梅隆大學(Carnegie Mellon University)材料科學與工程副教授傑伊·惠特克(Jay Whitacre)表示，儘管電池起火原因尚不得而知，但波音的做法從技術上講是合理的。他說，波音的系統設計目的是限制任何一個電池單元的故障，避免故障蔓延到飛機的其他部分。
“They are taking a systemic approach, and not a find-the-problem approach,” he said. “They also recognize that figuring out what went wrong is a very complicated question, like a crime scene investigation. Meanwhile, they are focused on getting back the planes in the air.”
“他們採取的是系統性方法，而不是查找問題的方法，”他說。“他們也明白，找出究竟哪裡發生了問題是一個非常複雜的問題，就像犯罪現場調查一樣。同時，他們關注的是讓飛機重返藍天。”
But not everyone was comfortable with this approach.
但並不是所有人都對這種方式表示滿意。
“I think the real issue here is to develop a robust storage system that is immune to fire,” said Donald Sadoway, a materials chemistry professor at M.I.T. “I am not hearing anything about how to make that battery fire-resistant.”
“我想真正的問題是，研發一種防火的、堅固的存儲系統，”麻省理工學院(MIT)材料化學教授唐納德·薩多維(Donald Sadoway)說，“我沒有聽到有任何讓電池防火的內容。”
The long-awaited announcement by the F.A.A. helped propel Boeing’s stock on Tuesday to $84.16, a five-year high. The confidence of investors was also buoyed by earlier reports of a $15 billion order by Ryanair for 170 single-aisle 737s and Boeing’s decision to move forward with a new version of its best-selling twin-aisle 777.
FAA期待已久的宣布幫助推動波音股價在周二上漲到84.16美元（約合523元人民幣），達到五年來的新高。其他提升投資者信心的因素還有，較早前有報道稱，瑞安航空(Ryanair)以150億美元的價格訂購了170架單過道的波音737飛機，此外，波音也決定繼續開發新版本的777機型，這款雙過道客機是波音銷量最好機型。
Federal and industry officials said the tests would subject the battery to the most extreme conditions it was likely to face and see if the battery case could withstand a battery explosion. The lab tests will include bursts of power to stress the battery and check its flammability as well as how it performs in hot weather. 
聯邦和業內官員表示，測試過程中電池會經受可能會面臨的最極端的條件，從而檢查電池盒是否能夠承受電池爆炸的情況。實驗室測試中還會包括向電池施加電涌，在受壓狀態下測驗其可燃性，以及在炎熱天氣下的性能。
The F.A.A. has also approved limited test flights for two aircraft. One plane will conduct tests on the old battery, and the other will test how the new system performs under normal flight conditions. The flight tests will begin within a week. 
FAA也批准了對兩架飛機進行有限的試飛。一架飛機將裝配舊電池進行飛行，另一架則會在常規飛行條件下測試新系統的性能。試飛將於一周內開始。
Boeing officials said they thought they had identified the most likely ways in which the batteries could fail. They contend that the changes would virtually eliminate the chances of future incidents and protect the plane and its passengers if a problem does arise.
波音公司管理人員表示，他們已經找到了電池出故障最有可能的原因。他們稱，提出的改進措施能基本消除將來發生事故的可能性，並在出現問題時保護飛機和乘客的安全。
Boeing said it also would require its Japanese battery maker, GS Yuasa, to improve its quality control procedures to guard against possible manufacturing defects.
波音表示，也會要求該公司的日本電池製造商GS Yuasa改進質量控制流程，從而避免發生可能的製造缺陷。
Some battery experts said they would feel more comfortable if a precise cause were found and the problems that happened on the two flights in January could be replicated in a lab.
一些電池專家表示，如果能夠找到確切的事故原因，兩架飛機在1月發生的事故能夠在實驗室里重現的話，他們會覺得更有信心。
“The tests have to be comprehensive insofar that they capture the conditions that started these fires,” Professor Sadoway said. “How is it that Boeing managed to log more than 50,000 flight hours on the 787 and didn’t manage to turn up any of the problems we are seeing today?” 
“這些測試必須要全面，能找到引發起火的原因，”薩多維說，“波音787客機飛行了超過5萬小時，他們卻沒有發現我們今天看到的這樣的問題。這是為什麼呢？”
Approval of the changes is a highly political process. Mr. LaHood is trying to balance safety concerns with the interest at Boeing and in the airline industry in getting the planes flying again. Perceptions of the traveling public also loom large as Boeing tries to restore confidence in the 787, known as the Dreamliner for its use of new technologies that reduce fuel costs by 20 percent.
批准這些改進措施，也是一個政治色彩很濃的過程。拉胡德正在努力地平衡安全顧慮，以及波音公司和航空業讓飛機重返藍天的利益考量。在波音努力恢復人們對787型飛機的信心的同時，廣大乘客的看法也很重要。因為使用了新技術，787客機將燃料消耗降低了20%，因此也被稱作“夢幻客機”。
