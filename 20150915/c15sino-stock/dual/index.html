分析：中国政府在股市动荡中发挥了什么作用

Assessing the Chinese Government’s Role in the Stock Markets

黄安伟, MIA LI, KIKI ZHAO
2015年9月15日
China’s stock market crisis rolls on. On Monday, markets experienced their biggest drops since late August. Throughout the summer, the Chinese government intervened forcefully to try to slow or reverse the fall, which many economic analysts say is a necessary and long-overdue correction. The government has spent as much as $235 billion to buy shares and bolster the indexes. More alarming for investors, it has placed strict limits on selling shares and ordered the police to investigate short-sellers, hedge funds and investment firms suspected of stoking the market downturn. A journalist for Caijing, a respected newsmagazine, was detained and forced to apologize on state television for writing an article that the authorities said may have harmed the market.
中国股市危机持续发酵。周一，股市创下了8月下旬以来的最大跌幅。整个夏天，中国政府极力干预股市，试图减缓或逆转跌势，但很多经济分析师说这种下挫是必要的调整，早就应该出现。政府已经花费了高达2350亿美元购买股票，试图推高股指。更令投资者担忧的是，中国已经对出售股票实施了严格限制，并下令警方调查涉嫌造成行情下滑的空头、对冲基金和投资公司。在声望卓著的新闻杂志《财经》，一位记者由于撰写了一篇文章而遭到羁押，并被迫在官方电视台上道歉。当局说，那篇文章可能给市场带来了不利影响。
The message is clear: Anything that drives down the market is a potential criminal act.
讯息很明确：造成股市下挫的任何行为都有可能是犯罪行为。
Several Chinese analysts recently shared their views with The New York Times on the government’s attempts to manage the market:
对于政府管控股市的做法，中国的一些分析人士近日与《世界经济观察报》分享了他们的观点：
He Liping, chairman of the department of finance at Beijing Normal University
贺力平，北京师范大学金融系主任
“The government seems to want the market to go up no matter what. The government has issued policies to encourage companies to maintain high stock prices that are not entirely sustainable. The policies include encouraging mergers and the issuing of more cash dividends, and banning the sell-off of parts of companies. Mergers might boost stock prices for a while, but after investors realize the merged company doesn’t perform much better, the stock prices will fall back down.”
“政府好像无论如何都要经济上行，从政策层面上出了一些现在看来有不可持续性的提振股价的政策，比如说鼓励兼并，鼓励现金分红，不鼓励资产出售等等。兼并可能短期对提振股价有用，但是投资者一旦看到新的公司业绩并没有增长，股价还是会回到以前的水平。
“I also don’t see why the government expects its crackdown on short-sellers to improve market performance. Market performance eventually depends on investors’ individual judgment and on how well companies perform.”
“我看不到政府打击做空和提振行情之间有任何联系。行情还是要看投资者个人对公司业绩的判断。”
Ye Tan, financial writer
叶檀，财经作家
“Earlier, the government made some mistakes. It gave free rein to some behaviors because it didn’t know how much margin trading was taking place outside the stock market. It loosened its control over risks, because it didn’t fully acknowledge the risks. But after the government noticed the risks, it had to rescue the market and keep it from collapsing. I don’t think that was wrong.”
“政府事实上此前的做法有错误的地方。他并不了解场外的配资是多少，放任了一些行为。它在不了解风险的情况下放纵了风险。但是当它发现风险之后，必须要从崩溃里面救出来。我觉得这个没错。”
Liu Shengjun, executive deputy director of the Lujiazui Institute of International Finance at the China Europe International Business School
刘胜军，中欧陆家嘴国际金融研究院副院长
“There is no evidence to prove that there is systematic risk. Regarding the government’s methods of trying to rescue the market, we’re unable to understand it. The government is in a panic. It has ignored the law and consequences, and the results aren’t good.”
“没有证据表明股市存在系统性风险。对于政府试图救市的方法，我们无法理解。政府处在恐慌之中，它忽视了法律和后果，救市的效果也并不好。
“It feels like the government will not give up until it reaches its goal and that it has to save the market no matter what it takes.”
“政府仿佛不达目的不罢休，感觉不管采取什么手段都要救。”
Yu Yongding, director of the Institute of World Economics and Politics at the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences
余永定，中国社科院世界经济与政治研究所所长
“The current stock market unrest does not constitute a systematic crisis, and so the government should not intervene. What is happening to China’s stock market now is not panic selling but a re-evaluation of the prices. It is inevitable that there is some overshooting in this process, but the government should not take the place of the market and put prices on stocks.”
“中国目前的股市动荡并不涉及系统性危机，所以政府不应该干预。现在股市发生的不是恐慌性抛售，而是对股价进行价值重估。在这个过程中超调是难以避免的，政府不应该取代市场对股票定价。”
Mao Yushi, honorary director at the Unirule Institute of Economics
茅于轼，天则经济研究所名誉理事长
“From the very beginning, China’s stock market hasn’t been acting in the way that a normal stock market would. China’s stock buyers are opportunists rather than investors. People rush to buy when they see the government has certain policies. Everyone is an opportunist in the stock market, and it becomes a gamble.”
“从一开始，中国股市就不是按正常股市那样运作的。中国股民不是投资者，而是机会主义者。看到政府出台了一定的政策，人们就会抢着买入。在股市里，每个人都机会主义者，股市就变成了一场赌博。
“When China’s stock market was set up, its aim was to help state-owned enterprises raise money. It went wrong at the very beginning.”
“中国股市建立之初，目的是帮助国有企业筹资。它从一开始就有问题。”

黄安伟(Edward Wong)是《世界经济观察报》北京分社社长。
