川普宣布對600億美元中國商品徵收關稅

Trump Hits China With Stiff Trade Measures

MARK LANDLER, JIM TANKERSLEY
2018年3月23日
President Trump said he would impose tariffs on about $60 billion worth of Chinese imports on Thursday as the White House moved to punish China for what it says is a pattern of co-opting American technology and trade secrets and robbing companies of jobs and billions of dollars in revenue.
隨著白宮著手懲罰中國，川普總統週四表示將對價值約600億美元（約合3800億元人民幣）的中國進口商品徵收關稅。白宮稱，中國採取的是一種一邊竊取美國的科技和商業機密，一邊搶奪美國企業的工作崗位和數十億美元收入的模式。
The measures come as the White House grants a long list of exemptions to American allies from steel and aluminum tariffs that go into effect on Friday, including the European Union, which has lobbied aggressively and publicly for relief from the trade action.
出台這些措施的同時，白宮列出了一份長長的名單，對包括歐盟在內的美國盟友，免徵於週五生效的鋼鐵和鋁材關稅。歐盟一直在積極遊說，並公開呼籲這一貿易行動不要波及自己。
“The word that I want to use is reciprocal,” Mr. Trump said in announcing the tariffs in the Diplomatic Room of the White House. “If they charge us, we charge them the same thing.”
「我想用的詞是對等，」川普在白宮外交廳宣布徵收關稅時說。「他們收我們多少稅，我們也要向他們收多少稅。」
The China tariffs are his strongest trade action yet against a country he has branded an “economic enemy.” They fulfill one of his core campaign pledges, to demand more reciprocal deals with trading partners around the world.
針對中國的關稅，是他迄今對一個被他標為「經濟敵人」的國家所採取的最強硬貿易行動。它們兌現了他的核心競選承諾之一，即要求與世界各地的貿易夥伴進行更加對等的交易。
But coupled with the administration’s decision to exempt the European Union, South Korea, Brazil, Canada, and Mexico from the tariffs on cheap metals, the action demonstrates how much Mr. Trump’s nationalist trade agenda is really targeted at a single country: China.
但結合聯邦政府決定對歐盟、韓國、巴西、加拿大和墨西哥的廉價金屬免稅，表明川普的民族主義貿易議程在多大程度上真正針對的只有一個國家：中國。
“What the United States is doing is strategically defending itself from China’s economic aggression,” said Peter Navarro, director of the White House National Trade Council and an architect of the measures. “We repeatedly aired our concerns about China as a nonmarket economy.”
「美國正在做的是從戰略上保護自己不受中國的經濟侵略，」設計了這些措施的白宮全國貿易委員會(National Trade Council)主任彼得·納瓦羅(Peter Navarro)說。「我們一再表示對中國作為一個非市場經濟體的擔憂。」
The tariffs, which the United States trade representative will publish within 15 days, will target 1,300 lines of Chinese goods — everything from shoes and clothing to electronics, administration officials said.
聯邦政府官員稱，美國貿易代表將在15天內公布的關稅，將把1300種中國商品作為目標，從鞋服到電子產品，無所不包。
Mr. Trump, the officials said, will also direct the Treasury Department to impose restrictions on Chinese investment in American technology companies — a practice that they said the Chinese government uses to develop its own “national champions” in cutting-edge industries like artificial intelligence and autonomous vehicles.
官員稱，川普還將指示財政部限制中國投資美國的科技公司。他們說，中國政府利用這種方式發展自己在人工智慧和自動駕駛車輛等尖端行業的「龍頭企業」。
Robert Lighthizer, the United States trade representative, told the Senate Finance Committee on Thursday that he had recommended the forthcoming actions against China include tariffs on Chinese products from all of the advanced industries the country has vowed to build up as part of its “Made in China 2025” plan. Those industries include electric vehicles, high-tech shipping and aerospace technology. Mr. Lighthizer called them “the ones I care about” for tariff purposes.
美國貿易代表羅伯特·萊特希澤(Robert Lighthizer)週四對參議院金融委員會(Senate Finance Committee)表示，即將到來的針對中國的行動，包括對中國商品徵收關稅，是他建議的。這些產品涉及中國在《中國製造2025》計劃中發誓要發展的所有先進行業，包括電動汽車、高科技船舶和航空航天技術。萊特希澤稱它們才是他建議徵稅「所關心的東西」。
“The end objective of this is to get China to modify its unfair trade practices,” Everett Eissenstat, the deputy director of the National Economic Council, said in a telephone call with reporters.
「最終的目標是讓中國改變它不公平的貿易做法，」國家經濟委員會(National Economic Council)副主任埃弗雷特·伊森史塔(Everett Eissenstat)在接受記者的電話採訪時說。
Mr. Navarro cast the tariffs as part of a seminal shift in how the United States views China. Rather than trying to draw it into the rules-based international economic order — a policy that dates back to Richard M. Nixon and Henry A. Kissinger — the United States now regards China as a strategic competitor, bent on eroding American security and prosperity.
納瓦羅稱關稅是美國對中國的看法發生重大轉變的一部分。美國現在視中國為一心想侵蝕美國的安全和繁榮的戰略競爭對手，而不是試圖將其納入以規則為基礎的國際經濟秩序——這一政策可追溯到理查德·M·尼克森(Richard M. Nixon)和亨利·A·季辛吉(Henry A. Kissinger)。
But the moves come at a time when Mr. Trump has enlisted President Xi Jinping of China to help pressure North Korea over its nuclear and ballistic missile programs. Last year, Mr. Trump said he had decided not to designate China as a currency manipulator, in part because China was cooperating in the pressure campaign.
但出台這些舉措時，川普已請求中國國家主席習近平幫助就核武器和彈道導彈計劃向朝鮮施壓。去年，川普稱已決定不把中國列為匯率操縱國，這在一定程度上是因為中國正在配合施壓運動。
Mr. Trump’s explicit linkage of trade and security raises questions about whether the tensions from these tariffs will spill over into the North Korea issue.
川普明確地把貿易和安全聯繫在一起的做法，引起了對這些關稅引發的緊張關係是否會擴大到朝鮮問題上的疑問。
The prospect of growing trade tensions between the world’s two largest economies spooked global financial markets.
全球最大的兩個經濟體之間的貿易緊張局勢可能會加劇這一點，令全球金融市場陷入恐慌。
The Stoxx Europe 600 index fell by nearly 2 percent. Germany’s DAX fell by more than 2 percent as investors digested the threat to the country’s export-driven economy. In the United States, the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index dropped by more than 1.5 percent.
斯托克歐洲600(Stoxx Europe 600)指數下跌近2%。在德國，隨著投資者意識到該國出口驅動型經濟面臨威脅，DAX指數下跌逾2%。在美國，標準普爾500股指(Standard & Poor\'s 500-stock index)下跌超過1.5%。
Boeing, one of America’s largest exporters, declined 3.8 percent. Money flowed to government bonds as investors sought safety, briefly driving yields on the benchmark 10-year Treasury note down below 2.8 percent. Yields move in the opposite direction of bond prices. Crude oil futures slipped 0.8 percent.
美國最大的出口商之一波音(Boeing)股價下跌3.8%。隨著投資者尋求安全，資金流向政府債券，短時間內促使十年期基準國債的收益率跌至不足2.8%。債券的收益率與價格走勢相反。原油期貨下挫0.8%。
