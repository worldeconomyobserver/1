外企憂心中國強勢推進反壟斷執法

China’s Energetic Enforcement of Antitrust Rules Alarms Foreign Firms

NEIL GOUGH, 儲百亮, NICK WINGFIELD
2014年8月11日
HONG KONG — When almost 100 government antitrust investigators simultaneously marched into four of Microsoft’s offices across China late last month, they were not looking for tea and gossip.
香港——上個月末，當近100名政府反壟斷調查人員同時走進微軟(Microsoft)在中國不同地區的四個辦公地點時，他們可不是來喝茶聊天的。
In what Microsoft characterized internally as “surprise visits,” the agents from China’s State Administration for Industry and Commerce interrogated a company vice president and other senior managers, copied contracts and financial records, and downloaded large amounts of data from the company’s computer servers, including emails and other internal communications.
在被微軟內部稱為「突訪」的上述活動中，國家工商總局的調查人員對公司的一位副總裁和其他高管進行了質詢，複印了一些合同和財務記錄，從公司的計算機服務器下載了大量數據，其中包括電子郵件等內部通訊記錄。
The regulators’ increasing application of the antimonopoly law, said Scott Kennedy, director of the Research Center for Chinese Politics & Business at Indiana University, embodies the party leadership’s efforts to overhaul the economy without abandoning control of the levers of power.
印第安納大學中國政治與商務研究中心(Research Center for Chinese Politics & Business at Indiana University)主任甘思德(Scott Kennedy)說，監管機構加大了反壟斷執法力度，這代表中共領導層在不放棄權力槓桿的前提下，全面整改經濟的努力。
“China’s not moving towards a free market, but it’s moving towards a wider palette of regulatory tools,” Mr. Kennedy said. “These aren’t meant to create a level playing field; they obviously want the field to be slanted, but they want to use what I’d guess we call more sophisticated tools.”
「中國沒有朝着自由市場邁進，而是開始使用更多種類的監管工具，」肯尼迪說。「這些行動不是為了創造公平的競爭環境；他們顯然想讓競爭環境向某些群體傾斜，我認為他們想要使用一些在我們看來更高級的工具。」
Foreign companies worry that the investigations could represent the rise of a newer, subtler form of protectionism, one cloaked in regulatory impartiality but intended primarily to promote Chinese companies, especially the big, powerful state-owned companies. The government contends that it is using the antimonopoly law, first established in 2008, to protect the interests of consumers against price gouging and other abuses.
外資企業擔心，這些調查行動可能意味着一種更新、更不易察覺的保護主義的抬頭，它披着公正監管的外衣，主要目的卻是推動中國企業的發展，尤其是強大的大型國有企業。中國政府則聲稱，自己正在利用2008年首次頒佈的反壟斷法，保護消費者的利益，使其免受價格欺詐或其他類型的侵害。
“If China is going to be the third leg in the global antitrust regime, along with the U.S. and the E.U., and that’s clearly coming, then the key question is, What sort of approach is China going to take?” John Frisbie, president of the US-China Business Council in Washington, said in a telephone interview. “Is it going to be more the socialist state-run model, or is it going to be more of a market- and consumer-oriented model, or something in between? I don’t think we know the answer yet.”
「如果中國將成為美國和歐盟之外，國際反壟斷體系的第三條腿，現實也的確如此，那麼關鍵的問題是，中國將採取何種模式？」華盛頓美中貿易全國委員會(US-China Business Council)主席傅強恩(John Frisbie)在電話採訪中說。「它將更偏向於社會主義的、國家運營的模式，亦或以市場和消費者為中心的模式，還是介於兩者之間？我覺得我們還不知道答案。」
Not everything in China is stacked against multinationals, some of which may well have engaged in questionable practices and often have their own political allies and business partners. Nor is China the only country where Microsoft and other companies have faced consumer ire and regulatory scrutiny. But multinationals appear to be facing new and substantial challenges in navigating a clutch of separate regulatory agencies across China, each keen to amass victories, and the fines and political kudos that go with them.
對於跨國公司來說，中國的環境中並非全是不利因素。有些跨國公司可能是存在大量可疑操作，常常擁有自己的政治盟友和商業夥伴。微軟等企業也並非只在中國遇到了消費者的不滿和監管部門的嚴密審查。但在應付中國各地大量彼此獨立的監管機構時，跨國企業似乎正面臨新的重大挑戰。這些機構都渴望着執法上的勝利，以及隨之而來的罰款和政治榮譽。
“China has a very large bureaucracy, but each agency has its incentives and missions, so when they enforce the law, they try to maximize their own interests,” said Angela Zhang, a lecturer in law at King’s College London, who studies Chinese antimonopoly law. “But I wouldn’t underestimate the power of some really large multinational companies, because these companies are also very deep-pocketed and have very good connections in China, and they can also do the lobbying.”
「中國擁有一個非常龐大的官僚系統，每個機構都有自己的動機和使命，因此他們在執法時，會設法使自己的利益最大化，」倫敦國王學院(King\'s College London)研究中國反壟斷法的法律系講師張湖月(Angela Zhang)說。「但我不會低估一些規模非常大的跨國公司的實力，因為這些公司財力很雄厚，在中國有強大的背景，他們也可以進行遊說。」
Over the last few weeks, investigators have been particularly busy — and particularly public — in their efforts, setting a pattern of making abrupt searches of foreign companies’ offices. These are then reported by local media, tersely confirmed by the companies and followed by the government declaring that the companies are suspected of violating pricing, distribution and bundling rules, harming Chinese businesses and consumers.
過去幾周里，調查機構的行動格外密集，也格外大張旗鼓，常常突擊搜查外資企業的辦公室。這些行動隨後會被當地媒體報道，被企業簡要地證實，然後政府會宣布，這些公司涉嫌違反定價、經銷和捆綁銷售方面的規定，有損中國企業和消費者的利益。
Last Wednesday, the State Administration of Industry and Commerce, or S.A.I.C., conducted a follow-up raid on two Microsoft offices in China and searched Accenture’s offices in the northeastern city of Dalian. Last week, officials at the National Development and Reform Commission searched the Shanghai offices of Daimler, which makes Mercedes-Benz cars, and said they planned to fine Chrysler and Audi.
上周三，國家工商總局對微軟在華的兩個辦公地點進行了後續突擊檢查，並搜查了埃森哲(Accenture)在中國東北的大連市的辦公室。上周，國家發改委搜查了戴姆勒公司(Daimler)在上海的辦公室，並表示將對克萊斯勒(Chrysler)和奧迪(Audi)實施罰款。戴姆勒是梅賽德斯-奔馳(Mercedes-Benz)的製造商。
The reform commission enforces pricing violations of China’s antimonopoly law and has been investigating how automakers price the spare parts that are sold by their distributors in China. In response to the investigation, BMW said on Friday that it would lower parts prices in China. Similar component price cuts have been made recently by Mercedes and Audi.
發改委負責監管違反中國反壟斷法的定價違規行為，一直在調查一些車企對由經銷商在華銷售的汽車配件的定價情況。作為對調查的回應，寶馬(BMW)周五表示，將下調零部件價格。奔馳和奧迪最近也採取了類似的降價措施。
The reform commission is one of three agencies with a role in enforcing the antimonopoly law, which includes rules against unfair pricing and sales restrictions, even when a company has plenty of competitors.
發改委是參與執行反壟斷法的三家機構之一。反壟斷法禁止不公平定價和銷售限制，即使一家企業擁有足夠多的競爭者。
The Ministry of Commerce vets domestic and overseas mergers and acquisitions to ensure that they don’t give any one company dominance over the market for a particular good or service in the country. The ministry has expanded the number of deals it examines — reaching decisions on 207 cases last year, an increase of 26 percent from 2012, and imposing significant conditions on those transactions.
商務部會對國內和國外的企業併購進行核審，確保不會有哪家企業通過併購控制住國內某種商品或服務的市場。這種對併購交易的審核數量在增多——去年就207宗併購案做出了裁決，比2012年多了26%，給這些交易制定了明確的條件。
Alongside the commerce ministry, S.A.I.C. and the reform commission have taken on a higher profile in antimonopoly cases since last year. The commission in August of last year fined six companies that sell infant milk powder a total of $109 million, accusing them of price fixing and anticompetitive behavior. Five of the companies, including Mead Johnson Nutrition of the United States, were foreign and the other was based in Hong Kong.
除了商務部，工商總局和發改委從去年開始也更高調地參與到了反壟斷案件中。發改委在去年8月對六家嬰兒奶粉公司開出了總額1.09億美元的罰單，指責它們存在價格壟斷和反競爭行為。其中有五家公司是外企，包括美國的美贊臣(Mead Johnson Nutrition)，剩下的一家總部位於香港。
Qualcomm has also been subject to an antimonopoly investigation by the reform commission since at least late last year, as have dozens of foreign pharmaceutical companies.
針對高通(Qualcomm)的一項反壟斷調查至少從去年底就開始了，此外還有數十家外國醫藥企業。
S.A.I.C., which investigates cases not directly related to pricing, started its own investigation a year ago into abuse of market position by Tetra Pak, a giant food packaging company based in Sweden.
工商總局負責調查與定價沒有直接關聯的案件，一年前該部門對涉嫌濫用市場地位的瑞典食品包裝巨頭利樂(Tetra Pak)展開了調查。
Analysts say foreign companies may be relatively more vulnerable to investigation because they generally lack the deep networks of political patronage that prevail in many sectors of China’s economy, particularly those like energy and finance that remain state-dominated.
分析人士說，外國企業更容易遭到調查，因為它們通常沒有那種深厚的政治裙帶關係，而裙帶關係在中國經濟的許多領域是普遍存在的，尤其是像能源和金融等至今仍由國家控制的產業。
“As Beijing prunes back outdated forms of government intervention, regulations to protect consumers and promote competition must come to the fore, which is why President Xi scoped this into his reform design,” said Daniel H. Rosen, the founding partner of the Rhodium Group, an economic and political consulting firm in New York. “It does seem that foreign firms are the subject of a disproportionate share of China’s new enthusiasm for competition policy.”
「北京正在削減已經過時的政府干預形式，而保護消費者和促進競爭的管制規定相應的必須擔負起重任，這就是為什麼習主席將這一點納入到他的改革規劃里，」紐約經濟和政治諮詢公司榮鼎諮詢(Rhodium Group)創始合伙人榮大聶(Daniel H. Rosen)說。「在中國最近這次對競爭政策的熱烈擁抱中，外國公司佔據的比例似乎格外高。」
But the enforcers, too, appear to be feeling the pressure from shifts in Chinese economic policy making. The powerful National Development and Reform Commission has traditionally been China’s top economic planning agency and remains responsible for setting the retail prices that state energy companies like PetroChina and Sinopec can charge for products like gasoline.
然而執法者本身看起來也承受着中國經濟決策轉變的壓力。手握重權的國家發改委在傳統上一直是中國最高層的經濟規劃機構，至今仍要負責給中國石油、中國石化等國有能源企業的汽油等產品定價。
But its influence has waned under Mr. Xi, who has shifted the oversight of some economic policies to newly established committees that he heads. Shoring up influence through enforcement of antimonopoly rules may help the agency compensate for that change.
但在習近平上台後，發改委的影響力在減弱，一些經濟政策的監管被轉移到了新成立的委員會，由他本人領導。通過執行反壟斷法規來強化自己的影響力，也許有助於該機構挽回這種轉變給它造成的損失。
Ms. Zhang, the law lecturer, noted that while antitrust cases involving foreign companies tend to grab the headlines, authorities have in many instances also gone after local and state-owned companies. To date, the biggest such case was in February 2013, when the reform commission slapped total price-fixing fines of 449 million renminbi, or about $73 million, on two of China’s leading state-owned liquor companies: Wuliangye Yibin Group and Kweichow Moutai.
國王學院的張湖月注意到，涉及外國公司的反壟斷調查比較容易成為新聞熱點，但當局在很多時候也會盯上本土和國有公司。迄今為止，此類調查中規模最大的一宗發生在2013年2月，當時發改委向兩家中國最大的國有酒業公司——宜賓五糧液集團和貴州茅台公司——開出了總額4.49億元人民幣的壟斷罰款。
Among foreign companies, few would appear to be better connected in China than Microsoft. Its co-founder, Bill Gates, has met with Mr. Xi several times to discuss the charitable work of the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, and he hosted Mr. Xi’s predecessor, President Hu Jintao, at a 2006 dinner at his home in Medina, Wash. Since then, however, the Chinese government’s wariness of Western technology providers has deepened, especially after Edward J. Snowden’s disclosures about surveillance done by the United States.
微軟在中國的人脈，外國公司鮮有能及。公司聯合創始人比爾·蓋茨(Bill Gates)曾和習近平多次見面，探討比爾及梅琳達·蓋茨基金會(Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation)的慈善工作，並於2006年在華盛頓州麥地那家中宴請習的前任胡錦濤主席。然而此後中國政府對西方技術供應商日益警惕，尤其是在愛德華·J·斯諾登(Edward J. Snowden)披露了美國政府的監控活動後。
It is not clear to Microsoft executives precisely how the Chinese authorities believe that they violated the country’s antimonopoly law. For months, Microsoft was in private discussions over competition issues with S.A.I.C. officials. The discussions were not hostile, said one person briefed on the meetings who spoke on condition of anonymity.
公司高層並不十分清楚，中國當局認為微軟通過何種方式違反了該國的反壟斷法。微軟幾個月來一直在私下裡與工商總局官員接觸，商談有關競爭的問題。一位要求匿名的會談知情人士稱，談話的氣氛是友好的。
So it was a surprise to Microsoft executives when investigators raided its offices. S.A.I.C. officials have said publicly that the investigation is related to software compatibility, bundling and file verification issues for Windows and Office.
因此當調查人員突擊搜查微軟辦公室時，公司高層是始料未及的。工商總局官員公開表示，調查涉及到Windows和Office的軟件兼容性、搭售和文件驗證等問題。
Late last week, The People’s Daily, the chief newspaper of the party, told foreign companies to get used to tougher scrutiny. “In the future, the focus on market order will continue to be constantly enhanced,” it said. “Every kind of business should adjust its behavior and thinking to this new regulatory normal.”
上周末，中國的中央黨報《人民日報》稱，外國企業要適應這種更嚴厲的調查。「未來，對於市場秩序的重視，還將不斷提高，」文章寫道。「各類企業都應該在新監管常態下，調整自己的行為和思路。」
