「全新的摩托羅拉」意義何在？

One on One: Jim Wicks, Design Chief at Motorola Mobility

BRIAN X. CHEN
2013年1月7日
Motorola Mobility, which Google acquired in May, recently released new smartphones and reintroduced itself as the "New Motorola." But what does that even mean? One thing is for sure: Motorola is not the same company that it was when it was riding the huge success of the first Razr in 2004.
去年5月（即2011年，本文最初發表於2012年12月21日——編注）被谷歌(Google)收購的摩托羅拉移動公司(Motorola Mobility)最近發佈了新款智能手機，並稱自己為“全新的摩托羅拉”(New Motorola)。但這有任何意義嗎？有一點是肯定的：它肯定已經不是2004年推出第一款Razr時，風光無限的摩托羅拉了。
In an interview, Jim Wicks, senior vice president for consumer experience design at Motorola Mobility, who has been with the company for 11 years, talked about the company's design strategy and how it will change going forward. An edited transcript of the interview follows.
在一次採訪中，摩托羅拉移動公司消費者體驗設計部門副總裁、為摩托羅拉服務11年的吉姆·維克斯(Jim Wicks)談到了公司的設計策略，以及未來將做何種改變。以下是經過編輯的採訪實錄。
Q. A few months ago Motorola introduced itself as the "New Motorola." What exactly does that mean?
問：數月以前摩托羅拉將自己稱為“全新的摩托羅拉”。這有何具體意義呢？
A. To me the new Motorola, based on our plans, is to have the opportunity to focus on doing less, better - to do fewer products but to really engage in those products and experiences and drive a level of quality and expression of the brand that we just were not able to do for a number of years. To me that's a huge opportunity and it's a great motivator in the business.
答：對我來說，根據計劃，全新的摩托羅拉即是有機會做少、做精。推出的產品少了，但是真正地全力投入這些產品和其用戶體驗，推動質量提升和品牌的展示，這是我們過去很多年無法做到的。對我來說，這是一個大好的機會，也是對我們業務的巨大推動力。
Q. What is Motorola's design process?
問：摩托羅拉的設計流程是怎樣的？
A. We established this design organization about 11 years ago. The whole point was to build one centralized design organization in the business, one that was very holistic in nature, that covered both the physical and digital disciplines of design of product.
答：我們大概在11年前建立起這個設計架構的。其全部意義就是在公司內建立起一個集中化的設計組織，就其性質來說是全面的，既考慮到產品設計的外觀原則，也考慮到它的數字化原則。
For the physical design, we have within our groups industrial designers, surface engineers, advanced mechanical engineers, material scientists and trends forecasters. On the digital side we have user experience designers. Additionally we would have design researchers, and there are people who are engaged in ethnographic studies on automobiles and homes. They track how people use technology and identify some of the needs that are unmet at this stage.
就外觀設計來說，我們團隊內有工業設計師、表面工程師、高級機械工程師、材料科學家以及時尚預測師。從電子方面來看，我們有用戶體驗設計師。除此之外，我們還有設計研究員，也有人員從事人種學和汽車、房屋的研究。他們跟蹤人們使用科技產品的習慣，並發現人們在目前階段還未滿足的需要。
That's what our design group is, and we're located in Chicago, California and Korea.
這是我們的設計團隊做的事，我們的團隊分佈在芝加哥、加利福尼亞和韓國。
Q. How has the "New Motorola" changed your approach to design?
問：“全新的摩托羅拉”這一概念對你們的設計方式有何改變？
A. Not many years ago we were actually producing 40 to 50 products a year. When you do that you build a design organization that is very "matrixed." The challenge there is that any one product that you do in that scenario has to be able to match up against any other company's one product in a retail setting.
答：就在幾年前，我們每年會設計40到50款產品。在那種情況下，我們建立起了一個非常具有“矩陣型”特徵的設計團隊，每個人都身兼數職。那樣做的困難是，我們在這種環境下設計出的任何一款產品，都要能和其他公司在量少的情況下設計出的每一款相抗衡。
If another company is only making two products and four products, and they're putting all their resources into that, and you're making 50, you can imagine the challenges you have. Do you feel like you have the best talent, the best testing, when you're doing 50 products that cover smartphones, tablets and accessories, which are all in their own right highly complex products? You're going to have to admit you're not going to always have all the world-class talent on every product at the same time.
如果另一家公司只設計兩款或四款產品，他們能夠投入所有資源，而我們要製作50款產品，你可以想像我們面臨的挑戰。當你在設計50款涵蓋智能手機、平板電腦和配件的產品，且這些產品每一款都非常複雜時，你還會覺得自己有最優秀的人才和最棒的測試技術嗎？你不得不承認，在同時進展的項目上，你不可能總能為每一款產品全部配置世界級的人才。
The position we're taking now going forward with design is supporting key franchises in the market. You create signature attributes that you carry from generation to generation. You use things like - the thing that always feels defining about a Razr product or the Droid product is the signature chin on the bottom that we always have.
我們目前所計劃的未來在設計方面的方向是，支持市場上的關鍵領域。你可以創造一些具有標誌性的特色，並將其一代一代傳承下去。你可以利用一些這樣的東西，那些讓人感到具有定義性的特色，比如，在Razr系列的產品或Droid系列的產品，它的標誌性特色就是它的底部。
Q. I remember the first Razr was a revolutionary design for a cellphone, when we were all coming from bulky Nokias. But now like everything else, the Razrs are rectangles with touch screens. Where do we go from here?
問：我記得，第一款Razr產品帶來了手機領域的一次設計革新，當時我們都還拿着笨重的諾基亞手機。但現在，和其他所有產品一樣，Razr系列也不過是長方形一個觸摸屏。那麼，我們下一步會走向何方？
A. I think now for a consumer, from a distance, everything might feel like in some ways everything is just a rectangle with a touch screen. But I think consumers are also becoming much more discerning. There's a lot of opportunities for innovation: the way the devices look and feel, additionally how devices talk with other devices.
答：我覺得，現在對一位消費者來說，如果只是從遠處看，在某種程度上，所有產品都只是一個有觸摸屏的長方形設備。但我認為，消費者也正在變得更有鑒別力。還有很多可以創新的機會，比如設備的外觀以及手感，此外還有設備與設備間如何互動。
We're dealing with a space now where predominantly the front face of the device is the display, so the ability to develop something that's unique and readily identifiable is much more challenging.
我們所處的環境是，現在手機的正面基本上都是顯示屏，因此，能設計出獨特且很容易辨認的產品更有挑戰性。
For us we start with some basics - whatever you do, you have to have something that's meaningful to the consumers. Over the last two years, our big areas of focus - the first one is display. When you go and start to engage with consumers and talk with them, what they really want is to see nothing but a display. When you buy a TV, you're not buying it for the frame around it, it's the content, it's the screen. We do everything we can to increase the active area of the display to the perimeter of the device. We try to get close to 70 percent of the device being nothing but active pixels and reducing everything else around it. Every millimeter you squeeze, you're giving them more screen per square inch.
對於我們來說，是從一些最基礎的方面入手。不論你怎麼做，你必須做出一些對於消費者來說有意義的東西。在過去的兩年里，在我們廣泛關注的各領域中，首當其衝的就是顯示屏。當你走過去，並開始與消費者交流時，他們真正想要看到的無非就是顯示屏。在你買一台電視時，你買它可不是為了它的框架，而是裡面的內容，是屏幕。我們竭盡所能地來把顯示屏的活躍區域擴大到設備的每一邊。我們努力將設備70%的區域變為有效像素，並減小周圍其他的所有設計。所能擠出的每一毫米，都能夠變成顯示屏上的面積。
The other dimension is power. We absolutely will never sacrifice battery power. Our two areas we want to satisfy are biggest screen/smallest device and enough battery to get them through the day so they don't have to worry.
另一個領域是電力。我們絕不會犧牲電池電力。我們想要滿足的兩大領域，其一是在最小的設備上得到最大的顯示屏，其二是讓這些設備有運行一整天的充足電力，這樣消費者就不必擔心。
Q. It seems fairly obvious that if you do less stuff, you'll do a better job. Why was Motorola making so much in the first place?
問：做得更少就能做得更好，這一點似乎顯而易見。那摩托羅拉為什麼早先要每年生產那麼多的產品？
A. Before our acquisition by Google as a business, you have to go quarter to quarter to make your numbers. To make a fundamental reset in your product strategy will result in some near-term financial challenges. You're not going to just change your strategy without having an impact on your business.
答：在我們被谷歌收購之前，我們必須在每個季度爭取營業額。對於我們產品策略的根本性改革，將帶來一些短期的財務上的挑戰。戰略的改變不可能不影響到業務。
That's the way we were operating up until after the Google acquisition. The ability to have Google behind us has allowed us to say, what's the long-term and midterm innovation play? Where do we want to take the brand, and make some of the difficult choices to focus on certain products in the portfolio? You can't do that as a stand-alone company. I think we've been given the opportunity to reset our strategy.
這就是在被谷歌收購之後，我們一直採取的運營方式。有了谷歌的支持，我們則有能力說，我們中、長期的創新計劃是什麼？我們品牌的目標市場是什麼？然後在所有產品中做出艱難的選擇，並將主力集中在一些特定的產品。這些都是一家獨立公司所做不到的。我想我們已經得到了改革我們戰略的機會。
We've been working this direction for the last couple of years, so it's not a complete right turn. It's refreshing for the designers and people in marketing - everyone is really excited about it. They believe in the strategy and see it in their work every day.
在過去的幾年裡，我們已經在朝着這個方向走了，所以這也不是算是天翻地覆的改變。這使得設計師和營銷人員都提起了精神，每個人都對此很興奮。他們相信新的策略，並且每天都能看到這一策略在穩步開展。
