被社交平台封殺的川普在中國找到支持者

As Trump Clashes With Big Tech, China’s Censored Internet Takes His Side

袁莉
2021年1月15日
After Twitter and Facebook kicked President Trump off their platforms, and his supporters began comparing his social media muzzling to Chinese censorship, the president won support from an unexpected source: China.
Twitter和Facebook把川普總統踢出平台後，他的支持者開始把他在社群媒體上遭到封殺和中國的審查制度相提並論，於是總統在一個意想不到的地方得到了支持：中國。
“Legally he’s still the president. This is a coup,” said one comment, which included an expletive, that was liked 21,000 times on Weibo, the Chinese social media platform.
「法律上他還是總統，這就是政變啊，」其中一條含有髒話的評論在中國社群媒體平台微博上被點讚2.1萬次。
“A country as big as the United States can’t tolerate Trump’s mouth,” another popular comment said. “U.S. democracy has died.”
「若（偌）大的美國竟容不下川普一張嘴，」另一條廣受歡迎的評論說。「美國的民主已死。」
The comments were solicited by Guancha.com, a nationalistic news site, which created the hashtag #BigUSappsunitedtosilenceTrump# on Weibo. They were echoed by Global Times, a tabloid controlled by the Communist Party.
這些評論是由民族主義新聞網站觀察者網徵集的，它在微博上創建了話題標籤#美國各大軟體聯合封殺川普#。中共控制的小報《環球時報》也發表了類似言論。
廣告
Mr. Trump “lost his right as an ordinary American citizen,” it wrote in an editorial. “This, of course, goes against the freedom of speech the U.S. political elites have been advocating.”
川普「失去了作為普通美國公民的這一權利」，該報在一篇社論中寫道。「這當然違反了美國政治精英們通常主張的的言論自由原則。」
Mr. Trump’s expulsion from American social media for spurring the violent crowd at the Capitol last week has consumed the Chinese internet, one of the most harshly censored forums on earth. Overwhelmingly, people who face prison for what they write are condemning what they regard as censorship elsewhere.
川普上週因煽動國會裡的暴力人群而被逐出美國社群媒體，這在中國的互聯網上引起了轟動。中國互聯網是世界上審查最嚴厲的論壇之一。那些可能因自己所寫內容而面臨牢獄之災的人，正在一邊倒地譴責他們心目中別處的審查制度。
Much of the condemnation is being driven by China’s propaganda arms. By highlighting the decisions by Twitter and Facebook, they believe they are reinforcing their message to the Chinese people that nobody in the world truly enjoys freedom of speech. That gives the party greater moral authority to crack down on Chinese speech.
大量譴責來自中國的宣傳武器。通過強調Twitter和Facebook的決定，他們認為自己正在向中國人民傳達一種信息：世界上沒有人真正享有言論自由。這給了共產黨更大的道德權威來鎮壓中國的言論。
“Some people may believe Twitter’s decision to suspend the account of the U.S. president is a sign of democracy,” Hu Xijin, editor of the Global Times, wrote in an opinion piece with the headline “Twitter’s suspension of Trump’s account shows freedom of speech has boundaries in every society.”
「有人說，社會輿論平台對總統噤聲，這恰恰展現了美國的民主，」《環球時報》主編胡錫進在一篇題為《川普的Twitter帳號被暫停使用表明言論自由在每個社會都是有界限的》的評論文章中寫道。
It would be tough for the United States to come back and play the role of “the beacon of democracy,” Mr. Hu added in a Weibo post.
這一切很不利於美國今後繼續以「民主燈塔」自居，胡錫進在微博文章裡還說。
Many Chinese online users bought the official line. Nearly two-thirds of the roughly 2,700 participants in one Chinese online poll voted that Twitter shouldn’t have shut down Mr. Trump’s account. The poll’s sponsor was a newspaper owned by the Xinhua News Agency, the Chinese government’s official mouthpiece.
許多中國網民相信了官方說法。在一項中國網上調查中，約2700名參與者裡，有近三分之二的人投票認為，Twitter不應該關閉川普的帳戶。這項調查的發起者是中國政府的官方喉舌新華社旗下的一家報紙。
廣告
“I just learned in the past few days that the U.S. social media platforms frequently delete posts and suspend accounts too,” wrote a verified Weibo account called “Su Jiande.” “I lost the last hint of respect for the country.”
「這幾天才知道美帝的自媒體平台也是動不動就刪帖、封號，」微博名為「蘇健的」的認證用戶寫道。「對這個國度的最後一絲敬意也沒了。」
The user thanked Weibo for allowing users to say whatever they want in pursuit of truth. (I read through the user’s Weibo timeline and found no hint of sarcasm.) Many Weibo users urged Mr. Trump to open a Weibo account.
該用戶感謝微博允許用戶在追求真相的過程中暢所欲言。（我瀏覽了該用戶的微博時間線，沒有發現任何諷刺的跡象。）許多微博用戶敦促川普開通微博帳戶。
“This is not the U.S. as we know it,” commented a Weibo user named Xiangbanzhang. “This is Saddam’s Iraq and Gaddafi’s Libya.”
「感覺這不是那個美國了，」微博用戶「向班長」評論道。「這是薩達姆的伊拉克、卡扎菲的利比亞。」
Trump defenders compare the president’s ouster from social media to China-style censorship. “This is not China, this is United States of America, and we are a free country,” Sarah Huckabee Sanders, Mr. Trump’s former press secretary, wrote on Twitter.
川普的辯護者將總統被趕出社群媒體比作中國式的審查。「這裡不是中國，這裡是美利堅合眾國，我們是一個自由的國家，」川普的前新聞秘書薩拉·赫卡比·桑德斯(Sarah Huckabee Sanders)在Twitter上寫道。
Chinese censorship doesn’t work that way. In China, speech about top leaders is closely monitored and harshly censored. The people who run Facebook and Twitter have the First Amendment right to choose what can and can’t go on their platforms.
中國的審查制度不是這樣運作的。在中國，有關最高領導人的言論受到嚴密監視和嚴格審查。而運營Facebook和Twitter的人擁有憲法第一修正案的權利，能選擇什麼內容可以出現在他們的平台上，什麼內容不可以。
The Chinese government requires news websites to dedicate their top two daily items to Xi Jinping, China’s paramount leader. On Tuesday, for example, online outlets extolled a speech Mr. Xi gave at a party seminar, while another piece explained the classical literary allusions used in an article under his byline in a Communist Party magazine.
中國政府要求新聞網站將每天最重要的兩篇文章用於報導中國最高領導人習近平。例如，週二，網路媒體讚揚習近平在黨內研討會上的講話，另一篇文章解釋了他在一份共產黨雜誌上發表的署名文章中使用的古典文學典故。
廣告
The government has strict rules regarding which social media accounts and websites can post articles and photos of leaders like Mr. Xi. Young censors spend much of their workdays blocking and deleting links that contain photos of the leaders, even if the content supports the government. In other words, ordinary Chinese don’t even have the right to post photos of Mr. Xi, much less criticize him.
政府對哪些社群媒體帳號和網站可以發布習近平等領導人的文章和照片有嚴格規定。年輕的審查者在工作日大部分時間裡都忙於屏蔽和刪除包含領導人照片的鏈接，即使這些內容是支持政府的。換句話說，普通中國人甚至沒有權利發布習近平的照片，更不用說批評他了。
Those who dare to criticize him face severe punishment. Ren Zhiqiang, a retired businessman and an influential social media personality, was silenced on Chinese online platforms in early 2016 after he criticized Mr. Xi’s directives that the Chinese news media should serve the party. He was sentenced to 18 years in prison last year after writing an essay that was critical of Mr. Xi’s response to the coronavirus outbreak.
膽敢批評他的人將面臨嚴厲的懲罰。任志強是一位退休商人，也是一位有影響力的社群媒體人，2016年初，他批評習近平關於中國新聞媒體應該為黨服務的指示後，在中國網路平台上被封殺。去年，他寫了一篇批評習近平應對新冠病毒暴發的文章，後來被判處18年有期徒刑。
Chinese internet companies conduct their own censorship, but they do so out of fear of what Beijing officials might do to them. Last February, ifeng.com, a news portal, was punished for running original content about the coronavirus outbreak. Under the Chinese regulations, these websites can’t produce original news content.
中國互聯網公司也進行自己的審查，他們這樣做是因為擔心北京官員會對他們採取措施。去年2月，新聞門戶網站鳳凰網因刊登有關新冠病毒疫情的原創內容而受到處罰。根據中國的規定，這些網站不能生產原創新聞內容。
According to the national internet regulator, websites and regulators in December processed more than 13 million items deemed to be illegal and unhealthy, an 8 percent increase from a year earlier. Among them, six million were processed by Weibo.
根據國家互聯網監管機構的數據，去年12月，網站和監管機構處理了1300多萬條被視為非法和不健康的內容，比去年同期增長了8%。其中，微博處理了600萬條。
For those reasons, many Chinese are dumbfounded by the idea that private companies such as Twitter and Facebook have the power to reject a sitting American president.
出於這些原因，許多中國人對Twitter和Facebook等私營公司有權拒絕一位在任美國總統感到震驚。
“When Twitter banned Trump, it was a private platform refusing to serve the president,” a Weibo user called Xichuangsuiji wrote in trying to explain the distinction. “When Weibo bans you, it’s simply executing government guidelines to censor an individual’s speech.”
「推特封了川普，是私人平台拒絕了為總統也就是為權力服務，」一位名為「西窗隨記」的微博用戶試圖解釋二者的區別。「微博封了你，是執行權力制訂的規則封殺了個人言論。」
廣告
Some Chinese dissidents and liberal intellectuals oppose the bans because they suffered harsh censorship in China or because they support Mr. Trump, whom they see as tough on the Communist Party.
中國一些持不同政見者和自由派知識分子反對封殺川普，是因為他們在中國遭受了嚴厲的審查，或者是因為他們支持川普，認為川普對共產黨態度強硬。
“Twitter and Facebook permit propaganda from the Global Times and the People’s Daily, and yet today, they went to war with their own president by censoring his expression,” Ai Weiwei, a dissident artist, posted on Twitter in Chinese. He was famously censored online in China, harassed by the police and confined to his home by the authorities before he was allowed to flee.
「推特和臉書允許來自環球時報、人民日報的宣傳存在，卻對自己的總統大動干戈，」持不同政見的藝術家艾未未在Twitter上用中文發帖。很多人都知道，他在中國的網上受到審查，受到警方騷擾，曾被當局軟禁在家中，後來才被允許離開。
“Freedom of speech,” Mr. Ai added, “is a pretense and nothing more.”
艾未未補充說：「言論自由也只是幌子而已。」
Kuang Biao, a political cartoonist in the southern city of Guangzhou, has had multiple Weibo accounts shut down and has created many cartoons that were censored, including one last year about Li Wenliang, the Wuhan doctor who was silenced by the police for sharing information about the coronavirus. In the cartoon, Dr. Li was wearing a mask of barbed wires.
南方城市廣州的政治漫畫家鄺飈已經被關停了多個微博帳號。他畫了許多遭到審查的漫畫，其中包括去年一幅關於武漢醫生李文亮的漫畫，他因分享有關新冠病毒的信息而被噤聲。在漫畫中，李文亮醫生戴著被鐵絲網包圍的面具。
But when Mr. Kuang created two cartoons to express his displeasure at Mr. Trump’s bans, China’s censors did nothing. In one of them, President Trump’s mouth was brutally sewn up. In another, the Facebook founder Mark Zuckerberg is portrayed as Qin Shi Huang, China’s first emperor, a brutal tyrant who burned books and executed scholars more than 2,000 years ago.
但是當鄺飈創作了兩幅漫畫以表達對川普禁言的不滿時，中國的審查員什麼也沒做。其中一幅漫畫中，川普總統的嘴被殘忍地縫了起來。在另外一幅中，Facebook創始人馬克·祖克柏(Mark Zuckerberg)被描繪成秦始皇——中國的第一個皇帝，在兩千年前焚書坑儒的殘忍暴君。
By Tuesday evening, the first had garnered more than 170,000 views on the short video site Douyin, a sister site of TikTok.
到週二晚上，第一幅漫畫已在TikTok的姐妹短片網站抖音上獲得了超過17萬次觀看。
廣告
“Everyone is entitled to freedom of speech,” Mr. Kuang said. “It’s a sacred human right.” He said he’s a strong supporter of President Trump, who, he believes, is “a man who serves the people wholeheartedly.”
「人有言論的自由，」鄺飈說。「這是神聖的人權。」他說自己是川普的堅定支持者，認為他是「全心全意為人民服務的人」。
Some people in China have noted the disconnect, saying people who are defending Mr. Trump’s freedom of speech are the victims of a far worse type of censorship.
一些中國人注意到了這種脫節現象，稱捍衛川普言論自由的人是一種糟糕得多的審查制度的受害者。
“Sheep that can be eaten up by the tiger at any time are angry that the tiger has been put in a cage,” wrote Chen Min, a former journalist who usually goes by the pen name Xiao Shu.
筆名笑蜀的前記者陳敏說：「很可能隨時被虎吃掉的羊，反而憤怒於老虎被關進籠子裡。」
On his account on WeChat, the popular Chinese social media platform, Mr. Chen wrote that a powerful leader like President Trump has a lot of responsibilities, including the consequences of his speech. Mr. Chen is frequently censored and harassed by the state security officers for what he writes online.
在流行的中國社群媒體平台微信上，陳敏寫道，像川普總統這樣的強大領導者肩負著許多責任，包括講話的後果。陳敏經常因他在網上寫的東西而受到國家安全人員的審查和騷擾。
The journalist Zhao Jing, who goes by the name Michael Anti, is puzzled why Chinese Trump supporters so zealously defend his freedom of speech. Mr. Trump has the White House, executive orders and Fox News, he wrote: “What else do you want for him to have freedom of speech?”
筆名安替的記者趙靜感到困惑，為什麼中國的川普支持者如此熱衷於捍衛他的言論自由。川普擁有白宮、行政命令和福克斯新聞(Fox News)，他寫道：「你要怎麼他才算他有言論自由？」
China’s censors don’t seem to agree. He Weifang, a renowned law professor at Peking University, wrote a long post on WeChat supporting the restrictions on Mr. Trump. The article has since disappeared.
中國的審查員似乎不這麼想。北京大學著名法學教授賀衛方在微信上發表了一篇長篇文章，支持對川普的限制。此後該文章消失了。
“This content has violated rules,” said a message with a red exclamation mark where the article was once posted, “so can’t be viewed.”
曾經的文章現在變成了一個紅色感嘆號，下面寫著：「此內容因違反規定無法查看」。
