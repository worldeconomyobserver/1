安倍经改狂飙突进

Back in Power, Abe Aims to Spend Japan Back to Economic Vitality

HIROKO TABUCHI
2013年1月24日

TOKYO - Perhaps the pendulum was destined to swing back.

东京——钟摆可能注定要摆回来了。

Japan, once lauded as an economic miracle, has spent much of the last two decades playing the world economy's poster child for policy gone wrong. But as austerity budgets bring no end to economic dormancy in Europe, the Japanese government's profligate ways are getting an unlikely second look.

日本一度曾被誉为经济奇迹。但过去20年的大部分时间，日本都是世界经济中政策失误的典型。然而，随着预算紧缩无法唤醒沉睡中的欧洲经济，日本政府的大手大脚行为不禁引人侧目。

Enter Shinzo Abe, Japan's intrepid new prime minister, who promises to blaze a trail even further away from deep cuts and lean economics.

新晋日本首相安倍晋三(Shinzo Abe)表现得大胆无畏，他承诺进一步远离大力削减支出与节衣缩食的经济道路。

He and his predecessors have been warned countless times by economists that Japan, saddled with a sky-high debt and rapidly graying population, must also focus on austerity to avoid financial ruin. But Mr. Abe, who has returned as prime minister after a short-lived stint in 2006-7, has decided to flout such advice.

经济学家无数次警告安倍晋三及其前任说，日本身负巨债，人口迅速老龄化，必须也专注于紧缩，以避免金融领域的灭顶之灾。现在，曾在2006年到2007年短暂出任首相的安倍晋三再次上台，决定不去听取这些建议。

Not yet a month back in office, he has essentially ordered the Bank of Japan to fire up its printing presses without limit and has promised to pump 12 trillion yen ($134 billion) into public works and other government projects. Mr. Abe has also assembled two panels of economists and executives to advise him on a path that might finally lead Japan out of its economic malaise.

安倍晋三履新不足一月，已经下令日本银行(Bank of Japan)无节制地大力印钞，并承诺为公共项目及其他政府项目注入12万亿日元（1340亿美元，约合人民币8040亿元）。安倍晋三也召集了两个经济学家及高管专家组为他献计献策，以走出一条可能最终把日本带出经济困境的道路。

"We are making a break with the shrinking economy of the past," Mr. Abe told the Japanese people as he announced the stimulus spending. "From now on, the government is spearheading the economics of growth."

安倍晋三在宣布刺激方案时，告诉日本国民说，“我们正在与过去的经济萎缩状况决裂。从现在开始，政府会引导经济进行增长。”

With his newfound audacity, Mr. Abe has made Japan a living experiment in how long a country can defy the seemingly inevitable reckoning that accompanies living well beyond its means. And Mr. Abe has also implicitly demanded a whole new understanding of the causes of Japan's prolonged economic slump.

安倍晋三最近变得颇具胆魄，把日本变成了一个活生生的实验，以发现一个大举举债度日的国家能够在多长的时间里逃脱那随之而来且似乎不可避免的大清算。安倍晋三也含蓄地要求大家，以全新的观点理解是什么导致了日本旷日持久的经济衰退。

"Finally, we have a politician who understands what's been wrong with Japan's economy all along," said Koichi Hamada, an economist at Yale University who has long argued for an aggressive monetary solution to Japan's economic woes.

耶鲁大学(Yale University)经济学家滨田弘一(Koichi Hamada)说，“我们终于有了个明白日本经济一直以来错在哪里的政客。”滨田弘一一直呼吁以激进的货币政策解决日本经济问题。

Like any country, Japan has its structural faults, but the main culprit behind its malaise has been the central bank, Mr. Hamada said.

滨田弘一说，日本同其他国家一样有自身的结构缺陷，但是日本弊病背后的元凶是日本央行。

The bank's failure to do enough to fight deflation, the across-the-board fall in prices, profits and wages that has sapped the strength from Japan's economy since the late 1990s, has trumped all other efforts by Japan to grow, he said.

他说，日本央行未能全力击退通货紧缩，从20世纪90年代开始，各行各业的价格、利润及工资下降吸光了日本经济的力量。这些压倒了日本的其他一切增长努力。

Mr. Hamada, 77, an adviser to the prime minister, has been named in the local media as one of Mr. Abe's preferred picks to take over as governor at the central bank. Mr. Hamada said it was unlikely he would accept, however, citing health reasons.

77岁的滨田弘一是首相的顾问。当地媒体称,滨田弘一是安倍晋三倾向于选择的央行行长候选人之一。然而，滨田弘一却以健康为由说他不太可能会接受这一职位。

So far, markets have reacted positively to Mr. Abe's economic push. Foreign investors have helped to push up the Nikkei 225 share average more than 20 percent since mid-November, when Mr. Abe, an opposition leader at the time, began promoting aggressive monetary and fiscal policies.

迄今为止，对于安倍晋三的经济行动，市场的反应很积极。去年十一月中旬，当时还是反对党领袖的安倍晋三开始宣扬激进的货币及财政政策。从那时起，在外国投资者的推动下，日经225指数(Nikkei 225 share average)上升了20%多。

The yen fell last week to a two-year low against the dollar, a boon to Japanese exporters. Meanwhile, Mr. Abe's talk of ramping up spending has not, for now, brought about the feared disruption in government bond markets; interest rates on the government's long-term borrowing remain well under 1 percent.

上周，日元对美元汇率跌至两年来的最低点，使得日本出口商受益。同时，在政府债券市场上，安倍晋三加大支出的言论尚未引起人们所担心的波动；政府长期借款的利息率仍远低于1%。

Still, the enthusiastic market response has hardly silenced the debate between those who declare that Japan is now on the threshold of a welcome rise in prices, profits and tax revenue, and doomsayers who warn that officials are ignoring risks of fiscal insolvency, bond sell-offs and runaway inflation.

然而，即使市场反应很热情，辩论却未偃旗息鼓。有人说日本即将出现价格、利润及税收的可喜增长，而末日论者却警告道，官员们忽视了财政破产，证券抛售及恶性通胀的风险。

For Mr. Abe's critics, his pump-priming could energize markets and buoy the economy in the short term, but would eventually push Japan even further toward the fiscal brink. Interest rates may be low now, they say, but will rise with inflation - or even spike - if investors start to question the effectiveness of Mr. Abe's growth policies or grow too jittery about government debt, which is now more than twice as big as Japan's entire economy.

安倍晋三的批评者称，他的经济刺激行为可能会在短期内活跃市场，并且维持经济。但是，这种做法最终会把日本进一步推向财政崩溃边缘。他们说，现在利率可能很低，但是如果投资者们开始怀疑安倍晋三的增长政策是否有效，或者过于担心政府的债务，利率会随着通胀上升，甚至激增。政府债务现在已经是日本经济总量的两倍多。

Even a moderate rise in interest rates, critics warn, could weigh heavily on a debt-servicing burden that already makes up a quarter of Japan's annual budget. The country's widening trade deficit and a graying population that is eating into its savings are further worries.

批评者警告道，即使利率增长幅度不大，也会对日本的偿债负担造成很大影响。偿债负担已经占到日本年度预算的四分之一。日益增长的贸易赤字，老龄化人口对储蓄的消耗，都是令人更担忧的问题。

"Investors are mainly worried about Europe right now, but that concern could easily jump to Japan," said Toshihiro Nagahama, chief economist at the Daiichi Life Research Institute in Tokyo. "The Europeans at least have a plan to try to get back to fiscal health. The Japanese have no plan."

东京第一生命研究所(Daiichi Life Research Institute)首席经济学家长滨敏弘(Toshihiro Nagahama)说，“现在投资者主要担心欧洲，但是这种担心很容易就会落到日本头上。欧洲各国至少有恢复财政健康的方案，日本却没有。”

Mr. Abe's supporters, on the other hand, argue that Japan will better stoke investor confidence by finally beating deflation and generating growth, not by simply tinkering with budgets. Those advocates, referred to as the reflation school or "refle-ha" in Japanese, argue that aggressively aiming for a healthy level of inflation targeting - and the weaker yen the policy has already brought about - will galvanize exports, revive production, spur profit, expand employment, raise wages and drive up tax revenue in a virtuous cycle of growth, one that will more than make up for the higher debt-service costs.

另一方面，安倍晋三的支持者称，日本最终将会通过抗击通货紧缩和促进经济增长，而不是简单地调整预算，来更好地增强投资者信心。这些倡导人士在日本被称为通货再膨胀派，他们认为将通货膨胀保持在健康水平是一个积极目标，此类政策已经促使日元疲软，这将会刺激出口、恢复生产、促进利润增长、扩大就业、提高工资，增加税收，带来经济增长的良性循环，这些好处将远远超过更高的债务成本所带来的负担。

"Ninety-nine percent of Japan's economic stagnation can be put down to the abnormally tight monetary policies of the Bank of Japan," said Takuro Morinaga, an influential economist who has led the refle-ha charge. "It's written in every college freshman's yearbook, that an increase in the money supply will lead to rising prices. It's basic economics."

“日本经济停滞不前百分之九十九是因为日本银行(Bank of Japan)实施了过于紧缩的货币政策，”知名经济学家、通货再膨胀派的领军人物森永卓郎(Takuro Morinaga)说。“每一个大学新生的课本上都写着，货币供给的增加将会导致物价上涨。这是个经济学基础问题。”

The argument between the two camps is, as Mr. Morinaga points out, a debate over the fundamental causes of Japan's decades of economic stagnation since the collapse of its bubble economy after 1990. And here, Mr. Abe, until now better known for his revisionist views of Japan's wartime history, also pushes an alternative reading of Japanese economic past.

正如森永卓郎指出的那样，两大阵营之间的论战是一场辩论，主题则是在1990年泡沫经济破灭后，日本经济数十年停滞不前的根本原因。到目前为止，安倍晋三在日本广为人知之处是他对日本战时历史持有的修正主义观点，而他现在又在推动对日本的经济史进行另一种解读。

The now widely accepted take on Japan's economic malaise has focused on the structural faults that hold back its economy, from an inflexible labor market to the heavy-handed role played by a largely uninspired and vested public sector. Many of Japan's once-stellar exporters have done a terrible job at innovation, and have lost much of their dynamism, critics say, even as Japan has been unable to retool its economy to generate more domestic demand.

现在，人们普遍认可日本经济不景气集中体现在结构缺陷上，劳动力市场缺乏灵活性，缺乏创新、充斥既得利益的国营企业在经济领域占据重要地位，这些缺陷阻碍了经济的发展。批评人士称，就在日本一直无法调整经济结构刺激国内需求时，很多风光一时的出口企业在创新方面做得非常差，活力大幅下降。 

And public works spending in the 1990s fueled pork-barrel politics and built many "bridges to nowhere," but did almost nothing to bring growth to the wider economy. All the while, the lavish spending was disastrous for Japan's public debt.

20世纪90年代毫无限制的政治拨款加剧了“猪肉桶政治” （指为赢得选票而在地方工程上花费大笔政府拨款——译注）的趋势，建设了很多“哪里都不通往的桥梁”，但却几乎没有给更广大的经济领域带来增长。 

Mr. Abe's critics charge that flooding that broken system with more money, with no bold restructuring of the economy, is not effective. The money will go to all the wrong places, they predict, like keeping uncompetitive "zombie companies" afloat or helping to maintain excess capacity in industries ranging from autos to consumer electronics with little regard to international or domestic consumer demand.

安倍晋三的批评者指责称，不去大胆地调整经济结构，只向一个支离破碎的系统注入更多资金不会带来成效。他们预言，这些资金将会全部用于错误的用途，比如维持一些毫无竞争力的“僵尸公司”的运营，或者帮助维持汽车、电子消费品等行业的过剩产能，而不关心国际或者国内的消费需求。

And without a fundamental restructuring of the economy, these critics say, there are limits to what the central bank can do. And worse, they warn, a loose monetary policy could inflate another damaging bubble, memories of which still remain fresh in financial circles here.

这些批评人士称，如果不从根本上调整经济结构，中央银行能够做的事情十分有限。更糟的是，他们警告，宽松的货币政策可能会制造出另一个具有破坏性的泡沫，日本金融界对此类泡沫仍然记忆犹新。

"All that extra money becomes like morphine for the Japanese economy - we become dependent on it, and we keep on asking for more," said Mana Nakazora, chief credit analyst for Japan at BNP Paribas. "In the meantime, fiscal restraint weakens. It's a very risky strategy for Japan."

“增加资金像是为日本经济注射吗啡，我们变得依赖它，会想要更多，”法国巴黎银行(BNP Paribas)驻日本的首席信贷分析师曲真仲日（Mana Nakazora，音译）说。“与此同时，财政紧缩有所减弱。对于日本来说，这是一个非常冒险的策略。”

But the refle-ha say Japan needs to do much more to combat the persistently strong yen that has battered its exporters. They point especially to the punishing gap between the South Korean won, which weakened by 60 percent in the wake of the global financial crisis, and the yen, which strengthened 20 percent. That gave South Korean exporters the decisive upper hand as they crushed Japanese rivals in markets everywhere.

但通货再膨胀派称，日本需要采取更多措施抗击一直强劲的日元，日元升值已对出口企业造成打击。他们特别指向韩元与日元之间的巨大差距，全球金融危机爆发后，韩元汇率下跌60%，而日元汇率却上涨20%。这让韩国的出口企业在各个市场打压日本竞争对手时占据了决定性优势。

The refle-ha are somewhat more divided on public works spending, mainly for construction, which many argue did help soften the blow of Japan's post-bubble recession. But the spending did not lead to a wider recovery, they acknowledge, either because the government pulled back too quickly or because the spending was aimed at the wrong targets and had only a weak ripple effect on the rest of the economy.

在公共工程拨款方面，主要是建筑工程领域，通货再膨胀派存在很大分歧，许多人认为建筑工程建设确实在一定程度上减轻了日本在后泡沫衰退时期遭受的冲击。但他们承认，此类拨款没有带来更广范围的经济复苏，要么是因为政府过快地紧缩开支，要么是因为此项拨款瞄准了错误的目标，只对其他经济领域带来微弱的连锁影响。

Mr. Abe has gone to pains to argue that his Liberal Democratic Party - which for many in Japan has become synonymous of the pork-barrel spending of an earlier era - has changed, and that his stimulus will focus on more productive sectors. But a look at Mr. Abe's recent stimulus plans, with up to 6 trillion yen earmarked for public works, suggests he will have a lot of convincing to do.

对于很多日本人来说，自民党已经成为早年毫无限制的政治拨款的同义词。但安倍晋三极力辩称，自民党已经有所改变，其刺激计划将会涉及更具生产力的领域。看看安倍晋三近期的刺激计划——投入6万亿日元用于公共工程建设，就会知道他将要做大量说服工作。

"We get the impression that the Liberal Democratic Party is going on a spending spree out of elation that it was able to take back the reins of the government," the Asahi daily newspaper said in an editorial last week.

《朝日新闻》上周发表的一篇社论中写道，“我们有一种感觉，自民党为自己赢回执政权而兴高采烈，因此开始无节制地花钱。”
