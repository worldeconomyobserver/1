中國廣泛採集男性DNA用於監控

China Is Collecting DNA From Tens of Millions of Men and Boys, Using U.S. Gear

黃瑞黎
2020年6月19日
Sign up for NYT Chinese-language Morning Briefing.
（歡迎點擊此處訂閱NYT簡報，我們將在每個工作日發送最新內容至您的郵箱。）
The police in China are collecting blood samples from men and boys from across the country to build a genetic map of its roughly 700 million males, giving the authorities a powerful new tool for their emerging high-tech surveillance state.
中國警方正在採集全國各地的成年和未成年男性的血液樣本，以建立一套涵蓋7億男性的基因圖譜，為這個以新興高科技監控國家的面貌出現的當局提供一個強大的新工具。
They have swept across the country since late 2017 to collect enough samples to build a vast DNA database, according to a new study published on Wednesday by the Australian Strategic Policy Institute, a research organization, based on documents also reviewed by The New York Times. With this database, the authorities would be able to track down a man’s male relatives using only that man’s blood, saliva or other genetic material.
據研究機構澳洲戰略政策研究所(Australian Strategic Policy Institute)週三發布的一項新研究，中國警方自2017年底就在全國各地行動起來，為建立一個龐大的DNA數據庫採集足夠多的樣本。《紐約時報》也查閱了這項研究所基於的文件。有了這個數據庫後，當局就可以通過一名男子的血液、唾液或其他遺傳物質來追溯他的男性親屬。
An American company, Thermo Fisher, is helping: The Massachusetts company has sold testing kits to the Chinese police tailored to their specifications. American lawmakers have criticized Thermo Fisher for selling equipment to the Chinese authorities, but the company has defended its business.
一家名為賽默飛世爾科技（Thermo Fisher Scientific，簡稱賽默飛）的美國公司正在提供幫助：這家總部設在麻薩諸塞州的公司把按照中國警方規格定製的檢測試劑盒出售給他們。一些美國議員批評賽默飛向中國當局出售設備的做法，但該公司為自己的業務進行了辯護。
廣告
The project is a major escalation of China’s efforts to use genetics to control its people, which had been focused on tracking ethnic minorities and other, more targeted groups. It would add to a growing, sophisticated surveillance net that the police are deploying across the country, one that increasingly includes advanced cameras, facial recognition systems and artificial intelligence.
這個項目是中國試圖利用遺傳學來管控人口的重大升級，此前中國把這項技術專門用於追蹤少數民族和其他目標群體上。它將為一個日益強大的先進監視網提供新能力，中國警方已在全國部署了這個包括高級攝像頭、人臉識別系統和人工智慧的監視網。
The police say they need the database to catch criminals and that donors consent to handing over their DNA. Some officials within China, as well as human rights groups outside its borders, warn that a national DNA database could invade privacy and tempt officials to punish the relatives of dissidents and activists. Rights activists argue that the collection is being done without consent because citizens living in an authoritarian state have virtually no right to refuse.
警方說，他們需要這個數據庫來抓獲犯罪分子，而且，捐獻血樣的人同意交出他們的DNA。中國國內的一些官員以及境外的人權組織警告，一個全國性的DNA數據庫可能會侵犯隱私，誘使官員懲罰異見人士和活動人士的親屬。人權活動人士認為採集工作是在未經同意的情況下進行的，因為生活在威權國家的公民實際上沒有拒絕的權利。
Already, the program is running into an unusual amount of opposition in China.
這個項目已在中國遭到了不同尋常程度的反對。
“The ability of the authorities to discover who is most intimately related to whom, given the context of the punishment of entire families as a result of one person’s activism, is going to have a chilling effect on society as a whole,” said Maya Wang, a China researcher for Human Rights Watch.
「考慮到因一人的行動讓全家受懲罰這種背景，當局發現誰與誰關係最密切的能力將對整個社會有一種寒蟬效應，」人權觀察組織(Human Rights Watch)的中國問題研究員王松蓮說。
The campaign even involves schools. In one southern coastal town in China, young boys offered up their tiny fingers to a police officer with a needle. About 230 miles to the north, officers went from table to table taking blood from schoolboys while the girls watched quizzically.
這項行動甚至涉及學校。在中國南方的一個沿海小鎮，小男孩們向一名拿著針頭的警察伸出小手指。在這個鎮子以北約370公里的地方，在女孩子們困惑的眼光下，警官們從一張課桌到另一張課桌採集男生的血。
Jiang Haolin, 31, gave a blood sample, too. He had no choice.
31歲的江浩林也提供了血樣。他別無選擇。
The authorities told Mr. Jiang, a computer engineer from a rural county in northern China, that “if blood wasn’t collected, we would be listed as a ‘black household,’” he said last year, and it would deprive him and his family of benefits like the right to travel and go to a hospital.
江先生是一名計算機工程師，來自中國北方農村，當局告訴他，「不採血就拉黑戶，」他去年說，那會剝奪他和他的家人享有的福利，比如旅行和去醫院看病。
Tracking China’s Males
跟蹤中國的男性
The Chinese authorities are collecting DNA samples from men and boys for one simple reason: They commit more crimes, statistics show.
中國當局採集男人和男孩DNA樣本出於一個簡單原因：從統計數據來看，男性犯罪更多。
廣告
The impetus for the campaign can be traced back to a crime spree in the northern Chinese region of Inner Mongolia. For nearly three decades, the police there investigated the rapes and murders of 11 women and girls, one as young as 8. They collected 230,000 fingerprints and sifted through more than 100,000 DNA samples. They offered a $28,000 reward.
推動這項行動的原因可以追溯到中國北部內蒙古地區的一系列犯罪。內蒙古警方花了近30年的時間調查11名女人和女孩（其中年齡最小的僅8歲）被強姦殺害的案件。他們採集了23萬個指紋，篩查了逾10萬個DNA樣本，還曾懸賞20萬元徵集線索。
Then, in 2016, they arrested a man on unrelated bribery charges, according to the state news media. Analyzing his genes, they found he was related to a person who had left his DNA at the site of the 2005 killing of one of the women. That person, Gao Chengyong, confessed to the crimes and was later executed.
據官方新聞媒體報導，2016年，一名與此案無關的男子因行賄罪被捕。警方通過分析此人的基因發現，他與2005年被殺害的其中一名女性案發現場發現的DNA存在親屬關係。那個人叫高承勇，他對罪行供認不諱，後來被處決。
Mr. Gao’s capture spurred the state media to call for the creation of a national database of male DNA. The police in Henan Province showed it was possible, after amassing samples from 5.3 million men, or roughly 10 percent of the province’s male population, between 2014 and 2016. In November 2017, the Ministry of Public Security, which controls the police, unveiled plans for a national database.
高承勇的抓獲促使官方媒體呼籲建立一個全國性的男性DNA數據庫。河南省警方在2014年至2016年間採集了530萬份男性樣本（約佔該省男性人口的10%），證明了這項工作的可行性。2017年11月，公安部宣布了建立全國數據庫的計劃。
China already holds the world’s largest trove of genetic material, totaling 80 million profiles, according to state media. But earlier DNA gathering efforts were often more focused. Officials targeted criminal suspects or groups they considered potentially destabilizing, like migrant workers in certain neighborhoods. The police have also gathered DNA from ethnic minority groups like the Uighurs as a way to tighten the Communist Party’s control over them.
據官方媒體報導，中國已經擁有世界上最大的遺傳物質庫，總共有8000萬份樣本。但早期的DNA採集工作往往有更大的針對性。官員們將目標鎖定在犯罪嫌疑人或他們認為可能破壞穩定的群體身上，比如某些社區的農民工。警方還採集了維吾爾族等少數民族的DNA，以加強中共對他們的控制。
The effort to compile a national male database broadens those efforts, said Emile Dirks, an author of the report from the Australian institute and a Ph.D. candidate in the department of political science at the University of Toronto. “We are seeing the expansion of those models to the rest of China in an aggressive way that I don’t think we’ve seen before,” Mr. Dirks said.
澳洲研究所這份報告的作者之一是加拿大多倫多大學(University of Toronto)政治科學系博士候選人埃米爾·德克斯(Emile Dirks)，她說，編纂全國男性DNA數據庫是以前那些努力的擴大。「我們正在目睹這些樣板以一種前所未見的激進方式向中國其他地區展開，」德克斯說。
In the report released by the Australian institute, it estimated that the authorities aimed to collect DNA samples from 35 million to 70 million men and boys, or roughly 5 percent to 10 percent of China’s male population. They do not need to sample every male, because one person’s DNA sample can unlock the genetic identity of male relatives.
澳洲研究所在發布的報告估計，當局的目標是採集3500萬至7000萬名成年與未成年男性的DNA樣本，約佔中國男性人口的5%至10%。政府不需要採集每個男性的樣本，因為通過一個人的DNA樣本可以找到男性親屬的基因特徵。
廣告
When The Times tried to fax questions about the database to the Ministry of Public Security, an employee said it could not accept them “without permission from a senior official.”
當時報試圖把有關數據庫的問題用傳真發給公安部時，部裡的一名工作人員說，「沒有高級官員的允許」，不能接收這些問題。
Local officials often publicly announce the results of their sampling. In Donglan County in the Guangxi region, the police said they had collected more than 10,800 samples, covering nearly 10 percent of the male population. In Yijun County in Shaanxi Province, the police said they had collected more than 11,700 samples, or one quarter.
地方官員經常公開宣布他們的採集工作。廣西東蘭縣的警方說，他們已經採集了逾10800個樣本，涵蓋了當地近10%的男性人口。陝西宜君縣的警方說，他們已經採集了逾11700份樣本，占當地男性人口的四分之一。
To estimate the project’s ambitions, the Australian institute looked at sampling rates in 10 counties and districts, then studied purchase orders for DNA test kits from 16 more jurisdictions. The Times reviewed the same public documents, along with 15 similar orders from the past six months that were not included in the report.
為了判斷這項行動的目標，澳洲研究所使用了10個縣和地區的採樣率，然後研究了另外16個司法管轄區的DNA檢測試劑盒的採購訂單。時報看到了同樣的公開文件，以及沒有包括在報告中的過去6個月的15份類似訂單。
Purchase orders were often filled by Chinese companies, but some contracts went to Thermo Fisher, the Massachusetts maker of genetics testing equipment.
採購訂單通常交給中國企業，但也有一些合同被麻薩諸塞州的基因檢測設備製造商賽默飛拿到。
Thermo Fisher has sold DNA testing kits to police agencies in at least nine counties and cities for establishing a “male ancestry inspection system,” or a male DNA database, according to corporate bidding documents found by Mr. Dirks and verified by The Times.
德克斯找到的企業招標文件顯示，賽默飛曾向至少九個縣市的警察部門出售過DNA檢測試劑盒，用於建立「男性家族檢測系統」，即男性DNA數據庫。這些文件已得到時報核實。
The company actively sought the business. In 2017, one week before the start of the DNA collection program, a company researcher, Dr. Zhong Chang, said at a conference in Beijing that the company could help, according to a video of the event. The company designed one testing kit to look for the specific genetic markers sought by the Ministry of Public Security, Dr. Zhong said, a common industry practice. Another was tailored to gather genetic information from members of China’s ethnic groups, including Uighurs and Tibetans, he said.
賽默飛在中國積極開展業務。據一個影片顯示，2017年，在公安部宣布DNA採集項目的一週前，賽默飛研究員鍾昌博士在北京的一個會議上說，賽默飛可以提供幫助。鍾昌說，賽默飛按照公安部要尋找的特定基因標記設計了一套檢測試劑盒，這是該行業的常見做法。他說，還有一套試劑盒是專門用來收集中國少數民族人士的基因信息的，包括維吾爾人和西藏人。
廣告
Dr. Zhong did not respond to requests for comment.
鍾昌沒有回覆記者的置評請求。
Thermo Fisher said its DNA kits “are the global standard for forensic DNA testing.” In a statement, the company said it recognized “the importance of considering how our products and services are used — or may be used — by our customers.”
賽默飛說，公司生產的DNA試劑盒「是全球法庭科學DNA檢測的標準」。賽默飛在一份聲明中表示，公司認識到「考慮我們的產品和服務被我們的客戶如何使用或可能使用的重要性」。
“We are proud to be a part of the many positive ways in which DNA identification has been applied, from tracking down criminals to stopping human trafficking and freeing the unjustly accused,” it added.
「我們對於能成為從追蹤罪犯到阻止人口販賣，再到讓受到不公正指控的人獲釋等許多DNA鑒定正面結果的一部分感到自豪，」公司還說。
China has other reasons to buy Thermo Fisher’s equipment aside from compiling genetic data to track people: The company’s gear can help Chinese physicians screen for deadly diseases. Thermo Fisher also sells DNA equipment to the police in many other countries.
除了構建用來追蹤人的基因數據外，中國也有其他原因要購買賽默飛的設備：該公司的設備能幫助中國醫生篩查致命疾病。賽默飛也向許多其他國家的警方出售DNA分析設備。
But scientists, medical ethicists and human rights groups say its equipment can also become a critical tool for social control. Last year, in the wake of criticism, the company said it would stop selling its gear to the authorities in Xinjiang, in northwestern China, where the police are collecting DNA from the largely Muslim Uighur minority group for social control purposes.
但科學家、醫學倫理學家和人權組織說，賽默飛的設備也能成為對社會控制至關重要的工具。去年，在受到批評後，賽默飛表示將停止向中國西北部的新疆當局出售產品。新疆警方出於社會控制的目的，正在收集主要信奉穆斯林的維吾爾族人的DNA。
Privacy and Consent
隱私和同意
Though the Chinese authorities are still building their database, its contents are already being used to ramp up surveillance.
儘管這個數據庫仍在建設中，但它已經被用於加強監控。
廣告
In March, officials in the Guanwen township in southwestern Sichuan Province said the male blood samples they had collected would be used to bolster the local Sharp Eyes Project, according to a government notice found by the Australian Strategic Policy Institute. The project is a major government surveillance program that encourages people in the countryside to report on their neighbors.
據澳洲戰略政策研究所獲得的一份政府通知，今年3月，中國西南部四川省關文鎮的官員說，他們採集的男性血樣將用於支持當地的「雪亮工程」，這是政府鼓勵農村居民報告鄰裡情況的重大監控項目。
Anke Bioengineering, a biotech company based in eastern Anhui Province, is using the male DNA database to build a “DNA Skynet,” according to Hu Bangjun, a spokesman for the company. Skynet is China’s policing system that combines video surveillance and big data.
總部設在安徽省的安科生物工程有限公司正在用男性DNA數據庫建立一個「DNA天網」，公司發言人胡邦君說道。天網是將影片監控與大數據結合起來的警務系統。
But the national male DNA collection program is running into an unusual amount of opposition in China. Generally, China’s citizens have accepted intrusions by the central government into their internet use and other facets of life. But DNA collection is not regulated under Chinese law, and officials worry that the public would react negatively to a broad database containing their genetic secrets and family ties.
但是，全國男性DNA採集計劃正在中國遭到程度不同尋常的反對。一般來說，中國公民已經接受了中央政府侵擾他們上網以及生活其他方面的做法。但中國法律沒有關於DNA採集的規定，官員們擔心公眾會對一個涵蓋他們的基因祕密和家庭關係的龐大數據庫產生負面反應。
At a session of China’s Parliament in March, two delegates to China’s political advisory body proposed that the government regulate DNA collection. One of them, Wang Ying, an official from Beijing, said that when the technology reached a certain scale, the government needed to protect the rights of users “in a timely manner.”
在3月份的兩會上，兩名政協委員建議政府規範DNA採集。其中的北京官員王英說，當這種技術的使用達到一定規模時，政府需要「及時」保護使用者的權利。
In 2015, Liu Bing, the deputy chief forensic physician at the Ministry of Public Security’s Institute of Forensic Science, warned in the ministry’s forensic journal that the collection of blood samples “with improper measures” could cause social instability, especially “in today’s society where the citizens’ awareness of their legal rights is increasing.”
2015年，公安部物證鑒定中心副主任法醫師劉冰曾在該部主辦的法醫期刊上發文警告，採集血液樣本「措施方式不當」可能導致社會不穩定，尤其是「在公民法制意識日益增強的當今社會」。
The authorities have moved quietly. Mr. Dirks, co-author of the Australian paper, said nearly all of the collection was taking place in the countryside, where there was little understanding of the implications of the program.
當局已經悄然行動。澳洲這份報告的合著者德克斯說，幾乎所有的採集都是在農村進行的，那裡的人們不太了解這個項目可能的影響。
In rural areas, many officials show pride in their work. Officials in the city of Dongguan posted a photo showing boys in an elementary school lining up to have a teacher collect their blood. Officials in Shaanxi Province also posted online a photo of six boys gathered around a table at an elementary school watching a police officer take blood from one of their friends.
許多農村地區的官員們在自豪地展示他們的工作。東莞市官員發布的一張照片顯示，一所小學的男孩們排隊讓老師採集血樣。陝西省官員也在網上發了一張照片，照片中，一所小學的六個男孩圍坐在桌子旁，看著警察採集其中一名小朋友的血樣。
In another photo from northwestern Shaanxi, a young boy bawls in front of two police officers as one squeezes his fingertip for blood. A woman consoles him.
在來自陝西的另一張照片中，一個小男孩在兩名警察面前嚎啕大哭，而其中一名警察則捏著他的指尖取血樣。一名女子在安慰這個男孩。
It is unclear whether the people in those photos fully understood what the blood collection was for. Interviews and social media posts have suggested that the failure to give blood would result in punishment.
目前還不清楚照片中的這些人是否完全明白采血樣的目的。採訪獲得的信息以及社群媒體的帖子暗示，不讓采血樣的人將受到懲罰。
Mr. Jiang, the computer engineer, lives and works in Beijing but is originally from a village in Shaanxi. In February 2019, the police told him that he had to return to his village to give his DNA samples.
雖然身為計算機工程師的江先生在北京生活工作，但他老家在陝西省的一個村莊。2019年2月，警方通知，他必須回老家提供DNA樣本。
In an interview, Mr. Jiang said he had paid a hospital in Beijing to collect his samples and mailed them. He was not told why his blood was needed, nor did he ask. He shrugged off privacy concerns. Since China’s people are required to carry identity cards and use their real identities online, he said, “all our information is with them already.”
江先生在採訪中說，他花錢讓北京的一家醫院採集樣本，寄回老家。警方沒有告知他為什麼需要他的血，他也沒有問。他不在乎隱私問題。由於中國人需要隨身攜帶身份證，並在網上使用實名，他說，「他們已經掌握了我們所有的信息。」
But rights activists say genetic science gives the Chinese authorities unprecedented powers to prosecute people they do not like. They could cite DNA to give their accusations even more credibility in the eyes of the public. In the hands of local officials, said Li Wei, a rights activist, DNA evidence could also be planted. The Beijing police already have a sample of his DNA, collected while he was serving a two-year sentence for “disrupting public order,” a charge that the authorities use against many dissidents.
但維權活動人士說，基因科學讓中國當局有了前所未有的權力，起訴他們不喜歡的人。當局可以用DNA證據讓他們的指控在公眾眼中更具可信度。維權人士李蔚說，地方官員也可能使用DNA證據來栽贓。北京警方已經有了他的DNA樣本，那是他被以「擾亂公共秩序」罪名判處兩年監禁期間採集的，當局起訴許多異見人士時都使用這個罪名。
Two years ago, the police in Hangzhou tried to get a sample of their own, he said. They knocked on his hotel room door soon after he checked in. Mr. Li said that when he refused to go to the police station, they hit him with rubber batons and dragged him there. But when they asked for a DNA sample, Mr. Li said, he stood fast, fearing that the Hangzhou police could use it against him.
他說，兩年前，杭州的警察曾試圖採集樣本。他剛在一家酒店登記入住後不久，警察就來敲門。李先生說，當他拒絕跟他們去派出所後，警察用橡膠警棍打他，並把他強行帶到那裡。但當警察要求他提供DNA樣本時，他說，他沒有讓步，他擔心杭州警方會用這個樣本來對付他。
“In some cases, your blood and saliva, which was collected in advance, can be put at the crime scene later,” Mr. Li said. “You’re not there, but your DNA might be on the scene. This is what I’m worried about — the possibility of being framed.”
「在一些情況下，甚至說你的血液、唾液，我可以提前採集，之後放到犯罪現場，」李先生說。「你沒有去，但你的DNA在現場，這也是我擔心的，就是栽贓陷害。」
