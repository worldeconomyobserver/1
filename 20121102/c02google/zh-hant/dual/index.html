歐洲出版機構要求谷歌為鏈接付費

A Clash Across Europe Over the Value of a Click

ERIC PFANNER
2012年11月2日
PARIS — Google got rich by selling a simple proposition: The links it provides to other Web sites are worth a lot of money, so much that millions of advertisers are willing to pay the company billions of dollars for them.
巴黎——谷歌(Google)靠販賣一個簡單的命題賺了大錢：為其他網站提供鏈接，而這些鏈接價值不菲，千百萬家廣告商願意為此向谷歌支付數十億美元。
Now some European newspaper and magazine publishers, frustrated by their inability to make more of their own money from the Web, want to reverse the equation. Google, they say, should pay them for links, because they provide the material on which the Web giant is generating all that revenue.
現在一些歐洲報刊雜誌出版機構，因為無力通過網絡為自己賺取更多收入而感到受挫，想要扭轉這一格局。他們提出，谷歌應該為顯示這些鏈接向它們付錢，因為谷歌的全部利潤都來源於出版商提供的材料。
In several of the biggest European countries, they are close to getting their way.
在歐洲的幾個大國，他們就快要如願以償了。
A bill working its way through the German Parliament would enable publishers to collect a fee from Google and other search engines and news “aggregators” when they display excerpts from news articles alongside links to newspaper and magazine Web sites.
德國議會正在審議一項法案，根據該法案，如果谷歌或其他搜索引擎及新聞“聚合器”展示新聞報道的節選，並附上報刊雜誌網站的鏈接時，出版方可以向它們收費。
This week, President François Hollande of France threatened Google with similar legislation unless it came up with a way to compensate news sites by the end of the year. Last week, publishers in Italy said they would also lobby for such a law.
本周，法國總統弗朗索瓦·奧朗德(François Hollande)也威脅道，除非谷歌能在年底前找出補償新聞網站的方法，否則也會採取類似的立法。上周，意大利出版商聲稱他們也將為這樣的法律進行遊說。
But Google so far is standing its ground. Already facing possible sanctions from European antitrust and privacy regulators, Google says that having to pay for links could threaten its “very existence.”
但谷歌至今尚未讓步。該公司表示，如果必須為鏈接付費，可能會威脅到自己的“生存”。谷歌目前正面臨著遭受歐洲反壟斷和隱私監管機構制裁的可能性。
And it warned that the demand for money could backfire. If Google had to pay up, it “would consequently be forced to stop indexing the French sites,” Google wrote in a “position paper” it sent to the French government. Because 30 percent to 40 percent of the traffic on French news sites comes from Google’s links, the company’s threat is not an idle one.
而且谷歌還警告說，出版商要錢的行為可能會搬起石頭砸自己的腳。谷歌在致法國政府的“意見書”中寫道，如果谷歌必須付費的話，谷歌“可能會因此被迫停止對法國網站的索引。”法國新聞網站30%到40%的訪問量來自於谷歌的鏈接，所以谷歌這一威脅並非虛張聲勢。
Google said such laws would undermine its commitment to an open Internet and the free flow of information. It would also invert the company’s main business model. A majority of the company’s $38 billion in annual revenue comes from the sale of “sponsored links,” which appear alongside free search results. Google also sells advertisements on behalf of outside partners, including news sites, and shares revenue with them.
谷歌說，這種法律會破壞谷歌對於互聯網開放及信息自由流動的承諾。這種法律也會顛覆谷歌主要的商業模式。谷歌公司年收入380億美元（約合2280億元人民幣），大部分來自於銷售那些在免費的搜索結果旁邊顯示的“贊助商鏈接”。谷歌也代表包括新聞網站在內的外部夥伴銷售廣告，並與他們分享收入。
European publishers say their existence is even more precarious, unless they can start to generate more money from their Web sites and other digital products, like mobile applications. While advertising revenue continues to rise at Google, it has flattened out or is even falling at many European online publications.
歐洲出版商說，除非能夠開始從其網站及其他數字產品，如手機應用軟件中獲得更多收入，否則它們自身的生存會更加岌岌可危。儘管谷歌的廣告收入在持續上升，但許多歐洲在線出版商的廣告收入卻一直持平，甚至還出現下降。
“We effectively feed the search engine and the algorithm, constantly giving them fresh content, content that you can rely on, because it’s checked and it’s accurate,” said Nathalie Collin, the head of the I.P.G., an organization of French newspaper and magazine publishers. “This is why they can sell advertising.”
法國報刊出版商行業組織IPG負責人娜塔莉·科蘭(Nathalie Collin)說，“實際上我們是在餵養搜索引擎，餵養它的算法，我們一直都在為它提供新鮮的內容，而且這些內容是可靠的，都經過核實，是準確的。所以他們才能銷售廣告。”
Mr. Hollande appears to agree with them. In a meeting with Eric E. Schmidt, the executive chairman of Google, at the Élysée Palace this week, the president “expressed his desire that negotiations between Google and news publishers begin rapidly and conclude by the end of the year,” his office said in a statement.
法國總統奧朗德似乎同意他們的說法。總統辦公室在一份聲明中說，本周在愛麗舍宮會見谷歌執行董事長埃里克·E·施密特(Eric E. Schmidt)時，奧朗德“表達了他的期望。他希望谷歌和新聞出版商之間的談判迅速開始，並在年底結束”。
Mr. Hollande “stressed that dialogue and negotiation between partners appeared to him to be the better option, but that if necessary, a law could be implemented on this question, following the example of Germany.”
奧朗德“強調，他認為開展夥伴之間的對話及談判是較好的選項，但是如有必要，也可以效仿德國，用法律處理該問題”。
The German proposal cited by Mr. Hollande would create a so-called ancillary copyright, protecting online news content and regulating secondary uses of it, including the snippets that search engines and aggregators like Google News display to detail links to other sites.
奧朗德提到的德國議案，會創設所謂的“鄰接權”，保護網絡上的新聞內容，並規範間接使用這些內容的行為，包括搜索引擎及谷歌新聞(Google News)等新聞聚合器展示新聞片段，並以此描述指向其他網站的鏈接的行為。
Business users, like Google, would have to pay royalties in order to display news publishers’ material, even short excerpts. A collecting society, like the agencies that gather royalties on behalf of musicians, would collect the payments and distribute the money owed to publishers.
商業使用者，如谷歌，必須支付版稅才能展示新聞出版者的材料，即使是短小的摘要也不例外。像代表音樂人徵收版稅的機構一樣，一家徵收機構會收取費用，並向出版商發放應得的收益。
The government of Chancellor Angela Merkel introduced the legislation last winter. The Bundesrat, or upper house of Parliament, has approved the measure, and debate is set to begin in the lower house, the Bundestag, in November.
德國總理安格拉·默克爾(Angela Merkel)領導的政府去年冬天引入了這一立法。德國國會上議院聯邦參議院(Bundesrat)已經批准了該項舉措，相關辯論定於今年11月在國會下議院聯邦議院(Bundestag)展開。
In Italy, discussions are at an earlier stage, but the main trade association for news publishers pledged last week to work with their French and German counterparts and to lobby the government for relief. Media owners in Austria, Switzerland and other European countries are also studying the issue.
在意大利，相關討論還沒有深入到這種程度。不過該國新聞出版機構主要的行業協會上周宣稱，將與法國和德國同行共同努力，遊說政府進行扶持。奧地利、瑞士，以及其他歐洲國家的媒體經營者也正在研究這個問題。
The push for stricter copyright protection on the Continent runs counter to the trend toward greater liberalization elsewhere, as other countries update their intellectual property laws for the free-flowing digital era.
歐洲大陸推動對版權實行更嚴格保護的努力，與其他地區擴大自由化的前進趨勢可謂背道而馳。為了適應數字時代信息的自由流動，其他國家正在更新各自的知識產權法規。
In Britain, for example, a government-sponsored panel recently recommended loosening copyright restrictions that outlaw even private copying of protected material. In the United States, the “fair use” provision of copyright law permits short citations of protected material.
例如，在英國，政府支持的一個委員會最近建議放寬版權限制。就連私人複製受版權保護的材料，在現有限制下也被視為違法。在美國，版權法中的“合理使用”條款允許簡短引用受版權保護的材料。
“Countries around the world are moving fast to enact forward-looking, 21st-century copyright rules that encourage innovation, creativity and economic growth,” said Simon Morrison, public policy manager at Google. “We hope to work with publishers in France and Germany to achieve the same goal.”
“世界各國都在迅速採取行動，頒佈具有前瞻性、適應21世紀的版權法規，從而鼓勵創新、創意和經濟增長。”谷歌公司公共政策經理西蒙·莫里森(Simon Morrison)說，“我們希望與法國和德國的出版商共同努力，達成相同的目標。”
While copyright rules have traditionally been more restrictive in Continental Europe than in the United States, publishers say there is another important difference making new protection — and funding — necessary. The Internet has given the biggest English-language publishers a global presence, in some cases providing them with tens of millions of new readers. This improves their chances of using Google’s systems to raise advertising revenue, though many English-language papers are struggling online, too.
歐洲大陸的版權法規傳統上一直都比美國更加嚴格，但出版商表示，由於還存在另一項重要差異，保護（以及付費）是必要的。互聯網讓最大的英語出版機構走向了全球市場，在一些情況下為它們帶來了數千萬新讀者。這就提高了這些出版商通過谷歌的系統，提高廣告收入的機會，儘管許多英語報紙在互聯網上處境也十分艱難。
In countries like Germany, France and Italy, where news sites’ online audiences are more limited by language and nationality, online ad revenue has been further restricted. Total Internet ad sales at French newspaper and magazine sites is about €150 million, or $194 million, a year, Ms. Collin said.
在德國、法國和意大利等國，新聞網站的網絡受眾在語言和國籍方面受到了更大局限，一直以來網絡廣告收入也受到了更大限制。科蘭說，法國報紙和雜誌網站互聯網廣告的年銷售額總計約1.5億歐元（約合1.94億美元）。
While Google does not break down its revenue country by country, media buyers say it generates around €1.5 billion in France. At current growth rates, the company would soon overtake the country’s leading television network, TF1, as the biggest advertising-supported medium in France.
儘管谷歌並不按國別公布廣告收入，但購買媒體資源的廣告業者透露，該公司在法國的收入約有15億歐元。按當前的增長速度計算，谷歌很快就會超越法國的主要電視網TF1，成為法國最大的靠廣告收入支撐的媒體。
The copyright proposal in Germany has drawn opposition from a wide range of groups, including lobbyists for nonpublishing businesses, who see it adding costs, because they might have to pay more for online information. The Green party and libertarian groups have denounced it as a restraint on free speech. Bloggers have mocked it as backward-looking and cumbersome to enforce.
德國的版權提案在很多群體中引發了廣泛的反對，其中包括代表非出版業企業利益的遊說人士，他們認為這一提案會增加成本，因為他們可能需要為網絡信息支付更多資金。綠黨(Green Party)和自由意志派團體譴責這一提案是在限制言論自由。博客作者則嘲笑這個提案觀點守舊，而且執行起來十分困難。
“In the end, what underlies the term ‘ancillary copyright’ is nothing more than an attempt to subsidize German newspapers at the expense of other sectors,” wrote Justus Haucap, president of an independent commission that advises the German government on competition matters, in the newspaper Der Tagesspiegel. “There is no objective justification for it. Such a subsidy merely slows down structural change and hinders the development of innovative business models.”
尤斯圖斯·豪卡普(Justus Haucap)領導的獨立委員會就競爭議題為德國政府提供諮詢。豪卡普在德國《每日鏡報》(Der Tagesspiegel)撰文指出，“說到底，‘鄰接權’一詞背後，無非是在嘗試損害其他行業的利益，為德國報紙提供補貼。這樣做並沒有客觀的理由，提供這種補貼只會延緩結構性轉變，阻礙具有創新性的商業模式的發展。”
Google argues that its links are responsible for four billion visits to French news sites each month, and that they would be foolish to risk losing this traffic. But publishers say they doubt that Google would actually follow through on its threat to stop indexing their content, saying this would undermine the company’s stated commitment to free speech.
谷歌表示，其鏈接每月為法國的新聞網站提供了40億次的訪問量，甘願冒險失去這些流量恐怕有些愚蠢。然而出版商則表示，他們認為谷歌並不會真的切實執行上述威脅，停止索引出版商的內容。他們指出，那樣做會破壞谷歌公司對言論自由作出的明確承諾。
In Brazil, more than 150 newspapers recently decided to collectively pull out of Google News, which displays headlines and the first few lines of news articles — enough, publishers say, that many readers do not bother clicking to the article. A similar battle has been going on for years in Belgium, where publishers won a copyright lawsuit requiring Google to stop including their content in Google News but to keep it in the search engine.
在巴西，有超過150份報紙最近決定集體退出谷歌新聞。谷歌新聞顯示新聞報道的標題和頭幾行內容，但出版商表示，這就足以讓許多讀者不再點擊鏈接閱讀文章了。一場類似的爭議也在比利時持續了很多年，出版商贏得了一場版權訴訟，要求谷歌不要在谷歌新聞中顯示其內容，但仍在搜索引擎中列出。
Rather than free speech, many publishers say, what is really at stake is money. How much revenue would be raised by such a system remains unclear, because the proposals do not specify the fees that would be charged.
許多出版商表示，真正受到衝擊的不是言論自由，而是錢。但沒人知道通過這一體系能夠獲得多少收入，因為提案中並沒有明文規定會收多少費用。
Christoph Keese, head of public affairs at Axel Springer, publisher of the German newspapers Bild and Die Welt, said rates would “not be prohibitive.”
阿克塞爾·施普林格出版社(Axel Springer)公共事務主管克里斯托弗·克澤(Christoph Keese)表示，費率不會“高得付不起”。這家出版社旗下有《圖片報》(Bild)和《世界報》(Die Welt)。
“Publishers don’t want to hinder aggregation; they want to foster it,” he said. “The only thing we want to stop is aggregation at the price of zero.”
“出版商並不想阻礙新聞聚合，而是想促進它。”他說，“我們想要阻止的只是零成本的聚合。”
