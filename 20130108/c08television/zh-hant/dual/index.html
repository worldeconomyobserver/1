更大更智能，電視廠商戰未來

As Sales Slip, TV Makers Strain for the Next Sensation

BRIAN X. CHEN
2013年1月8日
LAS VEGAS — Your smartphone is the screen in your pocket. Your computer is the screen on your desk. Your tablet is a screen for the couch.
拉斯維加斯——智能手機是你口袋中的顯示屏；電腦是你寫字檯上的顯示屏；平板電腦是你在沙發上玩的顯示屏。
Almost every major electronic device you own is a black rectangle that is brought to life by software and content. So how can hardware companies make their products stand out in a sea of black rectangles?
幾乎你的每一件主要電子設備都是一個黑色矩形，通過軟件和內容發揮作用。那麼，硬件公司如何讓它們的產品在一片黑色矩形中嶄露頭角呢？
That challenge will be on display at the Las Vegas Convention Center on Tuesday through Friday at the 46th annual Consumer Electronics Show, one of the largest technology conventions based on attendance, which is expected to exceed 150,000 this year. And one that is particularly acute for television makers. “The hardware is no longer what’s driving the future,” said James L. McQuivey, an analyst for Forrester Research. “The hardware is kind of boring.”
拉斯維加斯國際會展中心(Las Vegas Convention Center)舉行的消費者電子產品展(Consumer Electronics Show)是到場人數最多的年度技術展會之一，預計今年的第46屆展會的人數將超過15萬。在下周二到周五的這次展會上，我們將看到硬件商面臨怎樣的挑戰。對於電視廠家來說，挑戰尤其艱巨。“硬件已經不再是未來發展的推動力了，”弗雷斯特研究公司(Forrester Research)的分析師詹姆斯·L·麥奎維(James L. McQuivey)說，“硬件有點無聊了。”
More exciting things are happening in software, Mr. McQuivey said. For example, dozens of tablets are on the market, but Apple and Amazon lead the pack because of the impressive apps and digital content available for their devices, he said.
麥奎維說，更有趣的發展都出現在軟件領域。他說，比如，市場中有幾十種平板電腦，但只有蘋果和亞馬遜成了領頭羊，原因是它們的設備上有令人印象深刻的應用程序和數碼內容。
This year, television makers like Samsung, Sony, LG and Panasonic are trying to grab attention by supersizing their television screens and quadrupling the level of detail in their images. And manufacturers continue to push the idea of “smart” sets by adding apps and other interactive elements.
今年，諸如三星(Samsung)、索尼(Sony)、LG和松下(Panasonic)等電視製造商正試圖吸引消費者，它們擴大了電視顯示屏尺寸，並且將其畫質水平翻了兩番。而且製造商繼續推動“智能”的理念，為電視機加上了應用程序和其他的互動元素。
For the electronics industry, the television is an important but increasingly difficult product to sell. Just seven years ago, big-screen sets that cost thousands of dollars were major profit generators. But more recently, even as televisions have gotten bigger and better looking, they have dropped significantly in price amid heated competition.
對於電子產業來說，電視是一種重要的產品，但已經變得越來越難賣。僅僅七年前，價格數千美元的大屏幕電視還是主要的收入來源。但最近，即便是更大更清晰的電視，其價格在激烈的競爭中也掉得厲害。
To make matters worse, consumers are buying new televisions as often as they buy a new car, not as often as a new computer or phone. And people can now watch video on smartphones, tablets and computers, reducing the need to buy a television at all.
更糟的是，消費者更新換代電視的頻率，如今和他們購買一輛新車的頻率差不多，不如換一台電腦或手機那麼頻繁。而且人們現在可以在智能手機、平板電腦和台式電腦上觀看視頻，進一步降低了對電視的需要。
As they try to prop up profits, electronics makers are trying hard to establish a new high-end category of televisions. They are promoting what they call Ultra High-Definition televisions, which have four times as many pixels as their high-definition predecessors. Some of these new televisions can cost as much as a car, like Sony’s 84-inch Ultra HDTV, which is priced at $25,000. But Sony says it will unveil Ultra HDTVs at the show that are smaller and less expensive.
電子製造商試圖增加利潤，嘗試創建一種新的高端電視類別。它們正在推一種所謂的“超高清電視”，其分辨率比前一代高清電視翻了兩番。其中一些新電視的價格堪比一輛汽車，例如索尼的84英寸超高清電視，售價2.5萬美元（約合15.6萬元人民幣）。不過索尼稱，將在這次展會上展出一種超高清電視，其尺寸要小一些，也便宜一些。
Mike Lucas, a senior vice president at Sony, called its 84-inch set the Ferrari of televisions. But he said that with the new versions, “we’re moving out from the Ferrari world and more into the Audi, Lexus and Mercedes side of the world.” He declined to say how much the smaller Ultra HD sets would cost, but said they would be more expensive than the older HDTVs.
索尼高級副總裁邁克·盧卡斯(Mike Lucas)稱其84英寸超高清電視為“電視中的法拉利(Ferrari)”。不過他說，隨着新款超高清電視的推出，“我們從電視世界中的法拉利，正在變成電視世界中的奧迪(Audi)、雷克薩斯(Lexus)和梅賽德斯-奔馳(Mercedes)。”他拒絕透漏這個小一些尺寸的超高清電視的價格，但他說它們會比舊款高清電視貴出很多。
Samsung will also introduce new televisions this week, including an Ultra HDTV that emphasizes software. Joe Stinziano, senior vice president for home entertainment at Samsung Electronics America, said a majority of the new Samsung sets this year would be smart televisions — Internet-enabled televisions that run apps for things like Netflix and Facebook.
三星將同樣在這周推出新款電視，包括強調軟件功能的超高清電視。三星電子美國分公司負責家庭娛樂的高級副總裁喬·斯蒂尼澤埃奧(Joe Stinziano)說，三星今年推出的新款電視機中的大多數都將是智能電視——這些具有上網功能的電視可以安裝用於瀏覽Netflix和Facebook的應用程序。
“The television has always been the center of the entertainment of the home,” Mr. Stinziano said. “Now it will be the center of a connected home.”
“電視一直是家庭娛樂的中心，”斯蒂尼澤埃奧說，“現在它將成為一個聯網家庭的中心。”
Raymond Soneira, president of DisplayMate Technologies, a consulting firm that studies displays, said he was not enthusiastic about the new higher resolution sets. Mr. Soneira said Ultra HD would make a difference only on screens that were at least 80 inches, measured diagonally. For smaller screens, the extra pixels would not be visible to a person with 20/20 vision viewing from a normal distance of at least 7.2 feet, he said.
一家研究顯示器的諮詢公司“顯示伴侶科技”(DisplayMate Technologies)的總裁雷蒙德·索內拉(Raymond Soneira)說，他對於新的更高分別率的電視不是十分熱衷。索內拉說，超高清技術只有在至少80寸（測量對角線）大的屏幕上才能顯示它的魅力。他說，在更小的屏幕上，一個視力是1.0的人在7.2英尺以外觀看，是看不出分別的。
“It’s a bit of a marketing push,” Mr. Soneira said. “It’s really pointless for a small TV under typical viewing conditions.”
“這是一種營銷手段，”索內拉說，“對於一台小電視機來說，在正常的視覺條件下，這種技術毫無意義。”
Mr. McQuivey of Forrester agreed, adding that very little content is available that takes advantage of the Ultra HD format. In the last two years, television makers have tried a similar push with 3-D sets, but consumers decided that the ability to watch a handful of movies in 3-D did not justify spending thousands of dollars on a new television. He predicted that Ultra HD TVs would also be a flop.
弗雷斯特研究公司的麥奎維同意這一觀點，他還補充道，市場上能發揮超高清格式優勢的視頻內容很少。在過去兩年，電視製造商曾採用相似手段來推廣3D電視，但是消費者認為，花成千上萬美元買一台新電視，僅為了能看幾部3D格式的電影，不值得。他預言，超高清電視將以失敗告終。
Industry officials cited reasons for more optimism. Gary Shapiro, chief executive of the Consumer Electronics Association, which puts on the electronics show, said he once predicted that high-definition television would fail because of the lack of HD content available at the time. But it turned out that early adopters were buying the sets because they made DVDs look better.
但產業界的管理者則認為，有理由看好超高清電視。消費者電子產品展的主辦方消費者電子協會(Consumer Electronics Association)的總裁加里·夏皮羅(Gary Shapiro)說，他曾預言高清電視將會失敗，因為當時高清製作的視頻內容不多。但結果是，早期採用該產品的人購買這種電視是因為它使DVD影碟的視覺效果更佳。
“With Ultra HD, you’re going to see people stepping up, too, and you’ll see at the show it’s going to be a big deal,” Mr. Shapiro said.
“超高清電視也一樣，你會看到人們開始跟上潮流，在這場展會上，你會看到這些產品引起轟動，” 夏皮羅說道。
Mr. Lucas of Sony said its Ultra HD TVs would use software to pump up the number of pixels in videos to make them look as if they were shot at a higher resolution.
索尼公司的盧卡斯說，他們的超高清電視將使用軟件來增加視頻中的像素數量，使視頻看上去是以更高像素拍攝而成的。
But Mr. Soneira said that this approach, called “upconverting,” cannot add details that were not in the original image, and would not make a difference on screens smaller than 80 inches.
但是，索內拉說，這種稱為“升頻轉換”的手段不能增加原本圖像中沒有的細節，且在80英寸以下的屏幕上也體現不出其效果。
Apple, the technology industry’s most profitable hardware maker, does not attend trade shows, including the Consumer Electronics Show, because it can easily reach customers through its retail stores. But it is always on competitors’ minds. Rumors have been swirling for years that Apple would make its own television, and many analysts expect it to introduce one this year.
科技領域最賺錢的硬件製造商蘋果公司不參加任何行業展覽，包括消費者電子產品展，因為它可以通過自己的零售店很容易地面向顧客。但它的競爭對手們都不會忘了它。傳說蘋果將生產自己的電視，這種說法已傳了好幾年，很多分析人士預計，蘋果今年就將推出自己的電視。
Mr. McQuivey of Forrester has been writing for some time about what Apple should do with a television. He said Apple should ignore the Ultra HD game and make the first “non-TV TV.”
弗雷斯特研究公司的麥奎維近來一直在寫文章分析蘋果應該怎麼做電視。他說，蘋果應該別管這場超高清的角逐，製作第一台“非電視的電視”。
He imagined a 32-inch screen that can be controlled with touch and voice commands, hung on the wall of a room where a family would get together to talk or eat. Apple could push software developers to make apps that serve as the hub of family life, to supplement the built-in shared calendars, photos and video chats with Grandma, he said.
他設想一面32英寸的顯示屏，可用過觸摸以及聲音進行控制，可以掛在家人常聚，聊天或吃飯的房間的牆上。他說，蘋果可以推動軟件開發者製作可以作為家庭生活樞紐的應用，增強其自帶的可共享的日曆、照片功能，以及和祖母視頻聊天的功能。
“Apple needs to create a new experience for the television, not a new dimension of hardware,” Mr. McQuivey said. “That’s part of what has to change in the long run.”
“蘋果需要創造一種全新的電視體驗，而不是開發一個新尺寸的電視硬件，”麥奎維說道，“從長遠來看，這種變革是必然的。”
