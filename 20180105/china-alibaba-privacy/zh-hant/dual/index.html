用戶隱私意識增強，中國互聯網巨頭面臨挑戰

Internet Users in China Expect to Be Tracked. Now, They Want Privacy.

孟建國
2018年1月5日
SHANGHAI — China’s biggest online payment company offers its hundreds of millions of users a breakdown on their spending each year, showing everything from their environmental impact to their ranking among shoppers in their area. Many spenders — not shy, and occasionally even a bit boastful about their personal finances — in turn share the details on social media.
上海——每年，中國最大的在線支付公司都會向數億用戶提供消費帳單，列出從用戶對環境的影響到他們在所在地區購物者排名的各類數據。很多用戶反過來又把這些詳細信息分享在社群媒體上。他們不僅不害羞，偶爾甚至還對自己的個人財務沾沾自喜。
This year, the marketing stunt has run into a problem: China’s growing sense of personal privacy.
今年，這項營銷活動遇到了一個問題：中國日益增強的個人隱私意識。
Ant Financial, an affiliate of the e-commerce giant Alibaba Group, apologized to users on Thursday after prompting an outcry by automatically enrolling in its social credit program those who wanted to see the breakdown. The program, called Sesame Credit, tracks personal relationships and behavior patterns to help determine lending decisions.
週四，在自動將希望看到帳單的用戶納入其社會信用體系的行為引發強烈抗議後，電子商務巨頭阿里巴巴集團的子公司螞蟻金服向用戶道歉。名為芝麻信用的該體系會追蹤用戶的人際關係和行為模式，幫助做出貸款決定。
That Sesame Credit program is part of a broader push in China to track how people go about their day, one that could feed into the Chinese government’s ambitious — and, some people would say, Orwellian — effort to use technology to keep a closer watch on its citizens.
芝麻信用是中國追蹤人們日常生活的廣泛行動的一部分，可能會促進中國政府進行一項宏大的，有人也會說是歐威爾式的行動。該行動的內容是利用科技手段，更加密切地監視中國公民。
廣告
The episode was a rare, public rebuttal of a prevailing trend in China. The country’s largest internet companies, and the government itself, have gathered ever more data on internet users. While Chinese culture does not emphasize personal privacy and Chinese internet users have grown accustomed to surveillance and censorship, the anger represents a nascent, but growing, demand for increased privacy and data protections online.
此次事件是中國民眾反抗盛行趨勢的罕見案例。中國最大的互聯網公司和政府自己收集的互聯網用戶的信息更多。雖然中國的文化不強調個人隱私，網路用戶也習慣了監控和審查，但這種憤怒代表著對加強隱私和網路信息保護的需求。這種需求剛出現不久，卻日益強烈。
Fueled in part by widespread internet fraud and personal information theft, the call for privacy, if it continues, could become a major challenge to China’s internet titans, and eventually to the cyber-authoritarian aspirations of the Chinese government itself.
廣泛的網路詐騙和個人信息被盜現象在一定程度上推動了保護隱私呼聲的出現。如果繼續下去，這種呼聲可能會成為中國互聯網巨頭，乃至中國政府自己的網路威權主義傾向面臨的一大挑戰。
China has long had high levels of surveillance, from ubiquitous closed-circuit cameras in its big cities to the monitoring of much of its citizens’ online communications. But such efforts have proliferated as new technologies like facial recognition have begun to be rolled out and artificial intelligence has made crunching vast amounts of data much easier.
中國長期實行嚴密的監視，從大城市的閉路攝像頭到監視許多公民的線上交流。但隨著人臉識別等新技術開始推出和人工智慧大大降低了處理海量數據的難度，這種監視迅速增加。
For now, it is far from clear that this rising discomfort will give way to policy changes. Many Chinese people, even as they express concerns about how companies use their data online, offer little criticism of Beijing’s desire to use that data to help compile a broad social tracking system that rewards what it regards as good citizens and punishes others.
目前還不清楚這種日漸加劇的不安會不會帶來政策上的改變。很多中國人儘管對企業如何利用他們在網上的信息表示擔憂，卻很少批評中國政府希望利用相關數據來建立一個廣泛的社會追蹤系統的行為。該系統會獎勵被其視為好公民的人，並懲罰另一些人。
Still, recent signs indicate Chinese consumers are beginning to express some of the same privacy concerns long found in the United States and elsewhere.
儘管如此，最近的一些跡象表明，中國消費者開始表現出一些在美國和其他地方早已出現的隱私顧慮。
This week, one of China’s most visible business leaders bemoaned what he said was a lack of privacy in China in general, and specifically on the country’s most widely used mobile chat service, WeChat.
本週，中國最有名的商業領袖之一感嘆他所謂的中國普遍缺乏隱私，尤其是在中國使用最廣的移動聊天服務平台微信上。
廣告
“There’s no privacy and information security these days,” Li Shufu, the chairman of Geely Holding Group and Volvo Cars, said at a New Year’s forum. “When you walk on the road, there are surveillance cameras everywhere.”
 「現在的人幾乎是全部的透明了，沒有任何的隱私和信息的安全，」吉利控股集團和富豪汽車公司董事長李書福在一個新年論壇上說。「你在路上走，到處是攝像頭。」
“Pony Ma must be reading our WeChat messages every single day,” he added, referring to the founder of Tencent, the Chinese internet giant that runs the social media and chat app, which has almost one billion users.
「馬化騰肯定天天在看我們的微信，」他接著說。這裡提到的是微信所屬的中國互聯網巨頭騰訊的創始人。作為一個社群媒體和聊天應用，微信有近10億用戶。
In a statement, Tencent said that the company did not store the chat history of users and that it would never use chat history for big data analytics. The comments were met with widespread disbelief: WeChat users have been arrested over what they’ve said on the app, conversations have turned up as evidence in court proceedings, and activists have reported being followed based on WeChat conversations.
騰訊在一份聲明中說，公司沒有存儲用戶的聊天記錄，也不會用聊天記錄來進行大數據分析。這一言論遭到了廣泛的質疑：有用戶因在微信上的發言被捕、微信對話作為證據出現在法庭審理中、活動人士反映自己被人利用微信上的對話跟蹤。
“It has become a default setting for me now to assume that we have no privacy in the face of Alibaba and Tencent,” said Li Luyao, a college student in Beijing.
「反正我現在是默認對於阿里和騰訊來說，我們是沒有什麼隱私的，」北京的大學生李路遙說。
Mr. Li said he did not believe Tencent’s assurances that it was not storing chat logs, and he expressed anger at Ant Financial for resorting to what he called “old tricks.”
李路遙說他不相信騰訊不存儲聊天記錄的保證，並對螞蟻金服訴諸於他所說的「習慣玩法」感到憤怒。
In its end-of-year feature, Ant Financial had customers click through a screen with a line in small font saying the user agreed to the terms of service, which were hidden. A toggle was on by default to indicate that the user agreed, and enrolled them in the Sesame Credit program.
在其年度帳單中，螞蟻金服供用戶點擊的螢幕上有一小行字說用戶同意服務條款，但這些條款被隱藏起來了。顯示用戶同意的選項已默認選好，用戶由此被納入芝麻信用體系。
廣告
Ant Financial said in the terms of service that it reserved the right to share the data it collected with third parties — including government agencies assembling a state-sanctioned social credit system — and could not be held responsible for what happened after it shared the information.
螞蟻金服在服務條款中表示，將保留與第三方分享收集得來數據的權利——其中包括一個政府機構批准組建的社會信用系統——並且無須為其分享信息後發生的事情承擔責任。
In its statement, Ant Financial said users should have had to actively change the toggle to indicate they agreed, and added that it would remove all of those who had been inadvertently added to the credit system.
在聲明中，螞蟻金服表示用戶應必須主動更改勾選，以表示同意該協議，並且補充說該公司將移除那些曾因疏忽被加入信用系統的用戶。
User complaints went beyond the narrow concern over how they joined, to broader questions regarding what they were asked to join.
用戶投訴的內容遠遠超過了對他們加入方式的擔憂，而是變成了對他們被要求加入的究竟是什麼的範圍更廣的疑問。
“The tone of the agreement makes me feel so uncomfortable because every sentence literally goes, ‘If this happens, we are not responsible, if that happens, we are not responsible,’” said Zhou Yang, a 25-year old investment professional in Shanghai. “They sound like sneaky unscrupulous merchants who will sell your data and don’t want to be responsible for anything, and you have to agree to all of it.”
「這個條款寫的語氣太讓人不舒服了，寫的都是『如果這個發生了，我們不負責，如果那個發生了，我們不負責』，」現年25歲的周揚說，他是上海的一名專業投資人士。「很像狡詐的無良商家，賣你數據還什麼都不想負責，然後你還得同意。」
Ms. Zhou’s fears about data sales underscore more widespread worries about controlling personal information. China’s official media have reported an increasing number of cases of internet fraud, and personal data is widely available for sale through online marketplaces. A 2017 survey by China’s state-run Beijing Youth Daily found that leaking personal data was the No. 1 concern among consumers.
周揚對出售數據的擔心，突顯了人們對控制個人信息的普遍擔憂。中國的官方媒體已經對越來越多的網路詐騙案件進行了報導，此外，經由網上市場，個人數據四處可見。一項由官媒《北京青年報》在2017年進行的問卷調查發現，個人數據洩漏是消費者最為擔心的。
The issue has filtered to the top of the Chinese leadership. Even as Beijing continues to assemble one of the most sophisticated and wide-scale surveillance systems in the world, politicians have called for better privacy protections. Laws have been beefed up to better protect consumer data. And it has had an impact overseas, too: Ant Financial’s deal to buy MoneyGram, the money-transfer company, collapsed amid concerns in the United States over a Chinese company having access to a large amount of financial data.
這一問題也傳到了中國領導高層那裡。即使是在北京繼續打造世界上最為成熟、範圍最廣的監控系統之時，政治人士也對更好地保護隱私進行了呼籲。為更好地保護消費者的數據，當局加強了法律。而且這個問題在海外也造成了影響：由於美國對中國公司獲取大量金融數據的擔憂，螞蟻金服收購匯款公司速匯金的交易告吹。
廣告
In part, the Sesame Credit moves by Ant Financial might have been trying to get around newer, stricter laws that would prevent them from sharing user data unless they had explicit permission, said Zhai Zhenyi, a lawyer at Yingke, a China-based law firm.
螞蟻金服旗下的芝麻信用做出的舉措，可能部分是為了繞開更新的、更嚴格的法律，除非它們獲得了明確許可，這些法律將防止它們分享用戶數據，盈科的一名律師翟振軼說，盈科是一家總部設在中國的律師事務所。
“Problems like telecom fraud and malicious marketing have severely vexed the general public, so people’s consciousness about their rights is awakening,” he said. “This is a very good thing to see.”
「一直以來，像電信詐騙和惡意推銷這種類似的問題已經很大的困擾到了廣大群眾，所以人們的權利意識也在慢慢覺醒，」他說，「這是一個非常好的事情。」
That consciousness may not extend to government collection of data, even though China’s biggest internet companies often provide user information to Beijing.
這種認知可能不會適用於政府對數據的收集，即使中國最大的網路公司常常會將用戶信息提供給北京。
Ant Financial’s credit system resembles one the government itself plans to soon implement.
螞蟻金服的信用系統與政府自己計劃即將實行的系統類似。
Beijing has said it will introduce in 2020 its own social credit system that is expected to give and take away privileges based on spending habits, online and real-world behavior, and social relationships. Foreign travel, speedy internet, school access, and social benefits could all be granted or denied based on a person’s score. The government system will most likely be at least partly dependent on data collected by companies like Alibaba and Tencent.
北京已經表示過，將在2020年推出自己的社會信用系統，預計該系統將基於消費習慣、線上和現實生活中的行為以及社會關係賦予或拿走公民特權。基於一個人的得分，出國旅遊、快速的網路、上學及社會福利可能都會被給予或被否決。這一政府系統很有可能將至少部分依賴像阿里巴巴和騰訊這樣的公司收集的數據。
It is unclear how many Chinese are truly against such programs.
尚不清楚有多少中國人真的會反對這樣的項目。
廣告
Mr. Li, the Beijing college student, said that many of his friends still shared the shopping breakdowns — which now include a number indicating a user’s Sesame Credit score.
那位北京的大學生李路遙說，他有不少朋友還是會分享那些對購物的分析——裡面如今還包括了一個數字，表明了用戶的芝麻信用分數。
Such is the power and ubiquity of Alibaba and Tencent in China, he added, that customers would find voting with their feet and quitting the services difficult.
這就是阿里巴巴和騰訊在中國的影響力和無孔不入的體現，他補充說，消費者會發現，用腳投票，不用這些服務是很難的。
“Being angry doesn’t do us any good,” he said. “Maybe you can stop using Alipay if you are angry, but there’s no way you could stop using WeChat.”
「生氣沒有用，」他說。「生氣能不用支付寶，還能不用微信嗎？」
