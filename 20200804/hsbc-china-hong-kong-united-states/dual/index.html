中西对抗新时代，汇丰银行面临“站队”困境

In Showdown Between China and the West, HSBC Gets Caught in the Middle

艾莎
2020年8月4日
Sign up for NYT Chinese-language Morning Briefing.
（欢迎点击此处订阅NYT简报，我们将在每个工作日发送最新内容至您的邮箱。）
HONG KONG — Like Hong Kong, HSBC has long sat at the crossroads between East and West, a big global bank based in Britain that has reveled in and profited from its deep relationship with China. And like Hong Kong, it is now caught in the middle of a new era of confrontation between Beijing and major Western governments.
香港——和香港一样，汇丰长期处在东西方的十字路口，这家总部位于英国的大型全球银行一直陶醉于跟中国的深厚关系，并从中获利。和香港一样，它现在也陷入了北京和主要西方政府对抗的新时代。
In China, HSBC has been accused of “setting traps” to ensnare the Chinese tech giant, Huawei. In Britain, it has been admonished for seeming to back Huawei’s ambitions in the country.
在中国，汇丰被指控“构陷”中国科技巨头华为。在英国，汇丰因为似乎支持华为在该国的野心而受到谴责。
Straddling neutral ground is no longer an option. HSBC got called out in China for not publicly backing the new national security law in Hong Kong. When the bank eventually expressed support on its Chinese social media account, members of the British Parliament demanded an explanation and urged HSBC to rescind the statement.
两全中立已不再是一种选择。由于没有公开支持香港的新国安法，汇丰在中国遭受批评。而当这家银行最终在其中文社交媒体账户上表示了支持，英国议会成员则要求汇丰做出解释，并敦促其撤回声明。
广告
Global businesses are increasingly under pressure to pick sides as the United States and its allies target the political and economic agenda of China.
随着美国及其盟友把矛头指向了中国政治和经济议程，全球企业的站队压力越来越大。
American technology companies like Facebook, Twitter, Google and LinkedIn have pledged to defy data requests in Hong Kong under China’s new security law. Manufacturers have disentangled their supply chains to cut out Chinese companies that the American government has banned over human rights violations or national security concerns. Big banks are scouring client lists to ensure compliance after the United States pledged to impose sanctions on individuals considered to be eroding Hong Kong’s autonomy.
Facebook、Twitter、谷歌和领英等美国科技公司已承诺，在中国的新国安法之下，他们将拒绝香港索取数据的请求。制造商对供应链进行清理，排除被美国政府因侵犯人权或国家安全问题封禁的中国企业。在美国承诺对那些被认为削弱香港自治权的个体实施制裁后，大银行正在仔细筛查客户名单，以确保合规。
Many multinationals also worry that complying with American law may mean breaking the new Chinese rules in Hong Kong — or cost them access to the second largest economy in the world. A recent survey by the American Chamber of Commerce found that nearly half of the firms it surveyed are “extremely concerned” about recent developments with the national security law.
许多跨国企业还担心，遵守美国法律可能意味着违反中国在香港的新规矩——或使他们失去进入世界第二大经济体的机会。美国商会最近的一项调查发现，近一半的受访企业对国安法的最新进展表示“极为担忧”。
“There are multiple tailwinds pushing the global business world toward this highly geopolitically sensitive environment where the landscape has shifted fundamentally and you can no longer be agnostic,” said Jude Blanchette, a China scholar at the Center for Strategic and International Studies in Washington. “It is the logical extension of this new paradigm where economic security is now considered national security.”
“多种因素推动全球商业走向这一地缘政治高度敏感的环境，在这种环境下，世界格局已经发生根本转变，你不能再做不可知论者，”华盛顿战略与国际研究中心(Center for Strategic and International Studies)的中国问题学者裘德·布兰切特(Jude Blanchette)说。“经济安全如今被视为国家安全，这是这种新范式的逻辑延伸。”
HSBC and Huawei declined to comment.
汇丰和华为都拒绝置评。
HSBC, which now derives most of its revenues in Hong Kong and China, has long navigated politics and profits.
如今大部分收入都来自香港和中国的汇丰，长期以来对政治与利益进行权衡。
广告
During Japan’s occupation of Hong Kong during World War II, the head of HSBC was forced to sign bank notes for the Japanese government. He also secretly provided aid to employees interned at the time.
在第二次世界大战日本占领香港期间，汇丰总裁就被迫在日本政府发行的银行钞票上签字。他还秘密为当时被拘捕的员工提供帮助。
As Britain and China negotiated the handover of Hong Kong in the early 1980s, HSBC was still viewed with mistrust by the Chinese Communist Party for the role it played in helping to finance the trade of opium during the previous century. In a move to help win back Beijing’s favor, it made plans for a splashy new headquarters in Hong Kong, while other foreign companies considered leaving the city.
当英国和中国在80年代初就香港移交进行谈判时，因其上个世纪在资助鸦片贸易中扮演的角色，中共仍用不信任的眼光看待汇丰。为了赢回北京的青睐，汇丰计划在香港打造引人注目的新总部，当时其他外国企业都在考虑离开这座城市。
HSBC also began to lend to the local businessmen who would go on to build the conglomerates that turned the city into a global financial center. As Hong Kong profited from its role as a gateway between China and the west, HSBC’s own business boomed.
汇丰也开始贷款给本地商人，让他们继续建设企业集团，把这座城市变为了全球金融中心。作为中国与西方之间的门户，香港从中获利，而汇丰自身的业务也蓬勃发展。
But HSBC’s unique standing, once an advantage, has become a pitfall.
但汇丰曾经作为一种优势的独特地位，现在却变成了一个陷阱。
During the anti-government demonstrations last year, HSBC was targeted for its Beijing connections. Protesters smeared red paint on the bronze lions outside the bank’s headquarters and set one on fire — the other lion still bears shrapnel damage from the Japanese invasion.
在去年的反政府示威活动期间，汇丰因与北京的关系而成为攻击目标。抗议者在这家银行总部外的铜狮上喷涂红漆并点火——另一只狮子上至今还有日本侵略时被弹片损坏的痕迹。
One of HSBC’s clients, Huawei, has been the major source of political tension for the bank.
汇丰的客户华为，一直是这家银行政治紧张的主要根源。
The Trump administration put Huawei and other Chinese technology companies on a so-called entity list over national security concerns, which prevents them from using American technology and software. In response, China created its own list and at one point last year, Chinese state media suggested that HSBC be put on it.
特朗普政府出于对国家安全的担忧，将华为和其他中国科技公司列入所谓的实体名单，阻止它们使用美国的技术和软件。作为回应，中国创建了自己的名单，去年中国官媒一度建议把汇丰加进去。
广告
As Washington lobbied Western governments to block Huawei from building their 5G networks, the British newspaper, The Telegraph, citing unnamed sources, reported that HSBC’s chairman, Mark Tucker, met with advisers to Prime Minister Boris Johnson on behalf of the Chinese company and expressed worry about the repercussions in China if Britain blocked Huawei. The British news media and politicians lashed out at HSBC after the report.
就在华盛顿游说西方政府阻止华为建设他们的5G网络时，英国报纸《每日电讯报》(The Telegraph)引用匿名消息称，汇丰主席杜嘉祺(Mark Tucker)代表这家中国公司与英国首相鲍里斯·约翰逊(Boris Johnson)的顾问会面，并对英国封锁华为在中国的影响表示担忧。在该报道之后，英国媒体和政客都对汇丰进行了猛烈抨击。
“The binary choice being forced on companies like HSBC is not coming from the U.K. We are not asking private businesses to endorse policy or face punishment. We are not threatening to withdraw economic cooperation if a U.K. company fails in a bid,” Tom Tugendhat, a member of the Conservative Party and chairman of Britain’s Foreign Affairs Committee, wrote on Twitter.
“像汇丰这样的公司面临的二选一压力并非来自英国。我们不要求私企支持政策，否则面临惩罚。如果英国公司竞标失败，我们不会威胁取消经济合作，”英国保守党(Conservative Party)成员、外交委员会(Foreign Affairs Committee)主席汤姆·图根达特(Tom Tugendhat)在Twitter上写道。
HSBC’s back channel efforts didn’t sit well with China’s state-run media either. “The HSBC chairman’s warning to Downing Street is far-fetched and absurd, and seems more like a political statement than a business comment,” The Global Times wrote in an editorial on its English website in June.
汇丰在幕后的努力也不受中国官媒的欢迎。“汇丰主席对唐宁街的警告既牵强又荒谬，更像一种政治声明，而非商业评论，”《环球时报》6月在其英文网站的一篇社论中写道。
Britain, in July, banned Huawei from selling its 5G equipment in the country.
7月，英国禁止华为在本国销售5G设备。
In recent days, Beijing has hurled a series of accusations against HSBC through its state controlled news media for cooperating in the case against the Huawei chief financial officer, Meng Wanzhou, who is in detention in Canada and fighting extradition to the United States.
最近几天，北京通过官方媒体对汇丰提出了一系列指控，称其在针对华为首席财务官孟晚舟的案件中提供了合作，孟晚舟目前被扣留在加拿大，正力图避免被引渡到美国。
Huawei is accused of flouting Iranian sanctions and misleading HSBC about its dealings with an Iranian company. Ms. Meng’s legal team has argued that Huawei did not hide anything from HSBC about its dealings in Iran.
华为被指蔑视美国对伊朗的制裁，就其与一家伊朗公司的交易误导汇丰。孟晚舟的法律团队辩称，华为没有对汇丰隐瞒任何在伊朗的交易。
“Wallowing in degradation and with its reputation at rock bottom, HSBC may struggle to continue to enjoy treatment in China,” one state-controlled newspaper warned. Piling on, other state controlled newspapers accused HSBC of malice and dishonesty.
“自甘堕落、信誉扫地的汇丰，恐怕再难在中国享受吃饭砸锅的待遇，”一家官方报纸警告。其他官方报纸也纷纷跟进，指责汇丰的恶意和欺诈。
广告
The People’s Daily, China’s most prominent newspaper and a mouthpiece for the Communist Party, accused HSBC of “setting traps” and argued that the evidence filed to the court revealed that the case was “entirely a political case” with “fabricated criminal evidence.”
中国最著名的报纸和共产党的喉舌《人民日报》指责汇丰“构陷”，并辩称提交法院的证据表明该案完全是“捏造罪证”的“政治案件”。
On its Chinese social media account, the bank offered a simple defense of its cooperation with prosecutors. “HSBC has no malice against Huawei, nor has it ‘framed’ Huawei,” it said in its statement.
在其中文社交媒体账户上，该银行为其与检方的合作进行了简单辩护。“汇丰对华为没有任何恶意，也没有‘构陷’华为，”它在声明中表示。
