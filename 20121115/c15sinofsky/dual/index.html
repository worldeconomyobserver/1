多家媒体评论辛诺夫斯基离职微软

Why Sinofsky Left: A Web Roundup

《世界经济观察报》
2012年11月15日
The announcement of Steven Sinofsky's ouster from Microsoft came late on Monday, so the story has only begun to be reported. Here is what various news organizations and bloggers are saying:
史蒂文·辛诺夫斯基(Steven Sinofsky)离开微软(Microsoft)的消息于周一晚间公布，这个故事才刚刚开始。以下是多家新闻机构和博客作者对此事发表的评论。 
Ashlee Vance at Bloomberg Businessweek wrote:
彭博商业周刊(Bloomberg Businessweek)的阿什莉·万斯(Ashlee Vance)写道：
"The big knock on Sinofsky was his often-prickly nature. He wasn't seen as a team player within Microsoft and was instead known for protecting his fiefdom. That approach doesn't go over well at today's Microsoft, which needs to prove that Windows is just one piece of a larger collective that includes phone software, online services, and entertainment products delivered via the Xbox. Sinofsky also proved reticent to speak with the press and was barely heard from as Windows 8 hit the market late last month."
"对辛诺夫斯基最大的批评是他经常的刺头性格。在微软，他不被视为一个易于合作的人，相反，他被认为是一个保护自己地盘的人。但是这种做法在如今的微软不受欢迎，因为今天的微软需要证明Windows系统只是一个更大的产品集体中的一部分，该集体包括了手机软件、在线服务和通过Xbox平台提供的娱乐产品。辛诺夫斯基还证明他不愿意与媒体交流，自从Windows 8在上月底进入市场后几乎听不到他的声音。"
Mary Jo Foley, one of the wisest Microsoft watchers, wrote at ZDNet:
最理智的微软观察者之一、玛丽·乔·福利(Mary Jo Foley)在ZDNet写道：
"Windows 8 launched commercially just about three weeks ago; it's too soon to judge if the latest Windows release and the Microsoft Surface tablet will be deemed successes or failures. And still months before anyone will be sacrificed if internal projections are unmet.
"Windows 8三周前刚刚进行了商业发布；现在就对这个最新的Windows系统和微软Surface平板电脑会成功还是失败进行评判还为时过早。如果说是因为没有满足内部预测而惩罚某人的话，那就更谈不上了。
"I give more credence to the politics theory."
"我更相信政治斗争的理论。"
She referred readers to a recent article on Mr. Sinofsky by Jay Greene of CNet. Then she noted:
她向读者提到CNet上最近的一篇关于辛诺夫斯基的文章，作者是杰伊·格林(Jay Greene)。接下来，她评论道：
"Remember that word: collaboration. It can mean anything from being willing to use other teams' code, to not standing in the way when another division launches its product on a competitive platform.
"记住这个词：合作。它含义的范围很大，可以是愿意使用其它团队的代码，也可能是在别的部门发布用于竞争平台的产品时不起妨碍作用。
"Sinofsky is known inside and outside the company as a guy who got things done and done his way. Rumors regularly reappeared about Sinofsky angling to take over more business units. And until recently, it seemed like Microsoft's own senior leadership team, as well as Ballmer himself, had capitulated, allowing Sinofsky to make whatever management decisions he deemed fit. Those who disagreed left or were shown the door (and probably won't be back, though never say never).
"公司内外的人都知道辛诺夫斯基是一个能把事情做好的人，而且是按自己的方法把事情做好。不断有谣言称辛诺夫斯基想要接管更多的部门。直到最近，包括鲍尔默(Ballmer)本人在内的微软高层领导人，似乎没有抵抗，允许辛诺夫斯基做出任何他认为合适的管理决定。而反对者要么自行离开，要么被扫地出门（而且有可能永远不会再回来，虽然永不说永不）。
"But more recently, something seemingly changed, including the rhetoric."
"然而新近有些情况看起来有所改变，包括一些说辞。"
Kara Swisher, at AllThingsD, concurred, writing:
AllThingsD的卡拉·斯威舍(Kara Swisher)表示赞同，她写道：
"In the case of the seemingly sudden departure of Windows head Steve Sinofsky yesterday, several high-level sources at the company said that it came down to former C.E.O. and co-founder Bill Gates's backing of current C.E.O. Steve Ballmer in the controversial decision to part ways with the powerful exec.
"周一微软大佬史蒂文·辛诺夫斯基离职一事，表面上看起来十分突然，而公司的几名高级消息人士透露说，此事归根结底还是前总裁兼联合创始人比尔·盖茨(Bill Gates)支持现任总裁史蒂夫·鲍尔默(Steve Ballmer)关于和这位强有力的执行官分道扬镳的决定。
"The goal? To better allow various units work together more closely going forward."
"其目的？让各个部门在未来能够更好更紧密地一起工作。"
An industry blog, Tim Anderson's ITWriting, said:
一家行业博客，Tim Anderson’s ITWriting说：
"One line of thought is that Windows 8 and Surface RT are failing because users do not like the dramatic changes, with the new tiled personality and disappeared Start menu, and therefore its architect is departing.
"一种看法是Windows 8和Surface RT正走向失败，原因是用户不喜欢如此巨大的变化，包括新的磁贴特性和“开始”菜单的消失 ；因此，其设计者离职理所应当。
"I do not believe this for several reasons. One is that the promoted Julie Larson-Green is a key creator and proponent of the new design (call it Metro if you like). She worked with Sinofsky on the Office Ribbon way back, a project that has some parallels with Windows 8: take a critically important product and revamp its user interface in ways that customers are not requesting or expecting."
"我有几条理由不相信这种看法。一条是，新设计（如果你愿意可以称之为Metro）的关键开发者和支持者朱莉·拉森-格林(Julie Larson-Green)受到了提拔。她很早以前曾与辛诺夫斯基一起在Office Ribbon部门共事，那是一个与Windows 8有不少相似之处的项目：翻新一个至关重要产品的用户界面，使其超乎消费者的要求或期望。"
Brier Dudley at the Seattle Times went to town listing eight reasons for Mr. Sinofsky's ouster. Here are the first three:
《西雅图时报》(Seattle Times)的布赖尔·杜德利(Brier Dudley)兴致勃勃地开列了辛诺夫斯基去职的八条理由。以下是前三条：
"1. Sinofsky was passed over for the opportunity to succeed Ballmer as chief executive. Perhaps Kevin Turner or someone else internally was given the nod, despite Sinofsky's huge achievements building the company's flagship Office and Windows products.
"1. 辛诺夫斯基失去了接班鲍尔默成为首席执行官的机会。虽然辛诺夫斯基为建造公司旗舰Office软件和Windows系统作出了巨大贡献，但也许凯文·特纳(Kevin Turner)或其他人得到了内部的默认。
"2. Ballmer was going to divvy up Sinofsky's kingdom in a way that didn't go over well. Perhaps it was time to split Windows Live services from big Windows and give Chris Jones, VP of Live services, more responsibility?
"2. 鲍尔默正在瓜分辛诺夫斯基的地盘，这在某种程度上引发冲突。也许是把Windows Live的业务从大Windows系统中分离出来，赋予Live业务的副总裁克里斯·琼斯(Chris Jones)更多职责的时候了？
"3. Could Microsoft be hiring Scott Forstall, the ousted head of mobile software development at Apple? Forstall is a local guy who interned at Microsoft and has family in this area. If he was hired by Microsoft, he'd take a top spot and carve out some of Sinofsky's turf."
"3. 微软也许会雇用被苹果(Apple)解职的移动软件开发高管斯科特·福斯特尔(Scott Forstall)？作为一个本地人，福斯特尔曾在微软实习，并有亲戚住在这里。如果他被微软聘用，他会获得高位，并得到辛诺夫斯基的一些领地。"
And then there was Twitter silliness. Charles Cooper of CNet captured a lot of it - ties to the Petraeus affair and Scott Forstall's recent departure from Apple.
再有就是Twitter上的一些愚蠢可笑的言论。CNet的查尔斯·库珀(Charles Cooper)一句话概括了其大部分：与彼得雷乌斯风流韵事和斯科特·福斯特尔最近从苹果离职有关。
