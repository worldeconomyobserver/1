尿素短缺衝擊全球經濟，或將推高世界各地食品價格

This Chemical Is in Short Supply, and the Whole World Feels It

RAYMOND ZHONG
2021年12月9日
This is a story about one of those unsung forces that quietly keep the world running. It is a story about the clockwork interconnectedness of modern civilization, about how disturbances in one part of the planet can kick up storms in another.
這個故事是關於那些默默維持著世界運轉的無名力量。它講述了現代文明如何精密相聯，也講述了地球上一個地方出現的騷動如何在另一個地方掀起風暴。
This is a story, naturally, about urea.
自然，這個故事說的是尿素。
Prices for the humble chemical — yes, the stuff in urine — are soaring to levels not seen in over a decade. In this time of everything shortages and inflation worries, that alone might not sound too surprising. But urea links up several disparate-looking strands of global economic disruption, showing how easily extreme weather and shipping turmoil can cause supply shortfalls to radiate.
這種不起眼的化學物質——是的，這種尿液裡的東西——的價格正在飆升至十多年來未曾見過的水平。在這個短缺普遍存在、通膨令人擔憂的時代，光是這一點聽起來可能還不會讓人太驚訝。但是，尿素將全球幾股看起來不相干的經濟動盪連接了起來，這表明極端天氣和航運危機是多麼容易導致供應短缺擴散。
People and industries of all kinds are feeling the shocks. In India, a lack of urea has made farmers fear for their livelihoods. In South Korea, it meant truck drivers couldn’t start their engines.
各行各業的人們都感受到了衝擊。在印度，尿素的缺乏讓農民擔心自己的生計。在韓國，這意味著卡車司機無法啟動引擎。
廣告
Urea is an important type of agricultural fertilizer, so rising prices could ultimately mean higher costs at dinner tables around the world. The United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization’s index of food prices is already at its highest level since 2011. The coronavirus pandemic has caused huge numbers of people to face hunger, and increased food prices could cause even more to have trouble meeting basic dietary needs. Prices of two other widely used plant foods are skyrocketing as well.
尿素是一種重要的農業肥料，因此，價格上漲最終可能意味著世界各地餐桌上的成本增加。聯合國糧農組織的食品價格指數已經達到了2011年以來的最高水平。新冠疫情已經導致大量人口面臨飢餓，食品價格的上漲可能導致更多人難以滿足基本的飲食需求。另外兩種廣泛使用的化肥價格也在飛速上漲。
One big reason for surging fertilizer prices is surging prices of coal and natural gas. The urea in your urine is produced in the liver. The industrial kind is made through a century-old process that turns natural gas or gas derived from coal into ammonia, which is then used to synthesize urea.
化肥價格飆升的一個重要原因是煤炭和天然氣價格高企。人類尿液中的尿素是在肝臟中產生的。而工業尿素則是通過一種有著百年歷史的工藝製成，它將天然氣或從煤炭中提取的氣體變成氨，然後再用來合成尿素。
But a freakish confluence of other factors is pushing up prices as well.
還有其他因素的異常結合，也在推高價格。
China and Russia, two of the biggest producers, have restricted exports to ensure supplies for their own farmers. In China’s case, an energy crunch led some areas to ration electricity, which forced fertilizer factories to slash production.
中國和俄羅斯是世界上最大的兩個尿素生產國，為了確保本國農民的供應，它們對出口採取了限制措施。在中國，能源短缺導致一些地區停電限電，這迫使化肥廠削減產量。
Hurricane Ida drove several large chemical plants to suspend operations when it tore through the U.S. Gulf Coast in August. Western sanctions on Belarus have hit that nation’s production of potash, the key ingredient in another fertilizer. Port delays and high freight fees — plant food is bulky stuff — have added to costs.
颶風艾達在8月橫掃美國墨西哥灣沿岸時，幾家大型化工廠不得不停工。西方國家對白俄羅斯的制裁打擊了該國的鉀肥生產，而鉀肥是另一種化肥的關鍵成分。港口延誤和高昂的運費（化肥屬於大宗商品）都導致了成本的增加。
All of this is rippling around the world in unexpected and sometimes painful ways.
所有這一切正以意想不到、有時甚至是痛苦的方式，波及世界各地。
廣告
In India, fears of fertilizer shortages have led crowds of desperate farmers to gather outside government distribution centers and clash with the police.
在印度，出於對化肥短缺的擔憂，大批絕望的農民聚集在政府分配中心外，與警方發生衝突。
Truckers in South Korea have worried about not being able to work. The reason? Urea goes into an industrial elixir that reduces trucks’ greenhouse gas emissions and that South Korea doesn’t allow diesel engines to start without.
在韓國，卡車司機擔心他們將無法工作。原因？尿素是一種行業靈藥，可以減少卡車的溫室氣體排放，韓國不允許柴油發動機在沒有尿素的情況下啟動。
Britons have fretted about running out of the tiny bubbles in their carbonated drinks. Why? A big fertilizer maker, CF Industries, halted operations at two plants in England in September, citing high natural gas prices. And food-grade carbon dioxide is a byproduct of the ammonia production process. One of the two plants has since restarted production.
英國人擔心，碳酸飲料裡以後就沒有小氣泡了。為什麼？9月的時候，大型化肥製造商CF Industries以天然氣價格上漲為由，將英國兩家工廠停工。食品級二氧化碳是合成氨生產過程中的副產品。其中一家工廠目前已經恢復生產。
As for the big question of whether food prices are about to shoot up globally, John Baffes, a World Bank economist who studies commodity markets, said he believed farmers had already largely locked in fertilizer prices for the current crop season.
至於全球食品價格是否即將飆升的大問題，世界銀行研究商品市場的經濟學家約翰·巴菲斯表示，他相信農戶已經基本鎖定了當前作物季節的化肥價格。
But a different picture could emerge early next year, when the U.S. Department of Agriculture publishes the first results of its yearly survey of American farmers’ planting intentions. These will give clues about how growers worldwide are responding to the latest market conditions.
但明年年初，當美國農業部發布美國農民種植意願年度調查的初步結果時，情況可能就不一樣了。這些結果將為全球種植戶該如何應對最新市場形勢提供線索。
“Normally, those are boring reports,” Mr. Baffes said. “Nobody knows about them. Nobody reads them.”
「那些報告通常都很無聊，」巴菲斯說。「沒有人知道它們。沒有人去讀。」
廣告
Not this time, he said.
但這次不同了，他說。
“If we see coal prices and natural gas prices staying at the levels we are seeing currently, then we are certainly going to see higher food prices,” he said. “There’s no question about it.”
「如果我們看到煤炭和天然氣價格還保持在目前的水平，那食品價格肯定會更高，」他說。「這是毫無疑問的。」
China is a linchpin of the global fertilizer trade. The country accounts for about a tenth of the world’s urea-based fertilizer exports and a third of exports of diammonium phosphate, another type of crop nutrient, according to the World Bank.
中國是全球化肥貿易的關鍵環節。據世界銀行統計，中國的尿素類肥料出口約佔全球的十分之一，另一種農作物肥料磷酸二銨的出口則佔到了約三分之一。
As prices of fuel and fertilizer began rising this year, China’s cabinet in June authorized billions of dollars in subsidies and other support for farmers. The next month, the country’s major fertilizer producers met with the state planning agency and agreed to halt exports.
隨著燃料和化肥價格今年開始上漲，中國國務院在6月批准了數十億美元的補貼和其他對農戶的支持政策。7月，國家發改委約談了國內部分重點化肥企業，達成了暫停出口的決定。
In the fall, soaring electricity demand led the southwestern province of Yunnan, a key phosphate producer, to order drastic production cuts by energy-hungry industries, including fertilizer. And in October, China’s customs authority imposed extra inspection requirements on exports of 29 fertilizers and related products.
到秋季，電力需求飆升，導致中國主要的磷酸鹽產地雲南省下令大幅削減高耗能行業的產能，包括化肥生產。10月，中國海關對29種化肥及相關產品的出口實施了額外的檢驗規定。
China’s leaders have been paying much more attention to food security since the pandemic began, said Darin Friedrichs of Sitonia Consulting, an advisory firm in Shanghai that focuses on Chinese agricultural markets.
Sitonia Consulting是上海專門研究中國農業市場的諮詢公司，該公司的達林·弗里德裡希表示，自疫情開始以來，中國領導人對糧食安全的重視程度大為增加。
“They were probably ahead of the curve in realizing how much this was going to disrupt global supply chains,” Mr. Friedrichs said. “And in a situation like that, it’s obviously better to err on the side of trying to have more food rather than less.”
「他們可能更先認識到這將對全球供應鏈造成多大破壞，」弗里德裡希說。「在這樣的形勢下，顯然是寧可糧多也不要糧少。」
South Korea relies heavily on Chinese urea for the industrial fluid that breaks down harmful gases in diesel exhaust. Under the country’s environmental regulations, electronic control systems in diesel trucks prevent the engine from running when the urea tank is empty.
韓國非常依賴中國尿素生產的一種工業液體，這種液體可以分解柴油尾氣中的有害氣體。根據韓國的環境法規，當尿素箱為空，柴油卡車的電子控制系統會阻止發動機運轉。
廣告
As prices of urea solution soared as much as tenfold last month, some South Korean truck drivers said they had forfeited jobs that would consume more urea, such as ones involving long distances or big hills. On a construction site, if just one heavy-duty vehicle runs out of urea, the entire project might be paralyzed.
由於尿素溶液的價格在上個月暴漲了十倍之多，一些韓國卡車司機表示，他們放棄了跑長途或高山地區等需要消耗更多尿素的工作。在建築工地，只要有一輛重型汽車的尿素用完，整個項目都可能癱瘓。
“If my truck stops, my family’s livelihood, my children’s tuition, everything stops,” said Kim Jung-suk, 47, who drives a dump truck in Seoul.
「如果我的卡車開不了，那我全家的生計，我孩子的學費，什麼都沒了，」47歲的金程石（音）說道，他在首爾開自卸車為生。
Kim Woo-hyun, 50 — another driver, and no relation — said he spent evenings and weekends in pursuit of urea.
另一位50歲的司機金宇賢（音）說，他晚上的時間和週末都在尋找尿素。
“I would call up a bunch of gas stations until one said it had some left over, and make an appointment with them to pick it up,” Mr. Kim said. “Then I’d show up, and it would be gone.”
「我會給許多加油站打電話，直到某一家說還有剩餘，然後和他們約好去取，」金宇賢說。「等我到那裡，剩下的也沒了。」
South Korea airlifted thousands of gallons of urea from Australia before striking a deal with China to allow a few months’ supply to be imported.
在與中國達成數月進口協議之前，韓國從澳洲空運來數千加侖尿素救急。
More than half of China’s urea exports this year have gone to India. The Indian government subsidizes fertilizer to keep prices low, but distributing it to growers requires coordination between national and state authorities who are often at odds for partisan and other reasons.
中國今年的出口尿素超過一半都流向了印度。印度政府對化肥的補貼使價格保持在較低水平，但將化肥分配給種植戶需要國家和地方當局之間的協調，而雙方經常因為黨派鬥爭等原因產生矛盾。
廣告
When the fertilizer squeeze hit this fall, Danpal Yadav, 44, a rice grower in the central state of Madhya Pradesh, was already reeling under debt because of low crop yields last season. After coming home empty-handed from visits to government fertilizer distribution centers, he grew anguished and talked about suicide, his family said.
今秋化肥短缺的時候，印度中部中央邦44歲的稻農丹帕爾·亞達夫已經因為上一季的低收成而負債累累。他的家人說，他去了政府的化肥分配中心卻空手而歸，他愈發痛苦，還有了自殺的念頭。
Time was running out for Mr. Yadav to nourish his fields. On Oct. 28, after sleeping outside a fertilizer center for three days and getting nothing, he returned home and bolted the door.
留給亞達夫滋養他土地的時間不多了。10月28日，他在化肥中心外等了三天，還是什麼也沒得到，他返回家中，鎖上了門。
His brother Vivek later found him unconscious. He had consumed poison. Doctors declared him dead at a hospital.
後來，他的兄弟維維克發現了昏迷不醒的亞達夫。他服了毒。醫生在醫院宣布了他的死亡。
“He was desperately trying to find fertilizer,” Vivek Yadav said. “This is the story of every farmer during this season.”
「他拚命地尋找化肥，」維維克·亞達夫說。「這是這個時候每個農民都在經歷的事。」
