中國將中歐貿易爭端訴諸WTO

China Files W.T.O. Case Against Europe

KEITH BRADSHER
2012年11月6日
HONG KONG — The Chinese government announced on Monday that it had filed a case with the World Trade Organization accusing some European Union member countries of violating free trade rules with policies that favored the purchase of solar energy equipment produced in Europe.
香港——中國政府本周一宣布，它已經向世界貿易組織（World Trade Organization，簡稱世貿組織）提起訴訟，指控一些歐盟成員國對歐洲生產的光伏設備實施採購補貼政策，違反了自由貿易準則。
The W.T.O. case is the latest in a series of retaliatory moves by China in response to efforts by the European Union and the United States to limit imports of Chinese solar panels. European and United States officials have accused China of dumping solar panels in foreign markets below the cost of manufacturing them and of subsidizing their manufacture — accusations that China has denied.
為了回應歐盟和美國限制從中國進口太陽能電池組件的措施，中國已經採取了一系列報復性舉動，這次的世貿組織訴訟案是最新的一項舉措。歐洲和美國官員指責中國以低於製造成本的價格在海外市場傾銷太陽能電池組件，並為製造商提供補貼，中國已對這些指控表示否認。
In a statement posted late Monday on its Web site, the Chinese commerce ministry did not identify the countries or the specific equipment covered by its trade complaint. But the ministry contended that the countries’ policies violated W.T.O. rules requiring that goods from all W.T.O. member countries be treated equally, without discrimination in favor of locally produced goods.
中國商務部周一晚間在官方網站上發佈的聲明，並沒有指明這次貿易訴訟涉及的具體國家和設備。但商務部宣稱，這些國家的補貼政策違反了世貿組織協定關於國民待遇和最惠國待遇的規定。
The ministry also contended that the policies violated a free trade rule that countries not impose policies that have the effect of subsidizing domestic production as a substitution for imports.
中國商務部還認為，這些補貼政策違反了自由貿易準則，構成了世貿組織協定禁止的進口替代補貼。
The United States and the European Union acted after about a dozen solar panel manufacturers went bankrupt or cut back production on each side of the Atlantic. But Chinese manufacturers are also struggling after state-owned banks financed a burst of construction over the last four years that left the country with more than two-thirds of the world’s solar manufacturing capacity. The industry now faces overcapacity.
美國和歐盟之所以採取行動，是因為大西洋兩岸的大約十二家太陽能電池組件製造商已經破產或減少了產量。但是，中國的製造商也在苦苦掙扎。在過去四年間，中國國有銀行的資助讓中國的生產能力飛速提高，中國的太陽能設備生產能力佔到了世界的三分之二以上。現在，這個行業正面臨著產能過剩的問題。
The European Union announced on Sept. 6 that it had opened the largest antidumping investigation ever, into imports of Chinese solar panels worth $26.5 billion last year. China’s commerce ministry had tried for months to persuade European officials not to proceed.
歐盟於9月6日宣布，已經展開了歷史上規模最大的反傾銷調查，調查對象是去年從中國進口的總價值265億美元（約合1655億人民幣）的太陽能電池組件。此前，中國商務部曾經花費了幾個月的時間來試圖說服歐盟官員不要追究。
In an initial retaliatory move, the Commerce Ministry said on Thursday that it had begun its own anti-dumping investigation of imports from Europe of polysilicon, the main raw material for making solar panels. China previously started an antidumping investigation of polysilicon imports from the United States after the Commerce Department began an investigation a year ago that led to antidumping and antisubsidy tariffs on solar panels from China.
商務部在上周四宣稱已經對原產於歐洲的進口多晶硅展開反傾銷調查，啟動了報復行動。多晶硅是用於製造太陽能電池組件的主要原材料。在此之前，中國已經開始對原產於美國的進口多晶硅進行反傾銷調查，原因是美國商務部(Commerce Department)在一年前啟動了一項調查，最終導致美國對中國生產的太陽能組件徵收反傾銷和反補貼關稅。
The Commerce Ministry officially asked the European Union on Monday for consultations under the auspices of the W.T.O. in Geneva. This is the first step in W.T.O. dispute resolution procedures that can lead, in about a year and a half, to a decision by a panel of trade judges on whether the accused countries must rewrite the offending regulations.
周一，商務部在日內瓦正式要求歐盟在世貿組織領導下進行磋商。這是世貿組織爭端解決程序的第一步，之後，貿易法官小組可能會在大約一年半的時間之內作出裁決，判定遭受指控的國家是否必須改寫令人不快的規則。
“We have received the consultation request and are studying it,” said John Clancy, the European Union’s spokesman on trade issues.
歐盟貿易問題發言人約翰·克蘭西(John Clancy) 表示，“我們已經收到磋商請求，目前正在進行研究。”
Commerce Ministry officials have sought to portray the trade frictions over solar power in broader environmental terms, emphasizing that solar panels do not release gases that contribute to climate change, unlike the combustion of fossil fuels like oil, coal and natural gas.
商務部官員試圖將有關太陽能的貿易摩擦置於環境保護的大背景之下，他們強調說，與燃燒石油、煤炭以及天然氣等化石燃料不同，使用太陽能電池組件不會產生導致氣候變化的氣體。
“Developing solar photovoltaic renewable energy is conducive to resolving the serious challenges of energy security and climate change facing humanity, in line with the common interests of all countries,” the ministry’s chief spokesman, Shen Danyang, said in the statement on Monday. “States should focus on the long term, to strengthen industrial cooperation and the liberalization of international trade, rather than taking trade protectionist measures because of short-term interests.”
“大力發展太陽能光伏等可再生能源，有利於解決全人類面臨的能源安全和氣候變化等嚴峻挑戰，符合各國共同利益，”商務部首席發言人沈丹陽周一在聲明中表示，“各國應當著眼長遠，加強產業合作，開放國際貿易，而不應出於短期利益需要採取貿易保護主義措施。”
