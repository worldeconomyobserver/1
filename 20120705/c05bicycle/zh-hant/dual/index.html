電動單車前景看好

Batteries Add Power and Market Appeal to Bicycles

KATE GALBRAITH
2012年7月5日
AUSTIN, TEXAS — Kelly Hunka tried out an electric bicycle for the first time last week. Hours later, she was back for another ride.
得克薩斯奧斯汀—凱莉·洪卡（Kelly Hunka）上周第一次試騎了電動單車。幾小時之後，她又回來打算再騎一回。
“These are so cool,” Ms. Hunka, who works for an Austin technology company, said as she paced around an electric-bike store called Rocket Electrics that opened in the city in December. Asked about her impression of riding an electric bike, she responded with a single word: “Easy.”
在奧斯汀一家科技公司工作的洪卡繞着十二月才在城裡開張的一家名為火箭電氣 (Rocket Electrics) 的電動單車店踱步，她說：“這可太酷了！”當被問及騎電動車的感受，她就蹦出一個詞：“容易。
The concept of electric bicycles, whose riders can cruise up hills on battery power when they are tired of pedaling, is slowly catching on in cities around the world. The bicycles resemble their conventional counterparts but have a heavier frame and carry a battery pack, which is usually either behind the rider or integrated in the frame. The motor transmits energy to the rear wheel, and the battery can be detached for recharging.
騎着電動單車，人如果上坡踩踏板踩累了，可以依賴電池繼續前行。電動單車的概念逐漸在世界各地的城市流行起來。這種單車和傳統的單車很相似，但整體框架更沉，並帶有一個電池組。電池組通常不是安裝在騎車人的後面，就是裝在車架內部。發動機把能量轉換至後輪，而電池可以拆卸下來充電。
China is far and away the world leader in the technology, with over 100 million e-bikes on the road already. Demand is growing in Europe, where Germany and the Netherlands are the largest markets and Paris has a program to provide subsidies of up to €400, or nearly $500, to electric bike purchasers. In the United States, electric bike shops have recently sprouted in car-dependent cities like Austin and Houston, and an electric bike-sharing project is getting under way in hilly San Francisco.
毫無疑問，中國在這項科技上處於世界領先地位。在這個國家，每天上路的電動單車已經超過1億輛。歐洲的需求量在增長，德國和荷蘭是最大的市場，而巴黎則計劃為電動單車買家提供最高400歐元，約合500美金的補貼。在美國，電動單車商店在依賴汽車的城市，如奧斯汀和休斯敦，遍地開花。而山城舊金山則正在實施一項電動單車租賃計劃。
“It’s just a massive personal mobility shift,” said Christopher Cherry, an assistant professor of civil and environmental engineering at the University of Tennessee, who has studied e-bikes in China.
田納西大學城市和環境工程學教授克里斯托弗·謝里（Christopher Cherry）曾在中國研究電動單車。他說：“這是個人出行方式的巨大改變。”
The bikes, Mr. Cherry added, represent the largest-ever adoption of alternative-fuel vehicles — far more prevalent than electric cars or Segways, though they get less attention. Revenue from e-bike sales this year worldwide is expected to reach $6.9 billion, a figure that is projected to rise more than 70 percent by 2018, according to a recent report by Pike Research, a Colorado-based research and consulting firm.
謝里補充說，電動單車代表了有史以來對替代燃料交通工具的最大化應用—雖然它所受的關注很少，但它比電動汽車或電動踏板車普遍的多。根據位於科羅拉州的調研諮詢公司派克研究公司（PikeResearch）最近的報告，全球今年的電動單車銷售收入有望達到69億美元。預計這個數據到2018年還要上漲70%。
China is at the center of the growth. A total of 28 million e-bikes — the majority of the global market — will be sold there this year, Pike Research projects. They are an inexpensive and easy way to get around, and have become a logical substitute for motorcycles and motor scooters, which some Chinese cities ban because of air-pollution concerns, according to Geoff Rose, a professor at the Institute of Transport Studies at Monash University in Melbourne.
中國正處在這個產業增長的中心。派克研究公司預測，今年中國一共將賣掉2800萬輛電動單車，成為全球最大的市場。墨爾本莫納什大學交通研究學院的教授傑夫·羅斯（Geoff Rose）說，電動單車價格低廉，易於出行，成為摩托車的合理替代品。中國的一些城市出於環境污染考慮禁止摩托車上路。
But people in other Asian countries have been slow to adopt e-bikes, and environmentalists in China worry about the carbon footprint associated with the coal-dominated electric grid.
但亞洲其他一些國家對採納電動單車反應較慢，中國的環保主義者也擔憂以火力發電為主的電力供應會加重二氧化碳排放量。
“Although electric bikes do avoid quite a lot of gasoline usage, such bikes in China have pros and cons,” Li Ang, a climate and energy campaigner for Greenpeace in Beijing, wrote in an e-mail. “They can hardly be labeled as a typical ‘green’ means of transportation.”
李昂是綠色和平組織 (Greenpeace) 東亞地區氣候與能源項目主任。她在一封電子郵件里寫道：“雖然電動單車確實避免了大量使用汽油，但這種車在中國還是有利也有弊。很難說電動單車是一種典型的‘綠色’交通工具。” 
Safety is another challenge in China, with e-bikes zipping down sidewalks, sometimes with multiple riders. Lead-acid batteries are not adequately recycled, creating an environmental problem, and a proliferation of small e-bike makers in China makes crafting effective safety rules a challenge.
在中國，安全是另一個難題。這裡，電動單車時常在人行道呼嘯而過，有時還帶着好幾個乘客。 鉛酸電池也不能完全實現回收，造成環境問題。小規模電動單車廠泛濫，給制定有效的安全條例造成困難。
Some big automakers are edging into the e-bike business. BMW has presented an e-bike that can fold into the trunk of a car. Although it is still in the prototype stage, 200 will be used during the London Olympics. Audi and Smart are also working on e-bikes.
一些大型汽車製造商也設法進入電動單車行業。寶馬發佈的一款電動單車能摺疊放入汽車後備箱。雖然只是初具雛形，但200輛這樣的電動單車將用於倫敦奧運會。奧迪和精靈（Smart) 也在開發電動單車。
Manufacturers can get premium prices outside China. Whereas electric bikes cost 1,060 renminbi, or $167, on average in China, according to Pike, they average over $800 in the United States and more than $1,500 in Western Europe. And top-notch bikes can go higher: those at Rocket Electrics are assembled in California (from Chinese-made components) and sell for $1,900 to $2,400 — far more than some varieties sold at places like Walmart.
製造商在中國之外的市場能把電動單車賣到更好的價格。根據派克研究公司，電動單車在中國平均售價1060元，即167美元，而在美國，它的平均售價是800美元，在西歐，售價達到1500美元。第一流的電動單車的價格可能更高：火箭電器店裡銷售的電動單車在加利福尼亞組裝（零件產自中國），售價1900到2400美元—比沃爾瑪（Walmart）及類似賣場所售的各類電動單車要貴得多。
One factor affecting price is battery type. Many (though not all) electric bikes sold in Europe and the United States have lithium-ion batteries, which are more energy-dense and costlier than the lead-acid type predominant in China. Other factors include the motor’s power and the warranty length.
影響價格的一個因素是電池。許多（雖然不是全部）在歐洲和美國銷售的電動單車採用鋰電池。鋰電池具有比中國通用的鉛酸電池更高的能效，成本也更貴。其他因素包括發動機的功率和質保期。
In Germany, e-bike sales have soared from about 25,000 in 2005 to 300,000 last year, according to Max Grünig, a Berlin-based fellow with the Ecologic Institute. He said that German e-bikes consumed little electricity and were good from a climate perspective — especially, of course, when they replace car trips.
根據生態研究所（Ecologic Institute）駐柏林研究員馬克斯·格呂尼希（Max Grünig) 的研究，在德國，電動單車銷售量從2005年的2.5萬輛飆升到去年的30萬輛。他說，德國電動單車耗電少，從環保角度看是很好的，特別是如果電動單車能替代開車出行的話。
In the Netherlands, the No.2 market in Europe, seniors are an important clientele. Aided by an electric bike, “older people will continue to cycle longer,” said Jeroen Buis, a bicycle consultant who works with the Dutch Cycling Embassy, a public-private networking initiative. Bicycle highways between cities are becoming more popular, he said — for example, a route between Rotterdam and The Hague, about 24 kilometers, or 15 miles, long, is planned. Such paths make it easier for cyclists to travel long distances and go faster, but they can be expensive, and the intersections with roadways require careful design.
荷蘭是歐洲第二大電動單車市場。老年人是重要的用戶。公共及私人網絡倡議組織“荷蘭騎車大使館”（Dutch Cycling Embassy）的單車顧問杰倫·比伊（Jeroen Buis）說，藉助於電動單車，“老年人能繼續騎行更長的時間。”他說，城市之間的單車公路路越來越普及，比如，鹿特丹和海牙之間全長24公里，即15里的單車公路正在規劃當中。這樣的道路更便於騎車人騎得更遠更快，不過造價不菲，另外單車公路和普通道路的交叉路口也需要小心設計。
One of the toughest markets may be the United States, where people are accustomed to driving even short distances. Still, more than 105,000 e-bikes will be sold in the United States this year, Pike Research projects, with sales more than tripling by 2018. The high price of oil, traffic congestion and an aging baby boomer population may create demand, but Americans must learn that the technology exists.
美國的市場可能是最艱難的。在這裡，就算是短途出行，人們也習慣於開車。即便如此，派克研究公司預計，今年美國仍將銷售掉10.5萬輛電動單車，到2018年銷售量將再翻三倍。高油價，交通擁堵和嬰兒潮人口的老齡化可能帶來需求，但美國人必須首先知道，這樣的科技已經存在。
“Nobody really knows what an electric bike is in the U.S.,” Mr. Cherry said.
謝里說：“在美國，沒人真正知道電動單車是什麼。”
Nicole Zinn, who owns Rocket Electrics, which rents e-bikes as well as selling them, said that first-time customers sometimes “think it’s cheating” to ride an electric bike. But when they are able to power up a hill without much effort, the skepticism fades.
火箭電氣提供電動單車銷售及租賃服務。店主妮科爾·津恩（Nicole Zinn）說，有些第一次來的顧客認為騎電動單車“是騙人的。”但當他們能毫不費力地翻過一座山坡，這種疑慮也就隨之打消了。
“They come back beaming like a kid who just got training wheels off,” Ms. Zinn said.
津恩說：“他們再來的時候就都興沖沖的了，好像是剛剛學會了騎單車的孩子。”
