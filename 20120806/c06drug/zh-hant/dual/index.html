新興市場將拉動全球藥品消費增長

Drug Makers’ Growth Is Linked to Emerging Markets

KATIE THOMAS
2012年8月6日
Rising incomes among consumers in emerging markets like China, India and Brazil are poised to drive global growth in pharmaceutical spending over the next five years, according to a report released by the IMS Institute for Healthcare Informatics.
艾美仕醫療信息研究所(IMS Institute for Healthcare Informatics)發表報告稱，中國、印度、巴西等新興市場的消費者收入水平日益上升，勢將在未來5年里拉動全球藥品消費增長。
The news is likely to be a welcome development for the drug industry, which is struggling to maintain sales in the United States and Europe as patent expirations and price controls eat into profits. The report predicts that the market for drugs will rebound in the next few years, to 5 to 7 percent growth in 2016 from an expected low point of 3 to 4 percent growth this year. Global spending will total almost $1.2 trillion by 2016, compared with about $956 billion in 2011.
這對製藥產業來說應該是個好消息，後者正忙於維持美國和歐洲市場的銷售量，努力應對專利到期以及價格管控導致的利潤損失。該報告預測說，藥品市場將在幾年內回升，今年的預期增長率僅為3%到4%，到2016年可以增至5%到7%。2011年全球消費總額為9560億美元左右，而到2016年，消費總額將會達到大約1.2萬億美元。
Governments and insurers in the United States and Europe, eager to curtail growing health care costs, are expected to take an even more critical look at drug prices, said Michael Kleinrock, director of research development at the institute.
艾美仕研究所研究發展部主任邁克爾·克蘭羅克(Michael Kleinrock)表示，美國和歐洲地區的政府部門和保險公司都渴望縮減日益增長的醫療保健費用，估計他們將來會對藥品價格更加在意。
“As payers continue to spend more and more on medicines, the questions of the value and return for that spend become even more front-of-mind,” he said. The report found that rising incomes and more generous health care policies in emerging pharmaceutical markets — countries that will see more than $1 billion in drug growth over the next five years, but where the per capita gross domestic product is less than $25,000 — is leading to small but widespread increases in drug spending.
他說，“消費者在藥物上的花費繼續增加，有關投入的價值和回報問題變得更為重要。”該報告發現，在未來5年里，新興醫藥市場日益上升的收入水平以及愈加慷慨的醫療保健政策將會使藥品花費發生幅度小卻範圍廣的增長。新興醫院市場指的是這樣一些國家，它們的人均國內生產總值低於2.5萬美元，未來五年內的藥品消費增長卻會超過10億美元。
“As incomes rise, the ability to afford medicines is one of the first things that come to these people,” Mr. Kleinrock said.
克蘭羅克說，“收入的增加會給人們帶來許多好處，能夠負擔醫藥費就是其中之一。”
In China, the report estimates, drug spending will amount to about $121 a person in 2016, which is far less than the $892 a person expected to be spent in the United States. But given that two-thirds of the world’s population lives in those emerging markets, even modest increases can make a big difference.
該報告估計，到2016年，中國的藥品支出將會達到大約121美元/人，遠遠低於美國的892美元/人。但是，由於這些新興市場國家擁有全世界三分之二的人口，藥品支出的小幅增長也會帶來很大變化。
Spending is expected to double in these markets in the next five years, growing by a projected range of $150 billion to $165 billion, the report found. By 2016, 30 percent of global spending on medicines will be in the emerging markets, compared with 20 percent in 2011.
該報告指出，新興市場的藥品支出有望在未來5年里翻倍，增長額預計會在1500億美元至1650億美元之間。到2016年，全球藥品銷售額的30%將會來自新興市場，高於2011年的20%。
The story is very different in developed markets like the United States, Europe and Japan, where the share of global drug spending is expected to decrease to 57 percent in 2016, from 66 percent in 2011.
而在美國、歐洲以及日本等發達市場，情況卻完全不同。2011年，這些市場在全球藥品銷售額所佔比例為66%，據估計，到2016年，這個比例將會降至57%。
The decline is expected to be sharp in the United States. In 2006, the American share of global spending was 41 percent. By 2016, it is projected to be 31 percent. That decrease is driven largely by a wave of patent expirations and the spread of low-cost generic drugs, both in the United States and Europe. The brunt will be felt this year, with at least nine major drugs — including Plavix, Seroquel and Singulair — losing their patent protections in the United States. That will ebb by 2016, when just four major drugs are set to lose exclusivity in this country.
美國市場的銷售額預期會急劇下降。2006年，美國市場在全球所佔的比例為41%，到2016年，所佔比例預計為31%。專利到期以及低價的非專利葯的廣泛使用是導致美國和歐洲市場銷售額下降的主要原因。這些衝擊將會在今年顯現，在美國，包括波拉維(Plavix)、思瑞康(Seroquel)、順爾寧(Singulair)在內的至少九種重要藥品不再享受專利保護。到了2016年，這一勢頭將會有所減退，到時只有四種重要藥品失去專利保護。
As the patent wave crests this year and next, Mr. Kleinrock said, growth in pharmaceutical spending in developed markets will be almost nonexistent, between zero and 1 percent. But by 2014, “we’ll start to see less negative impact on overall brand spending; we’ll start to see slightly higher growth rates in developed markets,” he said.
克蘭羅克表示，由於專利權風波將在今年和明年達到高峰，發達市場的藥品銷售幾乎不會出現增長，漲幅在0%到1%之間。但是到2014年，“專利權對所有品牌銷售情況的負面影響將會開始減小，發達市場的銷售增長率將會小幅上升，”克蘭羅克說。
About half of the expected growth in the American market in 2014 — about $6 billion, Mr. Kleinrock said —will be because of the health care law, which is predicted to increase demand for drugs as more people become insured.
克蘭羅克表示， 2014年美國市場預期增長額大約為60億美元，其中有一半左右歸功於醫保法的實施。醫保法有望使藥物需求量增加，因為將有更多人被納入保險覆蓋範圍。
