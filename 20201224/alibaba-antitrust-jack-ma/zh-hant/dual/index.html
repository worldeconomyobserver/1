阿里巴巴涉嫌壟斷遭中國監管機構立案調查

China Opens Antitrust Investigation Into Alibaba

RAYMOND ZHONG
2020年12月24日
China’s market regulator said on Thursday that it had opened an antimonopoly investigation into the e-commerce giant Alibaba, ratcheting up the government’s scrutiny of one of the world’s most valuable internet companies.
中國的市場監管機構週四表示，已對電子商務巨頭阿里巴巴啟動反壟斷調查，加強了政府對這個市值名列世界前茅的互聯網企業的審查力度。
In a terse statement, the State Administration for Market Regulation said it had started the investigation amid reports that Alibaba had engaged in monopolistic conduct such as placing unreasonable restrictions on merchants or other users of its platforms.
國家市場監督管理總局在一份簡短聲明中表示，根據舉報，對阿里巴巴集團控股有限公司實施「二選一」等涉嫌壟斷行為。
Alibaba said in a statement that it would cooperate with regulators and that its businesses were operating normally in the meantime.
阿里巴巴在一份聲明中表示將配合監管部門，與此同時，其業務運行正常。
The investigation in China coincides with efforts by the United States and European Union to curb the power of Western internet giants such as Google and Facebook. Frustration has been building for years in Washington and Brussels over the outsize influence that a few tech companies wield over commerce, speech and advertising across a vast swath of the globe.
中國展開此次調查同時，美國和歐盟正努力遏制谷歌和Facebook等西方互聯網巨頭的勢力。多年來，幾家科技公司對全球大片地區的商業、言論和廣告施加了巨大影響，這讓華盛頓和布魯塞爾越來越感到沮喪。
廣告
China has produced its own crop of powerful internet titans, including Alibaba, JD.com and Pinduoduo in digital commerce; Tencent in gaming, social media and mobile payments; ByteDance in short-form entertainment; Didi Chuxing in ride hailing; and Meituan in food delivery. For years, the companies had been celebrated as icons of China’s technological advancement. Now, the authorities are taking a closer look at how their size and clout are affecting competition and the business landscape.
中國已經培育了一批自己的強大的互聯網巨頭，包括數字商務領域的阿里巴巴、京東和拼多多，遊戲、社群媒體和移動支付領域的騰訊，短片娛樂領域的字節跳動，叫車服務領域的滴滴，以及外賣領域的美團。多年來，這些公司一直被譽為中國技術進步的象徵。現在，當局正在密切關注它們的規模和影響力如何影響競爭和商業環境。
In November, the market regulator released proposed rules aimed at combating anticompetitive behavior at internet companies. Earlier that month, Chinese regulators halted the initial public offering of Ant Group, Alibaba’s finance-focused sister company, quashing a listing that had been on course to be the largest in history. The move came after Mr. Ma publicly criticized Chinese regulators for being too obsessed with containing financial risk.
11月，市場監管機構就平台經濟領域的反壟斷指南公開徵求意見。當月早些時候，中國的監管機構叫停阿里巴巴的姊妹公司、專注金融業務的螞蟻金服的首次公開募股，取消了原本有望成為史上最大的上市交易。在此之前，馬雲曾經公開批評中國監管機構過於痴迷控制金融風險。
 Four regulatory agencies, including the country’s central bank, said on Thursday that officials would meet with Ant soon to discuss new supervision for the financial industry. Ant said in a statement that it would “seriously study and strictly comply with all regulatory requirements and commit full efforts to fulfill all related work.”
包括中國央行在內的四家監管機構週四表示，管理部門將於近日約談螞蟻金服，討論對金融業的新監管事宜。螞蟻金服發表聲明表示，將「認真研究並嚴格遵守所有監管要求，全力做好相關工作」。
Alibaba holds a strong position in online shopping in China. It runs the Taobao marketplace, an online bazaar where merchants set up electronic stands to sell to customers. Alibaba’s Tmall platform caters to larger Chinese and global brands. Alibaba makes money by hosting the marketplaces and charging vendors for services.
在中國網購市場，阿里巴巴佔據著強大的份額。集團擁有的淘寶網是一個商家設立電子商鋪向顧客銷售商品的網路市場。阿里巴巴的天貓平台則迎合更大的中國和全球品牌。阿里巴巴通過託管市場以及向賣家收取服務費賺錢。
People’s Daily, the main newspaper of the Chinese Communist Party, swiftly endorsed the investigation in an article that appeared to be a sign of broader backing and coordination behind the move.
中國共產黨的黨報《人民日報》立即發文，對該調查表示支持，這似乎表明此舉背後得到了更廣泛的支持和協調。
“This is an important step in strengthening antimonopoly oversight in the internet sphere,” said the article, which was published on the paper’s website Thursday morning. “This will be beneficial to regulating an orderly sector and promoting the long-term healthy development of platforms.”
「這是我國在互聯網領域加強反壟斷監管的一項重要舉措，」週四早間發表在該報網站上的文章稱，「有利於規範行業秩序、促進平台經濟長遠健康發展。」
廣告
Political insiders and investors in China have speculated for years that the national leader, Xi Jinping, would be tempted to move against Alibaba and Mr. Ma, worried that their influence was an increasing affront to the Communist Party and that it could undermine control of capital markets and the internet. But until recently, Chinese regulators had moved cautiously.
多年來，中國的政治內部人士和投資者一直在猜測，由於擔心其影響力會對共產黨造成日益加劇的冒犯，並有可能破壞對資本的控制，中國國家領導人習近平可能想對阿里巴巴和馬雲採取行動。但是直到最近，中國監管機構一直在謹慎行事。
Two recent party leadership meetings hinted that Mr. Xi was considering action.
最近兩次黨的領導會議暗示習近平正在考慮採取行動。
The Politburo, a council of the party’s top 25 officials that meets every month or so, called for stronger antimonopoly efforts when it met this month, though the official statement from the meeting did not specify any companies or sectors. That call was followed a few days later by an even clearer demand from the party leadership’s annual meeting on economic policy, which hinted that internet platform companies would face greater scrutiny.
中央政治局是一個由25名中共最高級別官員組成的委員會，約每個月舉行一次會議。該委員會在本月的會議上呼籲加強反壟斷監管，儘管會議的官方聲明並未具體點名任何公司或部門。幾天後，中共領導層在年度經濟政策會議上提出了更為明確的要求，這暗示著互聯網平台公司將面臨更多審查。
The Chinese government “supports the innovative development of platform enterprises and enhancing their international competitiveness,” the official summary from the meeting said. “At the same time, development must be regulated according to the law and digital rules must be improved.”
中國政府「支持平台企業創新發展、增強國際競爭力」，會議官方紀要說。「同時要依法規範發展，健全數字規則。」
Regulators should “resolutely oppose monopolies and improper competitive behavior,” the meeting summary said.
監管部門應「堅決反對壟斷和不正當競爭行為」，會議紀要說。
Mr. Ma’s remarks on financial regulation, delivered at a conference in October in Shanghai, appear to have helped catalyze the official pushback against Ant and Alibaba.
馬雲10月在上海的一次會議上發表的有關金融監管的言論，似乎促成了官方對螞蟻金服和阿里巴巴的反擊。
廣告
“In Chinese culture, if you are a rich guy and you have very strong economic power and social influence, then you are politically dangerous and you need to keep a very low profile to be safe,” said Gary Liu, an independent economist in Shanghai.
「在中國文化中，如果你是一個有錢人，有很強的經濟實力和社會影響力，那麼你在政治上是危險的，你需要保持低調以保安全，」上海獨立經濟學家劉勝軍說。
People in China see Mr. Ma and Ant as major beneficiaries of the authorities’ cautious approach toward regulating internet finance. “But still, he was complaining,” Mr. Liu said. “In Chinese culture, that kind of person is not respected.”
在中國，人們認為馬雲和螞蟻金服是當局監管互聯網金融的謹慎做法的主要受益者。「但他還是在抱怨，」劉勝軍說。「在中國文化中，這種人是不受尊重的。」
