中國信達香港上市籌資152億元

China Cinda Said to Raise $2.5 Billion in Hong Kong I.P.O.

NEIL GOUGH
2013年12月6日
Updated, 5:28 a.m. | HONG KONG — The hottest deal among investors in Hong Kong this year is a huge bet that a slowdown of the Chinese economy will create a new wave of bad debt.
香港——今年，在香港投資者中最熱門的交易是：豪賭中國經濟的放緩將催生新一波的壞賬浪潮。
On Thursday, China Cinda Asset Management, one of the biggest ‘‘bad banks’’ set up by the Chinese government to absorb deadbeat loans from the rest of the financial system, raised about $2.5 billion in a Hong Kong share sale, according to three people familiar with the deal.
\r中國信達資產管理公司是中國政府開設的最大的「壞帳銀行」之一，用來清理金融系統中其他機構的不良貸款。三位知情人士透露，本周四，它在香港的募股中籌集了大約25億美元（約合152億元人民幣）。
Cinda’s initial public offering is the city’s biggest so far this year. The deal priced at the top of the marketed range after receiving exceptional demand from both big, overseas funds and individual local investors, the people said, declining to be identified because the information is not yet public.
\r信達的首次公開募股(IPO)是香港股市今年規模最大的一宗上市。知情人士表示，由於來自海外大基金和本土個人投資者的認購均極為火爆，它的發行價達到了招股價區間的上限。因為該信息尚未正式公布，這些知情人士拒絕公布自己的姓名。
In the 20 years that have passed since Tsingtao Brewery became the first Chinese company to list directly on Hong Kong’s stock exchange, the sales pitch to foreign investors in the hundreds of Chinese I.P.O.’s that have followed has almost always been some variation on this: ‘‘It’s a chance to buy into China’s booming growth story.’’
\r自從20年前青島啤酒成為中國大陸第一家直接在香港證交所上市的公司後，數百宗大陸公司的IPO在面向外國投資者招股時，幾乎總是使用不同版本的同一主題：「這是一個機會，在中國的蓬勃發展中分一杯羹。」
Now, as it becomes clearer that China’s decades of double-digit growth are a thing of the past, and that the government under President Xi Jinping is serious about accepting some short- term economic pain for the sake of better balanced and more sustainable growth, investors are having to make significant shifts in their approach to China.
\r現在，中國幾十年來兩位數的增長率成為了過去，而且，為了實現更加平衡而可持續的增長，習近平政府的確願意承受一些短期經濟陣痛。當這些事情變得更加清晰的時候，投資者不得不在投資中國的方式上做出大幅調整。
A crackdown on conspicuous consumption among government officials? Dump shares in producers of baijiu, or Chinese rice wine — Kweichow Moutai Company’s stock, for example, is down 35 percent this year.
\r中國在打壓政府官員的鋪張浪費？那就拋售白酒企業的股票——比如，貴州茅台股份有限公司的股價今年下跌了35%。
Demographic shifts mean the population is graying fast? Buy shares in Fu Shou Yuan International, a fast-growing Shanghai company hoping to list in Hong Kong later this month, according to a preliminary term sheet for the deal. Fu Shou Yuan, meaning ‘‘Garden of Prosperous Longevity,’’ is one of China’s biggest operators of cemeteries and providers of funerary services.
\r人口結構的變化意味着中國正經歷快速的老齡化？那就買進福壽園集團的股份。這是一家快速增長的上海公司，一份初步條款清單顯示，它計劃本月晚些時候在香港上市。福壽園，意為「幸福長壽的花園」，是中國最大的公墓和殯葬服務企業之一。
Country on the verge of a new wave of bad debts after five years of unprecedented growth in lending? That’s where China Cinda’s I.P.O. comes into play.
\r貸款空前增長五年後，中國處於新一輪壞賬的邊緣？這正是中國信達IPO的吸引力所在。
A government agency created in 1999 to take on bad debts from state lenders and restructure deadbeat loans, China Cinda on Thursday sold 5.3 billion shares priced at 3.58 Hong Kong dollars apiece, raising a total of 19 billion dollars, or $2.5 billion. That compares with the marketed range of 3 dollars to 3.58 dollars per share.
\r中國信達創建於1999年，是一家處理國有銀行壞賬、重組不良貸款的政府機構。本周四，它發行了53億股，每股定價為3.58港元（約合2.81元人民幣），籌資總額達190億港元。而招股價在每股3到3.58港元的區間。
The deal drew strong support from Chinese and foreign investors, including from funds with long experience doing business in Asia and that specialize in distressed-debt investing.
\r這樁交易受到了中外投資者的熱捧，其中包括具有長期開展亞洲業務經驗的基金，以及專長不良債務投資的基金。
In the five years since the financial crisis, China’s banks more than doubled the size of their loan books, in many cases pumping money into state companies and government-affiliated investment entities to fund large-scale infrastructure projects or other ventures that analysts say may struggle to produce financial returns. If, as expected, many of these borrowers struggle to keep up with interest and loan payments, this represents a huge and steady supply of new business for big Chinese asset management companies like China Cinda in the years to come.
\r金融危機以來的五年中，中國的銀行發放的貸款規模增加了一倍以上。許多時候，這些資金注入了國有企業和政府下屬的投資機構，用以資助大型基礎設施建設等項目，但分析師們認為，這些項目可能很難產生財務回報。如果預期成真，即很多借款人難以償還本息，這就意味着，在未來數年中，像中國信達這樣的大型資產管理公司會獲得規模巨大、來源穩定的新業務。
At the same time, China’s bad banks have themselves been borrowing heavily in order to leverage their acquisitions of problem loans and other new financial assets.
\r與此同時，中國的壞賬銀行本身也在大量借貸，舉債收購問題貸款和其他新金融資產。
At China Cinda, for example, standard borrowings have risen more than twelve-fold in the last three years, from 7.8 billion renminbi, or $1.3 billion, at the end of 2010 to 104 billion renminbi at the end of June. These funds are used to acquire new deadbeat loans, often from companies in sectors that are struggling with overcapacity and other challenges to their profitability. Coal companies, for instance, accounted for 61.5 percent of all debt-to-equity swaps on China Cinda’s books as of June.
\r例如，中國信達的標準借貸規模已經在過去三年間提高了逾12倍，從2010年底的78億元人民幣增加至今年6月底的1040億元人民幣。這些資金被用於收購新的不良貸款，而無力償還這些貸款的公司往往來自產能過剩或在盈利方面遇到問題的行業。例如，截至今年6月，煤炭企業在中國信達的全部債轉股帳目上佔了61.5%。
Asset managers like China Cinda have also been busy expanding into new roles as alternative lenders to companies that struggle to get bank loans, a development that has raised concerns among analysts.
\r像中國信達這樣的資產管理機構也一直忙於拓展作為替代放款人的新角色，為難以獲得銀行青睞的公司提供貸款。這一趨勢已經引發了分析師們的擔憂。
‘‘The asset management companies are also lenders, they are technically part of the shadow banking system right now,’’ said Ted Osborn, a partner at PricewaterhouseCoopers in Hong Kong who specialized in debt restructuring.
\r「資產管理公司也在發放貸款，從技術上說，它們眼下也是影子銀行系統的一部分，」擅長壞賬重組的普華永道(PricewaterhouseCoopers)駐香港合伙人區兆邦(Ted Osborn)說。
‘‘As long as they can continue to access bank funding to help them with their various lending deals then all is good because they seem to be making a tidy profit at it,’’ he said. ‘‘But as soon as the loans get cut off, the question is: What are they going to do, and how are they going to replicate those revenues and cash flows?’’
\r「只要它們可以繼續從銀行獲得資金，來幫助他們達成各种放款交易，就什麼問題都沒有，因為它們似乎從中獲得了可觀的利潤，」他說。「但是，只要貸款被切斷，問題就來了：他們怎麼辦呢，如何去複製這些收益和現金流呢？」
For now, investors don’t seem overly concerned with these issues. Subscription orders for China Cinda’s I.P.O. were 15 times more than the number of shares available, one of the people familiar with the deal said, adding that the portion of the deal marketed to individual Hong Kong investors was more than 160 times oversubscribed.
\r就目前而言，投資者似乎並不過分關注這些問題。中國信達IPO獲得的認購申請，是可認購股份總數的15倍，了解該交易的人士透露。該人還說，面向香港個人投資者銷售的部分，被超額認購了逾160倍。
So-called cornerstone investors had last month committed to investing $1.1 billion in China Cinda’s I.P.O., accounting for 44 percent of the shares being sold and bolstering the deal’s chance of success. Those investors, who agreed to hold their shares for six months, included China Life Insurance Company, the American hedge fund Och-Ziff Capital Management Group and the Norwegian state investor Norges Bank, among others.
\r上個月，所謂的「基礎投資者」已經承諾在中國信達的IPO中投資11億美元，占待出售股份的44%，並增加了成功交易的機會。那些投資者同意在六個月內持有中國信達的股票，它們包括中國人壽保險公司，美國對沖基金奧奇-齊夫資本管理集團(Och-Ziff Capital Management Group)和挪威國有投資機構挪威銀行(Norges Bank)等等。
In preparation for its I.P.O., Cinda had also sold a 16.5 percent stake in April 2012 to four strategic investors: China’s National Social Security Fund, UBS, Citic Capital and Standard Chartered. Those four investors acquired 5 billion shares at 2.08 renminbi, or $0.34, per share — meaning that as of Thursday they were sitting on combined paper profits of about $600 million.
\r在準備IPO的過程中，信達還在2012年4月將16.5%的股份出售給了四家戰略投資者：中國全國社會保障基金、瑞銀集團(UBS)、中信資本(Citic Capital)和渣打銀行(Standard Chartered)。這四家投資機構以每股2.08元人民幣的價格購買了50億股——也就是說，截至本周四，它們的賬面利潤總計已達6億美元。
No fewer than 18 underwriters took part in Cinda’s I.P.O., led by Bank of America Merrill Lynch, Credit Suisse, Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley.
\r參與信達IPO的承銷商不下18家，牽頭的有美銀美林(Bank of America Merrill Lynch)、瑞信(Credit Suisse)、高盛(Goldman Sachs)和摩根士丹利(Morgan Stanley)。
