高通收購案背後：科技競爭重新定義中美博弈

The U.S.-China Rivalry Is, More Than Ever, a Fight Over Tech

CECILIA KANG, ALAN RAPPEPORT
2018年3月7日
WASHINGTON — As the United States and China look to protect their national security needs and economic interests, the fight between the two financial superpowers is increasingly focused on a single area: technology.
華盛頓——隨著美國和中國留意保護自己的國家安全需求和經濟利益，這兩個金融超級大國之間的鬥爭越來越多地集中到了一個領域：科技。
The clash erupted in public on Tuesday after the United States government, citing national security concerns, called for a full investigation into a hostile bid to buy the American chip stalwart Qualcomm — a review that is often a death knell for a corporate deal.
週二，美國政府以國家安全為由，要求全面調查針對美國晶片巨頭高通(Qualcomm)的惡意收購。這種審查通常意味著，某項公司交易會胎死腹中。
The proposed acquisition by the Singapore-based Broadcom would have been the largest deal in technology history, creating a major force in the development of the computer chips that power smartphones and many internet-connected devices. But a government panel said the takeover could weaken Qualcomm and give its Chinese rivals an advantage.
新加坡博通公司(Broadcom)提出的對高通的收購本可能成為科技史上規模最大的交易，造就電腦晶片開發領域的一股重要勢力，為智慧型手機以及許多與互聯網相連的設備提供動力。但美國政府的一個小組表示，收購可能會削弱高通的實力，致使它的中國競爭對手獲得優勢。
“China would likely compete robustly to fill any void left by Qualcomm as a result of this hostile takeover,” a United States Treasury official wrote in a letter calling for a review of the deal.
「中國很可能會激烈競爭，填補高通因惡意收購而留下的任何空白，」美國財政部的一名官員在一封要求對該交易進行審查的信函中寫道。
廣告
The fight over technology is redefining the rules of engagement in an era when national security and economic power are closely intertwined.
在國家安全與經濟實力緊密相連的時代，圍繞科技的鬥爭正在重新定義交戰規則。
China, under President Xi Jinping, has launched an ambitious plan to dominate mobile technology, supercomputers, artificial intelligence and other cutting-edge industries, putting huge resources behind an effort that it considers crucial to the country’s government, military and economy. Beijing wants to build its own technology champions and is encouraging companies to acquire the engineering, expertise and intellectual property from big rivals in the United States and elsewhere.
在國家主席習近平的領導下，中國啟動了一項雄心勃勃的計劃，以期在移動技術、超級計算機和人工智慧等尖端產業佔據主導地位，將大量資源投入到在它看來對中國政府、軍隊和經濟至關重要的一個努力之中。中國政府希望打造自己的技術領袖，鼓勵企業從美國等地的大型競爭對手那裡獲取工程技術、專業知識和知識產權。
The aggressive push has set off alarms in Washington, with policymakers and lawmakers fearful that American giants could lose their edge. President Trump is now building up the country’s defenses, as the government investigates potential violations of American intellectual property rights and intensifies scrutiny of overseas deals.
這種咄咄逼人的攻勢引起了華盛頓的警惕，政策制定者和立法者擔心美國的巨頭們失去優勢。川普總統正在加強美國的防禦，政府正在調查可能侵犯美國知識產權的行為，加強對海外交易的審查。
The secretive panel that is reviewing the Qualcomm deal, the Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States, or Cfius, has taken on a central role in the resistance to Chinese investment. The panel, which is led by the Treasury Department and made up of representatives from multiple agencies, has the authority to block foreign acquisitions of American companies for national security reasons; it has effectively killed several acquisitions over the past year linked to Chinese buyers. Lawmakers are also calling to expand the powers of Cfius to reflect the broader scope of China’s interests.
審查高通交易的祕密小組是外國在美投資委員會（Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States，簡稱Cfius）。該委員會在抵制中國投資方面發揮著核心作用。它由財政部牽頭，由多個機構的代表組成，它有權以國家安全為由阻止外國公司收購美國公司。在過去的一年裡，它有效地扼殺了幾宗與中國買家相關的收購交易。議員們還呼籲擴大Cfius的權力，延伸至與中國利益相關的更廣泛領域。
[ What is Cfius? It is the “ultimate regulatory bazooka,” according to an executive who works on mergers and acquisitions. Read more about the panel here » ]
（Cfius是什麼？一位從事併購交易的高管表示，它是「終極監管火箭炮」。如欲了解Cfius的更多情況，請點擊此處。）
“The Trump administration turbocharged it,” Tony Balloon, the head of the corporate China practice at the law firm Alston & Bird, said, referring to Cfius. “There is now a recognition in government that foreign investors, particularly from China, are getting more and more sophisticated on how they get access to technology in the U.S.”
「川普政府對它的權力進行了渦輪加壓，」奧斯頓與伯德律師事務所(Alston & Bird)的中國業務主管托尼·巴隆(Tony Balloon)在提到Cfius時說。「現在，美國政府意識到，外國投資者，尤其是中國投資者，在如何獲得美國的技術方面越來越老練。」
廣告
With Qualcomm, the government has articulated its evolving vision for global economic leadership.
在高通的案例中，美國政府明確表達了它對全球經濟領導力不斷變化的願景。
The company, which is a major supplier to the United States government, is a leading player in the race to build the next generation wireless technology known as 5G. These high-speed mobile networks will form the infrastructure backbone that ultimately connects home appliances, streetlights and driverless cars to the internet. And Qualcomm’s chips will be in the multitude of devices and machines that will run on those networks.
該公司是美國政府的一個主要供應商，在構建下一代無線技術5G的競賽中處於領先地位。這些高速移動網路將構成基礎設施的支柱，最終將家用電器、街燈和無人駕駛汽車都與互聯網連接起來。在這些網路上運行的眾多設備和機器將使用高通的晶片。
“Having a well-known and trusted company hold the dominant role that Qualcomm does in the telecommunications infrastructure provides significant confidence in the integrity of such infrastructure as it relates to national security,” the Treasury official wrote in the letter.
「讓一家知名且受信任的公司佔據主導地位，就像高通在電信基礎設施領域那樣，能讓人們對此類與國家安全息息相關的基礎設施的可靠性產生極大信心，」美國財政部的那名官員在那封信中寫道。
The government specifically cited Huawei, the Chinese telecom-equipment giant, as a potential competitor that could move into a breach created by a merger. The Chinese company has spent heavily on 5G, and the government said it owns 10 percent of the essential patents.
美國政府特別提到了中國電信設備巨頭華為，稱它是潛在競爭者，可能會填補併購造成的缺口。這家中國公司已經在5G網路上投入巨資，中國政府曾表示，它擁有這項關鍵專利的10%。
“It is the new paradigm,” said Paul Triolo, head of global technology policy at Eurasia Group, a geopolitical risk consulting company. “That implies technologies with 5G, artificial intelligence, biotech and automation are now considered more sensitive and part of a national innovation base that needs to be protected.”
「這是一個新例子，」地緣政治風險諮詢公司歐亞集團(Eurasia Group)的全球科技政策事務負責人保羅·特寥洛(Paul Triolo)說。「它意味著，5G、人工智慧、生物技術和自動化等技術現在被認為是更為敏感的技術，屬於需要保護的國家創新基礎。」
Broadcom said it was cooperating with Cfius, saying it was “making the combined company a global leader in critical 5G and other technologies.” Qualcomm, in an earlier statement, said the review was a “very serious matter.”
博通稱正在配合Cfius，並表示會「讓合併後的公司成為至關重要的5G和其他技術領域的全球領導者」。高通則在早前的一份聲明中表示，審查是一件「非常嚴肅的事情」。
廣告
The letter and the call for an investigation reflect the newly forceful position of Cfius.
這封信和要求展開調查的呼籲反映出了Cfius新近變得強硬的立場。
In most cases, the panel weighs in after a deal is announced. In Qualcomm, Cfius is taking a proactive role and investigating before an acquisition agreement has even been signed.
大部分情況下，該委員會是在交易宣布之後發表意見的。但在高通一事上，Cfius先發制人，在雙方簽署收購協議前就展開了調查。
Cfius has stymied several deals in the past year.
過去一年裡，Cfius叫停了多項交易。
MoneyGram, the money transfer company, and Ant Financial, the Chinese electronics payment company, called off their merger in January, citing regulatory concerns by Cfius. If the deal had gone through, Ant Financial would have had access to reams of data on financial flows, which could have created security issues. Ant Financial has disputed the claims.
轉賬公司速匯金(MoneyGram)和中國電子支付公司螞蟻金服1月取消合併計劃，給出的原因是Cfius的監管擔憂。如果這筆交易獲得通過，螞蟻金服將獲得大量資金流動數據，這可能會造成安全問題。螞蟻金服對有關說法提出了質疑。
Last year, the White House blocked a Chinese-backed investor from buying Lattice Semiconductor, which is a supplier to the United States government. China Venture Capital Fund Corporation, which was part of the investment group, is owned by state-backed entities.
去年，白宮叫停了一個得到中國支持的投資集團收購美國政府的供應商萊迪思半導體(Lattice Semiconductor)的計劃。該投資集團的成員之一中國風險投資基金公司的所有者是得到國家支持的實體。
“It’s very much an expansion of what is considered to be national security,” said Tai Ming Cheung, director of the Institute on Global Conflict and Cooperation at the University of California at San Diego. “This is about China’s efforts to invest and acquire key parts of the U.S. innovation system.”
「這完全是擴大國家安全的定義，」加州大學聖地牙哥分校全球衝突與合作研究所(Institute on Global Conflict and Cooperation at the University of California at San Diego)所長張太銘說。「涉及中國投資和收購美國創新體系關鍵組成部分的行動。」
廣告
Cfius could soon have even more muscle.
Cfius的權力可能很快還會增加。
There is new legislation to broaden the jurisdiction of Cfius; it has bipartisan support in the Senate. The Trump administration has expressed support for rewriting the rules governing Cfius, and Steven Mnuchin, the Treasury secretary, said last year that the administration is working closely with the House and the Senate.
有新的立法要求擴大Cfius的權限。該立法在參議院得到了兩黨的支持。川普政府已表示支持修訂管理Cfius的規定，財長史蒂文·努欽(Steven Mnuchin)在去年也表示聯邦政府正在與參眾兩院密切合作。
The bill, proposed by Senators John Cornyn, a Republican from Texas, and Dianne Feinstein, a Democrat from California, would give Cfius the authority to assess some types of joint ventures, minority investments and real estate transactions near military bases. The legislation would also widen the definition of “critical technologies” slated for potential review to include “emerging technologies that could be essential for maintaining the U.S. technological advantage over countries that pose threats, such as China,” according to a news release on the bill.
由德克薩斯州共和黨參議員約翰·科寧(John Cornyn)和加利福尼亞州民主党參議員黛安·費恩斯坦(Dianne Feinstein)提出的這項議案，將賦予Cfius評估某些類型的合資企業、少數股權投資和近軍事基地房地產交易的權力。就該議案發布的一份新聞稿顯示，這項立法還將擴大可能要接受審查的「關鍵技術」的定義，將「可能對保持美國相對於中國等構成威脅的國家的技術優勢至關重要的新興技術」包括在內。
Adding to the scrutiny, the United States Trade Representative has also opened an investigation into whether China is “harming American intellectual property rights, innovation or technology development.” One concern is that American companies have been forced to hand over technology, create joint ventures and otherwise help homegrown players, in exchange for access to the Chinese market. Qualcomm, for example, has been working with the Chinese government to develop drones, artificial intelligence and mobile technology.
除審查外，美國貿易代表辦公室(United States Trade Representative)還對中國是否「損害美國的知識產權、創新或技術發展」展開了調查。該機構的一個擔心是美國公司被迫交出技術、創辦合資企業或通過其他方式幫助中國本土企業，以換取進入中國市場的機會。比如，高通一直在與中國政府合作開發無人機、人工智慧和移動技術。
Technology companies are stuck in the middle of the fight between the United States and China. While there are concerns about Chinese encroachment, the industry also recognizes that such deals are the price for entry to the world’s second-largest economy. Companies have protested the proposed changes to Cfius, saying an expansion of its powers could be misused and that the new definitions of emerging technologies are unclear.
科技公司被夾在了美國和中國的較量中間。儘管存在對中國入侵的擔憂，但美國科技業也認識到，此類交易是進入世界第二大經濟體的代價。一些公司抗議Cfius的擬定改革，稱它的擴權可能會被濫用，並且對新興技術的新定義尚不明確。
IBM has said the bill would limit the “ability of American firms to do business abroad while empowering foreign competitors to capture global markets.” The tech trade group, the Information Technology Industry Council, has lobbied against the changes, saying they add complexity for Silicon Valley companies that often have intricate business ties in China.
IBM稱該議案會限制「美國公司在海外做生意的能力，同時授權外國競爭對手佔領全球市場」。行業組織信息技術產業協會(Information Technology Industry Council)遊說反對改革，稱它們會給矽谷公司增加難題。後者在中國的業務關係通常都錯綜複雜。
廣告
“They are between a rock and a hard place,” said Rob Atkinson, president of the Information Technology and Innovation Foundation, a think tank that is sponsored by technology firms like Microsoft. “The Chinese government says you have to do this to operate here. The U.S. says, but you can’t do that.”
「它們處境兩難，」由微軟(Microsoft)等科技公司贊助的智庫信息技術創新基金會(Information Technology and Innovation Foundation)會長羅布·阿特金森(Rob Atkinson)說。「中國政府說必須這麼做才能在這裡開展業務。美國說不能這麼做。」
