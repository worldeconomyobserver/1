中行二季度利潤增速創三年新低

Bank of China Profit Grows at Slowest Pace in 3 Years

NEIL GOUGH
2012年8月24日
HONG KONG - Profit at Bank of China, one of the country's biggest lenders, grew at the slowest pace in three years in the second quarter, weighed down by a slowing Chinese economy and increased competition.
香港——在中國經濟增速放緩和競爭不斷加劇的作用下，中國最大的貸款機構之一中國銀行(Bank of China)第二季度的利潤增速出現了三年來的最低水平。
Bank of China, the first of the country's big state-owned lenders to report results, said on Thursday that net income during the April-June quarter rose 5.2 percent, to 34.8 billion renminbi ($5.48 billion), from the period a year earlier.
中國銀行是第一家公布業績的國有大銀行。周四，該行公布，4至6月第二季度的凈利潤為348億人民幣（54.8億美元），同比增長 5.2%。 
The performance was in line with analysts' forecasts, but it was the bank's slowest growth in quarterly profit since the first quarter of 2009. For the first six months, profit rose 7.6 percent, to 71.6 billion renminbi, from the year-ago period.
此業績表現和分析人士的預測一致，但卻是自2009年第一季度以來該銀行季度利潤增速的最低值。今年上半年，該銀行利潤總計716億人民幣，同比增長7.6% 。
China's top lenders are among the biggest banks in the world by assets and profit, but they are increasingly competing for a slower growing pool of deposits at home. New measures to partially free up China's tightly regulated borrowing and lending rates have also put pressure on the banks' traditional reliance on interest income, leading them to seek alternative and often riskier ways to increase profit.
以資產和利潤而論，中國各大銀行在世界上的排名十分靠前，然而由於中國國內存款增速持續減緩，它們之間的競爭日益增大。中國政府在一定程度上放開中國存貸利率管制的新措施，也給傳統上依賴利率收入獲益的銀行帶了壓力，迫使它們尋找提高利潤的其他途徑，這些途徑往往風險較大。
"This more competitive environment provides banks with both freedom and increased responsibility," Joyce Poon, an analyst at GaveKal Dragonomics, wrote last month in a research note. "In the short term, their profits will be squeezed due to the compression in lending margins. With credit creation set to become far more market driven, Chinese lending cycles, and by extension economic cycles, will likely get much more volatile."
“在競爭性更強的環境下，銀行有了更多的自由，同時也要承擔更多責任，”龍洲經訊(GaveKal Dragonomics)的分析師喬伊斯·蓬(Joyce Poon)上月在一份研究報告中寫道。“短期來看，由於貸款利潤率下降，它們的利潤空間將受到擠壓。而隨着貸款業務更多地受市場支配，中國的借貸周期，以及與之相關聯的經濟周期，不穩定的程度很可能會比以前大得多。”
Bank of China, in which the government holds a 67.6 percent stake, counts Temasek Holdings of Singapore and BlackRock as its biggest foreign shareholders. The bank said net interest income in the first half of the year rose 12.6 percent, to 124 billion renminbi, accounting for 69 percent of operating income.
中國銀行由政府控股67.6%，最大的外資股東則是新加坡的淡馬錫控股公司(Temasek Holdings)以及貝萊德公司(BlackRock)。該銀行稱其上半年的凈利息收入增長了12.6%，達到1240億人民幣，占其運營收入的69%。 
But as Beijing took steps to make interest rates more market driven, the bank's net interest margins came under pressure as its deposit-related costs rose faster than its loan-related income. In June, China cut benchmark interest rates for the first time since 2008, and made a second rate cut last month.
然而，由於北京方面推出了加大利率市場化的舉措，該銀行付息負債成本的上升速度快於生息資產收益，凈息差受到擠壓。6月，中國自2008年以來首次降低了基準利率，並在上個月再次降低該利率。
At the same time, in an effort to counter an economic slowdown this year, China's policy makers slowed the appreciation of the renminbi against the dollar. The result was that Bank of China's margins narrowed in its foreign currency business.
同時，為應對今年經濟增速放緩的狀況，中國的政策制定者放慢了人民幣對美元升值的步伐，致使中國銀行的外幣業務利潤率降低。
Bank of China, which has a slightly more diversified revenue stream than some of its rivals, is in a better position to weather changes in Beijing's fixed-interest-rate regime, analysts at JPMorgan in Hong Kong wrote last month in a research note. "That said, it suffers from lower growth and profitability, and its onshore liquidity profile is weaker than state bank peers," they wrote.
上個月，摩根大通(JPMorgan)位於香港的分析師在一份報告中寫道，和一些競爭對手相比，中國銀行的收入來源稍微多元化一些，因此就更能適應中國固定利率體系的變化。“儘管如此，它的劣勢在於增速較慢、盈利能力較弱，在岸資金流動性也不如其它國有銀行，”他們寫道。
