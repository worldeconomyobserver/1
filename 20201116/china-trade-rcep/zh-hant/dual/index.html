中國與亞太14國簽訂全球最大規模區域自貿協議

China-Led Trade Pact Is Signed, in Challenge to U.S.

KEITH BRADSHER, ANA SWANSON
2020年11月16日
BEIJING — After eight years of talks, China and 14 other nations from Japan to New Zealand to Myanmar on Sunday formally signed one of the world’s largest regional free trade agreements, a pact designed by Beijing partly as a counterweight to American influence in the region.
北京——經過八年的談判，中國與日本、紐西蘭、緬甸等14個國家週日正式簽署了世界上規模最大的區域自由貿易協議，這個由北京方面設計的協議，在一定程度上是為了平衡美國在該地區的影響力。
The pact, the Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership, or R.C.E.P., is limited in scope. Still, it carries considerable symbolic heft. The pact covers more of humanity — 2.2 billion people — than any previous regional free trade agreement and could help further cement China’s image as the dominant economic power in its neighborhood.
這個名為《區域全面經濟夥伴關係協定》（Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership，簡稱RCEP）的協議涉及範圍有限。儘管如此，它仍具有相當大的象徵意義。協定覆蓋了22億人口，比以往任何一個區域自由貿易協定覆蓋的都多，有可能幫助中國進一步鞏固其作為周邊經濟主導力量的形象。
It also comes after a retreat by the United States from sweeping trade pacts that reshape global relationships. Nearly four years ago, President Trump pulled the United States out of the Trans-Pacific Partnership, or T.P.P., a broader agreement than the R.C.E.P. that was widely seen as a Washington-led response to China’s growing sway in the Asia-Pacific region. Joseph R. Biden, the president-elect, has been noncommittal on whether he would join the T.P.P.’s successor.
這個協議也是在美國退出重塑全球關係的全面貿易協定之後簽署的。差不多四年前，川普總統讓美國退出了《跨太平洋夥伴關係協定》（Trans-Pacific Partnership，簡稱TPP），這個協定比RCEP覆蓋的範圍更廣，曾被廣泛視為華盛頓牽頭的對中國在亞太地區日益增長的影響力的回應。候任總統約瑟夫·R·拜登(Joseph R. Biden)對是否會加入接替TPP的協議一直含糊其辭。
Because of the pandemic, the signing of the agreement on Sunday was unusual, with separate ceremonies held in each of the 15 member countries all linked by video. Each country’s trade minister took turns signing a separate copy of the pact while his or her head of state or head of government stood nearby and watched.
由於新冠病毒大流行，週日簽署的協議不同尋常，15個成員國各自在本國舉行了通過影片舉辦的儀式。每個國家的貿易部長先後在各自手中的協議上簽字，該國的國家元首或政府首腦站在旁邊觀看。
廣告
Simultaneously broadcast on a split screen, the different ceremonies offered a glimpse of each country’s political culture. Vietnam, the host nation for the talks this year, and South Korea and Cambodia each had one or two small desktop flags next to their ministers. At the other extreme, China’s ceremony was conducted in front of a wall of five very large, bright red Chinese flags.
這些通過分屏同時播放的不同儀式，讓人們得以一瞥每個國家的政治文化。今年談判的主辦國越南，以及韓國和柬埔寨在各自貿易部長的書桌上擺放了一面或兩面小國旗。與之相反，中國的儀式在五面巨大的鮮紅色中國國旗前舉行。
Premier Li Keqiang, China’s second-highest official after Xi Jinping, oversaw the Beijing event. In a statement released by the state news media, he called the pact, “a victory of multilateralism and free trade.”
僅次於習近平的中國第二號官員李克強總理主持了北京的儀式。他在官方新聞媒體發表的一份聲明中稱，該協定是「多邊主義和自由貿易的勝利」。
The R.C.E.P. encompasses the 10 countries of the Association of Southeast Asian Nations plus Australia, China, Japan, New Zealand and South Korea.
RCEP包括東南亞國家聯盟（Association of Southeast Asian Nations，簡稱東盟）的10個國家，以及澳洲、中國、日本、紐西蘭和韓國。
The pact will most likely formalize, rather than remake, business between the countries. The R.C.E.P. eliminates tariffs mainly for goods that already qualify for duty-free treatment under existing free trade agreements. It allows countries to keep tariffs for imports in sectors they regard as especially important or sensitive. The pact’s so-called rules of origin will set common standards for how much of a product must be produced within the region for the final product to qualify for duty-free treatment. These rules could make it simpler for companies to set up supply chains that span several different countries.
該協定很可能使這些國家之間的貿易關係正式化，而不是重塑貿易關係。RCEP取消關稅的主要是那些在現有自由貿易協議下已符合免稅待遇的商品。協定允許各國在其認為特別重要或敏感的行業保留進口關稅。協定所謂的原產地規則，將為一種產品的多大部分必須在該區域內生產，才能讓最終產品有資格享受免稅待遇設定共同標準。這些規則可以讓公司更容易建立跨越幾個不同國家的供應鏈。
It has little impact on legal work, accounting or other services that cross borders, and does not venture far into the often-divisive issue of ensuring greater intellectual property protections. The R.C.E.P. also skirts broad issues like protecting independent labor unions and the environment and limiting government subsidies to state-owned enterprises.
協定幾乎不影響法律工作、會計或其他跨境服務行業，在確保更大程度的知識產權保護上也沒敢走多遠，這是一個往往存在分歧的問題。RCEP還迴避了一些廣泛的議題，比如保護獨立工會和環保，以及限制政府對國有企業的補貼等。
Most conspicuously, the pact does not include India, another regional giant. The New Delhi government pulled out of the negotiations in July. China had rebuffed India’s demands for a more ambitious pact that would have done far more to tie together the region’s economies, including trade in services as well as trade in goods.
最明顯的是，這個協定不包括另一個區域大國印度。新德里政府在今年7月退出了談判。印度曾要求達成一項更雄心勃勃的協定，除了包括商品貿易，也包括服務貿易，將區域經濟體更廣泛地聯繫起來。但中國拒絕了印度的要求。
廣告
He Weiwen, a former Commerce Ministry official in Beijing and prominent Chinese trade policy expert, said that Sunday’s pact nonetheless represented a big step forward.
中國商務部前官員、著名貿易政策專家何偉文說，儘管如此，週日簽署的協定仍代表著向前邁出的一大步。
“The Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership, due to its size, will certainly contribute to world free trade,” he said.
「《區域全面經濟夥伴關係協定》由於其規模之大，必定會對世界自由貿易作出貢獻，」何偉文說。
The R.C.E.P.’s lower trade barriers could encourage global companies trying to avoid Mr. Trump’s tariffs on Chinese-made goods to keep work in Asia rather than shift it to North America, said Mary Lovely, a senior fellow at the Peterson Institute for International Economics in Washington.
RCEP降低貿易壁壘的做法，可能會鼓勵試圖避開川普對中國製造商品徵收關稅的跨國公司將工作崗位留在亞洲，而不是轉移到北美，華盛頓的彼得森國際經濟研究所(Peterson Institute for International Economics)高級研究員瑪麗·洛夫裡(Mary Lovely)說。
“R.C.E.P. gives foreign companies enhanced flexibility in navigating between the two giants,” she said. “Lower tariffs within the region increases the value of operating within the Asian region, while the uniform rules of origin make it easier to pull production away from the Chinese mainland while retaining that access.”
「RCEP讓外國公司在兩大經濟體之間尋找對付複雜情況的方法時有更大的靈活性，」她說。「在區域內降低關稅提高了在亞洲地區之內運營的價值，而統一的原產地規則使得在保留准入的同時，把生產帶出中國大陸變得更容易。」
The prospect of China’s forging closer economic ties with its neighbors has prompted concern in Washington. President Barack Obama’s response was the T.P.P., which had extensive provisions on services, intellectual property, independent labor unions and environmental protection. It also called for limits on state sponsorship of industries, serving as both a challenge to China and an enticement for Beijing to relax its grip on its economy, the world’s second largest.
中國與鄰國建立更緊密經濟聯繫的前景已在華盛頓引發擔憂。美國前總統巴拉克·歐巴馬(Barack Obama)對這種前景的回應是TPP。它在服務業、知識產權、獨立工會和環境保護方面都有廣泛的規定。該協定還呼籲限制國家對產業的補貼，這對中國來說既是一種挑戰，也是一種讓北京放鬆對世界第二大經濟體經濟控制的引誘。
The T.P.P. did not include China but encompassed many of its biggest trading partners, like Japan and Australia, as well as Chinese neighbors like Vietnam and Malaysia. After President Trump pulled the United States out of that arrangement, the other 11 countries then went ahead with it on their own.
TPP不包括中國，但包括了它的許多大貿易夥伴，比如日本和澳洲，也包括了越南和馬來西亞等中國鄰國。在川普總統讓美國退出該協定後，其他11個國家自行簽署了該協定。
廣告
China has been eager to move into that vacuum. Still, it must navigate India’s ambitions. India’s relations with China have deteriorated considerably in recent months amid clashes between troops on their mountainous shared border.
中國一直渴望進入美國留下的真空地帶。儘管如此，中國必須在對付印度的野心上找到正確方向。中印關係近幾個月來嚴重惡化，兩國的軍隊在位於高原地區的共同邊境多次發生衝突。
Beijing had initially tried to sway New Delhi into joining the R.C.E.P. However, Indian politicians were leery of lowering their country’s steep tariffs and admitting a further flood of Chinese manufactured goods. China ships $60 billion a year more in goods to India than it receives.
北京最初曾試圖說服新德里加入RCEP。但印度政界人士極力避免降低該國的高關稅，允許中國製成品進一步湧入印度。中國每年向印度出口商品的總額比從印度進口的高出600億美元。
India sought more flexibility to increase tariffs if imports surged. It also sought tariff reductions for low-end, labor-intensive industrial goods for which production has already been moving out of China. But Beijing has been wary of letting high-employment industries like shoe and shirt manufacturing move out of China too quickly.
印度曾尋求在中國進口激增的情況下對提高關稅有更多靈活性，還曾尋求降低低端勞動密集型工業產品的關稅，這些產品的生產已在轉移出中國。但北京一直對讓高就業行業，比如鞋和襯衫的生產過快離開中國持謹慎態度。
“As far as India is concerned, we did not join R.C.E.P. as it does not address the outstanding issues and concerns of India,” said Riva Ganguly Das, the secretary for Eastern relations at India’s Ministry of External Affairs, at a news briefing on Thursday.
「就印度而言，我們沒有加入RCEP，因為它沒有解決對印度來說重要的問題和擔憂，」印度外交部負責東部事物的秘書裡瓦·甘古利·達斯(Riva Ganguly Das)在週四的記者會上說。
Still, Ms. Das stressed that India remains interested in deepening trade ties in Southeast Asia.
儘管如此，達斯強調，印度仍對加深東南亞貿易關係保持興趣。
It is unclear how the United States will respond to the new trade pact. While Mr. Biden is set to assume office in January, trade and China have become fraught issues.
目前還不清楚美國將如何回應這項新的貿易協定。拜登將於明年1月就職，貿易和中國已成為困難重重的問題。
廣告
The T.P.P. came under fire from both Republicans and Democrats for exposing American businesses to foreign competition. It remains contentious, and Mr. Biden has not said whether he would rejoin the deal — renamed the Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership — once he enters office. But analysts say it is unlikely to be a high priority.
TPP曾受到共和黨和民主黨兩黨的猛烈抨擊，因為它讓美國企業面臨外國競爭。該協定仍充滿爭議，拜登還沒有表示他就職後，是否會重新加入已被重新命名為《全面與進步跨太平洋夥伴關係協定》(Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership)的協議。但分析人士說，這不太可能是優先事項。
Mr. Biden has said he would wait to negotiate any new trade deals. He wants to focus his energy on the pandemic, the economic recovery and investing in American manufacturing and technology.
拜登曾說，他將推遲任何新貿易協議的談判。他希望把精力集中在控制疫情、復甦經濟，以及投資美國製造業和技術上。
But to some trade experts, the signing of the R.C.E.P. shows that the rest of the world will not wait around for the United States. The European Union has also pursued trade negotiations at an aggressive pace. As other countries sign new deals, American exporters may gradually lose ground.
但在一些貿易專家看來，RCEP的簽署表明，世界上的其他國家不會等美國。歐盟也在加快步伐進行貿易談判。隨著其他國家簽署新的協議，美國出口商可能會逐漸失去市場。
“While the United States is currently focused on domestic concerns, including the need to fight the pandemic and rebuild its economy and infrastructure, I’m not sure the rest of the world is going to wait until America gets its house in order,” said Jennifer Hillman, a senior fellow for trade and international political economy at the Council on Foreign Relations. “I think there are going to have to be some responsive actions to what China is doing.”
「儘管美國目前的主要焦點是國內問題，包括需要遏制新冠病毒大流行和重建經濟和基礎設施，但我不敢擔保世界上的其他國家會等美國理順了國內事務後再說，」對外關係委員會(Council on Foreign Relations)貿易和國際政治經濟高級研究員珍妮佛·希爾曼(Jennifer Hillman)說。「我認為，必須對中國正在做的事情做出某種回應。」
