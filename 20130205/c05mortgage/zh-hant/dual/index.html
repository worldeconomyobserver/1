標普被控誇大房貸衍生投資評級

U.S. Accuses S.&P. of Fraud in Suit on Loan Bundles

MARY WILLIAMS WALSH, ANDREW ROSS SORKIN
2013年2月5日
The Justice Department late Monday filed civil fraud charges against the nation’s largest credit-ratings agency, Standard & Poor’s, accusing the firm of inflating the ratings of mortgage investments and setting them up for a crash when the financial crisis struck.
美國司法部在周一晚些時候向美國最大的信用評級機構標準普爾(Standard & Poor’s)提起民事欺詐指控，控告該公司誇大了住房貸款衍生投資的信用評級，使它們在金融危機來襲之際發生崩潰。
The suit, filed in federal court in Los Angeles, is the first significant federal action against the ratings industry, which during the boom years reaped record profits as it bestowed gilt-edged ratings on complex bundles of home loans that quickly went sour. The high ratings made many investments appear safer than they actually were, and are now seen as having contributed to a crisis that brought the financial system and the broader economy to its knees.
這起訴訟是在位於洛杉磯的聯邦法院提起的，這是聯邦政府對評級行業採取的首次重大行動。在經濟繁榮時期，評級行業對將住房貸款進行捆綁的結構複雜的衍生投資產品給予了很高的評級，因而收穫了空前的利潤，但那些貸款很快就出了問題。過高的評級讓很多投資看起來比真實情況更安全，現在看來，這對金融危機的發生起到了推動。危機對金融體系和宏觀經濟都造成了極大的破壞。
More than a dozen state prosecutors are expected to join the federal suit, and the New York attorney general is preparing a separate action. The Securities and Exchange Commission has also been investigating possible wrongdoing at S.& P.
屆時將有十多個州的檢察官參與聯邦訴訟，紐約州檢察長還在準備單獨行動。美國證券交易委員會(Securities and Exchange Commission)也在對標準普爾可能存在的過錯進行調查。
Settlement talks between S.& P. and the Justice Department broke down in the last two weeks after prosecutors sought a penalty in excess of $1 billion and insisted that the company admit wrongdoing, several people with knowledge of the talks said. That amount would wipe out the profits of S.& P.’s parent, the McGraw-Hill Companies, for an entire year. In one session with the government, S.& P. had proposed a settlement of around $100 million, the people said.
過去兩周標準普爾和司法部的和解談判最後以破裂告終。幾名了解談判的知情人士稱，談判破裂前公訴人希望標準普爾繳納超過10億美元（約合62.85億元人民幣）的罰款，並堅持讓該公司承認存在過錯。這個金額將花掉標準普爾的母公司麥格希集團(McGraw-Hill Companies)整整一年的利潤。這些知情人士說，在與政府的一次談判中，標準普爾曾提出以1億美元左右的金額進行和解。
S.& P., which was first contacted by federal enforcement officials three years ago, said in a statement Monday in anticipation of the suit that it had acted in good faith when it issued the ratings.
聯邦執法官員第一次聯繫標準普爾是在三年前。後者在周一訴訟提出之前發表的聲明中表示，它在發佈評級結果時一向誠實。
“A D.O.J. lawsuit would be entirely without factual or legal merit,” it said, adding that its competitors had given exactly the same ratings to all the securities it believed to be in question.
聲明中稱，“司法部的訴訟完全沒有事實或法律依據。”標準普爾還說，對於該公司認為案件涉及的證券，競爭對手也給予了完全相同的評級。
It was unclear whether state and federal authorities were targeting the other two major ratings agencies, Moody’s and Fitch.
州政府與聯邦政府是否會針對另外兩個主要評級機構穆迪(Moody’s)和惠譽(Fitch)採取行動，目前尚不明確。
During the settlement talks, S.& P. sought a deal that would allow it to neither admit nor deny guilt; the government pressed for an admission of guilt to at least one count of fraud, said the people who did not want to be named because the suit had not been filed. The company told prosecutors it could not admit guilt without exposing itself to liability in a multitude of civil cases.
在和解談判過程中，標準普爾希望能夠達成一項能讓它既不承認也不否認罪行的協議，而政府則迫使它至少承認一項欺詐罪名，由於當時訴訟還未提出，透露上述消息的人要求匿名。標準普爾告訴檢察官，一旦認罪，就會承擔大量民事訴訟的責任。
The case is said to focus on about 30 collateralized debt obligations, or C.D.O.’s, an exotic type of security made up of bundles of mortgage bonds, which in turn were composed of individual home loans. According to S.& P., the mortgage securities were created in 2007, at the height of the housing boom. S.& P. was paid fees of about $13 million for rating them.
據稱此案關注的是30種債務抵押債券（collateralized debt obligations，簡稱CDO），這種特殊的證券是以按揭債券捆綁而成的，而後者又是由個人住房貸款組成的。標準普爾說，住房貸款抵押證券產生於2007年，當時正值房產市場最繁榮的時期。標準普爾通過對這些證券進行評級，獲得了1300萬美元的收入。
Prosecutors, according to the people briefed on the discussions, have uncovered troves of e-mails written by S.& P. employees, which the government considers damaging. The firm gave the government more than 20 million pages of e-mails as part of its investigation, the people with knowledge of the process said.
對談判有所了解的人士稱，公訴人獲得了大量出自標準普爾員工之手的電子郵件，政府認為這些郵件不利於標準普爾。了解案件調查過程的人說，作為調查的一部分，公司向政府提供了2000多萬頁的電子郵件記錄。
Since the financial crisis in 2008, the ratings agencies’ business practices have been widely criticized and questions have been raised as to whether independent analysis was corrupted by Wall Street’s push for profits.
自從2008年金融危機以來，評級機構的業務行為受到了廣泛批評，人們開始質疑，華爾街對利潤的急迫追求是否有損獨立的分析。
“Rating agencies continue to create an even bigger monster — the C.D.O. market,” one S.& P. employee wrote in an internal e-mail in December 2006. “Let’s hope we are all wealthy and retired by the time this house of card falters.”
“評級機構在繼續創造一個更加龐大的怪物，也就是CDO市場，”一名標準普爾員工在2006年12月的一封內部郵件中寫道，“希望在這個搖搖欲墜的體系倒塌之前，我們都能發財、退休。”
Another S.& P. employee wrote in an instant message the following April, according to the Senate report: “We rate every deal. It could be structured by cows and we would rate it.”
根據參議院的報告，另一名標準普爾員工在2007年4月的一條即時消息中寫道：“每筆交易我們都會評級。即便交易結構是蠢驢設計的，我們也肯為它評級。”
The three major ratings agencies are typically paid by the issuers of the securities they rate — in this case, the banks that had packaged the mortgage-backed securities and wanted to market them. The investors who would buy the securities were not involved in the process but depended on the rating agencies’ assessments.
付費給這三家主要評級機構的通常是所評證券的發行者，在本案中，就是那些打包住房貸款抵押證券並希望能把它們賣出去的銀行。購買這些證券的投資者並沒有參與評級過程，但卻需要依賴評級機構的評價。
Although the three agencies tend to track each other, each has its own statistical methods for assessing the likelihood of a bond default. That has led to speculation that S.& P. analysts knew their method yielded unrealistic ratings, but issued the ratings anyway.
雖然這三家機構傾向於相互追隨，但每家機構都擁有自己的統計方法來評估某種債券違約的可能性。可以由此推測，標準普爾的分析師知道他們的方法得出了不真實的評級結果，但還是發佈了這些結果。
In its statement on Monday, S.& P. said it had sent out early warning signals, downgrading hundreds of mortgage-backed securities, starting in 2006. Nor was it the only one to have underestimated the coming crisis, it said — even the Federal Reserve’s open market committee had believed at the time that any problems within the housing sector could be contained.
標準普爾在周一的聲明中說，它早就發出了預警信號，從2006年開始下調了數百隻住房貸款抵押證券的信用評級。標準普爾說，況且它也並不是唯一一家低估了即將到來的風險的機構，就連美聯儲(Federal Reserve)的公開市場委員會當時都以為，房地產行業無論出了什麼問題，都能夠得到控制。
