巴克萊醜聞後LIBOR何去何從

Banks’ Ability to Rig Rates Shows Need for Change

FLOYD NORRIS
2012年7月12日
Is Libor — the London interbank offered rate — an idea whose time has passed? And if so, what can replace it?
倫敦同業拆借利率(Libor — the London interbank offered rate)這一概念是否已經過時？如果是的話，應該由什麼來替代？
The scandal that forced Robert E. Diamond Jr. to resign as chief executive of Barclays Bank has revealed that it — and apparently other banks as well — lied during the financial crisis about what it would cost them to borrow unsecured money. They did that to avoid the appearance that the quality of their credit was in question. It appears that bank regulators knew what was going on and did nothing to stop it.
巴克萊銀行(Barclays)的首席執行官小羅伯特·E·戴蒙德(Robert E. Diamond Jr.)已經迫於壓力而辭職。關於他的醜聞表明，巴克萊銀行，顯然還有其他銀行，在金融危機期間對無擔保貸款的代價撒了謊。它們那麼做是為了避免讓人覺得銀行信貸質量已成問題。似乎銀行監管機構了解狀況，卻沒有採取任何措施制止。
But Barclays’ settlements with regulators in the United States and Britain, including the Commodity Futures Trading Commission in the United States, which led the investigation, revealed that the bank was lying well before the crisis. Then, it was submitting false numbers because the bank had taken speculative positions in derivatives that would profit if the number moved in the desired direction.
巴克萊銀行與美國和英國的監管機構達成了和解，這些監管機構包括主持此次調查的美國商品期貨交易委員會(Commodity Futures Trading Commission)。 而它們的和解表明，該銀行在金融危機發生之前就在撒謊。由於銀行在衍生品市場採取了投機頭寸，如果利率能夠向有利的方向變動，則衍生產品就能獲利，所以銀行提交了造假後的數據。
Libor numbers are released each day by the British Bankers’ Association, covering 10 currencies and 15 different maturities. More than $10 trillion in loans to businesses and consumers have interest rates based on Libor, with the borrowers paying more or less depending on where Libor is on a particular day. Many derivative contracts are also based on it.
Libor數據由英國銀行家協會(British Bankers’ Association)每日發佈，涵蓋了10種貨幣和15種不同期限的利率。逾10萬億的商業和消費者貸款利率都基於Libor利率，借貸者差不多都依照當日的Libor利率進行支付。很多衍生工具合約也基於Libor利率。
The bankers’ association collects the rates at which each bank says it could have borrowed money that day without putting up any security for the loan. The highest and lowest responses are thrown out, and the rest are averaged to produce a number. There is no need for any bank to show that it actually borrowed at the rate it claims.
銀行家協會把每家銀行當日無擔保借款的利率收集起來， 然後去除最高利率和最低利率得出平均值。銀行無需表明它真的是按照提交的利率進行借貸的。
The original idea was to have a market-based rate that amounted to a virtually risk-free private sector rate on which to base rates charged on other loans. But whatever sense that once made, it seems foolish now.
最初的理念是尋求一個基於市場的利率，以達到幾乎無風險的私人部門利率，從而為其他借貸提供利率基礎。但不管這一理念當初如何有理，它現在看來實為愚蠢。
“The funding model that European banks have relied on since the adoption of the euro is largely broken,” a report by Larry Cofsky of Bridgewater Associates, a sponsor of hedge funds, said this week. “The unsecured bank market outside very short maturities” of up to a week, he added, “is now largely by appointment,” and even secured funding is harder to obtain.
“自採用歐元以來，歐盟銀行所依賴的融資模式大體已經破產，” 來自對沖基金布里奇沃特投資公司(Bridgewater Associates)的拉里·科夫斯基(Larry Cofsky)在這周的報告中說。他又說道，“對一周短期限之外的無擔保銀行市場，現在大部分只能通過預約。”就連擔保融資也比以前更加難以獲得。
Once banks were viewed as safe because their governments would bail them out if necessary. Now, some of those same governments are in financial distress, able to borrow money only because those same banks will buy government bonds with money they borrow from the European Central Bank.
銀行有一度被認為是安全的，因為它們的政府會在必要時進行紓困。現在，這些政府中的一部分連自己都身處財政困境之中，政府之所以能夠借錢只是因為這些銀行利用從歐洲中央銀行借來的錢購買政府債券。
Before Libor, many loan rates in the United States fluctuated based on the prime rates charged by banks. That was supposedly the rate banks charged their best corporate customers, but over time banks discovered that it could be used as a powerful marketing tool. They began offering home equity loans at prime, or even lower. Borrowers clamored for a market-based rate that banks could not manipulate so easily, and flocked to Libor-based loans.
在Libor利率之前，美國的很多貸款利率在銀行最優惠利率的基礎上浮動。這應該是銀行對最好的企業客戶收取的利率。漸漸地，銀行發現可以將其作為一種強大的營銷工具加以利用，於是銀行開始為住房權益貸款提供最優惠利率，甚至是更低的利率。借貸者亟需基於市場而不被銀行輕易操縱的利率，於是一窩蜂地朝着基於Libor的貸款而去。
Now we know that banks could, and did, manipulate Libor rates.
如今，我們知道銀行能夠，而且確實也對Libor利率進行了操縱。
It is interesting to note that even before the financial crisis, the manipulation was not, as one might expect from a lender, always in the direction of higher rates. The trading department at Barclays was rigging the report, and sometimes its trading positions called for lower rates, even though that might reduce the interest income received by the bank.
很有意思的是，即使在金融危機之前，操縱也不是像所想的那樣，總是朝着更高利率的方向進行。巴克萊銀行的交易部門篡改了報告，有時候它的交易頭寸需要較低的利率，儘管這樣會減少銀行所得的利息收益。
The accompanying chart looks at the market for credit-default swaps on debts issued by 15 international banks that are among the banks that set Libor. In early 2008, all of the banks were viewed as safe, and there was little difference in the cost of buying insurance on any of them. But since then, the costs have risen and become volatile. Last fall, the cost of insurance for debt issued by some banks rose to levels higher than it had been during the financial crisis.
附帶的圖表顯示的是債務信用違約互換的市場，由15家國際銀行發佈，它們都是制定Libor利率的銀行。在2008年初期，所有的這些銀行都被視為安全，而且在這些銀行中購買保險幾乎沒有價格差異。但是從那以後，價格開始上漲且劇烈波動。去年秋季，其中一些銀行發佈的債務保險價格已經飆升到金融危機時期的水平之上。
The charts show that at the end of last month, the annual premium for insuring $10 million in debt was under $100,000 for only one of the banks, Japan’s Norinchukin. The cost was over $200,000 for five of the banks. At the beginning of 2008, the rate was under $100,000 for all of them.
圖表表明，上月月末，只有一家銀行的1000萬美元債務的年保險費低於10萬美元，即日本的農林中央金庫(Norinchukin)。有五家銀行的此價格高達20萬美元以上。2008年初，所有銀行的價格都低於10萬美元。
Even assuming that accurate Libor numbers could be produced, the resulting rate would reflect far more risk than it did a few years ago. And that means borrowers would be paying higher rates than they would pay if banks were in better shape.
即使假設能夠生成準確的Libor數據，由此產生的利率也會比前幾年反映出更多風險。這說明，借方支付的利率會比銀行狀態尚好的時候的利率要高。
It is not clear what can be done about that, but one step may be to take into account the prices of credit-default swaps when setting the Libor rate, perhaps by considering rates paid only by banks that the swap market clearly views as safe.
還不清楚有何應對措施，但有一步可以走，那就是在制定Libor利率時考慮信用違約互換的價格，或許可以只考慮被互換市場視為安全的銀行支付的利率。
The Libor market also needs regulation — and not by traditional bank regulators. It is no accident that it was the commodity futures commission, a market regulator, whose investigation revealed the abuses.
Libor市場也需要規範，但不是由傳統銀行監管機構去規範。正是商品期貨交易委員會這一市場監管機構的調查揭開了黑幕，這絕不是偶然。
Bank regulators face serious conflicts when banks are under stress. They view their prime goal as keeping the banks healthy, or at least appearing to be healthy so as to not scare off customers and make a difficult situation worse. That has led them to put pressure on accounting rule makers to go easy on forcing banks to confess losses, and it seems to have led to a willingness to accept manipulation of the Libor market as a way to obscure the difficulties banks faced during the financial crisis.
當銀行受到壓力之時，銀行監管機構也面臨著嚴重的矛盾。銀行監管機構認為它們的的首要任務是確保銀行是健康的，或至少是讓銀行在表面上是健康的，這樣才不至於嚇走了客戶，把困境變得更糟。這就導致監管機構施壓於會計準則的制定者，讓他們不要嚴格迫使銀行報告損失，這似乎是默許了Libor市場的操縱，以便掩蓋銀行在金融危機時期面臨的困難。
