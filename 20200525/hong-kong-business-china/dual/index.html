新国安法动摇香港经济地位

China’s Tighter Grip on Hong Kong Shakes City’s Business World

艾莎
2020年5月25日
Sign up for NYT Chinese-language Morning Briefing.
（欢迎点击此处订阅NYT简报，我们将在每个工作日发送最新内容至您的邮箱。）
HONG KONG — China’s desire to take a stronger hand in running Hong Kong has imperiled its status as Asia’s financial capital, sending its stock market into its sharpest plunge in five years and spurring predictions that money and business could soon leave the former British colony.
香港——中国渴望以更强硬的方式掌管香港，这一目标已经削弱了这座城市作为亚洲金融中心的地位，使其股市经历五年来最严重的下跌，并令人猜测资金和业务可能很快会离开这座前英国殖民地。
The threat does not come just from Beijing, which late on Thursday outlined its plan to bypass Hong Kong leaders and enact national security laws affecting the territory of roughly seven million people. That move puts Hong Kong squarely in the middle of the growing conflict between China and the United States, which is increasingly challenging Beijing on a number of fronts and could retaliate in ways that hobble its appeal as a place to do business.
北京上周四晚公布了绕过香港领导人实施国安法的计划，将对这片人口约700万的半自治领土产生影响，但北京并非唯一的威胁来源。这一举动将香港置于中美之间日益加剧的冲突之中，北京在许多方面面临越来越多来自美国的挑战，可能采取的报复行动将削弱香港作为商业枢纽的吸引力。
Businesses and investors worry that a bruising clash between the superpowers could put an end to Hong Kong’s enviable position as a bridge between China’s powerful economic engine and the rest of the world.
企业和投资者担心，超级大国之间的激烈冲突，可能会终结作为中国强大经济引擎的香港与世界之间的令人羡慕的桥梁地位。
广告
“No matter what, they need to maintain Hong Kong’s unique status,” said Fred Hu, a prominent investor and former chairman of Goldman Sachs’s Greater China business. His investment firm, Primavera Capital Group, has put billions of dollars in investments into China over the years.
前高盛(Goldman Sachs)大中华区主席、著名投资人胡祖六(Fred Hu)表示：“无论如何，他们都需要保持香港的独特地位。”多年来，他的投资公司春华资本集团(Primavera Capital Group)已在中国投资了数十亿美元。
“It’s not only crucial for Hong Kong’s future, but also really for China as a whole, in today’s highly uncertain and volatile political climate,” he added.
他还说：“在当今高度不确定和动荡的政治环境中，这不仅对香港的未来至关重要，而且对整个中国都至关重要。”
Investors expressed their fears in no uncertain terms. Hong Kong’s stock market tumbled 5.6 percent on Friday. Futures trading in the Hong Kong dollar, which is pegged to value of the American dollar, indicated that investors were expecting money to flood out of the territory.
投资者毫无保留地表达了他们的担忧。周五香港股市下跌了5.6％。与美元汇率挂钩的港元期货交易表明，投资者预计资金会涌出香港。
At stake is Hong Kong’s status as a semiautonomous Chinese city with an independent judiciary, guarantees of free expression and assembly, loose business regulation, and low financial and trade barriers with much of the world.
香港是拥有半自治地位的中国城市，拥有独立的司法机构，保证言论自由和集会自由，拥有宽松的商业法规以及与世界许多地区有较低的金融和贸易壁垒。这些都将面临风险。
Though it benefits from China’s wealth, Hong Kong operates outside the authoritarian mainland, which offers none of those attributes. It is home to the regional headquarters of major global and Chinese companies alike.
尽管得益于中国的财富，但香港在不具备这些特征的中国大陆威权政府之外运作。它是大型跨国公司和中国公司区域总部的所在地。
Anti-Beijing protests last year, which led to pitched battles between protesters and the police and sometimes filled the central financial district with fires and tear gas, raised questions about how long Hong Kong could remain appealing as a place to do business. Beijing’s move on Thursday gave those questions new urgency.
去年的反北京抗议活动导致抗议者与警察之间展开激烈冲突，有时甚至在中央金融区放火和释放催泪瓦斯，这令人质疑香港的商业吸引力还能保持多久。北京周四的举动，令这些问题愈发显得紧迫。
广告
Brushing aside local leaders, Chinese officials announced that they planned to proceed with sweeping security measures for Hong Kong that could take effect this year. The officials did not disclose details.
中国官员无视地方领导人的存在，宣布他们计划对香港采取全面的安全措施，该措施可能于今年生效。官员们没有透露细节。
The sudden and unexpected move by Beijing has prompted fears that the Chinese Communist Party is willing to overstep freedoms that were promised to Hong Kong when Britain handed it back to China in 1997.
北京出人意料的突然举动令人们担心，中共想要打破1997年英国交还香港时对其承诺的自由。
It raised questions about whether businesspeople could fall afoul of China’s traditionally broad definition of national security. Could banks break the law by publishing skeptical research about Chinese economic data or state-run companies? Could crimes considered ordinary elsewhere be considered national security threats, to be tried by secretive mainland courts?
这让人不禁要问，商人是否会触犯定义一向宽泛的中国国家安全。银行发布怀疑中国经济数据或国有企业的研究，是否会违反法律？其他国家视为普通的犯罪，是否会被视为国家安全威胁，由大陆法院秘密审判？
“How tightly would legislation be drafted to avoid ambiguity for business?” said Tara Joseph, the president of the American Chamber of Commerce in Hong Kong. “Would people who seem to be breaking national security law be tried in Hong Kong or in mainland China? Over all, this raises the risk factor for Hong Kong.”
“立法的起草要有多严格，才能避免让商界感到含混不明？”香港美国商会会长早泰娜(Tara Joseph)说。“那些看起来违反了国安法的人，会在香港还是中国大陆受到审判？总体而言，这增加了香港的风险因素。”
Hong Kong officials moved quickly to assuage concerns. Carrie Lam, the territory’s chief executive, said the security law would target “acts of secession, subverting state power and organizing and carrying out terrorist activities,” blights that she said the business sector had been “worrying about over the past year.”
香港官员迅速采取行动以缓解担忧。香港行政长官林郑月娥(Carrie Lam)表示，《国安法》将针对“分裂、颠覆国家政权的行为以及组织和开展恐怖活动”。她说，这些是工商界“过去一年一直在担心的”。
Leung Chun-ying, a former chief executive of Hong Kong who is now a top Chinese adviser, said the national security measures were in the best interest of both Hong Kong society and investors.
前香港行政长官、现为中国政府高级顾问的梁振英说，国安法措施符合香港社会和投资者的最大利益。
广告
“With such a law, it does not hinder foreign investors from investing locally, nor does it hinder the freedom enjoyed by local residents according to law,” Mr. Leung said in an interview with Chinese state media.
梁振英在接受中国官方媒体采访时说：“有了这样的法律，不妨碍外国投资者在当地投资，不妨碍当地居民依法享有的自由。”
Hours after Beijing unveiled its plans, American lawmakers proposed legislation that would target Chinese officials and entities that trampled on Hong Kong’s semiautonomous status. A bill put forward by Senators Chris Van Hollen, Democrat of Maryland, and Patrick J. Toomey, Republican of Pennsylvania, would expose global banks to sanctions if they did business with entities that did the same.
北京公布其计划数小时后，美国立法者提出了针对践踏香港半自治地位的中国官员和实体的立法。马里兰州民主党参议员克里斯·范霍伦(Chris Van Hollen)和宾夕法尼亚州共和党参议员帕特里克·J·图米(Patrick J. Toomey)提出一项法案，如果全球银行与同样践踏自由的实体开展业务，该法案将使它们受到制裁。
“In many ways, Hong Kong is the canary in the coal mine for Asia,” Mr. Toomey said in an emailed statement. “Beijing’s growing interference could have a chilling effect on other nations struggling for freedom in China’s shadow.”
“在很多方面，对于亚洲来说，香港就是煤矿中的金丝雀，”图米在一封电子邮件声明中说。“北京日益增加的干预，可能会对其他在中国阴影下争取自由的国家产生寒蝉效应。”
The State Department has delayed its annual report to Congress assessing the special status that it awards to Hong Kong while it waits to see details on Beijing’s new proposed security law in the coming days.
美国国务院正在等待在未来几天内看到北京新拟议的国安法的细节，推迟了向国会提交评估香港特殊地位的年度报告。
If the United States decided that Hong Kong’s relative autonomy was coming to an end, President Trump could take away certain privileges it grants the territory.
如果美国认定香港的相对自治即将终结，特朗普总统可能剥夺其给予香港的某些特权。
Tens of billions of dollars in trade would suddenly be subject to tariffs and included in the tit-for-tat trade war between Washington and Beijing. American officials could curtail travel from Hong Kong and make it harder for its people to get American work visas. Hong Kong would also have a harder time buying technology deemed sensitive by Washington. The convertibility of Hong Kong’s currency — which is freely exchanged with the American dollar — could also be at stake.
数百亿美元的贸易突然会被加征关税，卷入华盛顿与北京之间你来我往的贸易战中。美国官员可能会限制来自香港的旅行，并使香港人更难获得美国工作签证。香港也将很难购买华盛顿认为敏感的技术。目前可与美元自由兑换的香港货币的可兑换性也将受到威胁。
“If they did abolish it, they are saying goodbye to Hong Kong,” said Ms. Joseph, of the chamber of commerce.
商会的早泰娜说：“如果他们的确要废除这些特权，那就是在向香港说拜拜。”
广告
Some are already saying goodbye. Last summer, money began to flow out of the city and into Singapore, a regional safe haven.
有些人已经在道别。去年夏天，资金开始流出香港，转向该地区的避风港新加坡。
The money appears to have started to flow out again this year. As of March, foreign currency deposits at both domestic and international banks operating in Singapore had nearly doubled since July, totaling $15 billion. The data does not say where the money is coming from, but economists point to the doubts over Hong Kong.
资金在今年似乎开始再次流出。截至3月，自7月以来在新加坡运营的国内和国际银行的外币存款几乎翻了一番，总计150亿美元。数据没有表明资金来源，但经济学家认为与香港的不确定性有关。
“Increasingly these concerns are seeping into business decisions,” said William Kaye, a longtime investor in China and founder of Pacific Group, the investment firm. “What is just a trickle could become a flood of capital out of Hong Kong.”
“这些担忧越来越多地渗入商业决策中，”在中国投资多年的太平洋集团(Pacific Group)创始人威廉·凯(William Kaye)说。“流出香港的资金可能会滴水成河。”

艾莎(Alexandra Stevenson)是时报驻香港商业记者，报道中国企业巨头、跨国公司以及中国在亚洲日益增长的经济和金融影响力。欢迎在Twitter和Facebook上关注她。
