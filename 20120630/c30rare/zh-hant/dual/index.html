中美日歐稀土之爭升級

U.S., Others Escalate Rare-Earth Dispute With China

PAUL GEITNER
2012年6月30日

BRUSSEL — The European Union, the United States and Japan ramped up their dispute with China over its restrictions on exports of rare earth metals Wednesday after failing to resolve the case through negotiations at the World Trade Organization. 

布魯塞爾——周三，在歐盟、美國和日本與中國未能通過世貿組織磋商解決問題的情況下，有關中國限制稀土資源出口的爭端愈演愈烈。

The European trade commissioner, Karel De Gucht, said the EU and its partners in the case had asked for a dispute settlement panel to be formed in the wake of inconclusive talks with China, which were held after the complaint was filed in March. 

歐盟貿易專員卡雷爾·德古赫特(Karel De Gucht)說，在與中國談判無果後，歐盟及其夥伴已要求成立解決爭端的專家組。前述談判的由來是今年三月，美國、歐盟和日本向世貿組織提起了相關投訴。

De Gucht noted that an appeals tribunal at the WTO in January upheld a ruling from last year in a related case, in which China was told to dismantle export duties and quotas on nine industrial raw materials, including bauxite. 

德古赫特指出，今年一月份世貿組織的上訴機構維持了去年對一件相關案例的裁決，要求中國及時取消九種工業原材料的出口稅和配額，包括鋁土礦。

The current complaint involves restrictions on rare earths, tungsten and molybdenum, which were imposed by China in 2006 and tightened since then. 

當前的投訴涉及稀土、鎢礦和鉬礦，中國從2006年起實施並逐步加強了針對前述資源的出口限制。
廣告

"Despite the very clear WTO ruling earlier this year in the first raw materials case, Beijing has not taken steps to remove these export restrictions," De Gucht said in a statement. "We regret that we are left with no other choice but to solve this through litigation."

“今年早些時候，世貿組織對第一宗原料爭端的裁決可謂十分清楚，儘管如此，北京仍沒有採取取消這些出口禁令的措施，”德古赫特在一份聲明中說道，“很遺憾，我們已經別無選擇，只能通過訴訟來解決問題。”

China currently produces more than 90 percent of the world's rare earth metals, which are essential to high-technology products like smartphones and electric car motors. The WTO case argues that Beijing is violating free trade rules by putting pressure on companies to move factories to China if they want access to Chinese raw materials. 

目前，中國的稀土產量佔到了全世界產量的百分之九十以上，而稀土對諸如智能手機和電動汽車發動機之類的高科技產品至關重要。世貿組織收到的投訴指出，北京的做法違反了自由貿易準則，因為它逼迫那些想獲得中國原材料的公司把工廠遷往中國。

Chinese officials have previously signaled that their defense in this case will be to use a provision of WTO rules that allows export restrictions for environmental protection and the conservation of scarce natural resources. 

此前，中國官員暗示，他們在這起訴訟中的辯護依據是世貿組織規則中的一項條款，該條款允許基於環保及保護稀缺自然資源的出口限制。

In what may have been an effort to buttress that argument, China's cabinet last week issued its first white paper on rare earth industry policies, saying that poorly regulated mining of rare earth metals had caused widespread environmental damage. The cabinet promised an extensive cleanup and a crackdown on illegal mines. 

或許是為了證明這一說法，中國國務院上周發佈了關於稀土產業政策的第一份白皮書，聲稱稀土金屬開採管理不善，業已造成大範圍環境破壞。國務院承諾實施全面清理，嚴厲懲罰非法開採。

The WTO dispute settlement panel, once established, has six months to deliver its report to the parties, who then can file an appeal.

世貿組織爭端仲裁專家小組將會在成立後六個月之內向當事各方提交報告，在此之後，各方可以提起上訴。
