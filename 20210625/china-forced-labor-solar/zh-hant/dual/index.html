美國禁止進口部分新疆太陽能產品，指其使用強迫勞工

U.S. Bans Imports of Some Chinese Solar Materials Tied to Forced Labor

THOMAS KAPLAN, 儲百亮, BRAD PLUMER
2021年6月25日
The White House announced steps on Thursday to crack down on forced labor in the supply chain for solar panels in the Chinese region of Xinjiang, including a ban on imports from a silicon producer there.
白宮週四宣布採取措施，打擊中國新疆地區太陽能電池板供應鏈中的強迫勞動，包括禁止從當地一家硅生產商進口產品。
A significant portion of the world’s polysilicon, which is used to make solar panels, comes from Xinjiang, where the United States has accused China of committing genocide through its repression of Uyghurs and other Muslim minorities.
世界上用於製造太陽能電池板的多晶矽中有很大一部分來自新疆。美國指責中國對那裡的維吾爾族和其他穆斯林少數民族進行鎮壓，實施種族滅絕。
In one of the newly announced actions, U.S. Customs and Border Protection banned imports of silica-based products made by Hoshine Silicon Industry Company as well as goods made using those products. The agency “has information reasonably indicating that Hoshine uses forced labor to produce its silica-based products,” Alejandro N. Mayorkas, the homeland security secretary, said at a news conference.
在新宣布的行動中，美國海關和邊境保護局(U.S. Customs and Border Protection)禁止進口合盛硅業公司生產的硅基產品，以及使用這些產品製造的商品。國土安全部長亞歷杭德羅·N·馬約卡斯(Alejandro N. Mayorkas)在新聞發布會上說，該機構「有合理的信息表明，合盛公司使用強迫勞工生產其硅基產品」。
The action was notable given the Biden administration’s push to expand the use of solar power in the United States to help achieve its climate goals. China dominates the global supply chain for solar power, producing a significant amount of the materials and parts needed for solar panels. Cutting off access to some imports could make it harder or more costly for the United States to expand solar use domestically.
鑒於拜登政府推動在美國擴大太陽能的使用，以幫助實現其氣候目標，這一行動值得注意。中國在全球太陽能供應鏈中佔主導地位，生產大量太陽能電池板所需的材料和部件。切斷一些進口管道，可能會使美國在國內擴大太陽能的使用變得更加困難，或成本更高。
廣告
Mr. Mayorkas addressed that tension at the news conference, saying, “Our environmental goals will not be achieved on the backs of human beings in a forced-labor environment.”
馬約卡斯在新聞發布會上談到了這種緊張關係，他說，「我們的環境目標不能通過強迫勞動環境下的人類來實現。」
The Commerce Department also added Hoshine Silicon Industry (Shanshan) Company and four other Chinese entities to a trade blacklist, a move that restricts American companies from exporting products and technology to them.
美國商務部還將合盛硅業（鄯善）公司和其他四家中國實體列入了貿易黑名單，此舉限制了美國公司向它們出口產品和技術。
The other entities are Xinjiang Daqo New Energy Company, Xinjiang East Hope Nonferrous Metals Company, Xinjiang GCL New Energy Material Technology Company, and the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps. The X.P.C.C. is a farming and industrial administration that helps protect Chinese control of the region, and the Trump administration imposed sanctions on it last year.
其他實體包括新疆大全新能源公司、新疆東方希望有色金屬公司、新疆協鑫新能源材料科技公司、新疆生產建設兵團。新疆生產建設兵團是一個有助於保護中國對該地區控制權的農業和工業管理部門，川普政府去年對其實施了制裁。
Those entities, the Commerce Department said, “have been implicated in human rights violations and abuses in the implementation of China’s campaign of repression, mass arbitrary detention, forced labor and high-technology surveillance against Uyghurs, Kazakhs and other members of Muslim minority groups” in Xinjiang.
美國商務部表示，這些實體「在中國對新疆維吾爾族、哈薩克族和其他穆斯林少數民族進行鎮壓、大規模任意拘留、強迫勞動和高科技監視的過程中，涉嫌侵犯人權和虐待行為」。
In addition, the Labor Department added Chinese polysilicon to a list of goods believed to be produced by child labor or forced labor. The list, which already contained a number of other Chinese goods, is intended to increase awareness about exploitative labor practices.
此外，美國勞工部將中國製造的多晶矽列入據信由童工或強迫勞動生產的商品清單。這份清單包含了其他一些中國商品，目的是提高人們對剝削性勞動做法的認識。
Calls to Hoshine were not answered late on Thursday in China. Xinjiang companies, including makers of polysilicon products, have repeatedly denied that the region’s labor transfer programs amount to forced labor.
在中國，記者於週四晚些時候致電合盛硅業，但無人接聽。包括多晶矽產品製造商在內的新疆企業多次否認該地區的勞動力轉移計劃是強迫勞動。
廣告
Allegations of forced labor in the solar panel supply chain have created a dilemma for U.S. officials. The Biden administration wants to press China over human rights abuses, but it also wants to expand the use of clean energy sources like solar power in the United States as it seeks to reduce carbon emissions.
太陽能電池板供應鏈中存在強迫勞動的指控讓美國官員陷入了兩難境地。拜登政府希望就侵犯人權問題向中國施壓，但同時也希望在尋求減少碳排放的同時，擴大太陽能等清潔能源在美國的使用。
The administration has set a goal of generating 100 percent of the nation’s electricity from carbon-free sources such as solar, wind or nuclear power by 2035, up from just 40 percent last year. To help meet that target, it is aiming to more than double the annual pace of solar installations nationwide and cut the price of solar power by more than half.
政府已經設定了一個目標：到2035年，全美國的電力都應由太陽能、風能或核能等無碳能源提供，而去年這一比例僅為40%。為了實現這一目標，政府的目標是將全國每年的太陽能安裝速度提高一倍以上，並將太陽能發電的價格降低一半以上。
“We’re going to root out forced labor wherever it exists,” Mr. Mayorkas said at the news conference, “and we’ll look for alternative products to achieve the environmental impacts that are a critical goal of this administration.”
「我們將根除任何地方存在的強迫勞動，我們將尋找替代產品來實現環境影響，這是本屆政府的一個關鍵目標，」馬約卡斯在新聞發布會上說。
Shortly before President Biden took office, the Trump administration banned imports of cotton and tomato products from Xinjiang. The Biden administration had faced pressure to take action regarding products containing polysilicon produced in the region.
在拜登總統上任前不久，川普政府禁止從新疆進口棉花和番茄產品。拜登政府之前一直面臨壓力，要求對該地區生產的含有多晶矽的產品採取行動。
In a letter this month to the acting head of Customs and Border Protection, a group of House Democrats wrote that there was “overwhelming evidence of the use of forced labor in polysilicon production,” adding, “Our government cannot sit idly by.”
在本月給海關和邊境保護局代理局長的信中，一群眾議院民主黨人寫道，「有大量證據表明在多晶矽生產中存在強迫勞動」，並補充說，「我們的政府不能袖手旁觀」。
The actions announced on Thursday could create diplomatic and economic ripples.
週四宣布的行動可能會在外交和經濟方面引發反響。
廣告
China is the dominant global producer of polysilicon, a raw material that most solar panels use to absorb energy from sunlight, and Xinjiang has over the past decade risen as the country’s main production base for the material. Xinjiang makes about 45 percent of the world’s polysilicon, according to InfoLink, a renewable energy research company.
中國是全球主要的多晶矽生產國，多晶矽是大多數太陽能電池板用來吸收陽光能量的原材料，而新疆在過去十年中已成為該國主要的多晶矽生產基地。根據可再生能源研究公司InfoLink的數據，新疆的多晶矽產量約佔全球的45%。
The import ban focuses on one company and not all polysilicon products from Xinjiang, but it could roil the market for solar panels in the United States. Hoshine and its subsidiaries supply at least some metallurgical-grade silicon to the world’s eight largest polysilicon producers, which together account for 90 percent of the global market, according to Johannes Bernreuter, a polysilicon market analyst at Bernreuter Research.
進口禁令針對的是一家公司，而不是來自新疆的所有多晶矽產品，但它可能會擾亂美國的太陽能電池板市場。伯恩羅伊特研究公司(Bernreuter Research)的多晶矽市場分析師約翰內斯·伯恩羅伊特(Johannes Bernreuter)表示，合盛及其子公司至少向全球八家最大的多晶矽生產商供應一些冶金級硅，這些生產商合計佔全球市場的90%。
Some U.S. solar companies had begun tracking and reshuffling their supply chains in anticipation of the administration’s move, but they may need to take additional steps to prove to customs officials that any imported panels do not contain material from Hoshine.
一些美國太陽能公司預料到政府的舉動，已經開始跟蹤和重組他們的供應鏈，但他們可能需要採取額外措施向海關官員證明任何進口面板不包含來自合盛的材料。
Customs and Border Protection estimates that the United States had about $6 million in direct imports from Hoshine over the past two and a half years and also imported more than $150 million in goods containing Hoshine products over that period, a small figure compared with the nation’s overall solar imports. U.S. imports of solar cells and modules totaled $8.2 billion last year, according to BloombergNEF, a research group.
海關和邊境保護局估計，在過去兩年半中，美國從合盛直接進口了約600萬美元的產品，並且在此期間進口了超過1.5億美元的含有合盛產品的商品。這一數字相比美國太陽能進口總額很小，根據研究機構彭博新能源財經(BloombergNEF)的數據，去年美國太陽能電池和組件的進口總額為82億美元。
Some analysts said the move on Thursday could be a prelude to further restrictions. “In our view, today’s action may only be the first step toward broader limits” on solar products from Xinjiang, Kevin Book, an analyst at ClearView Energy Partners, wrote in a research note.
一些分析師表示，週四的舉措可能是進一步限制舉措的前奏。ClearView能量夥伴(ClearView Energy Partners)的分析師凱文·布克(Kevin Book)在一份研究報告中寫道：「在我們看來，今天的行動可能只是對新疆太陽能產品進行更廣泛限制的第一步。」
The move could prove a boon to domestic solar manufacturers like First Solar, which does not use polysilicon in its panels and recently said it would double production in the United States by opening a third plant in Ohio by the middle of 2023. But U.S. manufacturers still account for only a small fraction of the solar market.
此舉可能對第一太陽能公司(First Solar)等國內太陽能製造商有利，該公司在其面板中不使用多晶矽，並且最近表示將在2023年年中之前在俄亥俄州開設第三家工廠，將其在美國的產量翻一番。但美國製造商仍然僅占太陽能市場的一小部分。
廣告
John Smirnow, general counsel and vice president for market strategy at the Solar Energy Industries Association, said the group supported the administration’s efforts on the issue of forced labor and was encouraging solar companies to shift away from materials produced in Xinjiang.
太陽能產業協會(Solar Energy Industries Association)總法律顧問兼市場戰略副總裁約翰·斯米爾諾(John Smirnow)表示，該組織支持政府在強迫勞動問題上的努力，並鼓勵太陽能公司放棄在新疆生產的材料。
“The fact is, we do not have transparency into supply chains in the Xinjiang region, and there is too much risk in operating there,” he said. “For that reason, in October, we began calling on solar companies to leave the region, and we provided them a traceability protocol to help ensure there is not forced labor in the supply chain.”
「事實上，新疆地區的供應鏈對我們來說缺乏透明度，在那裡經營風險太大，」他說。「出於這個原因，10月份，我們開始呼籲太陽能公司離開該地區，我們為他們提供了可追溯議定書，以幫助確保供應鏈中不存在強迫勞動。」
The Biden administration has pursued a strategy of pressing the Chinese government on areas of contention, such as Xinjiang, while seeking to cooperate on global priorities like climate change. The ban on importing some solar energy products — important for reducing fossil fuel use — will test how far Beijing is willing to go along with that bifurcated approach, and the Chinese government may hit back through its growing arsenal of retaliatory powers.
拜登政府奉行的戰略是，在新疆等有爭議的領域向中國政府施壓，同時尋求在氣候變化等全球優先事項上進行合作。禁止進口一些太陽能產品對減少化石燃料的使用很重要，因此這將考驗北京在多大程度上願意接受這種矛盾處境，而中國政府可能會通過其日益增長的報復力量和資源進行反擊。
A spokesman for China’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Zhao Lijian, suggested earlier this week that Beijing could retaliate against a possible ban on polysilicon imports from Xinjiang, though he did not specify what form that could take. Asked again on Thursday before the Biden administration’s actions were announced in Washington, Mr. Zhao said the United States wanted “turmoil in Xinjiang to contain China’s development.”
中國外交部發言人趙立堅本週早些時候表示，對可能禁止從新疆進口多晶矽，北京可以進行報復，但他沒有具體說明可能採取的形式。在華盛頓宣布拜登政府的行動之前，當趙立堅在週四再次被問到該問題時，他說，美國希望「禍亂新疆、遏制中國發展」。
“The U.S. is using lies as its basis,” he said at a regular news conference in Beijing. “This act not only violates the rules of international trade rules and market economies, it is also damaging global industry and supply chains.”
「美方以謊言為依據，」他在北京的一次例行記者會上說。「有關行徑不僅違反國際貿易規則和市場經濟原則，也破壞了全球產業鏈、供應鏈。」
