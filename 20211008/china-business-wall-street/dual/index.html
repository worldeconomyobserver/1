中国经济进入不确定时代，华尔街为何仍然乐观？

China is Rocked by Uncertainty. Why is Wall Street Bullish?

袁莉
2021年10月8日
This year has been unsettling for Chinese business. The ruling Communist Party has gone after the private sector industry by industry. The stock markets have taken a huge hit. The country’s biggest property developer is on the verge of collapse.
对于中国企业来说，这是令人不安的一年。私营部门各产业一个接一个地遭到共产党的打击。股市遭受重创。该国最大的房地产开发商濒临倒闭。
But for some of the biggest names on Wall Street, China’s economic prospects look rosier than ever.
但是对于华尔街的一些大公司来说，中国的经济前景看起来比以往任何时候都更加乐观。
BlackRock, the world’s biggest asset manager, urged investors to increase their exposure to China by as much as three times.
世界最大资产管理公司贝莱德(BlackRock)建议投资者将他们的对华投资增加三倍。
“Is China investable?” asked J.P. Morgan, before answering, “We think so.” Goldman Sachs says “yes,” too.
“可以投资中国吗？”摩根大通(J.P. Morgan)自问自答道：“我们认为可以。”高盛(Goldman Sachs)也说“是的”。
广告
Their bullishness in the face of growing uncertainty has puzzled China experts and drawn criticism from a wide political spectrum, from George Soros, the progressive investor, to congressional Republicans. Mr. Soros has called BlackRock’s stance a “tragic mistake” that’s “likely to lose money” for its clients and would “damage the national security interests of the U.S. and other democracies.”
面对日益增长的不确定性，他们的乐观态度令中国专家感到困惑，并招致了从进步投资者乔治·索罗斯(George Soros)到国会共和党人等各政治领域人士的批评。索罗斯称贝莱德的立场是一个“悲剧性错误”，“可能会给客户带来损失，”并会“损害美国和其他民主国家的国家安全利益”。
But Wall Street sees opportunity. Even while Beijing tightens its grip over business and the economy, it is giving global investment firms greater opportunities to serve Chinese companies and investors.
但华尔街看到了机会。尽管在加强对商业和经济的控制，北京正在给予全球投资公司更多的机会来服务中国公司和投资者。
At the height of a market sell-off in late July, the deputy chairman of China’s securities regulator, Fang Xinghai, summoned executives of BlackRock, Goldman Sachs and other firms to a meeting, trying to alleviate investor nervousness over Beijing’s crackdowns, according to a memo I reviewed.
根据我查阅的一份备忘录，在7月下旬市场抛售最严重的时期，中国证券监管委员会副主席方星海召集了贝莱德、高盛和其他公司的高管开会，试图缓解投资者对北京打压行动的紧张情绪。
Some 20 days later, regulators approved BlackRock’s application to offer mutual funds in China. Around the same time, a BlackRock executive told The Financial Times that China was underrepresented in global investors’ portfolios and in global benchmarks. The firm recommended that investors boost their allocations by two to three times.
大概20天后，监管者通过了贝莱德为中国发行投资基金的申请。大约同一时间，一名贝莱德高管告诉《金融时报》(Financial Times)，中国在全球投资者的投资组合以及全球基准指数中的占比偏低。该公司建议投资者将其配置增加到二至三倍。
BlackRock said in a statement that its global clients “can benefit from portfolio diversification that includes more deliberate asset allocation to China,” adding that Wall Street’s expansion in China is consistent with American government policy goals.
贝莱德在一份声明中表示，其全球客户“可以从投资组合多元化中受益，其中包括将资产酌情配置到中国”，并说华尔街在中国的扩张符合美国政府的政策目标。
Goldman Sachs and J.P. Morgan declined to comment.
高盛和摩根大通拒绝置评。
广告
Wall Street now stands as an increasingly lonely voice arguing for more engagement with China. Both American political parties are calling for a tougher stance. Positions have hardened in other countries, too. The broad business world has become more ambivalent: It still sees China has a huge market, but issues like trade, intellectual property and the government’s support of homegrown businesses have complicated their traditional support.
华尔街的这种增加与中国接触的主张，正显得越来越小众。美国两个政党都呼吁采取更强硬的立场。其他国家的立场也有所加强。在广泛的商业世界里，人们的心态要相对矛盾一些：他们仍然认为中国有一个巨大的市场，但贸易、知识产权和政府对本土企业的补贴等问题使曾经的一贯支持变得复杂。
Wall Street might be right to be bullish. China has defied bearish predictions in the past. Despite the party’s authoritarian rule in other matters, it has long brought a touch of laissez faire to the economy, helping growth.
华尔街持乐观心态也许是对的。以往看空中国的预言都一一落空。尽管共产党在其他事务上实行专制统治，但长期以来它一直为经济带来一丝自由放任，这有助于增长。
But Xi Jinping, China’s top leader, is ushering the country into a more uncertain era. The party’s rule is tighter and more authoritarian than before. It has not abandoned market principles broadly because it needs economic growth to sustain its legitimacy, but it is tinkering with tighter controls. The long-term impact is far from clear.
但中国最高领导人习近平正在带领中国进入一个更加不确定的时代。党的统治比以前更严格、更专制。它并没有广泛地放弃市场经济原则，因为它需要经济增长来维持其合法性，但它正在仔细捉摸更严格的控制。长期影响远未明朗。
This summer, China’s private sector suffered its harshest beating by the Communist Party in decades. With just a few abrupt orders, Beijing kneecapped the internet industry, sharply curtailed the after-school tutoring businesses and drove some property developers to the brink of default.
今年夏天，中国的私营部门遭受了共产党数十年来最严厉的打击。北京仅凭几个突如其来的命令，就让互联网行业受挫，课外辅导行业大幅缩减，有的房地产开发商濒临违约。
Didi, China’s dominant ride-hailing company, was a Wall Street darling when it went public in New York in late June, raising more than $4 billion. Its share price has fallen by nearly half after the Chinese government moved to limit its business two days after its listing, leaving many investors — including American funds — in limbo.
中国占主导地位的网约车公司滴滴6月底在纽约上市时是华尔街的宠儿，筹集了超过40亿美元。上市两天后，中国政府采取措施限制其业务，其股价下跌了近一半，使包括美国基金在内的许多投资者陷入困境。
“I don’t think we can use spreadsheet-type thinking to take a view on China in the 2020s and beyond,” said George Magnus, a China researcher at Oxford University. The country is going through “a sharp leftward lurch in politics,” he said, “which is creating a deep contradiction between the craving for political control and the desire for good economic and innovation outcomes.”
牛津大学中国研究员乔治·马格努斯(George Magnus)说：“我认为我们不能用报表思维来看待2020年代及以后的中国。”这个国家正在经历“政治上的急剧左倾”，他说，“这在对政治控制的渴望与对良好经济和创新成果的需求之间产生了深刻的矛盾。”
广告
“I think the former,” Mr. Magnus added, “is bound to win.”
马格努斯还说：“我认为前者肯定会赢。”
Some of Wall Street’s biggest names disagree. Ray Dalio, founder of the hedge fund Bridgewater, wrote in late July that people in the West should not interpret Beijing’s crackdowns as “the Communist Party leaders showing their true anticapitalist stripes.” Instead, he wrote, the party believed those moves were “better for the country even if the shareholders don’t like it.”
华尔街的一些大人物则不同意。对冲基金桥水基金(Bridgewater)的创始人雷·达利奥(Ray Dalio)在7月下旬写道，西方人不应将北京的镇压解读为“共产党领导人在展示他们真正的反资本主义信条”。相反，他写道，共产党认为这些举措“更有利于国家，即使股东不喜欢”。
The relationship has been good to Bridgewater so far. Mr. Dalio’s firm has raised billions of dollars from Chinese clients such as the China Investment Corporation, the sovereign wealth fund, and State Administration of Foreign Exchange, which manages the country’s currency reserves. (Bridgewater declined to comment.)
中国与桥水基金迄今一直保持着良好的关系。达利奥的公司已从中国投资有限责任公司、主权财富基金和管理该国货币储备的国家外汇管理局等中国客户那里募集了数十亿美元。（桥水基金拒绝置评。）
This is a balance that business has played with China for a long time: Say nice things to Beijing, lobby back home on China’s behalf, then ask for access to markets and capital.
这是商界长期以来与中国进行的一场平衡游戏：对北京说好话，代表中国回国游说，然后对市场和资本提出要求。
Goldman Sachs became the first foreign bank to seek full ownership of a securities business in China in December. BlackRock, which describes China as an “undiscovered” market, hired a former regulator to head its China business. So many global financial firms are expanding in the country that there’s a talent war.
去年12月，高盛成为首家寻求全资拥有中国证券业务的外资银行。贝莱德将中国形容为一个“未开发”市场，聘请了一位前监管官员来执掌其中国业务。太多跨国金融公司正在中国扩张，以至于出现了人才争夺战。
The Wall Street firms argue that, despite regulatory risks and slowing growth, China is too big to ignore and that its stocks are too undervalued to pass up.
华尔街企业认为，尽管存在监管风险和增长放缓的可能，但中国大到不容忽视，其股市价值也低到了不容错过的地步。
Many investors have listened. U.S. mutual funds and exchange-traded funds investing primarily in China held $43 billion in net assets at the end of August, up 43 percent, or $13 billion, from a year earlier, according to Morningstar.
许多投资者听取了这些意见。晨星(Morningstar)的数据显示，截至8月底，主要投资于中国的美国共同基金和交易所交易基金共持有430亿美元净资产，较去年同期增长43%，也就是130亿美元。
A lot of companies and investors have made a lot of money over the years from China. And despite the frosty talk between the two sides, they still share extensive business ties. China makes iPhones and buys iPhones. Same with Chevrolets. China’s economic growth, while slowing, is still stronger than in most places. That won’t change overnight.
多年来，大批企业和投资者从中国赚得盆满钵满。尽管对话语调冷淡，但中美两国仍保持着广泛的商业联系。中国生产iPhone，也购买iPhone。对雪佛兰(Chevrolet)汽车也是如此。中国的经济增长虽然有所放缓，但仍比大部分国家强劲。这不会在一夜之间改变。
广告
But even as Wall Street cheers on China, the balance between engaging with Beijing and confronting Beijing has fallen out of whack. And Americans lawmakers are starting to scrutinize those ties. Elected representatives from both the Democratic and Republican Parties have expressed concerns about American funds investing in China. A U.S. government retirement fund halted plans to invest in Chinese stocks last year after growing criticism that the move could work against national security goals.
尽管华尔街为中国打气，但与北京合作和对抗之间的天平已经失衡。美国立法者开始仔细审查这些商业联系。民主党和共和党的民选议员都对美国的在华投资表示担忧。美国政府的一个退休基金去年停止了投资中国股市的计划，因为对此举可能违背国家安全目标的批评声越来越多。
Matthew Pottinger, a deputy national security adviser under former President Donald J. Trump, warned recently in Foreign Affairs that these institutions “cling to self-destructive habits acquired through decades of ‘engagement,’ an approach to China that led Washington to prioritize economic cooperation and trade above all else.”
前总统唐纳德·J·特朗普(Donald J. Trump)的国家安全事务副主任马修·波廷格(Matthew Pottinger)最近在《外交事务》(Foreign Affairs)上警告称，这些机构“在数十年的‘接触’中养成了顽固的自毁习惯，这种对华策略导致华盛顿将经济合作与贸易视为高于一切。”
Compared with Wall Street’s confidence, China’s business community is jittery about what comes next. The wealthiest people are pledging to spend millions, sometimes billions, of dollars on charities and other projects to stay aligned with Mr. Xi’s goal of “common prosperity.”
相比华尔街的信心，中国商界却对未来惴惴不安。最富有的人承诺将在慈善和其它项目上投入数百万、有时甚至数十亿美元，只为与习近平“共同富裕”的目标保持一致。
Access to senior Chinese policymakers doesn’t work its magic as much as before, either. Stephen Schwarzman, head of the private equity giant Blackstone, has long cultivated relationships with the Chinese leadership. He is tight with Liu He, the country’s economic czar. Still, his firm was forced to call off a $3 billion deal to buy Soho China, a property developer, in September because they failed to get regulatory approval. Blackstone declined to comment.
与中国高层决策者的接触不再像以前那么有用了。私募股权巨头黑石集团负责人苏世民(Stephen Schwarzman)多年来一直在培养与中国领导层的关系。他与中国的“经济沙皇”刘鹤往来密切。尽管如此，由于未能获得监管部门的批准，他的公司被迫取消了以30亿美元收购开发商SOHO中国的交易。黑石集团拒绝置评。
The Wall Street firms are apparently betting that China’s past successes will continue. They have a long track record on their side, but they would do well to remember what they constantly tell their customers: Past performance isn’t necessarily indicative of future results.
华尔街的企业显然在押注中国能够延续过往的成功。长久以来的成就支持了他们的立场，但最好别忘了他们一直告诫客户的话：过去的业绩并不一定能预示未来的结果。
