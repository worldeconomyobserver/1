專利敗訴讓韓國重新審視三星

South Korea Reassesses Its Great Imitator

CHOE SANG-HUN
2012年9月4日
SEOUL, South Korea—When a jury in San Jose, Calif., ordered Samsung Electronics to pay $1.05 billion in damages for violating Apple's patents for the iPhone and iPad, it did more than decide who had infringed upon whose intellectual property. To South Koreans, the legal battle highlighted the strengths and weaknesses of both Samsung and their economy in general.
韓國首爾——美國加利福尼亞州聖何塞一家地方法院裁定三星電子(Samsung Electronics)侵犯了蘋果(Apple)產品iPhone和iPad的專利權，為此需要向蘋果支付10.5億美元賠款。這不僅僅是一個關於誰侵犯了誰的知識產權的裁決。對韓國人來說，這場法律糾紛凸顯了三星與韓國總體經濟的長項和弱點。
“The ruling makes us reconsider the brand value of Samsung because it depicts Samsung as a copycat,” said James Song, who monitors Samsung for KDB Daewoo Securities in Seoul. “But a copycat or not, what Samsung has done with its smartphones was a brilliant move.” 
“這項裁決使我們重新考慮三星品牌的價值觀，因為它把三星刻畫成了一個山寨生產商。” 首爾的KDB大宇證券(KDB Daewoo Securities)三星分析師詹姆斯·宋(James Song)說道，“不管三星是不是個山寨廠商，它在智能手機上的開發是一次了不起的飛越。”
“Look what has happened to companies like Nokia, Motorola and BlackBerry, which didn't do as Samsung did,” Song added, referring to competitors whose failures to adapt quickly to the smartphone boom driven by iPhones have drastically reduced their market shares. “Samsung may lack in innovation, but right now, no one can beat Samsung in playing catch-up.” 
“看看像諾基亞(Nokia)、摩托羅拉(Motorola)和黑莓(BlackBerry)這樣的公司經歷了怎樣的境況。他們沒有採取三星的做法。”詹姆斯·宋補充說，意指蘋果的競爭者們無法快速適應蘋果手機激發的智能手機熱潮，以至出現市場份額大幅下降的慘況。“三星可能缺乏創新。不過，三星在追趕行業領先者時的表現可謂無人能及。”
For months, the South Korean media have watched avidly as Samsung, the world's largest technology company by sales, clashes with Apple, the world's No. 1 company by market value, over patent lawsuits in various countries. 
過去幾個月，韓國媒體一直熱切地關注着三星和蘋果在多國展開的專利訴訟之戰。前者是全球銷量最大的技術公司，而後者在全球的市值首屈一指。
Calling Samsung South Korea's biggest, most profitable and most globally recognized brand barely explains the intense and often mixed emotions the name evokes among South Koreans. In Samsung, they see the crown jewel of their country's transformation from a war-torn agrarian society into a global technology powerhouse. But Samsung’s legal trouble was also seen as a referendum on the way Samsung — and, by extension, the South Korean economy — has done business.
三星被視作是韓國規模最大、盈利最多、也最為全球認可的品牌，但這還是很難解釋三星在韓國民眾當中激起的強烈情緒，這種情緒往往非常複雜。三星被視為韓國皇冠上的明珠，代表了經歷戰爭重創的韓國從農業社會向全球科技強國轉變的歷程。然而，三星的法律困境也被視為對三星經營理念的一次全民公決——往大了說，這也是對韓國經濟的一次公決。
Once an assembler of clunky transistor radios, Samsung is now the world's top seller of smartphones and high-resolution television sets, as well as memory chips and flat-panel displays that allow those devices and others to store and display data.
三星原本只是一家笨重半導體收音機的組裝企業，現在卻成為了全球第一的智能手機和高清電視銷售商，該公司還是這些設備主要元件的全球最大供應商商。它生產的內存芯片和平板顯示器讓這些設備可以存儲和顯示數據。
Japanese companies have beaten rivals to the market with hardware innovations like flat-panel televisions and high-end mobile phones. Samsung waited for the others to test the market, and when it determined the time was right, it joined the fray to cash in. 
日本公司曾在市場上最先推出平板電視和高端流動電話等創新硬件產品。而三星則等待其他公司幫它測試市場，當它認定時機已到時，就加入混戰，從中獲利。
Samsung's strategy was to build something similar to another company's product but to make it better, faster and at lower cost. When it pounced, it flooded the market with a wide range of models that were constantly updated with incremental improvements at a speed its rivals found hard to match—a strategy best illustrated by its smartphone business. 
三星的戰略是生產和其他公司相類似的產品，但是要以更好的質量，更快的速度和更低的成本完成生產。當它出擊時，會用型號繁多的產品佔領市場，而且通過頻繁的更新逐步改善產品，其更新之快令對手難以匹敵——它的智能手機業務就是這一戰略的最好注釋。
Heavy investments have not been a problem; it once secured low-cost loans from a government-controlled banking sector friendly to big businesses and now draws on its own coffers, which are sloshing with cash. 
巨額投資從來都不是個問題；政府控制的向來善待大企業的銀行部門曾確保該公司能獲得低成本貸款。現在，它可以動用自己充斥着現金的金庫。
Several years ago, Sony, Sharp and Panasonic were the first companies to market flat-panel televisions. But they obsessed over craftsmanship or held onto the old cathode-ray tube while dithering over the rival flat-panel technologies of plasma screen and liquid crystal displays, or LCDs.
幾年前，索尼(Sony)、夏普(Sharp)和松下(Panasonic)是第一批率先在市場推出平板電視的製造商。然而，他們面對等離子和液晶平板顯示(LCD)技術的競爭時猶豫不決，同時，不是過於糾纏產品質量細節，就是抓着陰極管射線技術不放手。
Samsung placed billion-dollar bets on mass production of television-size LCDs. Its economies of scale helped it drive down prices to create, then clinch, the LCD television market. 
三星投入了數十億美元，把賭注壓在批量化生產電視機大小的液晶顯示器上。它的規模化效應幫它壓低了價格，從而孕育了液晶顯示器市場，之後它又把這一市場牢牢地控制在自己手中。
Five years ago, Sony was the first company to make what is widely seen as the next-generation television. It featured the OLED display—organic light-emitting diode—which is thinner, more vivid and more energy-efficient. But Sony was never able to mass-produce or market it; it was too expensive. Samsung and its domestic rival, LG Electronics, tiptoed in, building mass-production capabilities by first making smaller OLEDs for high-end smartphones. Then last week they pounced, announcing the marketing of 55-inch OLED televisions. 
五年前，索尼是第一個製造被廣泛認可為下一代電視機的廠家。它的特色產品是有機發光二極管（organic light-emitting diode，簡稱OLED）顯示屏——這種顯示屏更纖薄、顏色更生動、能源利用率也更高。可是，索尼從未能大批量生產這種產品，或將其市場化，因為它太昂貴了。三星和它的韓國競爭對手LG電子(LG Electronics)不聲不響地跟進，通過為高端智能手機製造尺寸較小的OLED顯示屏鍛造了大批量生產的能力。然後，他們在上周發動突襲，宣布向市場推出55寸的OLED電視機。
“Koreans do things quicker than almost anyone,” said Anthony Michell, author of “Samsung Electronics and the Struggle for Leadership of the Electronics Industry.” “This allows them to change models, go from design to production faster than anyone at the present time. Korean companies continually set themselves challenges, like the challenge to overtake Sony in terms of brand value in the past.” 
 “韓國人行動迅速，幾乎超過了所有人。”《三星電子及電子產業領導權之爭》 ("Samsung Electronics and the Struggle for Leadership of the Electronics Industry")一書的作者安東尼·米歇爾(Anthony Michell)說道，“這使得他們能夠改變模式，從設計到生產，其速度超過了目前所有企業。韓國公司不斷給自己設置挑戰，比如，過去的一個目標就是在品牌價值方面趕超索尼。”
Samsung Electronics is the flagship of Samsung Group, a family conglomerate that controls more than 80 companies that build oil tankers and apartment complexes, run hotels and amusement parks and sell insurance to housewives and artillery pieces to the military. The flagship's operations are often faulted for their opacity. But analysts say that also allows Samsung to place huge bets and do so quickly. 
三星集團，作為一個家族集團企業，旗下有80多家公司，它們建造油輪、公寓群，運營賓館和遊樂場，向家庭主婦賣保險，並出售大炮零件給軍隊，而三星電子則是該集團的龍頭公司。三星電子經常因其運營不透明而受到批評。但分析人士稱，這也允許了三星進行巨額投資賭注，並且行動迅速。
Samsung makes not only hardware but also its components; it is Apple's biggest parts supplier and its fiercest competitor in the completed smartphone market. In a way, its rivals help Samsung compete with them.
三星不僅製造硬件，也生產元件；它是蘋果最大的元件供應商，同時也是蘋果在成品智能手機市場上最強有力的競爭對手。某種意義上來講，三星的對手在幫助三星和自己對抗。
The strategy worked well for the latest product it dominated—smartphones—until Apple declared war over intellectual property in April 2011, accusing the South Korean company of “slavishly copying” the feel and look of its iPhone and iPad. 
此戰略在三星最新主導的產品——智能手機——上發揮了很好的作用，直到蘋果在2011年4月向三星發起了知識產權之戰。蘋果指控該韓國公司“毫無創見地模仿”iPhone和iPad的風格和外觀。
After the court victory, Apple lawyers sought injunctions against sales of Samsung Galaxy smartphones and tablets in the U.S. market. 
訴訟取得勝利後，蘋果的律師希望法院做出裁定，禁止三星在美國市場銷售其Galaxy智能手機和平板電腦。
But those products had already lived through their life cycles in Samsung's fast-paced marketing plan, analysts and Samsung officials said. With characteristic speed, Samsung had already retooled its latest Galaxy S III smartphones to stay ahead of the patent battle—removing, for example, the function that makes the screen “bounce back” after being pulled to the end of a page. 
然而，在三星快節奏的市場計劃中，這些產品早已走完了其生命周期。以速度取勝的三星，在商標戰之前，就已重新設計了其最新的Galaxy S III智能手機。例如，該手機去除了“彈回”功能，這一功能會讓屏幕在拉至一頁末端時“反彈回來”。
Indeed, Samsung appeared to consider the legal trouble a necessary cost of squeezing out a giant in yet another field it is fast dominating. Samsung's smartphone sales in the second quarter grew 150 percent to 50.5 million units, or a record 35 percent of the market, according to the research firm Strategy Analytics. Apple grew 28 percent, to 26 million units. Apple will attempt a surge back with its new iPhone 5 this year, but Samsung is ready to roll out the Galaxy S IV as a counter. 
實際上，三星似乎是將法律糾紛看成在另外一個它快速主導的領域排擠強大對手時的必要成本。調查公司Strategy Analytics稱，三星智能手機第二季度的銷量達5050萬，上升了150%，創下了35%的市場份額記錄。而蘋果智能手機的銷量為2600萬，上升了28%。蘋果希望今年推出的iPhone 5能夠大幅增加其市場份額，而三星也已準備推出Galaxy S IV，做為對抗手段。
Song said that despite the verdict, the fight for the smartphone market was over and had been decided in Samsung's favor. 
詹姆斯·宋說，儘管判決已經生效，智能手機市場之戰已經結束，而三星取得了勝利。
“The patent ruling and Samsung's copycat image will have a negative impact on Samsung's sales of cellphones and other products,” said Kevin Lee, an analyst at Korea Investment and Securities. “But Samsung will further widen the gap with Apple in the third quarter, though not as much as expected before the ruling.”
“關於商標的判決和三星山寨的形象，會對該公司手機和其他產品的銷量造成負面影響，” 韓國投資證券公司(Korea Investment and Securities)的分析師凱文·李(Kevin Lee)表示。“但是三星將在第三季度進一步擴大與蘋果之間的差距，雖然幅度不會如判決之前預期的那樣大。”
In South Korea, where Samsung's achievements are a source of national pride, many perceived a bias against a foreign competitor in the U.S. jury verdict. Still, the ruling reminded South Koreans of something their country painfully lacked, a shortcoming magnified by comparisons between Samsung and Apple. 
在韓國，三星的成就是該國民族自豪感的來源之一，很多人認為美國陪審團做出的判決，反映了該國對外國競爭者的偏見。但不管怎樣，該判決提醒了韓國人，他們的國家嚴重缺乏一樣東西，而此不足通過對比三星和蘋果則顯得愈加明顯。
Although the name Samsung is synonymous with sophistication among South Koreans, the company has never created a product so innovative that it has defined an era in consumer culture, like the Sony Walkman or the Apple iPhone. 
儘管在韓國人看來，三星就是高級的代名詞，但該公司從未生產過一件足夠創新的產品，來定義某一時代的消費文化，比如像索尼的隨身聽或者蘋果的iPhone。
“Copying and clever upgrading are no longer viable,” the daily JoongAng Ilbo  said in an editorial. “Samsung must reinvent itself as a first-mover, despite the huge risks involved in acting as a pioneer, if it hopes to beat the competition.” 
“模仿並討巧地進行產品升級已經不再可行，” 韓國《中央日報》(JoongAng Ilbo)在一篇社評中寫道。“雖然作為一個開拓者，會承擔很大的風險，但如果三星希望在競爭中勝出，就應該重新給自己定位，成為創新者。”
