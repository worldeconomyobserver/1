当看电视剧不再需要电视

Don’t Touch That Remote: TV Pilots Turn to Net, Not Networks

BRIAN STELTER
2013年3月7日
In the television pilot that Cheyenne Jackson taped recently, he played an aggressive young news anchor whose ascendancy threatened an older colleague. It was a fitting metaphor for the industry itself, because while Mr. Jackson had taped pilots for ABC, NBC and USA before, this was his first time doing one for a new challenger to those alphabet networks: Amazon.com.
在夏安·杰克逊(Cheyenne Jackson)不久前拍摄的试播集中，他扮演了一位咄咄逼人的年轻新闻主播，他的上位威胁到了一名年纪比他大的同事。这是对这个行业本身极好的比喻，因为尽管杰克逊之前为ABC，NBC和USA拍过试播集，但这是他首次为这些以字母缩写为名的广播电视网的新挑战者拍摄试播集。而这个挑战者就是亚马逊(Amazon.com)。 
When Amazon sizes up the television marketplace, it sees opportunity. Internet-delivered TV, which until recently was unready for prime time, is the new front in the war for Americans’ attention spans. Netflix is following up on the $100 million drama “House of Cards” with four more series this year. Microsoft is producing programming for the Xbox video game console with the help of a former CBS president. Other companies, from AOL to Sony to Twitter, are likely to follow.
当亚马逊考察电视剧市场时，它看到了机遇。直到前不久，在互联网上播放电视节目的时机还不成熟，但现在，它已经是美国观众注意力争夺战的新战线了。今年，继推出耗资1亿美元（约合6.2亿元人民币）的剧集《纸牌屋》(House of Cards)之后，Netflix将再拍摄四部电视剧。在一名前CBS总裁的帮助下，微软(Microsoft)也正在制作将在Xbox电脑游戏机上播放的节目。其他公司，从美国在线(AOL)到索尼(Sony)再到Twitter，十有八九也会效仿。
The companies are, in effect, creating new networks for television through broadband pipes and also giving rise to new rivalries — among one another, as between Amazon and Netflix, and with the big but vulnerable broadcast networks as well.
实际上，这些公司正在通过宽带网络这一渠道创造新的电视网，这也同时也引发了新的竞争。竞争不仅存在于这些公司当中，比如亚马逊和Netflix之间，也涉及那些大而脆弱的广播电视网。
“These are the very first lab tests in a very grand experiment,” said Jeff Berman, the president of BermanBraun, a media company that makes programming for NBC, HGTV, AOL and YouTube, among others.
“在一场非常宏大的实验中，这些正是最初的实验室测试，”为NBC、HGTV、美国在线和YouTube等制作节目的媒体公司伯曼布劳恩(BermanBraun)公司的总裁杰夫·伯曼(Jeff Berman)说。

As he suggested, he competition has only just begun. Amazon is making pilot episodes for at least six comedies and five children’s shows. Sometime this spring it will put the episodes on its Amazon Prime Instant Video service and ask its customers which ones they like and dislike, then order full seasons of some of them.
正如他指出的那样，竞争才刚刚开始。亚马逊正在为至少六部喜剧和五部儿童剧制作试播集。今年春天的某个时候，亚马逊会将这些剧集放在Amazon Prime Instant Video服务平台上，并询问顾客喜欢哪些不喜欢哪些，然后再下单订购部分电视剧的全部剧集。
Netflix has been ordering entire seasons of its shows without seeing pilots first. Reed Hastings, Netflix’s chief executive, said last week that “House of Cards,” the political thriller starring Kevin Spacey and Robin Wright, had been a “great success” for the company. Its next program, a horror series called “Hemlock Grove” from the film director Eli Roth, premieres in April.
Netflix却是不预先观看试播集便订下全部剧集。其首席执行官里德·哈斯廷斯(Reed Hastings)上周表示，由凯文·斯佩西(Kevin Spacey)和罗宾·怀特(Robin Wright)主演的《纸牌屋》是公司的“巨大成功”。它的下一部电视剧是伊莱·罗斯(Eli Roth)执导的恐怖题材电视剧《铁杉树丛》(Hemlock Grove)，将于4月首播。
The two companies are commissioning TV shows because they have millions of subscribers on monthly or yearly subscription plans. Though the shows may be loss leaders, executives like Mr. Hastings say that having exclusive content — something that cannot be seen anywhere else — increases the likelihood that existing subscribers will keep paying and that new subscribers will sign up.
这两家公司都在订制电视节目，原因是有数百万用户按月或按年订购了它们的服务。尽管这些电视剧可能会亏本，但像哈斯廷斯这样的高管表示，拥有独一无二的内容，也就是观众在其他地方看不到的内容，增加了现有用户会继续订购、新用户会开始订购的可能性。
The proliferation of shows is generally seen as a good thing for viewers, who have more choices about what to watch and when, and for producers and actors, who have more places to be seen and heard. But the trend may inflame cable companies’ concerns about cord-cutting by subscribers who decide there’s enough to watch online. At the same time, the rise of Internet-only shows may make viewers more dependent on the broadband cord. (In many cases, both connections are supplied by the same company.)
人们普遍认为，剧目的增多对观众、制片方和演员都有利，因为观众在看什么和什么时候看方面有更多选择，而制片方和演员也有更多被观众看到和听到的机会。但这种趋势可能会引发有线电视公司对用户掐线的担忧，怕这些用户会认为网上的节目足够他们看了。同时，只能在网上观看的电视剧的兴起可能会让观众更依赖宽带。（在许多情况下，有线电视和宽带服务是由同一家公司提供的。）
Unlike the early stabs at Internet television, these shows look and feel like traditional TV. That is partly because more viewers are watching Internet content on big-screen TV sets, but it is mostly because the companies involved are throwing money at the screens: each of the Amazon comedy pilots cost the company upward of $1 million, according to people involved in their production, which is less than the $2 million invested in a broadcast comedy pilot, but more than is typically invested in cable pilots.
和先前尝试制作的网络节目不同，不论是外观还是感觉，这些节目都很像传统电视节目。其中的部分原因是，更多观众开始在大屏幕电视上观看网络节目，但更重要的原因是，相关公司正不惜将重金投入到网络电视节目的制作中。参与其节目制作的人士称，亚马逊推出的喜剧试播集，每集成本均超过一百万美元，这低于一集普通电视喜剧试播集两百万美元的投资成本，却高于一集有线电视试播集通常的投资成本。
Analysts say they expect more TV investment to come, including from companies that do not make money from subscribers. YouTube, for instance, the biggest video Web site of all, makes money from ads, not from subscriptions. But it has paid dozens of outside producers to start channels so that it has original, professional content, and its owner, Google, can afford to pay many more.
分析人士称，他们认为对电视节目的投资将进一步增加，其中就包括那些不靠收取用户订购费而盈利的公司。比如，最大的视频网站YouTube就是通过广告而不是用户订购费盈利。但它雇佣了数十名外部制作人来开通一些频道，这样一来，该网站就能拥有原创、专业的内容，而它的母公司谷歌(Google)则能雇得起更多此类人士。

Similar logic is spurring cable channels, which each receive a small piece of cable subscribers’ monthly payments, to come up with more dramas and sitcoms that they can call their own. This brings up a conundrum, of course: too much great TV to watch, and not enough time.
对于获得注册用户每月付费中很小一部分的有线电视频道而言，在类似原因的推动下，它们也越来越多地在制作属于自己的电视剧和情景剧。当然，这也带来了一个难题：好的电视节目太多，而时间太少。
“Viewers find organizing and managing all of their beloved TV options to be a bit stressful,” said Christy Tanner, the chief executive of TVGuide.com, which conducted research that found respondents who said “it feels like work” and “I’m afraid of missing something.”
TVGuide.com的首席执行官克里斯蒂·坦纳(Christy Tanner)说， “观众发现整理和观看所有他们喜爱的电视剧让人有点紧张。” TVGuide.com开展一项相关调查时发现，受众说“感觉像在工作”，还说“我很害怕错过什么。”
Nonetheless, the number of companies trying to elbow into the TV space is only going up — further proving the nearly 20-year-old adage that “content is king.” Witness DirecTV, the biggest satellite distributor in the country, which is planning to introduce its first homegrown show, a thriller called “Rogue,” next month. Christopher Long, who runs DirecTV’s original programming, said he wanted to buy several more shows: “Our opinion is, if we build good enough television, people will find it.”
尽管如此，想要挤进网络电视行业的公司的数量有增无减——进一步证明了那句20年前出现的箴言：“内容是王道。”比如，美国最大的卫星电视广播公司 DirecTV 就计划在下月推出其第一部原创剧：一部名为《恶棍》(Rogue)的惊悚剧。DirecTV原创节目负责人克里斯托弗·朗(Christopher Long)说，他想要再购进几部电视节目：“我们的想法是，如果我们的电视节目足够好，观众就会找来观看。”
