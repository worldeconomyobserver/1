奥巴马围堵中国汽车

Trade Case May Produce Few Results

KEITH BRADSHER
2012年9月19日
BEIJING — President Obama’s trade case against China on cars and auto parts will have little immediate impact on jobs and companies in the United States, but it is one of the few legal options available to the United States as China’s auto industry faces overcapacity problems and looks overseas to increase sales.
北京——奥巴马总统针对中国补贴汽车及零部件而提起的贸易诉讼，几乎不会对美国的就业形势和企业形势产生立竿见影的效果。然而，当下中国汽车产业面临产能过剩问题，正瞄准海外市场促进销售，提起这宗贸易诉讼是美国为数不多的几个可选的法律选项之一。
In filing the case on Monday with the World Trade Organization, Mr. Obama is making a political gesture to Midwestern states coping with the pressure that Chinese exports are placing on the American auto industry. But actual effects are likely to be delayed and limited.
奥巴马政府本周一向世界贸易组织(World Trade Organization)提起的诉讼，也向美国中西部各州摆出了一个政治姿态。这些州正在承受着中国出口给美国汽车工业造成的压力。不过，这起贸易诉讼的实际效果可能有限，而且也可能会滞后。
World Trade Organization cases typically take a year and a half to resolve. And unlike antidumping and antisubsidy cases, which can result in steep tariffs on imports that stay in place for years to come, the trade organization cases often end with the losing country simply abandoning the offending policy.
世贸组织解决贸易争端通常需要一年半时间。反倾销及反补贴诉讼的最终结果可能是，在未来几年内对进口商品征收高额关税。但世贸组织的贸易诉讼与此不同，最终结果常常仅是败诉国放弃违规政策。
There can be a requirement that companies repay previous subsidies, but that is often difficult to enforce and can require further years of legal wrangling.
世贸组织在裁决中可以要求受益公司退回过去获得的补贴，然而，这样的裁决常常很难执行，而且可能需要再花多年时间进行法律纠缠。
The subsidies at issue are also small relative to the scale of Chinese exports, which may mean that China’s low wages, high investment rate and other advantages may have played a bigger role in the spectacular expansion of Chinese auto exports than government subsidies.
与中国的出口规模相比，此案涉及的补贴金额相对较小，这或许意味着中国的低工资、高投资率和其他优势，在中国汽车出口的大幅扩张中，可能扮演了比政府补贴更为重要的角色。
American trade officials respond that while China may have many strengths underlying its export prowess, they are doing what they can to address those policies that may violate international trade rules — particularly China’s emerging policy of setting up so-called export bases in which automakers receive incentives to make cars and car parts for overseas markets.
美国贸易官员回应称，尽管中国强大的出口实力背后可能存在多重优势，但是他们正在竭尽所能地纠正那些可能违反国际贸易规则的政策——尤其是中国建立所谓“出口基地”的新政策。在这些基地中，为海外市场制造整车和零部件的汽车制造商，会得到政府的优惠政策。
“The Obama administration will use every available avenue to stand up for American workers and businesses in the global trading system, and in taking this case to the W.T.O. against China we’re doing so in a way that tackles China’s entire export bases program in this sector,” said Carol Guthrie, the spokeswoman for the Office of the United States Trade Representative.
美国贸易代表办公室(Office of the United States Trade Representative)发言人卡萝尔·格思里(Carol Guthrie)说，“奥巴马政府将通过一切可能的途径，在全球贸易体系内维护美国工人和企业的权益。我们向世贸组织提起本次诉讼时，我们针对的目标就是中国汽车产业的整个出口基地项目。”
She added: “This administration has a consistent record of supporting American jobs through wins at the W.T.O., smart use of all our enforcement options and dialogue that gets results for American workers as well.”
她补充道，“本届政府在支持美国就业方面，接连取得成绩，包括赢得世贸组织诉讼、妥善运用所有执法选项，以及开展对话，为美国工人争取利益。”
The Chinese export bases policies are so numerous, and affect so many different kinds of products, that it would take dozens of separate antisubsidy cases to tackle all of them. That would require a far lengthier legal effort than a single trade organization case, which the United States has started by essentially filing a list of the names of the offending policies to China.
中国的出口基地政策名目繁多，影响的产品也多种多样，以至于美国必须分别提起数十项反补贴诉讼来应对所有的政策。这样需要的法律努力就比单宗世贸组织诉讼更为耗时。通过向世贸组织提交了一份罗列了违规政策的清单，美国实质上已经开始了这样的努力。
Under the process, the United States essentially demands that Chinese officials start discussing how, if at all, the many policies are consistent with China’s commitment as a World Trade Organization member not to subsidize exports.
通过这一过程，美国本质上是在要求中国官员说明，中国作为世贸组织成员国，上述的大量政策是否符合不补贴出口的承诺，如果符合则请阐明理由。
Another advantage of such cases is that they require the United States only to prove that the Chinese policies violate organization rules. Antisubsidy cases at the Commerce Department also require proof that a domestic industry has suffered financial injury as a direct result of the foreign subsidies. That tends to be harder to prove when the industry’s health is improving, and auto sales are rising this year as Americans replace aging cars.
向世贸组织申诉的另一个便利之处在于，美国只需要证明中国的政策违反了世贸组织的规则即可。而向美国商务部(Commerce Department)提起反补贴诉讼，还需要提供证据证明，外国补贴直接造成国内产业的经济损失。在美国汽车产业经营状况正在改善，汽车销售因美国人更换旧车而有所提升的情况下，要证明这一点则更为困难一些。
The Obama administration said the subsidies to exporters in China totaled at least $1 billion from 2009 through 2011. But Chinese customs data shows that the country exported $56 billion worth of automotive equipment during these years.
奥巴马政府声称，从2009年到2011年，中国至少给汽车出口商提供了高达10亿美元的补贴。然而中国海关数据显示，中国同期汽车产品出口额高达560亿美元。
Obama administration officials say that something needs to be done about Chinese subsidies, and the automotive subsidy case is a start. As China has lost previous W.T.O. cases, subsidies have increasingly been hidden in local and provincial budgets, and it is these that Monday’s case pursued.
奥巴马政府官员称，需要采取某些行动，来应对中国的补贴问题，而汽车行业补贴案件只是一个开始。随着中国在世贸组织过去的案件中败诉，补贴越来越多地隐藏在地方政府和省级政府的预算中，而周一提起的诉讼追究的就是这些做法。
The filing lists more than 80 specific policies, often at the municipal level, that are part of China’s “export bases” program. W.T.O. rules explicitly ban countries from providing subsidies that are contingent on goods being exported.
诉讼文件中列出了80多项经常在市一级政府实行的具体政策，这些政策是中国“出口基地”计划的一部分。世贸组织规则明确禁止成员国针对出口商品提供补贴。
Finding offending policies at the municipal and provincial level has long been a challenge for governments filing W.T.O. cases worldwide, because of language barriers and because many rules are not published. The Obama administration persuaded Congress early this year to authorize more money for trade enforcement staff, and it said on Monday that the export subsidy case was one result of this.
由于存在语言障碍，而且许多政策并不明文公布，因此世界各国在向世贸组织提起诉讼时，查找市级、省级政府的违规政策，长期以来一直是一个挑战。奥巴马政府今年早些时候曾说服国会，向贸易执法人员提供更多预算。奥巴马政府表示，出口补贴案件就是这样做的成果之一。
The global trading system has long been founded in part on the assumption that companies will act to defend operations in their home countries, and will file antidumping and antisubsidy cases against imports. But that logic has eroded in recent years in the case of China as practically every Western multinational corporation — as well as a growing number of small and medium-size companies — has moved factories and other operations to China and become reluctant to risk retaliation by filing trade cases.
长期以来，全球贸易体系的基础，一定程度上在于假定企业会保护其本国的业务，并针对进口商品提起反倾销和反补贴诉讼。然而近几年，具体到中国的情况，这个逻辑已经渐渐动摇。几乎所有西方跨国公司，以及越来越多的中小型企业，都将工厂和业务转移到中国，而且由于担忧可能会遭到报复，不愿提起贸易诉讼。
When the Obama administration took office, it sought economic cooperation with China on many issues while bringing a series of trade cases on mostly industries that were either small or politically less prominent. The conspicuous exception was a decision in September 2009 to impose tariffs on tires imported from China, and even that decision was to some extent forced by complex legal deadlines in the United States.
奥巴马政府上台之时，曾寻求在多项议题上与中国开展经济合作，同时也提起了一系列贸易诉讼，但主要是针对规模或政治影响较小的产业。一个广受关注的例外发生在2009年9月，奥巴马政府当时决定对从中国进口的轮胎加征关税。但就连这个决定，在某种程度上也是因为迫于美国复杂的法律期限才作出的。
The administration chose not to take a broad action on Chinese solar panels after it was asked to do so in a legal filing by the United Steelworkers in September 2010. That left imports essentially unrestricted as a series of solar panel manufacturers in the United States then went bankrupt or cut production and laid off workers in the following year.
美国钢铁工人联合会(United Steelworkers)在2010年9月向奥巴马政府提出法律投诉，要求对中国太阳能组件采取行动。但奥巴马政府选择不对中国太阳能组件采取广泛的行动，因此进口实质上并未受到限制。次年，美国一系列太阳能组件生产商破产、减产或裁员。
What is left of the American solar panel manufacturing industry has only begun to stabilize after a German company with sizable operations in the United States and essentially none in China, SolarWorld, filed antidumping and antisubsidy cases late last year, resulting in the imposition in the spring of combined tariffs totaling more than 33 percent.
直到一家德国公司去年提出反倾销及反补贴投诉，美国太阳能组件制造产业所剩的企业的经营状况才稳定下来。这家名为SolarWorld的德国公司在美国有大量业务，而在中国几乎没有业务。投诉的结果是，今年春天美国开始对中国太阳能组件征收合计逾33%的关税。
A W.T.O. case does have one big advantage over antidumping and antisubsidy cases: the filing can take aim at policies that are distorting trade all over the world, helping American companies export to other countries. By contrast, antidumping and antisubsidy cases protect only a country’s home market.
与反倾销及反补贴案件相比，世贸组织案件有一个极大的便利之处：提起的诉讼可以针对扭曲全球贸易的政策，因而会帮助美国企业向其他国家出口商品。与此相对比，反倾销和反补贴案件只能保护一国的国内市场。
Chinese automakers have been rapidly expanding their shares of markets in emerging markets in South America, for example, traditionally an area of strength for Detroit manufacturers. The W.T.O. case is aimed at all Chinese automotive subsidies regardless of whether the exports are going to the United States.
中国汽车制造商在新兴市场的份额一直在迅速扩大，例如在南美洲。传统上，南美洲一直是底特律汽车厂商的优势地区。这宗世贸组织案件针对的是中国所有的汽车产业补贴，无论产品是否对美国出口。
The value of such complaints to W.T.O. dispute resolution, instead of domestic trade remedies like antidumping, “is that if the subsidies are withdrawn, it can improve the competitive position of U.S. exporters in other markets,” said Carolyn B. Gleason, a partner and longtime trade lawyer in the Washington office of McDermott Will & Emery.
McDermott Will & Emery律师事务所合伙人，从业经验丰富的贸易律师卡罗琳·B·格利森(Carolyn B. Gleason)说，将这样的投诉提交给世贸组织争端解决机制，而不是寻求反倾销制裁等国内贸易救济手段，意义在于“如果补贴取消了，就能改善美国出口商在其他市场上的竞争地位。”
