中國新金融監管機構亮相，疑問大於期望

China’s New Effort to Tame Its Financial System May Disappoint

KEITH BRADSHER
2017年11月9日
BEIJING — China on Wednesday released fresh details about a new financial regulatory body intended to calm a financial system that in recent years has endured a stock market crash, a huge exodus of money outside the country and the rapid accumulation of debt.
北京——中國週三公布了新成立的一個金融監管機構的最新細節。該機構旨在讓近年來經歷了股市崩盤、巨額資金外流和債務迅速累積的中國金融制度穩定下來。
But the details may raise more questions than they answer, and could disappoint those looking for a strong hand to rein in the financial system underpinning the world’s second-largest economy.
但這些細節引發的疑問多過它們解答的問題，並且可能會讓一些人失望，他們一直指望出現一隻強有力的手，控制支撐中國這個全球第二大經濟體的金融制度。
Official Chinese media reported late Wednesday that a new Financial Stability and Development Committee had held its first meeting, nearly four months after President Xi Jinping ordered its creation. It said the meeting was led by Ma Kai, a 71-year-old vice premier.
中國官方媒體週三晚上報導，在習近平下令成立金融穩定發展委員會四個月後，該委員會舉行了首次會議。報導稱，這次會議由現年71歲的副總理馬凱主持。
At the meeting, Mr. Ma stressed that China’s financial system should serve the real economy, according to the official Xinhua news agency — namely, making money available to businesses that need it. He also stressed financial security, the report said.
據官方通訊社新華社稱，馬凱在會上強調，中國的金融系統應該服務於實體經濟，也就是說要為需要的企業提供資金。報導稱，他還強調了金融安全。
The new committee’s mission appears to fall short of what many in China has expected. Many had speculated that Mr. Xi might combine the country’s central bank with the agencies regulating securities, banking and insurance to create a financial superregulator. Previous problems, like a stock market crash two years ago, were worsened by lack of coordination among different government agencies.
新委員會的任務似乎沒有達到中國國內很多人的期望。很多人本來猜測習近平可能會合併央行和監管證券、銀行、保險的機構，成立一個金融超級監管機構。之前的種種麻煩，比如兩年前的股市崩盤，因為不同政府機構之間缺乏協調而惡化。
But the commission that emerged seemed more a minnow than a whale. None of the current agencies disappeared, and the commission was merely assigned a small office in a corner of the central bank.
但新成立的這個委員會似乎更像是一條小魚，而不是大鯨魚。現有的機構無一消失，且該委員會只在央行的一個角落裡分到了一間小辦公室。
Mr. Ma himself is something of a lame duck, being past the age when Chinese officials typically retire. While he remains a vice premier in the Chinese government, he lost his seats on senior decision-making bodies within the Chinese Communist Party amid a broad reshuffle last month.
馬凱本人也可以說是一隻跛腳鴨（指處於困境需要幫助的人、事物或機構——譯註），已經過了中國官員通常的退休年齡。儘管依然擔任政府副總理，但在上月的一場全面改組中，他失去了在中共內部高級決策機構的席位。
Still, the committee could be strengthened later, or more details may emerge that further outline its powers.
但該委員會今後可能會得到加強，又或者以後會有更多細節出來，進一步勾勒出其權力的輪廓。
The decision to hold the committee as President Trump arrived in Beijing for a meeting with Mr. Xi may be an indication that the Chinese leadership is still focused on the country’s financial vulnerabilities, even if a long-term leader to address those vulnerabilities has not yet been chosen.
委員會在川普總統抵達北京與習近平會面時召開會議的決定，可能表明中國領導人的重點依然在中國的金融風險上，雖然還沒選出一個長期負責應對這些風險的領導人。
China’s stock market crash two years ago, and a subsequent flight of money that shaved about $1 trillion off its vast hoard of foreign exchange reserves, illustrated the weaknesses of the country’s financial system. While China has a vast cushion of savings and a still-considerable pile of foreign exchange reserves, its financial system has become increasingly reliant on debt. Critics say it also does a poor job of funneling money to small businesses and entrepreneurs who could spark new sources of growth.
中國兩年前的股市崩盤和隨後出現的資金外逃說明了中國金融制度的脆弱。資金外逃導致中國龐大的外匯儲備減少了大約一萬億美元（約合6.6萬億元人民幣）。儘管中國有規模龐大的儲蓄作為緩衝，並且現有的外匯儲備規模依然相當大，但中國的金融系統越來越依賴債務。批評人士還表示，中國金融系統沒有做好為可能會帶來新增長點的小企業和創業者提供資金的工作。
Zhou Xiaochuan, the governor of the country’s central bank for the past 15 years, and who is also expected to retire this winter, has been issuing increasingly dire warnings in recent weeks about the country’s mountain of debt, particularly at state-owned enterprises and local governments.
最近幾週，過去15年來一直擔任中國央行行長，並且預計也將於今年冬天退休的周小川對中國堆積如山的債務，尤其是國有企業和地方政府的債務，發出了愈發嚴峻的警告。
“In recent years, nonperforming loans have risen, eroding banking capital and risk resilience,” Mr. Zhou warned in a statement released Saturday. The statement went on to warn about rising bond defaults as well as Ponzi schemes masquerading as internet finance companies.
「近年來，不良貸款有所上升，侵蝕銀行業資本金和風險抵禦能力，」周小川在週六發表的文章中警告說。文章接著對對不斷增多的債券違約和冒充互聯網金融公司的龐氏騙局發出了警告。
President Xi had authorized the commission in July at a National Financial Work Conference: a gathering where China’s leaders just talk about financial regulation, held only once every five years.
習近平在7月的全國金融工作會議上授權成立該委員會。在每五年舉行一次的全國金融工作會議上，中國領導人只談金融監管。
Having a vice premier run the first meeting nonetheless raises the profile of the commission. It could establish a precedent that the commission may continue to be run by a vice premier. Vice premiers are at least two rungs more senior in the Chinese system than the minister-level officials who run the central bank and the financial regulatory agencies.
儘管如此，讓一位副總理主持首次會議提高了該委員會的形象。這可能開了一個先例，該委員會也許會繼續由副總理領導。在中國的制度中，副總理至少比領導央行和金融監管機構的部級官員高兩級。
Its next leader might also be considerably more influential and politically connected. Some experts suggest that Liu He, a close economic adviser to Mr. Xi, may emerge as the successor to Mr. Ma as the vice premier with responsibility over wide areas of the economy, including financial regulation.
它的下一任負責人也許會有影響力得多，政治人脈廣得多。一些專家稱，與習近平關係密切的經濟顧問劉鶴，可能會接替馬凱擔任副總理，負責廣泛的經濟領域，包括金融監管。
Mr. Liu has a reputation for advocating a greater reliance on market forces in economic reform policies. By contrast, Mr. Xi has taken a greater interest in strengthening the role of the Communist Party in most aspects of life in China, including the financial sector.
劉鶴以在經濟改革政策中主張更多依靠市場力量出名。相比之下，習近平更感興趣的是，加強共產黨在中國生活的大多數方面，包括金融領域的作用。
