2013年中國值得關注的八個趨勢

For China’s ‘Great Renewal,’ 8 Trends to Keep an Eye On

利明璋
2012年12月13日
BEIJING
北京
The Bo Xilai scandal, an economic downturn and the leadership switch from Hu Jintao to Xi Jinping made 2012 one of China’s most eventful years. It is hard to imagine that next year will be as exciting, but there will be change.
薄熙來醜聞、經濟增長放緩、從胡錦濤到習近平的領導換屆，2012年的中國大事不斷。很難想像明年會同樣跌宕起伏，但是改變卻在所難免。
Here are eight likely trends for 2013:
2013年可能出現的八個趨勢： 
SHIFT IN GROWTH The economy is rebounding after slowing sharply through the first three quarters of 2012. The recovery is mostly stimulus-driven and heavily weighted toward state-owned enterprises and large industrial companies. The 2013 official target for economic growth is expected to be set at 7.5 percent, and the economy should meet it, although excessive debt, a bloated state sector, entrenched special interests, income disparity, rural-urban inequality and lagging corporate profits will remain problems. Expect increased emphasis on the quality of growth, urbanization and rural reform as well as some painful progress in rebalancing from overinvestment to consumption-driven growth.
增長轉變。在2012年前三季度急劇放緩之後，經濟現在開始恢復動力。目前，經濟復蘇主要是由刺激措施驅動，而且主要傾向於國有企業和大型工業企業。預計官方會把2013年的經濟增長目標定在7.5%。儘管依然存在過度負債、國有部門結構臃腫、特殊利益根深蒂固、收入差距增大、城鄉分化嚴重以及企業利潤增長緩慢這些問題，中國經濟應該可以達到這個目標。預計重點將越來越多地放在增長的質量、城市化進程，以及農村改革上。經濟也會從過度投資向消費驅動增長轉變，而這個再平衡的過程會充滿艱辛。
NEW LEADER Xi Jinping has moved quickly in his first three weeks to project an image of change. The government faces a growing expectations gap with its citizens and needs to move quickly to rebuild public confidence. Mr. Xi is talking a lot about the Chinese dream and the “great renewal of the Chinese nation” — nationalist concepts with significant appeal. Mr. Xi’s choice of Shenzhen for his first domestic inspection trip is a symbolic tribute to Deng Xiaoping’s 1992 southern tour that restarted reforms after 1989. Mr. Xi’s visit is most likely a signal that he is serious about pushing forward with changes. Expect renewed energy around overhauls, primarily economic and administrative but possibly some political ones.
新任領導人。從上台伊始，習近平在前三個星期已迅速展現出一副變革的形象。目前，政府面臨著民眾不斷拉大的期望差距，需要快速重拾公眾信心。習近平談到許多關於“中國夢”和“中華民族偉大復興”的東西，這都是些很受歡迎的民族主義話題。他選擇深圳作為在國內視察的第一站，是對1992年鄧小平南巡致敬，頗具象徵性意義。鄧小平南巡使改革開放在1989年之後得以重續。習近平的這次視察很可能是他正在努力推動改革的一個信號，預計會給改革注入新的活力，其主要對象是經濟與行政改革，但也可能會涉及一些政治改革。
CRACKDOWN ON CORRUPTION The new leadership has begun a public crackdown on corruption. One relatively senior official and several minor officials have already fallen. Internet exposés brought down the minor officials, but there are reports that the Communist Party’s central disciplinary inspection commission has started new investigations into money-laundering through Macau. Expect this antigraft campaign to ensnare more senior officials and to be the most effective in recent memory, yet ultimately stop short of addressing the systemic issues that have allowed corruption to flourish.
打擊腐敗。新領導層已經展開了一輪公開的反腐行動。一名級別相對較高的官員和一些低層官員已經落馬。網絡曝光引發了一些地方官員下台，還有消息稱，共產黨中央紀律檢查委員會已針對在澳門的洗錢犯罪活動展開了一輪新調查。這次反腐運動有望會將更多高級官員繩之以法，成為近年來最有效的反腐舉措，但這最終卻依然不能解決滋生腐敗的體制問題。
TROUBLE WITH THE NEIGHBORS China’s external environment is increasingly complex. Mr. Xi is likely to work hard maintaining stable relations with the United States, while at the same time expanding China’s military capabilities and asserting its regional interests. China has unsettled its neighbors with its approach to the disputes over the Diaoyu/Senkaku Islands in the East China Sea and the various islands in the South China Sea. Both issues look intractable, so a solution is unlikely in 2013. Expect a quickening arms race in Asia, increasingly nationalist rhetoric, continued tensions with Japan, Vietnam and the Philippines and growing risks of misunderstandings — and possible mishaps — as the various countries send more vessels into the disputed areas.
與鄰國的問題。中國的外部環境正變得越來越複雜。習近平很可能會努力維持與美國關係的穩定，同時擴張中國軍事實力並維護中國在該地區的利益。中國對東海的釣魚島/尖閣諸島，以及南海一些爭議島嶼的主權問題的處理方式，已經讓鄰國感到不安。這兩個問題都很棘手，因此2013年不太可能給出解決辦法。預計亞洲會出現軍備競賽加速的態勢，民族主義言論越來越多、與日本、越南和菲律賓的緊張局勢繼續存在，而且隨着這些國家向有爭議的海域派出更多船隻，出現誤解或意外的風險越來越大。 
NOT EASY BEING GREEN China has some of the worst environmental problems in the world, and they increasingly pose a threat to social stability. The leadership is aware of the problems, and the report from the 18th Communist Party Congress included a section calling for improving the environment. Expect increased emphasis on clean energy, conservation and green growth, though the challenges are so huge that it is hard to be optimistic.
環保並非易事。中國有着世界上最棘手的一些環境問題，這給社會穩定帶來的威脅與日俱增。領導層意識到了這些問題，共產黨十八大報告也包括了相關內容，呼籲改善環境。預計將會越來越強調清潔能源、節能以及綠色增長的重要性，但面臨的挑戰依然非常嚴峻，所以很難持樂觀態度。
RISING ACTIVISM Environmental issues are leading to political activism. In 2012 China had several not-in-my-backyard protests involving thousands of people, many of whom mobilized online. Social media use, especially via smartphones, is rapidly expanding from urban to rural users and is making it much harder to cover up problems. The government now requires that all major industrial projects pass a social risk assessment before they begin. Expect more environmental protests as citizens become more aware of their rights and are emboldened to protect them.
行動主義不斷上升。環境問題正在引發政治行動主義。2012年，中國發生了一些“別在我家後院”式的抗議活動，參加抗議的人數成千上萬，很多人是被互聯網信息動員起來。社交媒體，特別是智能手機的使用，正快速從城市用戶向農村蔓延，這也讓掩蓋問題變得更加困難。現在，政府要求所有大型工業項目在開工之前都要通過社會風險評估。預計隨着市民自身權力意識增強、維護自身權力的勇氣增大，未來會出現更多環境抗議活動。 
INTERNET PRESSURE China has the most Internet users in the world. At some point in 2013 its population on the Internet will exceed 600 million people. China has a sophisticated Internet censorship and filtering system that is unlikely to disappear. But that does not mean the Internet will not pressure the government. Cheap smartphones with good cameras and 3G connectivity are proliferating, in large part thanks to Google’s Android mobile operating system. China is expected to have several hundred million smartphone users by the end of 2013. The Internet is unlikely to bring change to the system, certainly. But all those smartphone-wielding, social media-using citizens will force a more accountable authoritarianism. Expect more scandals to break online and increasingly responsive, accountable government at all levels.
網絡壓力。中國互聯網用戶人數已居世界之最。2013年，中國網民數量將會超過六億。中國不太可能會取消其精密複雜的互聯網審查和過濾系統。但這並不意味着互聯網就不會給政府帶來壓力。主要由於谷歌的安卓移動運行系統的出現，裝有攝像頭的廉價3G智能手機正在泛濫。預計，2013年底中國智能手機用戶將增至數億。互聯網不太可能會為制度帶來變化。但是，所有這些持有智能手機的人將會利用社交媒體迫使集權統治變得更加負責。預計互聯網上會爆出更多醜聞，這將迫使各級政府變得更負責，反應也更靈敏。
OVERSEAS INVESTMENT Chinese companies are going global, both by expanding their operations overseas and by acquiring foreign firms. Yet Chinese investment is a contentious issue in the United States and other developed countries, and several deals failed in 2012 at the hands of regulators. Huawei, a Chinese networking and telecommunications giant, is now effectively blocked from doing business in the United States because of security concerns. Regardless, Chinese companies will continue to invest overseas, both to increase and to secure resources. Tencent, for one, is on the verge of becoming China’s first globally competitive Internet company. Its WeChat mobile social networking application already has more than 200 million users and may give Facebook a run for its money in many developing countries. Expect growing overseas investment, increasing political scrutiny of Chinese deals and possibly invitations to WeChat from your social networking friends.
海外投資。中國公司通過擴展國外運營和收購外國公司，正在邁向全球化。然而，來自中國的投資在美國和其他發達國家卻引發爭議。在2012年，由於監管問題，出現了幾起失敗案例。由於美方對安全的擔憂，中國網絡線路和電信巨頭華為在美國的商業活動幾乎已無法進行。即便如此，中國公司將繼續在海外投資，來增加和獲取資源。例如，騰訊公司即將成為中國第一家具有國際競爭力的互聯網公司。其移動社交媒體應用程序微信已經擁有逾2億用戶，在很多發展中國家可能會成為Facebook的勁敵。預計，海外投資、對中國商業交易的政治審查，以及社交網絡上的朋友向你發出的網絡聊天邀請都會有所增加。
All in all, Mr. Xi appears to be a change-oriented nationalist. His energetic and straightforward style, his apparent commitment to fighting corruption and his determination to reinvigorate at least economic reform should buy the government time to tackle some of China’s difficult problems in 2013.
總之，習近平似乎是一個重視改革的民族主義者。他那充滿活力、直截了當的風格，打擊腐敗的公開決心以及至少要為經濟改革重新注入活力的決心，理應為政府爭取時間來在2013年解決其所面臨的一些棘手問題。
