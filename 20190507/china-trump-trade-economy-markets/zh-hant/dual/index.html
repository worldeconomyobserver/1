川普的新威脅給習近平帶來難題

Trump’s Trade War Threat Poses Problems for China and Investors

KEITH BRADSHER
2019年5月7日
Update: China’s Commerce Ministry announced on Tuesday afternoon that Vice Premier Liu He, one of the country’s leading economic policymakers, would join a delegation of Chinese officials traveling to Washington for further trade talks on Thursday and Friday with the Trump administration.
更新：中國商務部週二下午宣布，中國經濟政策制定者之一、副總理劉鶴將與中國官員代表團一同前往華盛頓，他們將在週四和週五與川普政府展開進一步談判。
The prospect of a wider trade war between the United States and China sent global financial markets whipsawing on Monday and could force Beijing to make difficult decisions if it hopes to preserve its nascent economic recovery.
中美貿易戰進一步擴大的前景週一在全球金融市場引發劇烈震盪，並可能會迫使中國做出艱難的選擇——如果它希望保持經濟復甦苗頭的話。
President Trump upended what appeared to be steady progress toward reaching a trade pact after he threatened on Sunday to impose still more tariffs on Chinese-made goods unless Beijing moves closer to a deal. Liu He, the Chinese vice premier overseeing economic policy and Beijing’s lead trade negotiator, had been set to travel to Washington for talks scheduled for Wednesday that were widely seen as the potential last round before reaching a trade deal.
川普總統週日威脅要對中國製造的商品加徵更多關稅，除非北京方面向達成協議更靠近一步，此舉打破了兩國正在為達成協議取得穩步進展的外表。中國負責經濟政策的副總理兼首席貿易談判代表劉鶴本來已計劃前往華盛頓，參加定於週三舉行的談判。外界普遍認為，這是達成貿易協議前的最後一輪談判。
Mr. Liu’s plans were unclear as of Monday. At a daily news conference on Monday, Geng Shuang, a spokesman for China’s Foreign Ministry, said a team of Chinese trade negotiators was still planning a trip to Washington. Still, Mr. Geng declined to say when the visit might take place or whether Mr. Liu would be on it.
截至週一，外界尚不清楚劉鶴的計劃。中國外交部發言人耿爽在週一的例行記者會上說，中國貿易談判團隊仍在準備前往華盛頓。不過，耿爽拒絕透露何時前往，以及劉鶴是否會去。
Earlier on Monday in China, people familiar with the talks said officials are considering whether Mr. Liu should keep his plans to visit Washington. The people spoke on condition of anonymity because they were not authorized to speak publicly.
中國時間週一早些時候，了解貿易談判情況的人士說，官員們正在考慮劉鶴是否應該繼續訪問華盛頓的計劃。這些人沒有被授權公開評論而要求不具名。
The uncertainty sent global markets reeling on Monday. Chinese shares fell more than 6 percent before recovering a modest amount of ground, while Hong Kong’s stock market fell 2.9 percent. Major markets in Europe began the day more than 1 percent lower, and futures that track shares in the United States suggested Wall Street would open considerably lower as well.
這種不確定性讓週一的全球金融市場動盪不安。中國股市的跌幅一度超過了6%，後來略有回升；香港股市下跌了2.9%。歐洲主要股市當天開盤時的跌幅超過1%，跟蹤美國股市的股票期貨顯示，華爾街開盤時也將大幅走低。
The threats pose a major problem for Xi Jinping, China’s top leader, who had been counting on a trade deal to keep China’s growth engine humming. Broad differences still separate the two sides on many issues, and Mr. Trump’s tweeted threat on Sunday indicated that the United States may be less willing to accept compromises than some of his senior aides had previously seemed to suggest.
這些威脅給中國最高領導人習近平帶來一大難題，他一直指望通過達成貿易協議來維持中國經濟增長引擎的運轉。中美雙方在許多問題上仍存在廣泛分歧，川普週日在Twitter上發出的威脅表明，美國可能不像他的一些高級助手此前似乎暗示的那樣，願意接受妥協。
廣告
China’s economic growth began to slow last year as Beijing tried to tame the country’s overreliance on lending. Mr. Trump’s initial tariffs last year hurt Chinese manufacturers and consumer confidence, worsening the slowdown. China’s economic slowdown limited Mr. Xi’s options to retaliate against American tariffs and put pressure on him to reach a deal.
隨著北京方面試圖遏制中國對借貸的過度依賴，中國經濟的增長速度去年開始放緩。川普去年加徵的首批關稅傷害了中國製造商和消費者的信心，令經濟放緩加劇。中國經濟放緩讓習近平在報復美國關稅的選項上受到限制，並對他施加了達成協議的壓力。
In recent months, thanks in part to new lending, China’s slowdown appeared to stabilize. The prospect of a trade deal also boosted consumer and investor confidence and led many economists to estimate China’s growth would improve.
近幾個月來，部分由於新增貸款，中國經濟放緩的趨勢似乎有所穩定。達成貿易協議的前景也提振了消費者和投資者的信心，並讓許多經濟學家預計中國的經濟增長將會改善。
New tariffs could derail that progress. Through Twitter, Mr. Trump warned on Sunday that he would increase tariffs on $200 billion in Chinese goods at the end of this week and “shortly” impose levies on $325 billion of additional imports.
新關稅可能會擾亂這個進程。週日，川普在Twitter上警告，他將在本週結束的時候對價值2000億美元的中國商品提高關稅，並「很快」會對另外3250億美元的中國進口商品加徵關稅。
“If tariffs are hiked this Friday and new tariffs come soon after that the biggest negative impact will likely occur in the next few months,” Tao Wang, an economist specializing in China at UBS, said in a research note.
「如果本週五提高關稅，並在之後不久加徵新關稅的話，最大的負面影響可能會在未來幾個月出現，」瑞銀(UBS)中國經濟學家汪濤在一份研究簡報中說。
She estimated that a full-blown trade war with the United States could cut China’s economic growth rate by 1.6 to 2 percentage points over the next 12 months. That would be a considerable cut: Last year China’s economy grew 6.6 percent, according to official figures, and the government has sent an official target of 6 percent to 6.5 percent this year.
她估計，中美爆發全面貿易戰可能會在未來12個月內讓中國經濟增長率下降1.6至2個百分點。這將是一個相當大的降幅：據官方數據，中國經濟去年增長了6.6%，政府給今年制定的官方目標是6%到6.5%。
Illustrating the sensitivity of the problem for China’s leaders, Chinese media was mostly silent on Mr. Trump’s threat, in a sign of tight censorship. Still, pictures of graphs showing China’s stock market plunge and veiled references to Mr. Trump were rife on Chinese social media. One popular meme compared Mr. Trump to Thanos, the villain in the blockbuster “Avengers: Endgame” film, saying the American president could kill half of China’s investors with a snap of his fingers.
作為嚴格審查制度的體現，中國媒體對川普的威脅大多保持沉默，表明了這個問題在中國領導人眼裡的敏感性。儘管如此，顯示中國股市暴跌的曲線圖和隱晦提及川普的圖片充斥了中國社群媒體。一個流行的迷因將川普比作影片《復仇者聯盟4：終極遊戲》(Avengers: Endgame)中的反派人物滅霸(Thanos)，稱美國總統打個響指，就能讓中國股民死一半。
廣告
Mr. Xi will face internal difficulties in coping with Mr. Trump’s aggressive threat. He risks weakening his position if he is seen as giving in to American demands, especially in areas where many people in China feel the country has to make progress, such as in technology, if it wants to keep growing at a healthy pace.
習近平在應對川普咄咄逼人的威脅上將面臨諸多內部困難。如果他被視為向美國的要求屈服——尤其在許多中國人覺得國家想保持健康的增長速度，就不得不取得進步的領域（比如科技），他有讓自己的地位削弱的風險。
In fact, Chinese internal politics may be at the root of the sudden hitch in the trade talks.
實際上，貿易談判突然受阻可能是中國的內部政治所致。
The Trump administration has reacted much more strongly than the Chinese government expected to an issue that came up in negotiations last week in Beijing between senior Chinese and American officials, the people familiar with the trade talks said. That was when Chinese negotiators said that any concessions in the trade agreement would need to be done through regulatory and administrative actions by the Chinese government, and not cemented in place through actual changes in Chinese law through the country’s legislature.
了解貿易談判情況的人士說，在中美高級官員上週的北京談判中出現了一個問題，川普政府對這個問題的反應遠遠超出中國政府的預期。中方談判代表當時說，貿易協議中的任何讓步都需要中國政府通過監管和行政手段來解決，而不是通過中國立法機構對法律的實際修改來確定。
Foreign critics have long derided China legislature, the National People’s Congress, as a rubber stamp for the Communist Party. Bills backed by top leaders are approved by overwhelming majorities, often with only a handful of votes against them. But top leaders also do a lot of back-room maneuvering before any bill is introduced. Sometimes the maneuvering goes on for years without a consensus being reached. The congress also meets only once a year, making it difficult to get legislation approved quickly.
長期以來，外國批評人士一直把中國立法機構全國人民代表大會嘲笑為中共的橡皮圖章。高層領導人支持的議案通常都以壓倒性的多數票通過，只有少數人投反對票。但在任何議案出台之前，高層領導人也要在幕後進行許多操作。有時，這種操作進行好幾年都達不成共識。而且，全國人大每年只召開一次會議，因此很難讓立法迅速獲得通過。
The National People’s Congress has a body of top leaders, called the standing committee, that has fairly broad powers and meets roughly every two months through the rest of the year. Still, major bills are supposed to be approved by the entire legislature.
全國人大有一個叫常務委員會的高層領導機構，擁有相當廣泛的權力，在全國人大年度會議結束後餘下的時間裡大約每兩個月開一次會。儘管如此，主要議案應當得到整個立法機構的批准。
James Zimmerman, a partner in the Beijing office of the Perkins Coie law firm, said that China’s State Council, its cabinet, could make the necessary changes on its own. However, handling legal changes quietly through the State Council would make them less visible to provincial and local officials in China. The United States has tried to force through changes in how all levels of government in China handle questions like forced technology transfers and export subsidies, not just the national government in Beijing.
博欽律師事務所(Perkins Coie)北京辦公室合伙人吉莫曼(James Zimmerman)說，中國的國務院可以自己進行必要的改變。但是，用國務院來悄悄處理改變法律的事情，會讓中國的省級和地方官員們不太注意到這些變化。美國試圖迫使中國各級政府改變做法，比如強制外企轉讓技術，為國內企業提供出口補貼等，而不只是讓北京的中央政府改變做法。
廣告
Already, the National People’s Congress in March enacted a new foreign investment law that changed many rules for overseas companies doing business in the country. Doing legislative changes then, before the trade talks had ended, was widely seen as a face-saving move by China. But foreign business groups criticized the new law from the start as inadequate.
全國人民代表大會已在今年3月通過了新的《外商投資法》，改變了海外公司在華經營的許多規定。在貿易談判結束前修改法律被普遍視為是中國挽回面子的做法。但外國商業團體從一開始就批評新法律不夠完善。
Outside the trade talks, Beijing has taken other steps to bolster the economy.
除了貿易談判之外，中國政府也採取了其他措施來提振經濟。
Premier Li Keqiang in March pushed through the legislature a tax cut for manufacturers that is likely to particularly help sectors that may be involved in exporting to the United States. Some Chinese companies have already been offering discounts to American corporate buyers to offset part or all of the cost of President Trump’s tariffs. China’s central bank announced on Monday morning that it was adjusting bank rules to make it easier for small and medium-sized banks to lend more money to the country’s private sector.
今年3月，國務院總理李克強推動立法機構通過了一項針對製造商的減稅措施，這很可能會對涉及向美國出口的行業特別有利。一些中國公司已向美國的企業買家提供折扣，部分或全部抵消川普總統加徵關稅所帶來的成本。中國央行週一上午宣布正在調整銀行規則，讓中小銀行更易向民營部門放貸。
There is little else that Beijing can do in the meantime to mollify its people and businesses or the broader markets.
與此同時，中國政府在安撫民眾、企業或更廣泛的市場方面，幾乎沒有什麼別的辦法。
“It’s a huge uncertainty,” Hao Hong, the chief strategist at the Bank of Communications’ international operations, said, “and there’s no model that allows you to estimate your risk.”
「這是一個巨大的不確定性，」交通銀行國際業務首席策略師洪灝說，「而且沒有一個能讓你用來估計風險的模型。」

Keith Bradsher是《紐約時報》上海分社社長。
