外企熱情消退，繼續留在中國變成一種磨練和戰鬥

As Zeal for China Dims, Global Companies Complain More Boldly

黃瑞黎
2017年4月20日
 BEIJING — When Amazon announced its entry into the Chinese cloud computing market last year, Chinese state media celebrated it as “a strong signal” that “there is tremendous room for foreign firms to expand in the Chinese market as long as they comply with local regulations.”
北京——去年，當亞馬遜(Amazon)宣布進入中國雲計算市場時，中國國有媒體將之譽為「一個強有力的信號」，表明「只要符合當地法規，外資企業在中國市場上就有極大的擴張空間」。
Today, cloud computing is the latest area of contention between China and increasingly frustrated global companies.
如今，雲計算成了日益沮喪的全球公司與中國發生爭執的又一個領域。
In late March, a group of more than 50 American lawmakers sent a letter to the Chinese ambassador to the United States, Cui Tiankai, taking direct aim at China’s restrictions on cloud computing. They wrote that current and draft regulations would force the transfer of valuable intellectual property to Chinese companies and effectively bar foreign cloud service providers “from operating or competing fairly in China.”
3月底，50多位美國議員致信中國駐美大使崔天凱，直接抨擊中國對雲計算領域的限制。他們寫道，現行條例和條例草案將會強制把有價值的知識產權轉讓給中國企業，並在實質上禁止外國雲服務提供商「在中國經營或公平競爭」。
“As we see it,” the lawmakers wrote in the letter, which was viewed by The New York Times, “these restrictions are fundamentally protectionist and anticompetitive.” Many of the lawmakers hail from states where two major cloud computing players, Amazon and Microsoft, have operations.
「在我們看來，」《紐約時報》查閱的這封信中寫道，「這些限制從根本上是保護主義和反競爭的。」許多寫信的議員都來自亞馬遜和微軟這兩個主要雲計算公司開展業務的州。
Big global companies, which have long worked to keep from rocking the boat in China, are showing a bit more tolerance for making waves. The business groups that represent them have become more critical of China’s industrial policies and ambitions. Businesses are increasingly complaining to friendly lawmakers, and those lawmakers have become more vocal on their behalf.
大型全球性公司長期以來一直努力避免在中國惹是生非，如今已經可以容忍掀起一些風浪。代表他們的企業團體對中國的產業政策與野心提出愈來愈多的批評。企業也日漸向那些友好的議員們抱怨，而議員們也愈來愈願意為他們發聲。
Profiles in courage are still hard to find, as businesses fear Chinese retaliation if they complain directly or too loudly. For example, among the companies that would benefit most from any shift in China’s cloud computing regulations, Microsoft, through a spokesman, declined to comment, and Amazon did not respond to requests for comment.
勇敢的人物依然難以找到，因為企業如果直接抱怨或是抱怨的聲音太大，就會擔心中國人的報復。就拿那些可能從中國雲計算規則轉變中獲益最大的公司來說，微軟通過發言人拒絕置評，亞馬遜並沒有回應置評請求。
The shift is also a gradual one, taking place in the years since the 2008 Olympics in Beijing, as the promise of a more open China faded. Still, people in business circles in China say the sense of disappointment in China’s business prospects is growing.
自從2008年北京奧運會以來，中國會越來越開放的承諾在漸漸褪色，這一轉變也開始漸漸發生。在中國的商界人士表示，對中國業務前景的失望之感還在增長。
“The enthusiasm is gone,” said James McGregor, chairman of the greater China region for the consulting firm APCO Worldwide. “Foreign businesses here used to be very excited about China and quite enthusiastic. Now, it’s kind of a grind and a battle.”
「熱情已經消失了,」諮詢公司安可公關顧問公司(APCO Worldwide)的大中華區董事長麥健陸(James McGregor)說。「這裡的外國企業過去對中國感到興奮，他們相當熱心。現在，這裡成了一種磨練和戰鬥。」
廣告
China remains a lucrative market for foreign-branded cars, iPhones, high-value engineering equipment and other costly items. Still, the growing frustration has muddled the corporate response to President Trump’s tough anti-China rhetoric.
對於外國品牌汽車、iPhone、高價值工程設備和其他昂貴物品來說，中國市場仍然利潤豐厚。然而，越來越多的挫折，令企業對川普總統的強硬反華言辭的看法複雜起來。
“There are companies, which in prior years have been strong supporters in Washington of the U.S.-China relationship, who are much quieter, and even waiting to see if a tougher approach might get more results with China,” said Jake Parker, vice president for China operations at the U.S.-China Business Council.
「在華盛頓，有些公司前幾年一直是中美關係的堅定支持者，現在他們變得更加安靜，甚至在觀望更強硬的做法是否能夠在中國取得更多成果，」美中貿易全國委員會(U.S.-China Business Council)中國區事務副會長彭捷寧(Jake Parker)說。
In one criticism on Tuesday, William Zarit, the chairman of the American Chamber of Commerce in China, expressed concern about President Trump’s offer of trade concessions in exchange for greater Chinese support in pressuring North Korea over its nuclear ambitions.
週二，中國美國商會(American Chamber of Commerce in China)主席蔡瑞德(William Zarit)發表批評，他擔憂川普總統提出貿易讓步，以換取中國在迫使朝鮮放棄核野心方面給予更多支持。
“If the United States gives concessions in the trade area so that we don’t push as hard on leveling the playing field in all these different areas,” Mr. Zarit said at a news conference in Beijing on Tuesday, “I think it’s a shame.”
「如果美國在貿易領域做出讓步，令我們不能在各種領域內努力推進，爭取平等的競爭環境，」週二，蔡瑞德在北京的記者招待會上說：「我認為這令人遺憾。」
In public, businesses work hard to strike a sunny note. Last November, Jay Carney, Amazon’s senior vice president for global corporate affairs and former White House spokesman, made no mention of his company’s challenges there when speaking at China’s World Internet Conference, according to a Chinese-language transcript of his remarks in state media.
企業在公開場合要強顏歡笑。去年十一月，在中國舉行的世界互聯網大會上，根據中國國有媒體的中文發言記錄，亞馬遜全球企業事務高級副總裁、前白宮發言人傑伊·卡尼(Jay Carney)完全沒有提到公司面臨的挑戰。
That reticence has bred frustration among some United States officials. In 2015, President Barack Obama told the Business Roundtable, a business group, regarding its complaints about China, “Don’t tell us on the side, ‘We’ve got this problem, you need to look into it, but then — but leave our names out of it.’”
這樣的三緘其口令一些美國官員感到沮喪。2015年，一個名為商業圓桌會議(Business Roundtable)的商業集團對中國有不少怨言，貝拉克·歐巴馬(Barack Obama)總統對他們說，「不要總是悄悄跟我們說，『我們有這個問題，你需要關注一下，不過——不要把我們公司的名字卷進來。』」
廣告
Still, executives say complaining can have an impact. Earlier that same year, Mr. Obama publicly criticized proposed Chinese rules that would require technology companies to turn over encryption keys to Chinese authorities. His administration had also expressed concern about Chinese draft regulations that would require China’s financial sector buy only from “secure and controllable” suppliers, which trade groups said effectively meant Chinese companies. In both cases China relented, temporarily scrapping the banking laws and tempering the language in the antiterrorism law.
然而，公司高管們仍然說投訴可能會產生影響。同年早些時候，歐巴馬公開批評中國在規定提案中要求技術公司將加密密鑰交給中國當局。他的行政部門也對中國的條例草案表示擔憂，其中要求中國金融部門只能從「安全可控」的供應商那裡採購，而貿易團體認為「安全可控」其實意味著中國企業。在這兩個案例裡，中國都讓步了，暫時取消了相關銀行法，並在反恐怖主義法中緩和了措辭。
Some intermediaries between business and China are pushing the government to do even more.
一些企業與中國之間的中介機構正在推動政府做更多的工作。
“Countries need to be persuaded. Words don’t do it,” said Max Baucus, a former United States ambassador to China, adding that the United States needs to use “properly targeted” leverage.
「國家需要被說服，言辭是做不到這一點的，」美國前駐華大使馬克斯·鮑卡斯(Max Baucus)說，美國需要使用「正中要害」的影響力。
“I don’t think the United States has done much of that,” Mr. Baucus said. “We need to have a more strategic economic approach to China.”
「我不認為美國在這方面做得太多了,」鮑卡斯說。「我們需要對中國採取更具戰略性的經濟手段。」
He said Mr. Obama had raised the issue of American companies’ unequal access to the Chinese market repeatedly with Beijing, but these appeals were “insufficient.”
他說，歐巴馬曾一再向北京提出過美國公司不能平等進入中國市場的問題，但這些懇切的要求「還不夠」。
Of all the sectors that China has set its sights on, tech has come under the most pressure. Services like Facebook and Twitter have long been blocked. Last November, China passed a cybersecurity law that would impose security checks on companies in industries like finance and communications and mandatory in-country data storage. In 2015, the American chip maker Qualcomm said it would pay $975 million for violating China’s antimonopoly law. In 2016, Apple’s iBooks Store and iTunes Movies were shut down in China, just six months after they were started there. In the same year, China said it would demand answers to new questions about Microsoft’s business practices in the country.
在中國重點監管的所有行業中，科技行業面臨最大的壓力。像Facebook和Twitter這樣的服務一直遭到封鎖。去年11月，中國通過了一項網路安全法，對金融和通信等行業的公司進行安全檢查，強制規定數據在國內存儲。2015年，美國晶片製造商高通公司(Qualcomm)表示，公司將因違反中國的反壟斷法而支付9.75億美元。2016年，蘋果的iBooks Store和iTunes電影在中國的業務僅僅開始了六個月就遭關閉。同年，中國方面表示，將就微軟在該國開展業務中出現的新問題向其提出質詢。
廣告
In cloud computing, China mandates that foreign companies work with a local partner, and the foreign companies are subject to equity restrictions that prevent them from owning a controlling stake in the cloud company. New draft regulations would make it even more difficult for them to get licenses for operation, block them from using their brands and logos to market their services and require them to “terminate the transmission of” and report any information posted or transmitted by users that “violates relevant Chinese laws and regulations.”
在雲計算方面，中國要求外國公司必須與當地夥伴合作，外國公司將受到股權限制，以阻止他們擁有這家雲公司的控股權。新的條例草案將使得他們更難獲得運營許可證，阻止他們在推廣自己的服務時使用自己的品牌和標識，要求他們「終止傳輸」並舉報用戶上傳或傳輸的任何「違反中國有關法律法規」的信息。
In China, the cloud computing market is estimated to be worth $20 billion by 2020, up from $1.5 billion in 2013, according to the consulting group Bain. Both Amazon and Microsoft operate in China through partnerships with local companies. In contrast, Aliyun, the cloud services unit of the Chinese web giant Alibaba, has its own data centers in the United States.
據諮詢組織貝恩公司(Bain)稱，2013年，中國的雲計算市場價值15億美元，到2020年估計將達到20億美元。亞馬遜和微軟都通過與當地公司的合作在中國經營。相比之下，中國網路巨頭阿里巴巴的雲服務部門阿里雲在美國擁有自己的數據中心。
For decades, Western companies have stood with China even during rocky moments in the Sino-American relationship. When Congress threatened to revoke China’s most-favored-nation trading status in the 1990s because of concerns over human rights, the American Chamber of Commerce in China, or AmCham, headed to Washington to defend Beijing.
幾十年來，西方公司即使在中美關係充滿動盪的時期也站在中國一邊。1990年代，當國會因為對人權的擔憂而威脅要撤銷中國的貿易最惠國待遇時，美國商會曾前往華盛頓捍衛北京。
More recently, the complaints from Western companies have gotten louder, as China’s economic growth slows and unease grows about Beijing’s failure to deliver on its promise to open markets for foreign companies as well as its Made in China 2025 plan to push for greater self-sufficiency in several industries. AmCham’s 2016 survey of its members showed that 81 percent of its companies felt less welcome in China than before, up from 77 percent in 2015. The survey also showed that 31 percent of its members said the investment environment was deteriorating — the most pessimistic response AmCham has received since it started asking the question in 2011.
最近，隨著中國經濟增長放緩，加之「中國製造」2025計劃推動若干行業內實現更大的自給自足，西方公司對北京未能履行其對外國公司開放市場的承諾感到日益不安。西方公司的怨言也越來越多。中國美國商會2016年的成員調查顯示，其中81％的公司感到在中國不如以前受歡迎，這一數字高於2015年的77％。調查還顯示，31％的成員表示投資環境正在惡化——這是美國商會自2011年開始提出這個問題以來收到的最悲觀的反饋。
“China’s heavy emphasis on building global champions through industrial policies is spiking new levels of concern about China’s commitment to a mutually beneficial relationship,” said Jeremie Waterman, senior director for the Greater China region for the U.S. Chamber of Commerce.
「中國對通過產業政策打造全球事業的重視，讓人進一步擔心中國能否信守互惠關係的承諾，」美國商會大中華地區高級主任王傑(Jeremie Waterman)說。
Mr. Zarit, of AmCham, said a “mixed bag” of companies were unhappy about the investment climate in China, adding that “they are afraid if anything is done, it will upset the apple cart.”
美國商會的蔡瑞德說，來自「各領域」的許多公司對中國的投資環境並不滿意，還說「他們害怕一旦實現這一目標，原有的秩序會被徹底打亂。」
廣告
Mr. Zarit, who led a delegation of eight former AmCham chairmen to meet with the Trump administration officials in Washington in February, said there was a new realization in Washington “that this asymmetrical commercial relationship needs to be addressed in some way.”
二月，蔡瑞德率領一個由八名前中國美國商會主席組成的代表團與華盛頓川普政府官員會晤，他表示，華盛頓產生了一個新的認識：「這種不對稱的商業關係需要以某種方式來解決。」
Mr. McGregor, who was on the trip, said they met Peter Navarro, a strident China critic in charge of trade and industrial policy, and Matthew Pottinger, Mr. Trump’s top Asia policy maker. There, the delegation pushed the concept of reciprocity, meaning that if American companies are bound by restrictions in China, their Chinese counterparts should be subject to the same limits in the United States.
麥健陸也參與了那次會晤，他說代表團會見了負責貿易和產業政策的彼得·納瓦羅(Peter Navarro)，他是一位強硬的中國批評者，此外還有川普政府的亞洲政策負責人馬修·波廷格(Matthew Pottinger)。會面時，代表團強調了互惠的概念，這意味著如果美國公司受到中國的限制，中國同行在美國也應該受到同樣的限制。
Mr. McGregor said the idea got “quite a bit of traction.” He added, “Our attitude is China should be treated as an equal, and an equal needs reciprocity.”
麥健陸說，這個想法得到了「不少認同」，他補充說：「我們的態度是應該平等地對待中國，平等的關係是需要互惠的。」

黃瑞黎(Sui-Lee Wee)是《紐約時報》駐京記者。Keith Bradsher自上海、孟寶勒(Paul Mozur)自香港報導。Zhang Tiantian自北京對本文有研究貢獻。
