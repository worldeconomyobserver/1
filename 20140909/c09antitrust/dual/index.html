美商会称中国反垄断或违世贸协议

U.S. Group Says China Could Be Violating Trade Accords

NEIL GOUGH
2014年9月9日
HONG KONG — A United States business group said on Monday that China’s recent aggressive enforcement of its antimonopoly law could be a violation of its trade agreements, a claim that potentially raises the stakes in a recent pushback among foreign companies that say they are being discriminated against in China.
香港——一个美国商业团体周一表示，中国最近声势浩大的反垄断法执法活动可能违反了其签署的贸易协定，这个宣称可能会让外国公司最近的反击升级，这些公司说他们在中国受到歧视。
Although foreign companies including Microsoft, Qualcomm and Daimler have in recent months been subjected to surprise raids, lengthy investigations and increasing fines by China’s government antitrust enforcers, they have had little recourse against such actions. The path to filing an administrative or legal appeal in China remains unclear and untested, and such steps could risk regulatory backlash. In the meantime, rhetoric from foreign business groups has largely been measured, calling for China to ensure a level playing field as it enforces its antimonopoly law, which took effect in 2008.
虽然包括微软(Microsoft)、高通(Qualcomm)和戴姆勒(Daimler)在内的外国公司都在最近几个月遭到中国政府反垄断执法者的突击搜查、长时间询问，以及不断增长的罚款，但他们几乎没有任何方法来对付这些行动。在中国提请行政或法律复议的途径仍不清楚，公司也未曾试过，而且这种做法可能带来监管加强的风险。与此同时，外国商业团体言论在呼吁中国在执行反垄断法期间仍要确保一个公平的环境时，在很大程度上仍非常克制。中国反垄断法于2008年生效。
But a new report, released on Monday by the U.S. Chamber of Commerce, which is based in Washington, raises the possibility of a new approach to China’s increasingly vigilant antitrust actions: lodging a complaint at the World Trade Organization, which China joined in 2001.
但是，总部设在华盛顿的美国商会在周一发布的一个新报告中，提出了一种对付中国日益严密的反垄断行动的新方法的可能性：向世界贸易组织投诉，中国于2001年加入了世贸组织。
“To the extent that China’s enforcement of the A.M.L. is discriminatory, it arguably violates commitments that China undertook when it acceded to the World Trade Organization,” said the report, referring to the antimonopoly law. “If China applies the A.M.L. in a manner inconsistent with its W.T.O. obligations, this would arguably constitute a violation of W.T.O. law despite being imposed under the guise of competition law.”
“在中国的反垄断法执法有歧视这个意义上，可以说是违反了中国加入世界贸易组织时的承诺，”报告说。“如果中国运用反垄断法的方式与其对世界贸易组织承担的责任不一致，这按理就构成了对世贸组织法律的违背，尽管这种执法是以增强竞争的名义进行的。”
Pursuing such a case at the W.T.O. would be unconventional, and experts noted it would be difficult to gather the extensive documentation required to prove systemic abuse of the antimonopoly law. While China’s National Development and Reform Commission, or N.D.R.C. — one of the three main government antitrust enforcers — recently issued a record price-rigging fine of about $200 million against Japanese auto parts and bearings manufacturers, it has also taken high-profile action against domestic companies, including two of the country’s top distillers of baijiu, a fiery Chinese spirit.
找世贸组织去评判这种案例是一种新奇做法，专家指出，收集表明反垄断法被系统性滥用的大量文字证据将会很困难。而中国政府的三大反垄断执法者之一、国家发展和改革委员会近日对日本汽车零部件和轴承制造商开出了超过12亿元人民币的创纪录罚单，理由是操纵产品供应价格。发改委也曾对国内企业，包括两家全国顶尖的白酒(一种中国烈酒)酿造商采取过高调行动。
The U.S. Chamber report noted that the antimonopoly law could be used as a tool to allow the market to play a primary role in China’s economy, a stated goal of the proposals announced in November 2013 by President Xi Jinping after a key plenary meeting of the Communist Party leadership.
美国商会的报告指出，反垄断法可以作为一种工具，让市场在中国经济中发挥主要作用，这是2013年11月的十八大后习近平宣布的党的目标之一，十八大是共产党领导人的一次重要的全体会议。
“Implementation of the A.M.L. provides an enormous opportunity for China to accelerate its economic transition by boosting competition and reducing the prominence of monopolies and oligopolies in its economy; increasing consumer welfare, choice and consumption; and stimulating market-driven innovation,” said the 91-page report.
“反垄断法的实施提供了一个巨大的机会，让中国能够通过扩大竞争、减少经济中的垄断和寡头，加速其经济的转型，增加消费者福利、选择和消费，以及刺激由市场驱动的创新，”这篇91页的报告写道。
“However, China’s enforcement of the A.M.L. is not yet living up to this ideal,” it said, airing some harsh criticism of the way the N.D.R.C., the Ministry of Commerce and the State Administration of Industry and Commerce carry out their duties in enforcing the law.
“但是，中国的反垄断执法尚未实现这个理想，“报告说，并对国家发改委、商务部和工商总局履行他们执法职责的方法提出一些严厉的批评。
Fines and other antimonopoly directives “often appear designed to advance industrial policy and boost national champions,” the report said. Regulators “rely insufficiently on sound economic analysis, intellectual property rights have been curtailed in the name of competition law, and A.M.L. enforcement suffers from procedural and due process shortcomings.”
罚款以及其他反垄断指导意见“经常看似是为推动产业政策和帮助国内大企业而设计的，”报告说。监管者“没有足够地依赖严谨的经济分析，以增加竞争的名义，限制了知识产权的保护，反垄断法执法也在步骤和正当法律程序上存在不足之处。”
China would no doubt be staunchly opposed to any attempt by foreign governments to raise the issue of its antitrust enforcement at the W.T.O. level. Senior Chinese officials have repeatedly said they are not focusing specifically on foreign companies and that equal treatment is being extended to all.
中国无疑会坚决反对外国政府把中国反垄断执法问题提到世界贸易组织层次的任何企图。中国高层官员一再表示，他们不是专门对付外国公司，而是平等对待所有企业。
Xu Kunlin, the head of the antimonopoly bureau at the N.D.R.C., which oversees pricing-related matters, repeated this defense last week.
国家发改委反垄断局局长许昆林负责价格监督，他在上周重复了这个说法。
“Some of the N.D.R.C. monopoly investigations involve overseas multinationals, but that does not mean that we are targeting them,” Mr. Xu said, according to a report on Wednesday by Xinhua, the official news agency.
据官方通讯社新华社上周三报道，许昆林说，“目前发展改革委调查处理的一些反垄断案件确实涉及外资企业，但不能据此认为反垄断执法针对外资企业。”
“As a law-enforcement agency, we treat local and overseas companies equally to ensure justice for all,” he added. “This is the spirit of the Chinese Antitrust Law and the principle we always adhere to during law enforcement.”
他补充道，“作为反垄断执法机构，我们始终坚持公平公正执法，对所有市场主体同等对待，这是反垄断法的精神，也是执法始终坚持的原则。”
