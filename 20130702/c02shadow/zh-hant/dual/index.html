高收益理財產品危機四伏

Shadow Banking in China Fuels Worries of Credit Risk

DAVID BARBOZA
2013年7月2日
SHANGHAI — Text message solicitations began arriving on the mobile phones of many of China’s wealthy several days ago, promising access to lucrative wealth management products with yields far above the government’s benchmark savings rate.
上海——幾天前，一些短訊邀請開始出現在許多中國富人的手機上，承諾向他們提供收益可觀的理財產品，這些產品的收益比政府的基準儲蓄率高出一大截。
One message read: “China Merchants Bank will issue a high interest financing product starting from June 28th to 30th. The product will be 90 days with a 5.5% interest rate. Please call us now.”
\r一條短訊寫道：「中國招商銀行將在6月28日至30日期間發售高利率理財產品。該產品期限90天，年化收益率5.5%。請即刻致電聯繫我們。」
A day later came another. “Warm reminder: The interest rate of yesterday’s product has been raised to 6%. (Product duration is 90 days). There is limited access to this product. First come first served.”
\r一天之後，又來了一條短訊。「溫馨提示：昨天的產品收益率已經被提至6%（產品期限為90天）。產品額度有限。先到先得。」
The offers are not coming from fly-by-night operators but some of China’s biggest banks. They are raising huge pools of cash to finance a relatively new and highly profitable sideline business: lending it outside the scrutiny of bank regulators.
\r這些邀約並不是來自不可靠的投機商，而是來自中國最大的一些銀行。他們正在籌措大量現金，為一個相對較新的高利潤副業提供資金，這個副業就是在銀行監管機構監管範圍之外發放貸款。
The complex way they go about making off-the-balance sheet loans is at the heart of China’s $6 trillion shadow banking industry, which the government is now trying to tame. Its attempts to rein in the dodgy lending practices put stock markets worldwide in a tizzy in late June.
\r這些銀行發放賬外貸款的複雜操作方式處於中國影子銀行業的核心，該產業規模達6萬億美元（約合36.8萬億元人民幣）。目前，政府正在設法遏制這個行業。為了遏制狡猾的貸款業務，中國政府的種種舉措讓全球股市於6月末陷入了瘋狂的震蕩。
China’s regulators — and a fair number of economists, policy makers and investors — worry that legitimate banks are using lightly regulated wealth management products to repackage old loans and prop up risky companies and projects that might not otherwise be able to borrow money.
\r中國的監管者，再加上相當一部分的經濟學家、政策制定者和投資者，都擔憂合法的銀行在利用監管較少的理財產品重新包裝老的貸款，支撐有風險的公司和項目，不然，那些公司和項目可能沒有別的辦法能借到錢。
Analysts warn that shadow banking is helping fuel the rapid growth of credit in a weakening economy, which could lead to — in a worst-case situation — a series of bank failures.
\r分析人士警告，在一個日漸衰弱的經濟體中，影子銀行業正在給快速增長的信貸提供助力，在最糟糕的情形之下，這樣的增長可能會導致一系列的銀行倒閉事件。
“This is the biggest uncertainty I’ve seen in my 18 years following the China market,” Dong Tao, an economist at Credit Suisse, says of shadow banking. “You don’t know how banks are deploying capital. And you don’t know the credit risks.”
\r提到影子銀行業時，瑞信(Credit Suisse)經濟學家陶冬表示，「這是我跟蹤中國市場18年以來見到的最大的不確定性。你不知道銀行如何部署資金，也不知道信貸風險。」
What banks are doing, analysts say, is pressing customers to shift money from the old, regulated part of their operations — savings deposits — into the new, less regulated part — high-yielding wealth management products that can circumvent government interest rate controls and be used to finance high-interest loans to desperate customers.
\r分析人士說，銀行目前的所作所為正在迫使客戶把錢從受監管的老式業務——也就是儲蓄——轉入監管較少的新式業務，亦即高收益的理財產品，這些產品能繞過政府的利率控制，籌得的資金可以用來向孤注一擲的客戶發放高利貸。
China’s leaders are so worried about credit risk that in June the country’s central bank tightened credit in the interbank market, where banks typically go to borrow money from other banks.
\r中國領導人極度擔憂信貸風險，以至於中國央行在6月收緊了銀行同業拆借市場的信貸，銀行通常會利用這個市場從其他銀行拆藉資金。
The move sent short-term interest rates soaring, and for a day at least, created a debilitating credit squeeze.
\r該舉措引發了國內短期利率的飆升，至少在一天的時間裡造成了破壞性極大的銀根緊縮。
The stock markets in China calmed down last week. But financial institutions are hinting that cash is still hard to come by. Some banks temporarily suspended lending in order to preserve cash, according to Caixin, the Chinese business magazine.
\r中國股市於上周平靜下來。不過，金融機構暗示，現金依然難以獲得。根據中國商業雜誌財新《新世紀周刊》的消息，為了保存現金，一些銀行暫停了貸款業務。
Other banks are raising cash by offering a new slate of wealth management products. Nearly every major Chinese bank is selling a short-term wealth management product that has to be completed by the end of June, according to a telephone survey. China Merchants Bank did not respond to requests for an interview.
\r其他一些銀行則正通過發行新一批理財產品來籌資。根據一項電話調查，中國幾乎所有的大銀行都在銷售要在6月底生效的短期理財產品。中國招商銀行沒有回應我們的採訪請求。
Many of the investments pay 6 percent annual interest, which is far above the highest savings deposit rate set by bank regulators: 3.3 percent.
\r許多投資產品支付6%的年化收益率，比銀行監管機構設定的3.3%儲蓄利率上限高得多。
Consumers withdraw money from their regular savings account and put it into a wealth management product that promises a much higher rate. “Usually banks will have higher-yielding products at the end of each quarter,” says Wang Yanan, a 24-year-old accountant who works in Shanghai. “If I happen to have money at those moments, I’ll buy some.”
\r客戶們紛紛把錢從常規的儲蓄賬戶里取出來，投入某個承諾收益率高得多的理財產品。在上海工作的24歲會計王亞楠（音）說，「通常，銀行會在每個季度末推出收益更高的產品。如果碰巧有錢的話，我就會買一些。」
Although the products are popular, their disclosure is often poor. Bank employees insist the principal is guaranteed, but contracts for wealth management products are usually vague, simply noting there could be risk. Most offer little detail about where the money will be invested.
\r儘管這些產品廣受歡迎，相關信息披露卻往往少得可憐。銀行員工堅稱本金不會損失，不過，理財產品合同往往內容含糊，只是簡單地指明交易可能存在風險。大多數合同都對這些錢的投資方向隻字不提。
Much of the money, analysts say, is lent to property developers and local government financing vehicles, areas that have government officials worried because of an explosion in property development and soaring housing prices. Regulated banks will not make the loans because the borrowers are too risky. So the loans are often made off the balance sheet, and therefore outside the purview of bank regulators, which is why experts call it shadow banking. They are made at higher interest rates, so everyone wins — the borrower, the banks and the investor of the wealth management product — as long as the borrower can repay.
\r分析人士稱，這些錢當中有很多都貸給了房地產開發商和地方政府的融資平台。由於房地產開發的迅速發展和飆升的房價，這些領域都讓政府官員感到擔心。因為這些貸款者風險太高，受監管的銀行不會貸款給他們。所以，這些貸款通常不會出現在銀行的賬目里，由此也就逃出了銀行監管者的視線範圍，這就是專家稱之為影子銀行的原因。這些貸款利率較高，結果就是人人都能獲利——貸款者、銀行和理財產品投資者——前提是貸款者能夠還錢。
“The banks now have these dark pools of money,” says Joe Zhang, a former investment banker and the author of “Inside China’s Shadow Banking: The Next Subprime Crisis?” He says, “To finance deals they usually have a trust company stand in the middle and simply put their stamp on it. The trust companies get a fee for that but often they do next to nothing. The bank does all the work.”
\r「現在，各家銀行都有了這些資金暗池，」前投資銀行從業者、《中國影子銀行內幕：下一個次貸危機？》(Inside China』s Shadow Banking: The Next Subprime Crisis?)一書的作者張化橋說，「為了給交易融資，他們通常會讓一個信託公司來充當中間人，借用他們的圖章來從事交易。這些信託公司會因此得到一筆報酬，但他們通常什麼都不做。所有工作都由銀行來操辦。」
The explosive growth of wealth management products began about five years ago, enticing Chinese banks, big and small, to engage in shadow banking.
\r理財產品的飛速發展始於大約五年之前，受此引誘，中國大大小小的銀行紛紛開始從事影子銀行業務。
By the end of last year, China’s shadow banking activity was valued at $6 trillion, twice the level in2010, and now equal to 69 percent of China’s gross domestic product, according to a report released last month by J.P. Morgan.
\r摩根大通(J.P. Morgan)上月發佈的報告顯示，直至去年年末，中國影子銀行活動的總價值為6萬億，是2010年水平的兩倍，現在相當於中國國內生產總值的69%。
Now, even state-run banks are doing shadow lending, extending financing to companies in high-risk sectors.
\r如今，甚至連國有銀行都在從事影子借貸，把融資對象範圍擴展到了高風險行業的公司。
Who is responsible for the loans is not always clear, and that’s where everyone starts getting nervous.
\r這些貸款並不總是有明確的責任方，正是這個問題讓所有人開始感到緊張。
“If a wealth management product defaults, who is on the hook?” asks Michael Pettis, a finance professor at Peking University in Beijing and senior associate at the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace. “It’s all very murky. In these things, the banks are technically acting as intermediaries.”
\r「如果某個理財產品違約了，該由誰來負責呢？」北京大學金融學教授、卡耐基國際和平基金會(Carnegie Endowment for International Peace)高級研究員邁克爾·佩蒂斯(Michael Pettis)問道，「這些都非常不明確。在這些事情當中，銀行從條文上說只是扮演着中間人的角色。」
Financial experts worry about the lack of transparency in the market when China’s economy is weakening. They also fret about whether some borrowers have sufficient cash flow to repay their loans.
\r中國的經濟勢頭正在減弱，金融專家都對市場缺乏透明度的狀況感到擔心。他們還擔心一些貸款者是否有足夠的現金流來償還貸款。
Fitch Ratings, the credit ratings agency, began warning two years ago that “wealth management activity carries unique liquidity and credit risks.” But the lending continued and, indeed, increased.
\r信用評級機構惠譽公司(Fitch Ratings)兩年前就開始發出警告，「理財活動具有獨特的流動性和信用風險。」但是，這樣的借貸仍在繼續，實際上還有所增加。
In a newspaper opinion piece last year, Xiao Gang, then head of the Bank of China, a leading commercial bank, and now the nation’s top securities regulator, referred to shadow banking as “fundamentally a Ponzi scheme.”
\r去年，時任中國銀行董事長、如今擔任中國首席證券監管者的肖鋼在一家報紙的評論文章中稱，影子銀行「根本就是個龐氏騙局(Ponzi scheme)」。中國銀行是中國的一家主要的商業銀行。
The government has so far tolerated shadow banking because getting rid of it is all but impossible, analysts say. Wealthy customers have grown accustomed to getting better returns, and a large segment of the economy is desperate for capital and cannot easily gain access to regular bank loans, largely because of government restrictions. But they are willing to pay the shadow banks 9, 10, even 15 percent interest.
\r分析人士說，政府之所以至今容忍影子銀行的存在，原因是完全清除它幾乎不可能。富有的客戶已經習慣了獲得更高的收益，又有相當一部分經濟部門迫切需要資金，並且無法輕易獲得常規的銀行貸款，得不到常規貸款的大部分原因則是政府的限制。不過，他們願意為影子銀行支付9%、10%，甚至15%的利率。
And to make that possible, China’s banks are raising pools of capital and creating increasingly complex financial instruments with outside institutions to make off-the-books loans to those willing to pay a premium.
\r為了獲得高利率，中國的銀行籌措了大量資金，並且與外部機構一起創造了日益複雜的金融工具，以便為那些願意支付額外費用的人提供賬外貸款。
Perhaps the biggest worry though is that this booming business could be undone by a major business failure that forces bank customers to suffer losses.
\r人們最大的擔憂或許是，這項蓬勃發展的業務會毀於一場重大的商業事故，銀行客戶也會因此被迫承受損失。
“This is a major problem,” says Ding Shuang, a Citigroup analyst based in Hong Kong. “Many of these are just three months, and they need to be rolled over. So if there’s a default that could change things. This is a problem regulators are very aware of.”
\r「這是一個大問題，」駐香港的花旗集團(Citigroup)分析師丁爽說，「這類貸款的期限很多都只有三個月，而且它們需要轉存。所以，一旦出現違約，事情就會改變。管理者都非常清楚這個問題。」
