如果中国呼吁抵制美国商品，会发生什么？

In a Trade War, China Might Boycott U.S. Goods. That Could Backfire.

黄瑞黎, ZHANG TIANTIAN
2018年4月20日
TAICANG, China —If China calls for a boycott of American goods, Chinese workers like David Xu could be in trouble.
中国太仓——如果中国呼吁抵制美国商品，像戴维·徐这样的中国劳动者可能会陷入麻烦。
Mr. Xu is one of thousands of residents of this port town who cash paychecks from American companies. He works as a technician at a Procter & Gamble manufacturing and distribution center here, one of the company’s biggest in China. Across town, Nike has opened a huge distribution center, its largest in Asia.
他在宝洁(Procter & Gamble)设在这里的制造和物流中心担任技术员，是这个港口城市数以千计从美国公司领薪水的人之一。这是宝洁在中国最大的制造和物流中心之一。在城市的另一头，耐克(Nike)也建立了它在亚洲最大的物流中心。
In all, more than 40 other American companies have set up shop here, making chemicals, lighters and a broad range of other products for a Chinese market eager for American goods.
总共有40多家美国公司在这里开店设厂，为渴望购买美国商品的中国市场制造化学品、打火机和其他门类广泛的产品。
“There is no deep hatred for American companies,” said Mr. Xu, who has worked for Procter & Gamble for 13 years. “The impact of this trade conflict shouldn’t be big. After all, American companies bring so many conveniences to the lives of Chinese people.”
“美企的话，对中国来说，没有什么样的深仇大恨，”在宝洁工作了13年的他说。“贸易的冲突影响应该不会大，美企对中国人民的生活带来方方便便。”
Some Chinese state media outlets have hinted darkly that Beijing could weaponize its hundreds of millions of shoppers should Washington go through with its recent tariff threats and start an all-out trade war. On Weibo, China’s version of Twitter, there are sporadic calls to boycott Apple’s iPhones. Beijing has done it before, ably punishing Japanese, South Korean and Philippine products and companies over political disputes.
一些中国官方媒体悲观地暗示，如果华盛顿兑现最近发出的关税威胁，发动一场全面的贸易战，北京可能会把自己的数亿消费者作为武器。在中国版的Twitter微博上，已经零星出现了呼吁抵制苹果(Apple)iPhone手机的呼声。北京之前就这么做过，因为政治争端而巧妙地惩罚日本、韩国和菲律宾的产品和企业。
A Boycott America plan could be much tougher to pull off.
但抵制美国的计划实施难度可能会大很多。
While competition from Chinese-made smartphones is rising, iPhones remain popular. Shopping mall developers, eager for the extra cachet, clamor for Starbucks to open stores. Nike dominates the sportswear market in China. Chevrolet is one of the country’s most popular brands.
尽管中国制造的智能手机构成的竞争越来越激烈，但iPhone依然广受欢迎。急于获得声望的的商场开发商极力支持星巴克(Starbucks)开店。耐克在中国的运动服饰市场占据主导地位。雪佛兰(Chevrolet)是中国最受欢迎的品牌之一。
And notably, many of those products are made by Chinese workers. Factories in China assemble iPhones, stitch up Nike apparel and footwear and make Chevrolets and Fords. It isn’t clear how many jobs this creates, but the American Chamber of Commerce in China said that more than one-third of its 800-plus member companies have more than 1,000 employees in the country.
特别是，这些产品中很多是中国工人制造的。中国的工厂组装iPhone，缝制耐克的服饰和球鞋，生产雪佛兰和福特(Ford)汽车。虽然不清楚它们创造了多少工作岗位，但中国美国商会(American Chamber of Commerce in China)表示，其800多家成员企业中逾三分之一在华员工超过1000人。
“China needs the U.S., the U.S. needs China,” said Max Baucus, a former United States ambassador to China. “We are joined at the hip economically.”
“中国需要美国，美国也需要中国，”前美国驻华大使马克斯·鲍卡斯(Max Baucus)说。“我们在经济上密不可分。”
The United States has also supplied much of the investment underpinning China’s economic growth. Between 1990 and 2017, America pumped more than $250 billion into China, according to a report by the Rhodium Group and the National Committee on United States-China Relations.
美国也提供了大量支撑中国经济增长的投资。荣鼎咨询(Rhodium Group)和美中关系全国委员会(National Committee on United States-China Relations)联合发布的一份报告称，从1990年到2017年，美国向中国输入资金逾2500亿美元（约合1.6万亿元人民币）。
“The U.S. multinationals have been playing a very critical part of China’s development story, providing investment, technology, brands,” said Erlend Ek, trade research manager of China Policy, a Beijing-based advisory firm. “They have a very good relationship with each other.”
“美国的跨国公司在中国的发展故事中扮演着非常重要的角色，提供了资金、技术和品牌，”总部设在北京的咨询公司齐纳百思(China Policy)的贸易研究经理埃伦迪·埃克(Erlend Ek)说。“相互之间关系非常好。”
There’s another reason that Beijing may be reluctant to try a boycott: American multinationals are aiding the Chinese government in some important projects. IBM and Walmart, for example, are collaborating with the e-commerce company JD.com and Tsinghua University to improve food safety in China, a priority for Beijing.
北京可能不愿尝试发起抵制的另一个原因是：在一些重要项目上，美国的跨国公司正在帮助中国政府。比如，IBM和沃尔玛(Walmart)正在与电商公司京东和清华大学合作，改善中国的食品安全。这是北京的一个优先事项。
That isn’t to say that the idea is off the table.
这并不是说就没有这种可能性了。
The Global Times, a nationalist tabloid controlled by the People’s Daily, the Communist Party’s official newspaper, warned that a “people’s war” could be waged against the United States. On Chinese social media, the phrase “China is not scared!” has become a popular hashtag, the People’s Daily illustrating it with an image of Chinese and American boxing gloves.
中共的官方报纸《人民日报》旗下的民族主义小报《环球时报》警告称，可能会对美国发动“人民战争”。在中国的社交媒体上，“中国不是吓大的！”已经成了一个热门标签，《人民日报》为它配了一张中美两国的拳击手套的图片。
“The patriotism and collectivism of the Chinese people will likely play a role,” the newspaper said in an editorial last month. “And the slogans to boycott American cars and other big commodities may ring through the Chinese internet and get a response.”
“中国人的爱国主义和集体主义情怀，届时很可能会发生作用，”《环球时报》在上月的一篇社论中写道。“抵制美国汽车等大路货的口号，说不定就会响彻中国互联网，并得到响应。”
Still, China has other ways to strike back without involving consumers.
但中国也有其他方法，可以在不卷入消费者的条件下进行反击。
China’s proposed retaliatory tariffs would hit companies like Boeing. It can tell its local governments to stop buying technology from IBM or Microsoft. It can gum up business plans with regulatory hassles.
中国提出的报复性关税会对波音这样的公司造成打击。它可以指示当地政府停止购买IBM或微软的技术。可以通过监管找麻烦，扰乱商业计划。
Tu Xinquan, the dean of the China Institute for W.T.O. Studies at the University of International Business and Economics in Beijing, warned that the possibility of a consumer boycott on American goods “cannot be ruled out.”
北京的对外经济贸易大学中国世界贸易组织研究院院长屠新泉警告称，“不排除”会出现消费者抵制美国商品的可能性。
“But perhaps once the battle begins, it’s possible that we wouldn’t even need the people to wage the boycott, because the government’s measures could be powerful enough,” Mr. Tu said.
“但其实即使打起来的话，可能都不需要老百姓抵制了，因为政府的措施就已经够厉害了，”屠新泉说。
Just the prospect of a consumer boycott worries some American business executives. Despite frustrations that include opaque regulations, the weak rule of law and demands to hand over technology to Chinese partners or officials, the allure of being able to access nearly 1.4 billion consumers with the help of a huge labor force still holds appeal.
单是受到消费者抵制的可能性，都会使美国的一些企业管理者感到担忧。尽管中国的法规不透明、法治不严以及向中国合作伙伴或官员交出技术的要求都令人失望，但是能获得14亿消费者市场和巨大劳动力规模，这个诱惑依旧诱人。
Consumers in China can be a potent force. In 2012, Chinese nationalists wrecked Japanese stores and car dealerships and boycotted Japanese cars because of a territorial dispute, hurting sales for years. In 2016, online vendors stopped selling dried mangoes from the Philippines after a United Nations tribunal ruled in favor of Manila in another territorial dispute.
中国的消费者可以成为一股强大的力量。2012年，由于领土争端，中国的民族主义者对日本商店和汽车经销商进行了破坏，并对日本汽车发起了抵制，影响了好几年的销量。2016年，当联合国法庭在另一起领土争端中作出对马尼拉有利的裁决后，网上的商贩不再出售菲律宾芒果干。
Last year, the South Korean conglomerate Lotte was forced to shut down more than 80 stores in China after the South Korean government provided land for an American missile defense system that Beijing strongly opposed.
去年，韩国政府为北京强烈反对的美国导弹防御系统提供用地后，韩国乐天(Lotte)集团随后被迫关闭了80多家中国门店。
More recently, Chinese nationalists have besieged the social media accounts of Western companies that labeled Taiwan, a self-governing island that Beijing considers a breakaway region, and Tibet as countries.
最近，中国的民族主义者对那些把台湾和西藏标为国家的西方企业社交媒体账户发起了围攻。台湾这个自治岛屿被北京视为分裂地区。
William Zarit, the chairman of the American Chamber of Commerce in China, said a boycott “is one of the many tools that the Chinese have in their toolbox.”
中国美国商会主席蔡瑞德(William Zarit)表示，抵制“是中国人的工具箱里诸多工具之一”。
“Because of the structure of the government and the political power of the party, they can call for a boycott and get a pretty good response,” Mr. Zarit said. “So that concerns me.”
“由于政府的结构和政党的政治权利，他们可以呼吁抵制并获得很好的反响，”蔡瑞德说。“所以这让我感到担心。”
Taicang, a 90-minute drive from Shanghai, underscores how interdependent the two economies are.
距离上海90分钟车程的太仓市体现了两个经济体之间相互依存的程度。
Some of the biggest names in corporate America, like Honeywell and Exxon Mobil, have converged on this city of close to a million people. Its economy is growing faster than the country’s as a whole. It is richer than Shanghai on a per-capita basis. Last year, Taicang topped a list of China’s 10 happiest “county-level cities” for the second year in a row.
这个人口近100万的城市汇集了霍尼韦尔(Honeywell)和埃克森美孚(Exxon Mobil)这样大名鼎鼎的美国公司。其经济增速比整个国家都快，人均收入高过上海。去年，太仓市在中国十大最具幸福感“县级市”中连续第二年位居榜首。
In a sprawling factory, Procter & Gamble makes its Head & Shoulders, Pantene and Vidal Sassoon shampoos and then distributes them along with other cosmetics and skin care products. When the factory opened, the company said it would provide 1,500 jobs for Chinese workers. Across town is a 200,000-square-meter Nike logistics center, which that company has also said would employ 1,500 people.
在一家规模庞大的工厂里，宝洁生产着海飞丝、潘婷和沙宣洗发水，然后与其他美妆护肤产品一起销往各地。公司在工厂开业时表示，将为中国员工提供1500个工作岗位。城市的另一边是20万平方米的耐克物流中心，公司也表示该中心将雇佣1500人。
In total, 42 American factories are in the Taicang economic development zone, with a total annual output value of about $4.7 billion, the government of the much larger city of Suzhou, which administers Taicang, said in February.
太仓隶属苏州管辖，据苏州市政府在2月表示，太仓经济开发区内总计有42家美国工厂，年产值约为47亿美元。
The influx of money can be seen in the relative affluence of the Procter & Gamble employees, most of whom drive Japanese-made cars to work. One female employee, surnamed Li, said workers were confident that what they made in the factory would appeal to Chinese consumers, who she said were rational about what they bought.
金钱的流入可以从宝洁员工的相对富裕上有所体现，他们大多开着日本车来上班。一位李姓女员工说，工人们都相信他们工厂制造的东西能吸引中国消费者，她说，这些人买东西都很理性。
Analysts say Beijing is aware of the importance of American companies to China’s economy. Ernan Cui, a consumer analyst at the research firm Gavekal Dragonomics, said a boycott could have many victims.
分析人士表示，北京知道美国企业对中国经济的重要性。研究公司佳富龙洲(Gavekal Dragonomics)的消费者分析师埃尔南·崔(Ernan Cui)表示，抵制带来的受害者或许不止一个。
“Due to the integration of the economies, whatever China does to the U.S. will end up hurting itself,” Ms. Cui said.
“由于经济一体化的关系，中国不论对美国做什么，最终都会伤及自己，”埃尔南·崔说。
