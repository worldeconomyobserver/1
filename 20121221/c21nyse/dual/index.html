纽交所82亿美元卖给洲际交易所

ICE Deal for N.Y.S.E. Creates Global Powerhouse

FLOYD NORRIS
2012年12月21日
It was the temple of commerce, the symbol of New York’s status as the nation’s — and for many decades, the world’s — financial center.
这里曾是商业圣殿，象征着纽约作为美国金融中心以及几十年来的世界金融中心的地位。
Today the New York Stock Exchange building at Broad and Wall Streets in Lower Manhattan is not much more than a television studio. Soon it seems likely that it will not even be owned by a New York company.
今天，位于曼哈顿下城百老汇大街和华尔街路口的纽约证券交易所(New York Stock Exchange)大楼比一个电视台强不了多少。看样子，用不了多久，这里甚至不会归纽约的公司所有。
The owner of the exchange, NYSE Euronext, agreed on Thursday to be acquired by the IntercontinentalExchange, an Atlanta-based upstart that has prospered by trading derivatives over the Internet, for $8.2 billion in cash and stock.
周四，纽交所的东家纽约泛欧交易所集团(NYSEEuronext)老板同意将该交易所售与美国洲际交易所(Intercontinental Exchange)，代价是82亿美元的现金和股票，后者是一家位于亚特兰大的新兴公司，通过在网上交易衍生产品而发家。
The transformation of the New York Stock Exchange from its position at the apex of the world financial system to an asset to be bought and sold like any other — and one that is not deemed to be worth as much as it would be if it traded more modern derivative securities rather than old-fashioned stocks — has been going on for decades, but has accelerated in recent years.
纽约证券交易所从世界金融体系的顶端跌到了像其他公司一样被买卖的田地，人们还认为，如果它交易更多的现代衍生证券、而不是传统证券的话，价值还会高一些。这一跌落过程已经持续了几十年，但最近几年才开始加速。
Along the way the exchange lost its pre-eminent market position to newer competitors, gave up most of its regulatory responsibilities, and stopped being owned by the brokers who traded there.
在此过程中，纽约证券交易所把抢先取得的市场地位输给了后来的竞争者，放弃了大多数监管责任，它的东家也不再是在该所交易的证券经纪人。
An institution that began as an attempt to limit competition prospered most when it was able to exert monopoly control. Its power and authority withered as regulators and changing technology forced it to compete.
能够实施垄断控制的时候，一家诞生于限制竞争企图的机构最是兴旺发达。等到监管机构和技术发展迫使它参与竞争之后，它的力量和权威便开始萎缩。
The exchange traces its history to the Buttonwood Agreement in 1792 — an agreement that was nothing more than an effort to fix the commissions that brokers charged their customers at a quarter of 1 percent, or $2.50 on a $1,000 bond. In the beginning, there was just one stock, the Bank of New York, and a handful of bonds that were traded.
纽约证券交易所的历史可以回溯到1792年签订的《梧桐协议》(Buttonwood Agreement )，该协议只是为了把经纪人向客户收取的佣金固定在0.25%，即每1000美元（约合6230人民币）的证券收取2.5美元。最初，市场上只有纽约银行(Bank of New York)这一支股票以及寥寥无几的债券。
The buttonwood tree — actually a sycamore — under which the agreement was signed lasted until 1865, long after the exchange moved indoors.
协议签订地点的那棵梧桐树——实际上是枫树——一直活到了1865年，那时候，交易所早已转入室内。
By the 20th century, the exchange had established its pre-eminent role. There were exchanges in other cities, but important companies were listed in, and traded in, New York.
到20世纪，纽约证券交易所确定了权威地位。虽然其他城市也有交易所，但重要公司都是在纽约上市并进行交易。
The brokers who were “members” of the exchange agreed they would not trade any stock listed on the Big Board anywhere except on that exchange. In practice, that meant there was no competition. If you wanted to buy or sell shares in AT&T or General Motors, the trade would take place at 18 Broad Street, in the 1903 edifice designed by George B. Post. The huge sculptures on the pediment were titled “Integrity Protecting the Works of Man.”
身为纽约证券交易所“成员”的各位经纪人一致同意，只在纽约证券交易所买卖大交易所(Big Board)的股票。实际上，这就意味着不存在竞争。想要买卖AT&T或者通用汽车(General Motors)的股票，你就必须来到百老汇大街18号，来到这座由乔治·B·坡斯特(George B. Post)设计的1903年启用的大厦。大厦三角楣上那个巨型雕塑的名称是“诚实保护人类的工作。”(Integrity Protecting the Works of Man)
Commissions were fixed, just as they had been in 1792.
佣金仍然是固定的，跟1792年一样。
The exchange was virtually unregulated until the 1930s, and for decades after that it was a “self-regulatory” organization that monitored and supervised its own trading under the sometimes cursory supervision of the Securities and Exchange Commission. The Big Board cultivated a high-class image, in which only the best companies were allowed listings. The reality did not always match the image, but a N.Y.S.E. listing became a symbol of quality that reassured investors around the world.
到20世纪30年代之前，纽约证券交易所完全没有监管，之后的几十年也一直处于“自我监管”状态，在证券交易委员会(Securities and Exchange Commission)时有时无的象征性监管之下自己监管自己的交易。大交易所树立起了一种高端形象，只有最好的公司才被允许在那里上市。尽管现实往往与这一形象不符，在纽交所上市仍然成为了一种足以让全世界投资者放心的品质标签。
The monopoly began to break down in the 1960s, as brokers who were not members of the exchange found ways to use computers to trade shares listed on the exchange for less than the Big Board charged. A pioneer in that business was Bernard L. Madoff, who would go on to infamy many years later as the creator of the largest Ponzi scheme in history.
20世纪60年代，这一垄断地位开始被打破，当时，一些不是纽约证券交易所成员的经纪人找到了办法，利用电脑来交易在纽交所上市的股票，收费比大交易所低。带头人之一便是伯纳德·L·麦道夫(Bernard L. Madoff)，很多年之后，他落下了历史上最大庞氏骗局制造者的恶名。
Then the government banned fixed commissions, and the exchange was forced to allow its members to trade N.Y.S.E.-listed securities anywhere. It lost market share, but remained the dominant marketplace, the one that set the price of any security it traded. If the N.Y.S.E. halted trading in a stock, so did every other exchange. Without price discovery within the walls of 18 Broad Street, no one could be sure what market prices were.
接下来，政府禁止了固定不变的交易佣金，纽约证券交易所也被迫允许其成员在任何地方交易在纽交所上市的股票。纽交所虽然失去了市场份额，但却仍然是最主要的市场，可以为在该所交易的任何证券定价。如果纽交所停止交易某种股票，其他交易所也都会停止。如果百老汇大街18号墙内没有报价的话，任何人都不能确定市场价格是多高。
But the financial world was changing, and the Big Board was not keeping up. Options on stocks began trading in the early 1970s. The Big Board could have dominated that market, but instead it sniffed at it and the market became centered in Chicago, where commodity exchanges had long existed, trading wheat and corn contracts. As financial futures were created, on things like currency values and interest rates, they too were traded in Chicago.
然而，金融世界在不断发生变化，大交易所却没有跟上。20世纪70年代初出现了股票期权交易。大交易所本来可以主导这一市场，但却对这一现象不屑一顾，致使股票期权交易集中在了芝加哥，那里早就有了商品交易，一直在买卖大麦和玉米合同。后来又出现了由币值和利率等衍生的金融期货，交易也都在芝加哥进行。
Within the world of stocks, more and more exchanges moved to computers. The N.Y.S.E. moved as well, but it tried to maintain the dominance of its members, particularly the specialists who were required to always be ready to buy or sell any stock listed on the exchange. It found it hard to keep up with competitors in speed or cost. Competitors did not have to finance their own regulatory apparatus, and the Big Board decided to follow, merging N.Y.S.E. Regulation into Nasdaq’s operation to create Finra, the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority. The Big Board became less distinctive.
在股票交易界，越来越多的交易搬到了电脑前。纽交所也随之跟进，但却试图维系该所成员的主导地位，尤其是专业炒家的主导地位，这些专业炒家被要求随时准备好买进或卖出在纽交所上市的任何股票。纽交所发现，它难以在速度和价格上与对手竞争。它的对手不必给自己的监管机构提供资金，纽交所决定依样画葫芦，于是就把纽约证券交易所监管部和纳斯达克的营业部合并，创立了美国金融业监管局(Financial Industry Regulatory Authority)。纽交所不再那么独树一帜了。
In 2006, the exchange stopped being owned by its members and went public. It acquired exchanges in Europe and renamed itself NYSE Euronext. It acquired a computerized market.
2006年，纽交所公开上市，不再由其成员拥有。它并购了欧洲的一些交易所，把自身更名为纽约泛欧交易所集团，还并购了一个电脑化的市场。
The N.Y.S.E. shares were hot for a time, but the reality of competition eventually took a toll. Under the new agreement, the IntercontinentalExchange will pay $33.12 in cash and stock for Big Board shares that traded as high as $90 in 2006. The company says it will have headquarters in both Atlanta and New York, but there seems to be little doubt that the power will be in Atlanta.
纽交所的股票一度受到热捧，不过，现实的竞争最终产生了负面影响。按照新签的协议，洲际交易所将通过现金和换股方式以每股33.12美元的价格购买纽交所的股票，而在2006年，纽交所的股价曾高达90美元。洲际交易所称，将在亚特兰大和纽约同时设立总部，不过，似乎毫无疑问的是，公司的权力中心将位于亚特兰大。
ICE represents everything the Big Board used to resent. Its history goes back to 2000, not 1792, and it does its trading via Internet, focusing on derivatives that the stock exchange once scorned. It advertises “sub-one millisecond trade execution times.”
洲际交易所代表着曾让纽交所深感厌恶的一切。洲际交易所的历史只能追溯到2000年，而不是1792年，它通过互联网进行交易，把重心放在纽交所一度蔑视的衍生产品上，还提倡“以亚毫秒的速度完成交易”。
There is still stock trading at 18 Broad Street, and that seems likely to continue even though most of the action long ago moved off the floor. The crowds of traders, which seemed overwhelming a generation ago, are now thin, but there are still plenty of television reporters.
百老汇大街18号依然有股票交易，看上去也多半会持续下去，虽然很久以前，大多数交易就已经不在交易大厅里进行了。短短一代之前还显得风头无两的交易员大军现在人数骤减。不过，那里依然驻扎着不少电视台的记者。
The decline of the exchange can be seen by glances at three stock market trading disasters, and the differing role it played.
通过股票市场遭遇的三次股灾，以及纽交所在其中扮演的不同角色，可以一窥纽交所的没落。
During the 1987 crash, the Big Board was the center of the action. Stock index futures had become important, and other stock markets could and did trade every security listed on the N.Y.S.E. But they all looked to the New York floor to learn what the market price was at any given moment. When the panic was at its worst, and the exchange began to halt trading in important stocks because of a lack of buy orders, the Chicago Mercantile Exchange threatened to halt trading in stock index futures. Without New York prices, the Chicago market could not operate.
1987年股市崩盘时，纽交所是行动的中心。当时，股指期货已经变得相当重要，其他股票市场能够也确实在交易于纽交所挂牌的每只股票。不过，他们都盯着纽交所的大厅，以便随时获悉市场价格。恐慌达到顶点的时候，因为缺乏买入指令，纽交所开始停止重要股票的交易，芝加哥商品交易所(Chicago Mercantile Exchange)随之威胁要停止股指期货的交易。没有纽交所的定价，芝加哥市场无法运作。
During the 2010 “flash crash,” when errant computers sent prices plunging, other markets could and did continue trading after the New York exchange halted trading, and some of those markets were indignant that the Big Board had slowed things down. The result was that customers whose orders had been sent to the New York Stock Exchange did not end up selling $30 stocks for $1. The Big Board’s people had applied common sense and — at least on that market — halted the craziness.
2010年的“闪电式崩盘”期间，电脑故障使得股价暴跌。在纽交所停止交易之后，其他市场能够也的确继续进行了交易，其中的一些市场还对纽交所拖慢进度的行为愤愤不平。最终结果就是，向纽交所下达了买卖指令的客户没能把原价30美元的股票以1美元的价格卖掉。纽交所的交易员运用常识——至少是在他们自己的市场上——阻止了这种疯狂的行为。
During this summer’s Knight Capital debacle, when errant computers again caused prices to go haywire, the New York Stock Exchange did nothing different from its competitors. Its customers were victimized, just like everyone else’s.
在今年夏天的骑士资本(Knight Capital)大劫难中，电脑故障再次让股价失去控制，纽交所采取的行动和对手没什么不同。它的客户和其他交易所的客户一样，也成了受害者。
It could be seen as a final indication that what made the New York Stock Exchange special had gone away. Five months later, the Big Board is set to disappear as an independent enterprise.
骑士资本大劫难可以被视作一个最后的暗示，意味着让纽交所与众不同的特质已经不复存在。按照计划，五个月之后，纽交所将不再以独立企业的身份存在。
