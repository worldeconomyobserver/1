美國銀行監管讓英國「很受傷」

In London, Censure of an Elite Bank Draws Harrumphs

LANDON THOMAS Jr.
2012年8月10日
LONDON — In this summer of revelations about bad behavior by British financial institutions, bashing the banks has been London’s other Olympic sport.
倫敦——今年夏天在對英國金融業醜聞的披露中，抨擊各大銀行成了倫敦的另一場奧運會比賽。
But now that a state regulator in New York has accused Standard Chartered, one of London’s most reputable banks, of colluding with Iran, Britain seems to be drawing a line against what many here consider American overreaching.
然而，當紐約州一家監管機構指責倫敦最有名的銀行之一渣打銀行(Standard Chartered)串通伊朗的時候，英國很多人認為這是美國越位，管得太寬了，他們似乎開始反對美國的這一行為。
On Wednesday, even Mervyn A. King, the central bank governor who has been one of the industry’s harshest scolds, seemed to defend Standard Chartered against the New York allegation that it had schemed with the Iranian government to launder billions of dollars for the potential support of terrorist activities.
周三，甚至連倫敦金融業最嚴厲批評者之一的央行行長默文·金(Mervyn A. King)，似乎也開始為渣打銀行辯護，反對紐約指責它與伊朗政府串通，幫助其洗錢金額達數十億美元，有可能用於支持恐怖主義活動。
“All that the U.K, authorities would ask is that the various regulatory bodies that are investigating a particular case try to work together and refrain from making too many public statements until the investigation is completed,” Mr. King said at a news conference. “That seems to me the appropriate time to make clear what the judgment is and what the punishment is.”
“英國官方的要求是正在調查某個案件的各監管機構應該緊密合作，在調查沒有完結之前請不要發表太多公開言論，”金在一次新聞發佈會上說。“在我看來，那時才是下結論和決定懲罰措施的合適時機。”
It was a notable demand for forbearance from Mr. King, who in June played the leading role in forcing the Barclays chief executive, Robert E. Diamond Jr., to resign after regulators in the United States and Britain implicated the bank in a rate-fixing scandal involving the London interbank offered rate, or Libor.
金要求保持克制的呼籲很引人注意。6月份，美、英監管機構發現巴克萊銀行(Barclays)涉嫌固定倫敦同業拆借利率(London interbank offered rate)，即Libor，醜聞之後，巴克萊首席執行官小羅伯特·E·戴蒙德(Robert E. Diamond Jr.)被迫辭職，金在其中發揮了主要的作用。
And John Mann, perhaps the most strident critic of Britain’s banking culture in Parliament, said in an interview on Wednesday that the Standard Chartered allegations reflected an anti-Britain bias by American regulators, who he said were trying to bolster Wall Street at the expense of the City of London.
約翰·曼(John Mann)或許是議會中對英國銀行業批評最嚴厲的人，但他在周三的一次採訪中稱，指責渣打銀行的行為反映出美國監管機構的反英偏見，他稱這些機構為了力挺華爾街，不惜敗壞倫敦。
“I am no apologist for British banking, but if this attempt to blame London succeeds it will give comfort to all the banks breaking the rules in the United States,” said Mr. Mann, who is a member of the parliamentary committee that will broadly scrutinize British banking behavior after the Barclays episode.
“我不為英國銀行業辯護，但如果指責倫敦的企圖成功，那會讓美國那些違反規定的銀行感到高興，”曼稱。他所在的議會委員在巴克萊事件之後，將對英國銀行業展開大規模調查。
Bolstering such sentiments, in part, were reports that the censuring of Standard Chartered on Monday by the New York state regulator surprised not only the bank’s executives but also stunned officials at the federal agencies investigating the bank, the Treasury and Justice Departments and the Federal Reserve. Some Treasury officials had reportedly concluded that while Standard Chartered’s handling of Iranian transactions might have been questionable, they were not necessarily illegal.
引發此番情緒的部分原因是，周一報道稱，審查渣打銀行的紐約州監管機構不僅震驚了銀行高管，還有正在調查渣打銀行的聯邦官員也吃驚不小。據說財政部一些官員的結論是，雖然渣打銀行與伊朗的交易可能有問題，但不一定就違法。
Standard Chartered has strongly rebutted the accusations. The bank says that 99.9 percent of its transactions with Iran were above board and that it ceased all new business with Iranian customers more than five years ago. The bank says that the state’s findings that it had violated American sanctions against Iran and other pariah nations for nearly a decade through 2010, handling $250 billion in transactions, “are incorrect as a matter of law.”
渣打銀行強烈反駁這些指責。該銀行稱其與伊朗99.9%的交易都是光明正大的，而且在五年多以前就已經停止與伊朗客戶的所有新交易。渣打銀行稱，紐約州說渣打銀行截至2010年的近10年間，違反美國對伊朗和其他不受歡迎國家制裁措施，與其進行2500億美元交易的說法“在法律上是不正確的”。
It has been a summer of terrible discontent for Britain’s banks, once seen as the powerhouse of its economy and a symbol of London’s emergence as an entrepôt of global finance.
倫敦銀行業曾被視為該國經濟的發動機以及倫敦躋身為全球金融交易中心的象徵，但在這個夏天，它遭受了一連串嚴重指責。
The Barclays scandal has stirred public outrage. Then came United States Senate accusations that the global banking giant HSBC had conducted business with terrorists — activities for which HSBC has apologized.
巴克萊醜聞已經引起了公憤。接着美國參議院指控全球銀行業巨頭滙豐銀行(HSBC)與恐怖分子進行交易——滙豐銀行對此進行了道歉。
But the latest regulatory broadside from Benjamin W. Lawsky, head of the New York State Department of Financial Services, is being seen as a salvo too far.
然而最近這起來自紐約州金融服務局(Department of Financial Services)局長本傑明·勞斯基(Benjamin W. Lawsky)的猛烈抨擊卻被認為太過火。
“To describe Standard Chartered as a ‘rogue’ institution is factually wrong — if anything, compared to the problems other banks face, they are an outlier,” said Mr. Mann, who during the rate-fixing hearings last month referred to Barclays as a “rotten, thieving bank.”
“說渣打銀行是‘無賴’銀行是完全沒有事實根據的錯誤，和其他銀行面臨的問題相比，他們要好得多，”曼稱。他曾在上個月的固定利率醜聞聽證會上指責巴克萊是“腐朽的盜賊銀行”。
To some extent, it is a matter of injured British pride. If the country’s banks are to be criticized to such a degree, it is deemed better to have a notable body inflict the flogging — such as the Commodities Futures Trading Commission in the case of Barclays, and the United States Senate for HSBC.
在某種程度上，此事關係到英國受傷的自尊。如果要對英國的銀行業發出如此嚴重的指責，最好是由一個有名的機構來發難——比如在巴克萊事件中的商品期貨交易委員會(Commodities Futures Trading Commission)和指責滙豐銀行的美國參議院。
An attack on British banks from a provincial New York regulator has raised suspicions that Wall Street is looking to give the City of London a good kicking now that it’s down — and perhaps secure a bit of its international business as well.
來自紐約州級監管機構的指責引起人們懷疑：美國金融業想趁着倫敦跌倒，落井下石，或許還可以趁機把倫敦的一部分國際業務搶過來。
“There would obviously be a benefit for U.S. banks, many of which are based in New York,” said Ian Gordon, a bank analyst in London for Investec Securities.
“這對主要集中在紐約的美國銀行業來說顯然有利可圖，”倫敦Investec Securities的銀行分析師伊恩·戈登(Ian Gordon)稱。
Part of the puzzlement comes from the target: Standard Chartered has until now been for the most part scandal-free. The bank has attained an elite reputation here for a strong run of impressive profit that has contrasted sharply with the financial struggles of its rivals.
部分疑團來自目標銀行本身：到現在為止，渣打銀行基本沒出過什麼醜聞。銀行的利潤相當可觀，讓那些資金困難的競爭對手相形見絀，贏得了極好的聲譽。
Before Monday, its stock had been a top performer among European banks. But the shares were down 16 percent in the first three days this week, lopping about $10 billion off Standard Chartered’s market value.
周一以前，渣打銀行的股票表現也穩居歐洲銀行之首。但本周前三天，其股票下跌了16%，損失了約100億美元的市值。
Without a prominent investment banking business or a large exposure to real estate in the United States and Europe, Standard Chartered has steered clear of many of the financial pitfalls that have hurt its peers in London and America. Instead it has focused on the steadier income and fees that come from providing loans and custodial banking services to countries in Asia, Africa and the Middle East.
渣打銀行沒有在美國和歐洲做大筆的投行和房地產業務，因此避免了給倫敦和美國同行帶來損失的很多金融陷阱。相反，渣打銀行專註於向亞洲、非洲和中東國家提供貸款和銀行監管服務，從中獲得穩定收益和服務費。
Not all critics of British banking are holding their fire.
並不是所有人都暫停了對英國銀行業的抨擊。
Richard Murphy, a London-based authority on tax evasion and money laundering schemes, argues that the accusations against Standard Chartered highlight an arrogant assumption on the part of British banks that they are immune to global rules of best banking conduct.
理乍得·墨菲(Richard Murphy)是倫敦反避稅和反洗錢的權威人士，他稱，對渣打銀行的指責顯示出英國銀行業過於自大，認為可以不受全球最佳銀行業行為準則的約束。
“So what if a regulator is a bit of a lone wolf — that just shows he is independent,” Mr. Murphy said of Mr. Lawsky. “This shows that there is a deep worry that institutions in London are ignoring the obligations of other regulatory environments. It is a tax haven mentality, and the U.S. has had enough of it.”
“如果只有一個監管機構提出問題又怎樣？——這正說明他的獨立性，”墨菲在提到勞斯基時這麼說。“這說明，人們很擔心倫敦金融業忽視了在其他監管環境下的責任。這是一種避稅天堂心態，美國已經受夠了。”
