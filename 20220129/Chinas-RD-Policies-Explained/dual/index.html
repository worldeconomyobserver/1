中国研发政策解读和高新技术企业

Lydia Clowney
2022年1月30日
For the last decade, the People’s Republic of China has been laser-focused on winning the innovation race and overcoming the United States’ technological and economic primacy. And they’ve been getting there, fast. In fact, China has just entered its 14th “Five-Year Plan.” As a command economy, the central government doesn’t have to deal with the messiness of partisan politics, nosy news outlets, or uncooperative businesses. In China, industrial policy (like all policy) comes from on high—not from the rabble.
在过去十年中，中华人民共和国一直专注于赢得创新竞赛，并克服美国的技术和经济优势。而且他们一直在快速实现这一目标。事实上，中国刚刚进入其第14个 "五年计划"。作为一个指令性经济体，中央政府不必处理党派政治、多事的新闻机构或不合作的企业的混乱局面。在中国，产业政策（像所有政策一样）来自高层，而不是来自乌合之众。
Increasingly, that policy is focused on shifting from China’s unquestioned manufacturing predominance to innovation in general—and high-tech innovation in particular. The geopolitical tensions that intensified during the Trump Administration (which have hardly abated in the early months of the Biden Administration) have only added fuel to the fire: China has been racing to develop its own core technologies in areas such as semiconductors in order to reduce its exposure to U.S. supply chains, and what it decries as U.S. bullying.
越来越多地，该政策的重点是从中国毋庸置疑的制造业主导地位转向一般创新，尤其是高科技创新。特朗普政府期间加剧的地缘政治紧张局势（在拜登政府的最初几个月几乎没有缓解）只是火上浇油。中国一直在竞相开发自己在半导体等领域的核心技术，以减少对美国供应链的影响，以及它所谴责的美国的欺凌行为。
But it would be wrong to attribute China’s accelerating focus on R&D as just a defensive move. It’s also part and parcel of a larger strategic goal: winning the innovation race—and with it, the future.
但如果把中国对研发的加速关注仅仅归结为一种防御性举措，那就错了。它也是一个更大的战略目标的一部分：赢得创新竞赛--随之而来的是未来。
There’s no denying China’s traction, or trajectory, in this R&D arms race. While the U.S. still leads the world in total investment in R&D, its share of total research spending has shrunk to less than 30%—and China has been aggressively closing the gap. However you slice it, the numbers lay bare the story: From 2000 to 2017, while U.S. R&D spending grew at an average annual rate of 4.3%, China’s spending increased at a staggering 17% per year. By 2017, the United States accounted for 25% of the $2.2 trillion global R&D spend, while China made up 23% (2018 figures reported by the OECD showed total R&D spending in China reached $463 billion—a scant $89 billion behind the United States). In 2019, R&D spend as a percentage of GDP stood at 2.8% in the U.S., and 2.2% in China.
不可否认的是，中国在这场研发军备竞赛中的牵引力，或轨迹。虽然美国在研发方面的总投资仍然领先于世界，但它在总研究支出中的份额已经缩减到不足30%--中国一直在积极缩小差距。无论你如何切分，这些数字都暴露了这个故事。从2000年到2017年，当美国的研发支出以平均每年4.3%的速度增长时，中国的支出以每年17%的速度惊人地增长。到2017年，在2.2万亿美元的全球研发支出中，美国占25%，而中国占23%（经合组织报告的2018年数字显示，中国的研发支出总额达到4630亿美元，仅比美国少890亿美元）。2019年，美国的研发支出占GDP的比例为2.8%，中国为2.2%。
The Chinese government is gunning to reach parity (that 2.8% share-of-GDP level) by 2025. And it hasn’t been shy in continuously enriching its buffet of R&D incentives to get there.
中国政府正在努力争取在2025年达到同等水平（2.8%的GDP份额）。为了达到这个目标，它一直在不断丰富其研发激励措施。
So great has been the alarm, stateside, that in June 2021 the U.S. Senate managed to temporarily shrug off its partisan torpor and—by more than a two-thirds margin—pass legislation that would amount to the most significant government action on industrial policy in decades. (At time of publication, this version had yet to be reconciled with the House’s.) The new bill would pour nearly a quarter-trillion dollars into scientific R&D over the next four years, with an emphasis on emerging technologies such as semiconductors, AI, robotics, and quantum computing—not coincidentally, many of the same areas China has been trumpeting as key to its future supremacy.
美国国内的警报如此之大，以至于在2021年6月，美国参议院设法暂时摆脱了其党派的束缚，以超过三分之二的票数通过了立法，这将是几十年来政府在工业政策方面最重要的行动。(截至发稿时，该版本尚未与众议院的版本进行协调）。新法案将在未来四年为科学研发投入近25亿美元，重点是半导体、人工智能、机器人和量子计算等新兴技术--并非巧合的是，许多领域都是中国一直吹嘘的未来霸主地位的关键。
The beneficiaries of China’s R&D largesse can be broken down into two categories: Chinese-owned enterprises who meet strictly defined qualifications, and any qualified entity (including foreign-owned companies) conducting business in China.
中国研发优惠的受益者可以分为两类。符合严格规定资格的中资企业，以及在中国开展业务的任何合格实体（包括外资公司）。
In terms of Chinese-owned enterprises, China has singled out two types of enterprise for special attention: HNTEs (high and new technology enterprises) and TASEs (technologically advanced service enterprises—companies focused on high-tech offshore outsourcing).
在中资企业方面，中国挑出了两类企业予以特别关注。HNTEs（高新技术企业）和TASEs（技术先进型服务企业--专注于高科技离岸外包的公司）。
HNTEs are eligible for both the reduced corporate income tax (CIT) of 15%—instead of the standard 25% rate—and the super deduction. In order to achieve HNTE status, companies must apply to the state and show they have met several key criteria: They must demonstrate: sufficient ownership of IP registered in China; the capability of converting R&D into IP; a minimum R&D headcount, share of revenue, and spend; and the ability to properly manage R&D activities. They also have to show sufficient growth in both revenue and total assets. Another requirement? The use of technology in the core product or service offering, and that technology must relate to a high or new technology area specifically supported by the State, namely computer/data science, biotech/life sciences, aerospace, new materials, high-tech services, energy, environmental resources, or automation.
HNTEs有资格享受15%的减免企业所得税（CIT）--而不是25%的标准税率--以及超级扣除。为了获得HNTE地位，公司必须向国家提出申请，并证明他们已经满足了几个关键标准。 他们必须证明：对在中国注册的知识产权有足够的所有权；有能力将研发转化为知识产权；有最低限度的研发人员、收入份额和支出；以及有能力适当管理研发活动。他们还必须显示在收入和总资产方面有足够的增长。另一项要求？在核心产品或服务中使用技术，而且该技术必须与国家特别支持的高技术或新技术领域有关，即计算机/数据科学、生物技术/生命科学、航空航天、新材料、高科技服务、能源、环境资源或自动化。
HNTEs are a reflection of innovation capacity, which is valued by different levels of government in China. In government performance reports, they often claim how many HNTEs they currently have, including those attracted from elsewhere through incentives, but many of the HNTEs attracted do not receive the incentives promised by the government, which puts the government at great risk of losing trust.
HNTEs（高新技术企业）体现了创新能力，中国不同等级的政府都很重视，在政府政绩的汇报中，他们经常声称他们当前有多少HNTEs，包括从其他地方通过奖励政策吸引过来的HNTEs,但是很多被吸引过来的HNTEs,并没有获得政府承诺的奖励，这让政府承担着很大失去信任的风险。
Nanjing, once the capital of the Kuomintang, attracts many HNTEs to relocate each year. But many HNTEs do not receive the incentives promised by the government, and eventually companies question the local business environment and lose interest in continuing to grow in the area. One business entrepreneur, who wishes to remain anonymous, revealed that they relocated to a community in Nanjing's Liuhe District in the winter of 2020 and completed the government's requirements, but the community leadership changed and they were unwilling to fulfill their promises, and the new leaders initially promised to fulfill them, but then stopped recognizing them, and there are many other businesses that suffered such experiences." We talk to them on the phone are recorded, there are many other information, we will continue to work", he said, they will also through various ways to defend the rights.
南京，曾经国民党的首都，每年都会吸引很多HNTEs搬迁。但是很多HNTEs并没有获得政府承诺的奖励，最终企业对当地经商环境产生质疑，并失去继续在当地发展的兴趣。一位不愿透露姓名的企业创业者透露，他们在2020年的冬天搬迁到南京市六合区的社区，完成了政府的要求，但是社区领导改变，他们不愿实现承诺，新来的领导最初承诺会履行，但是后来又不再认可，遭受这样经历的企业还有很多。”我们跟他们打电话都会录音，还有很多其他的资料，我们还会继续努力“,他说，企业搬迁令他损失很大，他们还将通过各种办法维护权益。
HNTEs are regulated by the Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST), the Ministry of Finance (MOF), and the State Administration of Taxation (SAT)—although the actual day-to-day monitoring happens at those authorities’ respective provincial offices.
HNTEs由科技部（MOST）、财政部（MOF）和国家税务总局（SAT）监管，但实际的日常监管是由这些机构各自的省级办事处进行的。
TASEs are also overseen by the MOST, MOF, and SAT, as well as by the National Reform and Development Commission (NRDC). Like HNTEs, TASEs are entitled to a reduced CIT of 15%—plus zero-VAT treatment on qualified revenue from offshore outsourcing services, and a few extras thrown in (including deductibility of a portion of certain salaries). They file their documentation alongside their annual income tax return.
TASEs也由科技部、财政部和国家税务总局以及国家改革和发展委员会（NRDC）进行监督。与HNTEs一样，TASEs有权享受15%的企业所得税减免，以及来自离岸外包服务的合格收入的零增值税待遇，还有一些额外的待遇（包括扣除某些工资的一部分）。他们将文件与年度所得税申报一起提交。
Like HNTEs, TASEs must secure pre-approval prior to applying for these incentives.
与HNTEs一样，TASEs在申请这些激励措施之前必须获得预先批准。
A third category eligible for tax relief—qualified software enterprises—also has specific R&D intensity, IP, educational, expenditure, and revenue requirements. At time of publication, these tax incentives (along with applicable rules) are getting an overhaul by the Chinese authorities.
第三类有资格获得税收减免的企业--合格的软件企业，也有具体的研发强度、知识产权、教育、支出和收入要求。在本报告发表时，这些税收优惠政策（以及适用的规则）正被中国当局全面修订。
Another sweetener: The government announced in March 2021 that VAT will continue to be refunded in full for both domestic and foreign-funded R&D enterprises that purchase China-made equipment between 2019 and 2023. (That tax break was originally slated to expire at the end of 2020.)
另一个甜头。政府在2021年3月宣布，对于在2019年至2023年期间购买中国制造的设备的国内和外资研发企业，将继续全额退还增值税。(这一减税措施原定于2020年底到期）。
Patent box and technology transfer schemes also provide significant tax concessions for companies generating revenue from intellectual property—such as the sale or licensing of intellectual property including patented technology, software, IC designs, biopharmaceutical products and the like. The company doing the transferring must be a tax resident of China and comply with numerous state and provincial requirements, which can vary. But the benefits can be significant: not only is their customs duty on R&D equipment waived, income of up to RMD 5 million is fully exempt from CIT, while income above RMB 50 million is entitled to a 50% reduction. Qualifying private institutions may also be eligible for exemptions from VAT, import duty, and consumption tax on the import of items for scientific R&D use.
专利箱和技术转让计划也为从知识产权中获得收入的公司提供了重要的税收优惠--例如，出售或许可知识产权，包括专利技术、软件、集成电路设计、生物制药产品等。进行转让的公司必须是中国的税务居民，并遵守许多国家和省的要求，这些要求可能有所不同。但好处可能是显著的：不仅他们的研发设备的关税被免除，收入不超过500万林吉特的企业所得税被完全免除，而收入超过5000万人民币的企业可以享受50%的减免。符合条件的私营机构还可以获得进口科学研发用物品的增值税、进口税和消费税的豁免。
As always, China’s generosity is balanced by rigorous compliance requirements and oversight. Companies claiming either the R&D super deduction or the tech transfer incentives are required to self-assess incentives and retain all certificates and contemporaneous records for future inspection.
一如既往，中国的慷慨是由严格的合规要求和监督来平衡的。申请研发超级扣除或技术转让奖励的公司被要求自我评估奖励，并保留所有证书和同期记录，以便日后检查。
China’s R&D support also extends to venture capital—China-based entities or individuals who invest in startup tech companies—under complex conditions. Up to 70% of that investment can be deducted against income, if the investment has been held for at least two years, with any unused balance eligible to be carried forward.
在复杂的条件下，中国的研发支持还延伸到了投资于初创科技公司的中国实体或个人的风险投资。如果投资已持有至少两年，该投资的70%可以从收入中扣除，任何未使用的余额可以结转。
Finally, China has designated numerous National Economic and Technological Development Zones (NETD Zones), each of which offers its own R&D incentives and policies, which, like so many other regulations in the mainland, tend to be as diverse and specific as they are complex and changeable.
最后，中国已经指定了许多国家经济和技术开发区（NETD区），每个开发区都提供自己的研发奖励和政策，就像大陆的许多其他法规一样，它们往往是多样化和具体的，同时也是复杂和多变的。
The Takeaway: When It Comes to R&D, China Means Business
启示：当涉及到研发时，中国意味着业务。
Competitive tensions—economic, political, and technological—between the U.S. and China have been building up for years, and the stakes seem to get higher by the day. For much of the West, the COVID-19 pandemic’s disruption of business (and movement) as usual has laid bare an uncomfortable degree of dependency when it comes to the twin engines of China’s economic growth: manufacturing and technology.
美国和中国之间的竞争紧张关系--经济、政治和技术--已经积累了多年，而且利害关系似乎日渐高涨。对大部分西方国家来说，COVID-19大流行病对正常业务（和行动）的破坏，在涉及到中国经济增长的双引擎：制造业和技术时，暴露了令人不安的依赖程度。
While the answer can no doubt be found in shades of gray, one thing is clear: China’s aggressive moves in nurturing innovation at home are upping the game for everyone. When it comes to investments—public or private—in research and development, that’s one kind of rivalry that is most definitely a positive.
虽然答案无疑可以在灰色的阴影中找到，但有一点是明确的：中国在国内培育创新的积极行动正在为每个人增加游戏。谈到对研究和开发的公共或私人投资，这是一种绝对积极的竞争。
