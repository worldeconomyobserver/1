美國將推遲加徵部分對華關稅

U.S. to Delay Some China Tariffs Until Stores Stock Up for Holiday Shoppers

ANA SWANSON
2019年8月14日
Sign up for NYT Chinese-language Morning Briefing.
 [《紐約時報》推出每日中文簡報，為你介紹時報當日的重點英文報導，並推薦部分已被譯成中文的精選內容。新讀者請點擊此處訂閱，或發送郵件至cn.letters@nytimes.com加入訂閱。]
WASHINGTON — President Trump on Tuesday unexpectedly put off new tariffs on many Chinese goods, including cellphones, laptop computers and toys, until after the start of the Christmas shopping season, acknowledging the effect that his protracted trade war with Beijing could have on Americans.
華盛頓——川普政府週二縮小了準備在9月1日起對中國商品加徵關稅的範圍，推遲對手機、筆記型電腦、玩具及其他商品的徵稅，讓購物者免於在返校季及節日裡為更高昂的價格埋單。美股應聲大漲。
The move, which pushed a new 10 percent tariff on some goods until Dec. 15 and excluded others entirely, came as President Trump faces mounting pressure from businesses and consumer groups over the harm they say the continuing trade war between the United States and China is doing.
這一舉動將對某些商品加徵的10%新關稅推遲至12月15日， 並且完全排除了對其他一些商品徵稅的可能性，此時正值川普面臨來自企業及消費者群體日益增長的壓力，他們表示，不斷持續的美中貿易戰造成了傷害。
Mr. Trump’s earlier tariffs on Chinese imports were carefully crafted to hit businesses in ways that everyday Americans would mostly not notice. But his announcement this month of the 10 percent tariff on $300 billion of Chinese goods meant consumers would soon feel the trade war’s sting more directly.
川普此前針對中國進口商品的關稅經過精心設計，它們打擊企業的方式是普通美國人一般注意不到的。但他本月宣布對價值3000億美元中國商品加徵10%的關稅，意味著消費者將很快更為直接地體會到貿易戰帶來的痛苦。
廣告
On Tuesday, Mr. Trump acknowledged as much.
週二，川普承認了這一點。
“We’re doing this for the Christmas season,” he told reporters around noon. “Just in case some of the tariffs would have an impact on U.S. customers.”
「我們這麼做是為了聖誕季，」他在中午告訴記者，「免得某些關稅會影響到美國消費者。」
Mr. Trump has been pressing Beijing since last year to make a trade deal that would, among other things, strengthen protections for American intellectual property, open Chinese markets to American business and result in China buying large quantities of American energy and agricultural goods.
自去年以來，為了達成貿易協定，川普一直向中國施壓。這項貿易協定將會保護美國知識產權、向美國企業開放中國市場，並且讓中國購買大量美國能源及農產品等等。
But negotiators have made little progress since May, when progress stalled over several issues. The stumbling blocks include whether Mr. Trump would roll back the 25 percent tariffs the administration has already imposed on roughly $250 billion of Chinese goods and whether Beijing would enshrine in law the reforms it has pledged to make.
但自5月以來，談判在幾個問題上停滯，幾乎毫無進展。談判的絆腳石包括：川普是否會撤銷該政府已經對約2500億美元中國商品加徵的25%關稅，以及北京是否會將其承諾的改革寫進法律。
The president’s comments on Tuesday about the tariffs’ impact on consumers followed an announcement by United States trade representative’s office that while the new tariffs would take effect as Mr. Trump had threatened, some notable items would not immediately be subject to them.
總統週二作出關稅影響消費者的評論前，美國貿易代表辦公室宣布，儘管新關稅會按照川普威脅的那樣生效，但一些引人注目的商品不會立即加徵新關稅。
Consumer electronics, video game consoles, some toys, computer monitors and some footwear and clothing items were among the items the trade representative’s office said would not be hit with tariffs until retailers had time to stockpile what they needed for their busiest time of year.
貿易代表辦公室表示，在零售商有時間為他們一年中最繁忙的時期所需要的商品做好備貨前，一些物品不會被加徵關稅，其中包括消費性電子產品、電玩遊戲機、一些玩具、計算機顯示器、一些鞋類及服裝類。
The administration also said some products were being removed from the tariff list altogether “based on health, safety, national security and other factors.” A spokesman for the trade representative’s office said the products being excluded from the tariffs included car seats, shipping containers, cranes, certain fish and Bibles and other religious literature.
川普政府還表示，「基於健康、安全、國家安全及其他因素」，他們從關稅清單上移除了一些商品。貿易代表辦公室一名發言人表示，被排除在關稅範圍外的商品包括汽車座椅、集裝箱、起重機、某些魚類、《聖經》及其他宗教文獻。
The S&P 500 climbed nearly 2 percent after the announcement, lifted partly by stocks of retailers and computer chip producers that have been sensitive to indications that trade tensions were getting either better or worse.
消息一經宣布，標準普爾500指數即攀升近2個百分點，這在一定程度上是受到零售商及計算機晶片製造商股票的提振，它們對貿易緊張關係得到緩解或惡化的跡象很敏感。
廣告
Best Buy, which gets many of the products it sells from China, was among the best-performing stocks in the S&P 500, up more than 8 percent in morning trading. The Nasdaq composite index rose more than 2 percent.
百思買(Best Buy)銷售的許多商品都來自中國，它是標準普爾500指數中表現最佳的股票之一，在早盤交易中，該公司股票上漲超過8個百分點。納斯達克綜合指數(Nasdaq composite index)漲幅超過2個百分點。
Liu He, China’s vice premier and the country’s lead trade negotiator, spoke with Robert Lighthizer, the United States trade representative, and Steven Mnuchin, the Treasury secretary, on Tuesday, and the three agreed to speak again in two weeks, the state-run Xinhua News Agency reported.
官方媒體新華社報導稱，週二，中國副總理、首席貿易談判代表與美國貿易代表羅伯特·萊特希澤(Robert Lighthizer)及美國財政部長史蒂芬·馬努欽(Steven Mnuchin)進行了通話，三人同意兩周後再次通話。
Earlier Tuesday, Mr. Trump had criticized China for not making large purchases of American farm goods, suggesting that the tariffs might force them into action.
週二早些時候，川普曾批評中國沒有大量購買美國農產哦，暗示關稅可能會讓他們行動起來。
“As usual, China said they were going to be buying ‘big’ from our great American Farmers,” he wrote. “So far they have not done what they said. Maybe this will be different!”
「與往常一樣，中國說他們會『大大』地從我們優秀的美國農民這裡購買，」他寫道。「迄今為止，他們還沒有實現自己的說法。可能這次會不一樣！」
