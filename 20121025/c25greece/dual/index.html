希腊同意实施新一轮严苛紧缩措施

Greece Reaches Agreement With International Lenders

LIZ ALDERMAN
2012年10月25日
ATHENS — Greece has reached an agreement with its international lenders on a harsh new austerity package and a raft of controversial labor market reforms, the country’s finance minister said Wednesday.
雅典——周三，希腊财政部长称，希腊已同国际债主达成一致，同意实施一揽子严苛的新紧缩措施，并在劳动力市场推行大量争议性改革。
The long-sought deal is aimed at releasing billions of euros in additional financial aid for Greece and could also alleviate fears that the country might leave the euro monetary union.
经过长期努力达成的这一协议旨在为希腊额外提供几十亿欧元的金融援助，有可能减轻人们对希腊退出欧元区的担忧。
The deal still requires passage by the Greek Parliament, where it is certain to test the viability of the country’s shaky governing coalition.
协议尚需希腊议会通过，肯定会对希腊摇摇欲坠的执政联盟构成一场生死考验。
The finance minister, Yannis Stournaras, also said Wednesday that Greece’s European partners had agreed to give Athens additional time to carry out the austerity program — about 13.5 billion euros ($17.5 billion) of spending cuts and tax increases — instead of adopting it all immediately. But Simon O’Connor, spokesman for the European economic affairs commissioner, Olli Rehn, rebuffed reports that an extended timetable had yet been agreed to.
周三，财政部长亚尼斯·斯图纳拉斯(Yannis Stournaras)还说，希腊的欧洲盟友们同意多给希腊一点时间来执行紧缩措施，不要求希腊一蹴而就。该措施要求希腊实现总额约135亿欧元（175亿美元，约合1092亿元人民币）的减支及增税。但是，欧盟经济事务专员奥利·瑞恩(Olli Rehn)的发言人西蒙·奥康纳(Simon O'Connor)否认了双方已就延长时间达成一致的报道。
The austerity package, months in the making, has fueled sometimes violent street protests by Greeks who are facing their third round of belt-tightening in three years. And getting the three-party coalition of the prime minister, Antonis Samaras, to approve the package remains an uphill battle.
花费数月才制定的这一紧缩措施意味着希腊人将要在三年里第三次勒紧腰带，已经使得希腊人多次发起时或衍生暴力的街头抗议。要让安东尼斯·萨马拉斯总理(Antonis Samaras)率领的三党联盟批准这一措施，还得颇费一番力气。
The measures need to be approved by Greek lawmakers before Nov. 12. That is when finance ministers from euro zone countries, scheduled to meet in Brussels, are expected to consider whether Greece should get its new tranche of aid.
这些措施需要在11月12日前获得希腊立法者的批准。按照计划，欧元区各国财长届时将在布鲁塞尔聚会，预计会把是否向希腊发放新援助的问题提上议程。
Should the measures pass, Greece’s so-called troika of lenders — the International Monetary Fund, the European Central Bank and the European Commission — would issue a report gauging whether Greece has made sufficient progress in mending its tattered finances to receive the 31.5 billion euros in aid that Mr. Stournaras has said is needed for Greece to avoid bankruptcy.
如果这些措施获得通过，希腊那些人称“三驾马车”的债主——国际货币基金组织(International Monetary Fund)、欧洲央行(European Central Bank)、欧盟委员会(European Commission)——将会发布报告，评估希腊是否在改善崩溃财政方面取得了足够进展，从而有资格获得315亿欧元（合人民币2552亿元）援助。按照斯图纳拉斯的说法，这笔援助是防止希腊破产的必要条件。
With state coffers virtually empty, an economy near depression and unemployment at a record high of 25 percent, Greece is counting on the money to keep the government running. It also needs to pay off a portion of more than 8 billion euros in arrears that it owes suppliers for basic products, like medicines, that some companies have stopped supplying to Greece until they are paid.
希腊国库几近空虚，经济濒临萧条，失业率也创下了25%的新高，就指望靠这笔钱来维持政府运行。此外，希腊还得向供应商偿还药品等基本物资的一部分积欠货款，欠款总额逾80亿欧元（约合648亿元人民币）。一些公司已经停止向希腊供应基本物资，要等希腊付了钱再说。
And yet most of the money would not be put directly back into the economy. Instead, about 85 percent of the loan installment would go to replenishing the capital reserves of Greece’s shaky banking system.
但是，大部分援助款项不会直接用于经济。相反，约85%的贷款将会用于补充希腊银行体系的准备金。希腊的银行体系已经岌岌可危。
Mr. Samaras, the prime minister, has said the money will enable banks to start lending again, fueling growth at a time when the government estimates that the economy will contract by 6.5 percent this year.
萨马拉斯总理说，有了这笔钱，银行就可以再次开始发放贷款，起到推动经济增长的作用。据希腊政府估计，今年希腊经济将缩水6.5%。
But many Greeks are concerned that the banks would simply hold onto the money rather than lend to businesses, continuing a trend that has already led tens of thousands of small and medium-size enterprises to fold in the last several years.
但是，许多希腊人担心银行会延续以往的作风，紧紧攥住这笔钱，不贷给企业。过去几年之中，这样的作风已经使得几万家中小型企业关门大吉。
A draft version of the deal, leaked to the media late Tuesday, included cutbacks in the public sector, where 5,000 civil servants would be placed on reduced wages this year before being either transferred to other jobs or dismissed. The same would happen to another 20,000 in 2013.
协议草案于周二晚间漏到媒体手中，其中包括针对公共部门的一些裁减措施。今年将有五千名公务员遭到降薪处理，之后还会面临调岗或解雇。2013年，还会有2万名公务员罹此厄运。

The retirement age would increase by two years, to 67 from 65, and the number of associate professors at state universities would be cut from 15,226 to 2,000.

退休年龄会被推迟两年，由65岁变为67岁。国立大学的副教授岗位将会由15226个削减至2000个。
