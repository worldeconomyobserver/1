中國政府解聘反壟斷顧問張昕竹

In China, Antitrust Adviser Is Ousted in Qualcomm Inquiry

NEIL GOUGH, 儲百亮
2014年8月14日
HONG KONG — An adviser to a Chinese government antitrust committee has been dismissed, accused of accepting payments from Qualcomm, the American technology company under investigation in China for potential antitrust violations, Chinese state news reports said on Wednesday.
香港——中國官方媒體周三報道，中國政府的一個反壟斷委員會解聘了一名顧問。這名顧問被指收受了美國科技企業高通公司(Qualcomm)的報酬，高通因為可能違反了反壟斷法，正在受到中國政府的調查。
Qualcomm is one of dozens of multinational companies under pressure in China recently as regulators have deepened enforcement of antimonopoly legislation, which also covers cases of illegal pricing. Foreign carmakers, including General Motors, the Audi unit of Volkswagen and Daimler have come under scrutiny in recent weeks over auto parts, while antitrust investigators raided Microsoft’s China offices twice in the past two weeks.
最近，隨着中國的監管機構加強了反壟斷執法的力度，幾十家跨國公司倍感壓力，高通便是其中之一。中國的反壟斷法律也管轄涉及非法定價的案件。最近幾周，通用汽車(General Motors)、大眾汽車(Volkswagen)旗下的奧迪(Audi)，以及戴姆勒(Daimler)等外國汽車製造商都因汽車零部件受到了檢查，而在過去兩周，反壟斷調查人員兩次突擊搜查微軟(Microsoft)位於上海的辦公室。
Qualcomm, which sells wireless technology and is one of the world’s biggest chip makers, has been the subject of an antitrust investigation in China since late last year related to patent licensing and pricing of smartphone chips. The reports of allegations against the adviser do not accuse Qualcomm of breaching any rules by hiring him, but may complicate the company’s efforts to defuse an investigation.
銷售無線技術的高通公司是全世界最大的芯片生產商之一。自去年年底以來，高通便成了中國反壟斷調查的對象，調查涉及專利許可，以及智能手機芯片的定價。指責該名顧問的相關報道中，並未指控高通聘請顧問的行為違反了任何法規，但或許會讓該公司平息調查風波的努力更加複雜。
The allegations published by Chinese state media outlets on Wednesday said the company had made “large payments” to Zhang Xinzhu, an economist at the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, while he also served as an adviser on an antimonopoly committee under the State Council, or China’s cabinet.
中國官方媒體周三公布的相關指控稱，高通向中國社會科學院經濟學家張昕竹支付了「高額報酬」，而張昕竹同時也是中國國務院下屬的一個反壟斷委員會的顧問。
Mr. Zhang declined to comment beyond an emailed reply: “I can only keep silent, the atmosphere of opinion is too vicious right now.”
張昕竹在回復的電子郵件中表示，「現在輿論環境太惡劣，只能沉默」，除此之外不願發表其他評論。
At issue appears to be a study Mr. Zhang helped write on Qualcomm’s pricing of licenses. Qualcomm submitted a copy of the report in May to the National Development and Reform Commission, the powerful economic planning agency that is one of three Chinese government departments charged with enforcing antitrust law in the country.
問題的緣由似乎是張昕竹參與撰寫的一篇研究報告，文章的主題是高通許可證的定價。今年5月，高通向握有重權的經濟規劃機構國家發展和改革委員會提交了這份報告。在中國，發改委是有權執行反壟斷法的三個政府部門之一。
Mr. Zhang, who previously helped write academic research with acknowledged support from Qualcomm, was recently dismissed from the State Council advisory group. It was unclear why the cabinet had recently acted, or whether the extent of any financial relationship between Mr. Zhang and Qualcomm had been disclosed.
張昕竹曾參與撰寫學術研究論文，且文中承認受到了高通的支持。前不久，張昕竹被前述國務院下屬的專家諮詢組解聘。尚不清楚國務院為何最近做出這一舉動，也不清楚張昕竹和高通之間的任何經濟往來得到了多大程度的披露。
The commission has been investigating Qualcomm, and last month an official told China Daily, a state-run newspaper, that “the investigation is nearly concluded and the result will be announced soon.” Representatives at Qualcomm in Beijing did not respond to voice messages and an email seeking comment. The company’s headquarters in San Diego also did not respond to emails early Wednesday that sought comment on the company’s relationship with Mr. Zhang. The commission asked for faxed questions, to which it did not reply.
該委員會一直在調查高通，而且上月，一名官員告訴官方報紙《中國日報》，「調查接近尾聲，很快將公布結果。」高通公司在北京的代表沒有答覆請求置評的語音留言和電子郵件。周三早些時候，該公司位於聖迭戈的總部也未回復請其就該公司與張昕竹之間的關係發表評論的電子郵件。前述反壟斷委員會要求記者傳真採訪問題，但後來並未作出答覆。
In an interview published Tuesday in The Paper, a Chinese news website, Mr. Zhang said he had been dismissed from the State Council advisory group for “speaking out for foreign businesses.”
在中國新聞網站「澎湃新聞」周二刊登的一篇採訪文章中，張昕竹稱自己被國務院下屬的專家諮詢組解聘是因為「幫外企說話了」。
“It’s like I was acting as the defense for a criminal under death penalty,” he said. “Any case has its two sides, pro and contra, and it can’t be that there’s not even the right to speak up.”
「就好比我給死刑犯做了辯護，」張昕竹說，「任何一個案子都有正方和反方，不能連說話的權利都沒有吧。」
China’s antimonopoly law is intended to protect consumers from price gouging and other abuses, but foreign companies fear that the authorities could use it to single them out in an effort to bolster domestic companies or respond to political directives.
中國的反壟斷法旨在保護消費者不受哄抬物價或其他行為傷害，但外國公司擔心，官方可能會用這部法律針對它們，從而扶持本土企業或回應某些政治指示。
Foreign companies often lack the political patronage enjoyed by local firms, especially state-owned ones, and can have little recourse. In a statement on Wednesday, the European Chamber of Commerce in China said it had “received numerous alarming anecdotal accounts from a number of sectors that administrative intimidation tactics are being used to impel companies to accept punishments and remedies without full hearings.”
外國企業通常不享有中國企業，尤其是國有企業所享有的政治支持，而且申訴途徑也極少。周三，中國歐盟商會(European Union Chamber of Commerce in China)在一份聲明中稱，商會從「許多行業聽到了大量令人警覺的傳言，說有關部門會使用行政恐嚇的手段，在不進行全面聽證的情況下，就迫使公司接受懲罰做出賠償。」
“Practices such as informing companies not to challenge the investigations, bring lawyers to hearings or involve their respective governments or chambers of commerce are contrary to best practices,” the statement said.
聲明稱，「告誡公司不要阻撓調查、不要讓律師參加聽證、不要讓相應的政府或商會幹涉，這都不符合最佳實踐的原則。」
Still, foreign and domestic companies have been targeted in the past year, including manufacturers of infant milk formula, liquor distillers, drug companies and a food packaging business.
不過，過去一年裡外國和本土公司都受到了調查，其中包括嬰兒配方奶粉生產商、釀酒廠、製藥公司，以及一家食品包裝企業。
When it comes to foreign technology providers, the government has shown an added layer of wariness that has deepened over the past year, especially after Edward J. Snowden’s disclosures about surveillance by the United States.
對於外國科技產品提供商，中國政府在過去一年表現得更加小心謹慎，尤其是在愛德華·J·斯諾登(Edward J. Snowden)揭露美國政府的監聽項目之後。
“With tech, China has a clearly and consistently stated goal: reduce dependence on foreign companies,” said Adam Segal, the director of the program on digital and cyberspace policy at the Council on Foreign Relations in New York. “The antimonopoly investigations are one tool in a shifting set that Chinese policy makers can use to pressure foreign companies. But they also still need the companies.”
「在科技方面，中國的目標一直明確而一貫：減少對外國企業的依賴，」紐約對外關係委員會(Council on Foreign Relations)數字和網絡政策項目的負責人亞當·謝加爾(Adam Segal)說。「中國政策制定者可以利用時常變化的一系列手段對外國企業施加壓力，反壟斷調查只是其中的一種工具。不過他們仍然需要這些公司。」
Qualcomm established a presence in China in 1999 and by last year had grown to rely on the country for nearly half its total revenue. But business has slumped in China recently because of lower device sales and the underreporting of sales, which hurts the company’s licensing revenue. In an earnings conference call last month, Qualcomm said this situation would most likely affect its 2014 results and projected an 8 percent reduction in sales in the second half of the year.
高通1999年進入中國市場。去年，該公司在中國市場的營收達到了公司總營收的將近一半。但是，因為設備銷量減少，披露的銷售額低於預期，而這又影響了公司的許可收入，於是高通的業績出現下滑。上個月，高通在披露業績的電話會議上表示，這種情況很可能會對其2014年的業績造成影響，並預計下半年公司的銷售額將減少8%。
A day after the earnings call, Qualcomm announced that its venture investment arm had committed to invest up to $150 million in Chinese start-up companies, focusing on firms developing mobile technologies.
電話會議的第二天，高通宣布其風投部門已承諾向中國初創企業投資高達1.5億美元（約合9.2億元人民幣），投資對象主要是研發移動技術的公司。
Qualcomm’s president, Derek Aberle, last month made his third recent visit to China to discuss the antitrust investigation with representatives of the National Development and Reform Commission. “This is a large priority for the company to resolve,” Mr. Aberle said on the conference call last month. “We are actively engaged with the N.D.R.C. to try and get it resolved as expeditiously as possible.”
高通總裁德雷克·阿伯利(Derek Aberle)於上個月訪華（這是近期的第三次），與中國發改委的代表就反壟斷調查進行了磋商。「這是公司急需解決的一大問題，」阿伯利在上個月的電話會議上說。「我們正在與發改委積極接觸，希望儘可能迅速地解決問題。」
According to the report in The Paper, Mr. Zhang was highly critical, saying the chief problems are “enforcement exceeding powers and unprofessional enforcement.” Mr. Zhang said, “Currently, China’s administrative law enforcement is extremely dangerous, with investigative powers and law enforcement powers bound together.” He added, “From the viewpoint of administrative enforcement of the law, China’s antimonopoly law is at a dead end.”
澎湃新聞報道，張昕竹對此持強烈的批評態度，他說最大問題是「超權限執法，執法不夠專業」。張昕竹說，「目前中國的行政執法非常危險，調查權和執法權在一起。」他還說，「從行政執法講，中國反壟斷法就是死路一條。」
