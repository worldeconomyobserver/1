中美互相威胁：北京称已准备好反击美国关税

Trump Threatens More China Tariffs, and Beijing Prepares to Retaliate

ANA SWANSON, KEITH BRADSHER
2019年5月9日
WASHINGTON — President Trump toughened his stance toward China on Wednesday, threatening to prolong his trade war with Beijing, which immediately signaled that it was prepared to fight back.
华盛顿——特朗普总统周三对中国采取更强硬立场，威胁要延长与北京方面的贸易战，北京方面则立即表示准备反击。
Mr. Trump, in a pair of tweets on Wednesday morning, said he would be happy to keep tariffs on Chinese exports rather than make a bad deal, setting the stage for a contentious round of talks between the United States and China that is set to begin on Thursday. Mr. Trump’s comments provoked a swift response from the Chinese Commerce Ministry, which suggested that it was once again ready to retaliate against American companies and their products with its own countermeasures.
在周三早间的两条推文中，特朗普表示他宁愿对中国的出口商品继续加征关税，也不愿签署一份糟糕的协议，这为定于周四开始的美中之间一轮充满争议的谈判打好了铺垫。特朗普的言论激起了中国商务部的迅速反应，表示已再度准备好反制措施对美国公司及其产品实施报复。
Chinese negotiators including Vice Premier Liu He, one of China’s top economic officials and a close confidant of the country’s president, Xi Jinping, are headed to Washington to try to salvage a trade agreement that has fallen apart. But significant gaps remain between the two countries, and Mr. Trump suggested that he was ready to impose higher tariffs on $200 billion worth of Chinese goods on Friday morning, indicating a potentially long road ahead.
中国的谈判代表已前往华盛顿，试图挽救一份已然破裂的贸易协议，其中包括中国国务院副总理、最高经济官员之一、中国国家主席习近平的亲信刘鹤。但两国之间仍然存在巨大鸿沟，特朗普也表示已准备好要在周五早晨，对价值2000亿美元的中国商品加征关税，这表明前路或许依然漫长。
“The reason for the China pullback & attempted renegotiation of the Trade Deal is the sincere HOPE that they will be able to “negotiate” with Joe Biden or one of the very weak Democrats, and thereby continue to ripoff the United States (($500 Billion a year)) for years to come,” Mr. Trump tweeted.
“中国之所以出尔反尔，试图就贸易协议进行重新谈判，是因为一心寄望于能跟乔·拜登(Joe Biden)或某个非常软弱的民主党人进行‘谈判’，以便未来数年继续从美国盗窃（每年5000亿美元），”特朗普发推称。
广告
“Guess what, that’s not going to happen! China has just informed us that they (Vice-Premier) are now coming to the U.S. to make a deal. We’ll see, but I am very happy with over $100 Billion a year in Tariffs filling U.S. coffers…great for U.S., not good for China!” the president added.
“猜怎么着，这事不可能发生！中国刚刚通知我们，他们（副总理）要来美国谈协议。我们拭目以待，但我非常乐见美国金库每年能多收入1000多亿美元关税……对美国好极了，对中国不利！”特朗普接着写道。
On Wednesday morning, the United States trade representative filed a public notice saying that tariffs on roughly $200 billion of Chinese products would increase to 25 percent from 10 percent on Friday “in light of the lack of progress in the additional rounds of negotiations since March 2019.” That would bring the tariff rate on the entire $250 billon worth of Chinese goods subject to levies to 25 percent.
周三早晨，美国贸易代表发布公告称，“鉴于自2019年3月以来的后续几轮谈判未取得进展”，约2000亿美元中国商品的关税将于周五从10%上调至25%。这将使得总共价值2500亿美元中国商品的关税税率要按25%收取。
“The escalation of trade friction is not in the interests of the people of the two countries and the people of the world,” the Chinese Commerce Ministry said in response. “The Chinese side deeply regrets that if the U.S. tariff measures are implemented, China will have to take necessary countermeasures.”
“升级贸易摩擦不符合两国人民和世界人民的利益，”中国商务部在回应中称。“中方对此深表遗憾，如果美方关税措施付诸实施，中方将不得不采取必要反制措施。”
The retrenchment in talks came as a surprise to businesses and investors, who expected the two countries to resolve the monthslong trade war as early as this week. Markets, which fell on Tuesday, recovered ground on Wednesday as Mr. Trump confirmed the Chinese delegation was headed to the United States.
谈判收紧令企业和投资者均感意外，他们原本期望两国最早能在本周结束持续数月的贸易战。股市周二下跌，在特朗普确认中国代表团赴美行程之后，周三有所上涨。
Companies that would be affected by a tariff increase on Friday span a range of industries, from seafood and fertilizers to handbags and copper alloys. Industry associations said on Wednesday that their members were uncertain whether the tariffs would go into effect on Friday and were reviewing contingency plans.
将受周五关税上调影响的企业涉及从海产、化肥到手提包、铜合金等多个行业。多个行业协会周三表示，会员不确定关税是否将在周五生效，现正在审议应变计划。
“When the U.S. and China fight, nobody wins, as the past year’s market gyrations, lost deals and strained diplomatic ties have made abundantly clear,” said Peter Robinson, the president of the United States Council for International Business, which represents global companies.
“美国和中国交战没有赢家，过去一年的市场动荡、交易损失、外交关系紧张再清楚不过了，”代表全球企业的美国国际工商理事会(United States Council for International Business)主席彼得·罗宾森(Peter Robinson)说。
广告
A spokeswoman for Mr. Biden, who is running for the Democratic nomination for president, pushed back against Mr. Trump’s tweet, saying on Twitter that the president’s approach to the China trade talks has hurt “US farmers, small business owners & consumers, who feel the brunt of his tariff war.”
拜登正在角逐民主党总统候选人的提名，他的发言人对特朗普的推文进行了反驳，发推称总统在中国贸易谈判上的做法损害了“美国农民、小企业主和消费者的利益，他们受他的关税战冲击最大”。
The atmosphere surrounding the trade talks has shifted dramatically since a week ago, when observers in both countries thought a deal was imminent and that the tit-for-tat tariff war would soon end. Both sides had been discussing lifting tariffs on each other’s goods, along with a commitment from China to open its markets to American business, protect intellectual property and direct large purchases of United States products.
本轮谈判的气氛较之一周前发生戏剧性转变，当时两国的观察人士均以为协议即将达成，针锋相对的贸易战很快将要结束。双方此前一直在探讨取消对彼此的商品加征的关税一事，中方还承诺要对美国企业开放市场、保护知识产权，并直接大量购买美国产品。
But steady progress toward a trade deal blew up last weekend, when China called for substantial changes to the negotiating text, and Mr. Trump responded by threatening to raise existing tariffs and impose new ones on an additional $325 billion worth of products.
但上周末，中方要求对谈判文本进行大幅修改，特朗普以威胁上调关税并对另外3250亿美元产品加征新的关税予以回应，稳步推进贸易协议的进展就此泡汤。
It was unclear whether the two countries could bridge their differences before higher tariffs go into effect on Friday morning. That outcome will hinge on whether Mr. Liu comes ready to make a deal with the United States or is merely seeking to gather more information on acceptable outcomes before returning to China to gauge what is palatable to Mr. Xi.
目前尚不清楚，两国能否在周五早间提高关税生效前弥合分歧。结果将取决于刘鹤此行是否已经准备好同美国达成协议，抑或仅仅是为了收集更多关于可接受结果的信息，并在回国后衡量什么样的结果对习近平来说是最好的。
“We’ve gotten an indication they want to make a deal,” Sarah Huckabee Sanders, the White House press secretary, told reporters on Wednesday.
白宫新闻秘书萨拉·赫卡比·桑德斯(Sarah Huckabee Sanders)周三对记者表示：“我们看到他们希望达成协议的迹象。”
But some China experts were skeptical of rapprochement, saying both sides suffer from significant gaps in understanding after a falling out last week. Mr. Liu’s main priorities, they said, would be gathering information on how the countries could proceed and trying to forestall an increase in Mr. Trump’s tariffs.
但一些中国问题专家对局面的改善持怀疑态度。他们说，在上周的争吵之后，双方在理解上存在重大分歧。他们说，刘鹤的主要任务是收集有关两国如何取得进展的信息，并试图阻止特朗普提高关税。
广告
“I think a deal this week is highly unlikely, because the optics of it would be very difficult for China,” said Eswar Prasad, the former head of the International Monetary Fund’s China division.
“我认为，本周达成协议的可能性非常小，因为这看上去对中国来说非常困难，”国际货币基金组织中国部前负责人埃斯瓦尔·普拉萨德(Eswar Prasad)表示。
The quickest path to an agreement would require the Chinese side to once again extend concessions it recently revoked. Mr. Trump’s advisers were surprised by developments during talks last week in Beijing and in an exchange of documents over this past weekend, when Chinese negotiators called for changes in the language of all seven chapters of the 150-page draft agreement, people familiar with the negotiations said.
以最快速度达成协议需要中方再次做出前不久它撤销的让步。知情人士说，特朗普的顾问们对上周在北京举行的会谈，以及上周末交换的相关文件中的事态发展感到意外，当时中国谈判代表呼吁修改150页协议草案中全部七章的措辞。
The Chinese requests covered everything from agreements to protect American intellectual property to limiting Chinese subsidies and currency manipulation. While Mr. Liu was given the authority to negotiate the terms of the agreement with the United States, much of what was agreed to was still in relatively abstract terms, according to another person familiar with the talks. When the draft agreement was brought back to a wider group of Chinese officials, it was met with concerns that certain provisions agreed to in principle were in conflict with Chinese laws and would require legal changes that would be complicated or unacceptable to enact, this person said.
中国的要求涉及方方面面，从保护美国知识产权的协议，到限制中国的补贴和汇率操纵的协议。据另一位知情人士透露，尽管刘鹤获得了与美国谈判协议条款的权力，但已经达成的协议大部分仍然是相对抽象的条款。这位人士说，当协议草案被提交给更大范围的中国官员时，有人担心某些在原则上达成的条款与中国法律相抵触，需要对法律进行复杂或无法接受的修改。
In particular, the administration had wanted the text of the agreement to specify that some of the changes would be made in Chinese law. But Chinese negotiators insisted that the changes would be carried out through regulatory and administrative actions by the government, and not cemented in place through legislation in the National People’s Congress.
特别是，特朗普政府曾希望协议文本明确规定，对中国法律进行一些改动。但中方谈判代表坚称，这些改革将通过政府的监管和行政行动来实施，而不是通过全国人民代表大会的立法来巩固。
Foreign critics have long derided the National People’s Congress, China’s legislature, as a rubber stamp for the Communist Party. But moving legislation through the body requires time and political capital, and it could provoke unwelcome challenges from within senior policy circles.
长期以来，外国批评人士一直嘲笑中国立法机构全国人民代表大会是共产党的橡皮图章。但推动该机构通过立法需要时间和政治资本，并可能引发高层政策圈内部不受欢迎的挑战。
The Trump administration’s refusal to roll back all of its existing tariffs on China also became an issue, prompting criticism within Chinese policy circles that negotiators were giving away too much in exchange for too little.
特朗普政府拒绝撤销对中国的所有现有关税也成为一个问题，引发了中国政策圈的批评，称谈判代表们付出太多，换来的太少。
广告
The retreat angered Mr. Trump’s advisers, including Robert Lighthizer, the United States trade representative, who has warned the president repeatedly about China’s penchant for reneging on its agreements and pushed for tough enforcement language in the deal.
此举激怒了特朗普的顾问，包括美国贸易代表罗伯特·莱特希泽(Robert Lighthizer)。他曾多次警告总统，中国有违背协议的倾向，并敦促在协议中使用强硬的执行措辞。
Senior Trump administration advisers have been circulating a five-page, single-spaced document detailing the economic promises that China has made to various American presidents and then broken.
特朗普政府的高级顾问一直在传阅一份五页的单行距打印的文件，其中详述了中国向多位美国总统做出但后来又食言的经济承诺。
In remarks on Monday, Mr. Lighthizer said that China had made significant commitments to the United States that were enforceable, and that “some people” in China had objected to them.
莱特希泽在周一表示，中国对美国做出了可执行的重大承诺，但中国“有些人”反对这些承诺。
“There is an enormous level of distrust between the two sides,” Mr. Prasad said.
“双方之间存在着巨大的不信任，”普拉萨德说。
Longtime China critics in the United States have pushed the president to hold out against what they see as China’s tough negotiating style, including changing the terms of a deal at the last moment.
美国长期批评中国的人士敦促总统坚持反对他们眼中中国的强硬谈判风格，包括在最后一刻改变协议条款。
“In my study of Chinese negotiating tactics, in almost every case, they believe the end game is where they can score the most points,” Michael Pillsbury, a China scholar at the Hudson Institute and an adviser on China to the White House. “They’ll be totally prepared for this final phase.”
“在我对中国谈判策略的研究中，几乎在所有情况下，他们都认为，他们在谈判的最后阶段能获得最多的成果，”哈德逊研究所(Hudson Institute)中国问题学者、白宫中国问题顾问白邦瑞(Michael Pillsbury)说。“他们会为最后阶段做好充分的准备。”

Keith Bradsher是《世界经济观察报》上海分社社长。
