蘋果擬斥資1000億美元回報股東

As Profit Slips, Apple Looks to Reward Shareholders

NICK WINGFIELD
2013年4月24日
Apple has found an investor that has not lost faith in its prospects: itself.
蘋果公司(Apple)找到了一個還沒有對其前景失去信心的投資者：它自己。
On Tuesday, the company announced that it planned to more than double its program to return cash to shareholders through stock buybacks and a higher dividend, spending $100 billion on the effort through the end of 2015. Its share repurchases alone will increase to $60 billion from the $10 billion it committed previously, the largest such plan in history, the company said.
該公司周二宣布，公司打算把通過股票回購和派發更高股息的方式向股東返現的計劃擴大不止一倍，為此將在2015年底之前投入1000億美元（約合6179.21億元人民幣）。該公司稱，僅股份回購一項就將從此前承諾的100億美元增加至600億美元，成為其歷史上規模最大的同類計劃。
The move to rekindle its relationship with investors came as Apple announced its first profit decline in a decade. The company said its net income fell 18 percent in its fiscal second quarter, as one of the most successful technology franchises in recent years, the iPhone, showed signs of slowing and other, less profitable products began to make up more of its sales.
蘋果這項與投資者重修舊好的舉措發生在該公司宣布利潤在十年來首次出現下滑之後。該公司稱，該公司在第二財季的凈收入下滑了18%，旗下近年來最成功的技術產品iPhone出現放緩跡象，其他一些利潤較少的產品開始佔據更大的銷售份額。
The rarity of Apple’s decline in profit, which was expected, underscores how one of the more remarkable winning streaks in business has come to an end, at least for now. Investors have battered the company’s stock for months, sending its shares down from their peak of more than $700 last year as warning signs began to emerge about its growth prospects.
蘋果公司利潤的罕見下滑雖然在意料之中，但卻突顯了一個最成功的商業贏家是走向末路的過程，至少是就現在而言。幾個月來，投資者一直在打壓該公司的股價，致使股價從去年超過700美元的峰值下滑。峰值出現之時，該公司的增長前景也出現了警兆。
In regular trading on Tuesday, Apple shares rose nearly 2 percent to close at $406.13, and they reached about $408 in after-hours trading as investors reacted to the quarterly earnings news.
在周二的常規交易中，蘋果的股價上漲了近2%，收於406.13美元，隨着投資者對公司財季收入的消息做出反應，股價在盤後交易中漲到了約408美元。
One of the biggest questions facing Apple is whether the company can innovate its way out of its funk by delivering a breakthrough new product, perhaps in a category like television, that rekindles growth and the passion of investors.
蘋果面臨的一個最大問題是，公司能否依靠創新衝破眼下的困境，通過發佈一款興許來自電視領域的突破性新品，再次推動增長和投資者的熱情。
Apple provided no detail about its plans on Tuesday, other than the vague hints it often shares about exciting new products to come.
周二，蘋果沒有透露關於其計劃的任何細節，只是像往常一樣，模糊地暗示公司即將推出令人激動的新產品。
“Our teams are hard at work on some amazing new hardware, software and services and we are very excited about the products in our pipeline,” Timothy D. Cook, the company’s chief executive, said in a statement.
蘋果公司首席執行官蒂莫西·D·庫克(Timothy D. Cook)在一份聲明中稱，“我們的團隊正在努力研發一些驚人的新硬件、軟件和服務，我們為開發中的產品感到十分興奮。”
For its fiscal second quarter, which ended March 30, the company said, its net income dropped 18 percent to $9.55 billion, or $10.09 a share, from $11.62 billion, or $12.30 a share, during the same period a year earlier.
該公司稱，在截止於3月30日的公司第二個財季，其凈收入比去年同期下降了18%，從116.2億美元降至95.5億美元，每股收益從12.30美元降至10.09美元。
Revenue, meanwhile, rose 11 percent to $43.6 billion from $39.19 billion a year before.
與此同時，該公司的總收入上升了11%，從去年的391.9億美元上升到436億美元。
Wall street analysts expected the company to report earnings of $10.07 a share and revenue of $42.59 billion, according to the average of estimates compiled by Thomson Reuters.
根據湯森路透(Thomson Reuters)彙編的平均估值，華爾街分析人士曾預計該公司報告的每股收益將為10.07美元，總收入為425.9億美元。
Months ago, Apple sought to brace investors by warning that profit could decline about 20 percent in the quarter. At that time, Apple forecast revenue of $41 billion to $43 billion.
幾個月前，蘋果曾為投資者打預防針，警告稱其利潤會在該季度下降約20%。當時，蘋果預計其總收入會在410億美元至430億美元之間。
Sales of iPhones, the company’s biggest business, grew only 3 percent to $22.96 billion in the second quarter. Apple has said the expected decline in profit would be the result of a top-to-bottom overhaul of its product lines, including Macs, iPads and iPhones, all of which tend to be trickier and more expensive to manufacture during their initial months on the market. Eventually, manufacturing costs could fall as kinks are worked out and sales volume increases.
作為該公司最大業務的iPhone銷售在第二季度僅增長了3%，達到229.6億美元。蘋果稱，利潤下滑在意料之中，因為公司對包括Mac，iPad和iPhone在內的產品線進行了自上而下的徹底調整。總體說來，所有這些產品在最初上市的幾個月里都比較難於生產，成本也更高。不過，等問題得到解決、銷量也開始增加之後，生產成本就會降低。
The company has also warned that new products like the iPad Mini have lower profit margins than older items like its full-size iPad sibling. That has stirred up worries that Apple’s efforts to cater to more budget-conscious consumers with low-price products, especially in smartphones, could steadily erode its considerable profits.
該公司還警告稱，像iPad Mini這樣的新產品的利潤空間要比諸如全尺寸iPad之類的老產品小。這引發人們擔心，蘋果利用低價產品尤其是低價智能手機來迎合那些比較在意價錢的消費者，這種做法會不斷侵蝕其豐厚的利潤。
Apple is widely thought to be preparing a low-cost version of the iPhone to compete more aggressively with smartphones based on Google’s Android operating system. A cheaper device could hold special appeal in huge markets like India and China where average incomes are far lower than in the West.
人們廣泛認為，蘋果準備利用低價版iPhone來和基於谷歌安卓操作系統的智能手機展開更激烈的競爭。更廉價的設備將會對印度和中國等規模巨大但人均收入遠低於西方的市場產生特別大的吸引力。
Pushing into inexpensive phones could hurt Apple’s admired profit margins, though. Last year, the company garnered almost 70 percent of the profit in the mobile handset business, according to estimates by Canaccord Genuity.
然而，推廣低價手機可能會損害蘋果令人艷羨的利潤空間。據加通貝祥(Canaccord Genuity)估計，去年，蘋果公司將近70%的利潤都來自手機業務。
Apple’s gross profit margins, one of the most closely watched measures of how profitable it is, are already declining, falling to 37.5 percent in the second quarter from 47.4 percent a year ago. This is the fourth consecutive quarter of declining gross margins at Apple, the longest stretch of such declines since 1993, according to Bill Moore, director of corporate development for Bloodhound Investment Research, a provider of online investment management tools.
蘋果的毛利率（這是衡量蘋果利潤率的最受關注的指標之一）已經出現下跌，第二季度的毛利率降到了37.5%，而去年同期則為47.4%。網絡投資管理工具提供商Bloodhound投資研究公司(Bloodhound Investment Research)負責企業發展的比爾·摩爾(Bill Moore)說，這是蘋果毛利率連續下降的第四個季度，也是自1993年以來下跌時間延續最長的一次。
As Apple’s holdings of cash and cash equivalents have swelled — a figure now over $140 billion — investors have clamored loudly for the company to step up its efforts to buy back shares or issue a bigger dividend.
鑒於蘋果持有的現金和現金等價物大幅增加，現在已經超過1400億美元，投資者開始強烈呼籲，蘋果要麼加大回購股份的力度，要麼提高分紅。
The company said on Tuesday that its board had approved a 15 percent increase in its quarterly dividend. It declared a dividend of $3.05 a common share, which will be paid to shareholders on May 16.
蘋果公司周二表示，董事會已經批准將每個季度的股息提高15%。公司宣布，普通股的每股股息為3.05美元，將於5月16日支付給股東。
Apple said it planned to borrow cash as part of its plan to return cash to shareholders. Even though Apple has far more capital than it needs in its coffers, much of it is held overseas and would be subject to taxes if the company were to bring it back to the United States.
蘋果說，公司打算借一些現金，以便兌現向股東返現的計劃。雖然蘋果賬戶上的資金遠遠超出了它的需要，但公司的大部分資產都在海外，要轉回美國就得交稅。
“We believe so strongly that repurchasing our shares represents an attractive use of our capital that we have dedicated the vast majority of the increase in our capital return program to share repurchases,” Mr. Cook said in a statement.
庫克在一項聲明中表示，“我們堅定地相信，回購股份是使用我們的資本的一個誘人方式，我們把資本返還計劃中的絕大部分增量都放在了股份回購上。”
