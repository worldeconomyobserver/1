標普創新高，美股走出危機陰霾

Broad-Based S.&P. 500-Stock Index Ends at Record High

NATHANIEL POPPER
2013年3月29日
The most widely followed barometer of the United States stock market rose above its 2007 peak to hit a high on Thursday, while most of the rest of the world could only look on in envy.
周四，美國股市最受人關注的指數衝破了2007年的最高紀錄，世界大多數其他國家只能滿懷羨慕地觀望着。
The nominal record set by the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index is the latest sign that the American economy is recovering some of the strength it had before the financial collapse of 2008, partly helped by stimulus from the Federal Reserve Bank.
標準普爾500(Standard & Poor’s 500)股指創下了最高名義紀錄，這是美國經濟在一些領域恢復到2008年金融危機之前水平的最新跡象，聯邦儲備銀行(Federal Reserve Bank)的刺激措施對此做出了部分貢獻。
It has been a little more than three weeks since the Dow Jones industrial average hit a milestone high, also set in October 2007, but the S.& P. is considered more representative of the breadth of American stocks.
三周多以前，道瓊斯(Dow Jones)工業平均指數曾創下歷史最高紀錄，此前的紀錄也是在2007年10月創下的。然而人們認為標準普爾更能全面代表美國股市。
The S.& P. reached its new nominal high after several days of flirting with the record as investors struggled with the turmoil caused by Cyprus’s banking crisis. The milestone capped a strong first quarter, in which the index rose 10 percent to hit the high.
隨着投資者竭力應對塞浦路斯銀行危機帶來的震蕩，標準普爾在最高紀錄的區域波動了幾天，並於之後創下了最高名義紀錄。這個紀錄意味着第一季度的強勁增長再創新高，在此期間標準普爾500指數上漲了10%。
Meanwhile, stock markets in nearly every other large economy around the world are still well below their previous records. An index of the entire world’s stock market, without the United States, is still down about 29 percent from the level it hit in 2007, according to analysis done by Ned Davis Research. Only some smaller nations, such as Denmark, Mexico and Colombia, have fully recovered their losses.
與此同時，世界其他經濟體的股市幾乎都還遠在其歷史最高紀錄之下。Ned Davis Research公司的分析顯示，除了美國，全世界股市指數依然處在2007年最高紀錄水平的約29%以下。只有一些較小的國家，比如丹麥、墨西哥和哥倫比亞，已經完全收復失地。
In many emerging economies, the lagging performances of the stock markets have not necessarily pointed to slower overall growth.
很多新興經濟體股市表現滯後並不代表其整體增長放緩。
China’s economy, for instance, continues to expand at a significantly faster pace than the American economy. But China’s stock market has suffered because growth is slower than many investors expected just a few years ago. In addition, Chinese companies are more reliant on exports to places like Europe than corporate America. That has contributed to the MSCI China index remaining down 42 percent from highs in 2007.
比如，中國經濟繼續在以比美國經濟快得多的速度增長。但是中國股市卻因增長低於很多投資者在幾年前的預期而受到負面影響。此外，中國公司比美國公司更加依賴對歐洲等地區的出口。這造成MSCI中國指數(MSCI China Index)依然比2007年最高紀錄水平低42%。
Workers in the United States have learned that the stock market’s performance is not always a good gauge of the underlying economy’s strength. Unemployment in the United States has remained stubbornly high at the same time that share prices have risen since bottoming out in March 2009. Even with the record level, the S.& P. 500 is still not back to its 2007 level when inflation is taken into account.
美國工人已經了解到，股市表現並不總能準確反應出經濟基本面的強度。2009年3月，美國股價開始觸底反彈，而美國的失業率一直以來依然頑固地居高不下。如果考慮到通脹因素，標準普爾這次創下的最高紀錄還沒有企及2007年的水平。
Still, the performance of the American stock market would have seemed improbable during the depths of the crisis, given that it was financial markets in the United States that led the global economy into recession. Strategists and economists have said that the divergence since then is largely a result of the relative speed with which the United States government and corporate sector responded to the causes of the 2008 crisis.
然而，鑒於美國金融市場引領全球經濟陷入衰退，在危機最嚴重的時候，沒有人能預測到美國股市的當下表現。策略師和經濟學家認為，自那時起出現分化的主要原因是，美國政府和企業界對引發2008年危機的根源問題做出了較快反應。
“The U.S. addressed the problems of the financial crisis faster and with much more ferocity than the rest of the world,” said Edward M. Clissold, a market strategist at Ned Davis Research.
Ned Davis Research公司的市場策略師愛德華·M·克利索爾德(Edward M. Clissold)稱，“在解決金融危機帶來的問題上，美國比世界其他國家的反應更快，也更加有力。”
The S.& P. 500 finished Thursday up 0.4 percent, or 6.34 points, at 1,569.19. The Dow Jones industrial average climbed 0.4 percent, or 52.38 points, to 14,578.54. That is 11 percent above its level at the beginning of the quarter.
周四，標準普爾500上升6.34點，漲幅0.4%，收於1569.19點。道瓊斯工業平均指數上升52.38點，漲幅0.4%，收於14578.54點，比一季度開始時的水平上升了11%。
The Nasdaq composite index rose 0.3 percent, or 11 points to 3,267.52, far below the heights it reached during the dot-com boom of the late 1990s.
納斯達克綜合指數(Nasdaq Composite index)上升11點，漲幅0.3%，收於3267.52點，遠低於20世紀90年代末互聯網泡沫時期達到的最高點。
Economists have given much of the credit for the market’s recovery to the Fed, which worked quickly with the rest of the federal government to bail out and revamp the nation’s banks and financial system, which the European Central Bank started doing in earnest only last year. While bank bailouts remain contentious, they have allowed the institutions to resume lending.
經濟學家把市場恢復的大部分原因歸功於美聯儲(Fed)，美聯儲和聯邦政府其他部門一起，快速解救並重整美國銀行和金融體系，而歐洲央行(European Central Bank)只從去年開始才真正努力展開行動。雖然銀行救援計劃仍存爭議，但它使得這些機構得以繼續發放貸款。
The Fed also acted on its own to pump money into the economy with bond-buying programs known as quantitative easing. Many central bankers around the world worried that those programs would result in extreme inflation. There are also fears that the American economy will not be able to remain on its current trajectory once the Fed draws back. But all that has not stopped other countries from beginning to copy the Fed’s lead.
美聯儲還自行通過量化寬鬆，例如債券購買計劃，向市場注入資金。很多各國的央行人士曾擔心，這些計劃會誘發惡劣的通貨膨脹。還有人擔心，一旦美聯儲收手，美國經濟將無法維持當下的增長勢頭。但是這些擔心都沒有妨礙到其他國家開始照搬美聯儲的做法。
“The Fed has won the battle, and continues to win the battle,” said Jack Malvey, the chief global markets strategist at BNY Mellon.
紐約梅隆銀行(BNY Mellon)國際市場首席策略師傑克·馬爾維(Jack Malvey)稱，“美聯儲已經贏了這場戰鬥，而且將會繼續獲勝。”
In Japan, the government of Prime Minister Shinzo Abe was elected in December after promising a more aggressive approach to monetary policy. Japan’s Nikkei index has been the best performer of any of the world’s large stock markets in the first quarter of this year, rising 19 percent. That still leaves the Nikkei almost 68 percent below the heights it scaled in 1989 before Japan’s real estate market soured.
在日本，去年12月安倍晉三(Shinzo Abe)當選為首相，此前其競選團隊曾承諾要採取更積極的貨幣政策。日經指數(Nikkei Index)今年第一季度的漲幅達19%，其表現位居全球大型股市之首。即便如此，日經指數依然比1989年日本房地產泡沫前的最高紀錄低幾乎68%。
The European Central Bank began taking more aggressive action to stimulate the continent’s economy last year, most notably after the bank’s president, Mario Draghi, said in July that he would do “whatever it takes to preserve the euro.” Since then, European stock indexes have roughly kept pace with the S.& P. 500, but the MSCI index of European stocks remains 28 percent below its 2007 peak.
去年，歐洲央行(European Central Bank)開始為刺激歐洲經濟採取更積極的措施，尤其是在該行行長馬里奧·德拉吉(Mario Draghi)去年7月稱，他將採取“一切措施來挽救歐元”之後。從那時起，歐洲股市開始與標準普爾500大致保持一致。但是MSCI歐洲股市指數依然比其2007年最高紀錄低28%。
The other major catalyst for the ascent of the American stock market is the cost-cutting strategy used by corporate executives, which has raised profit and allowed companies to increase dividends to shareholders. That strategy, however, has exacted a toll on American workers, who have faced layoffs and pay cuts.
促成美國股市這次破紀錄上攻的另一主要因素是企業高管使用了降低成本的策略，從而增加了企業利潤，使公司可以提高給股東的分紅。然而，這卻對美國的工人造成了壓力，他們面臨著裁員和降薪的危險。
In Europe, by contrast, the greater legal protections offered to workers have made it harder for companies to revamp their businesses for the current economic environment.
相比之下，歐洲對勞工有着更多的法律保護，這讓那裡的公司較難根據當前的經濟環境進行調整。
“There is a very aggressive corporate mind-set, that a lot of people don’t agree with,” said Joseph P. Quinlan, the chief market strategist for Bank of America’s private wealth management division. “But corporate America has proved that it is much nimbler and more resilient” in the new economic climate.
“美國有着一種頗具闖勁的企業思維模式，對此很多人都不贊同，”美國銀行(Bank of America)私人財富管理部門的首席市場策略師約瑟夫·昆蘭(Joseph Quinlan)稱，然而，在新的經濟環境下，“美國企業界已經證明這一模式更加靈活，也更具韌性。”
