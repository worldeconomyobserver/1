奔驰新一代S级轿车亮相，开启自动驾驶时代

A Benz With a Virtual Chauffeur

JACK EWING
2013年5月22日
Hangar doors slid open to reveal a fleet of white Mercedes-Benz vehicles arrayed on a rain-slicked runway.
机库门徐徐划开，露出了一列白色的梅赛德斯-奔驰汽车(Mercedes-Benz)，停在雨水冲刷过的跑道上。
As fireworks shot skyward, an imposing gray sedan zoomed forward onto a temporary stage, delivering Alicia Keys, in a dark floor-length evening gown, to the piano where she performed with a local backup band, the Hamburg Symphony.
随着烟花冲向天空，一辆气派的灰色轿车向前驶入一个临时舞台，把身着暗色曳地晚礼服的艾丽西亚·基斯(Alicia Keys)送到了钢琴前，然后她在本地乐队汉堡交响乐团(Hamburg Symphony)的伴奏下进行了演出。
Befitting the flagship of the Mercedes line, the premiere of the new S-Class at a vast Airbus jetliner factory here on Wednesday night was a grandiose event. Always a showcase for luxury appointments, this latest incarnation of the S-Class is notable for much more than features like the so-called hot-stone massages offered by its reclining rear seats. Or the Wi-Fi. Or the cup holders that keep drinks warm or cold.
周三晚间，作为梅赛德斯产品线的旗舰车型，新S级车型在空中客车(Airbus)的一间大型喷气式客机厂房内闪亮登场，场面壮观。该车型一直都是展现各种豪华配置的平台，不过这辆最新一代的S级轿车之所以引人关注，可不仅仅是所谓的倾斜后排座椅提供的热石按摩、Wi-Fi，或是能够保持饮品冷热的杯架。
The 2014 S-Class, which goes on sale in September at an estimated starting price of $100,000, is a significant advance in the development of autonomous autos. That is, while it still requires a human behind the steering wheel, in the right conditions the car can steer itself through city traffic or drive on the highway at speeds upward of 120 miles an hour using an array of radar, infrared and optical sensors to track lane markings or the car ahead — even around curves.
这两2014款的S级轿车将于9月份上市，预计起售价格10万美元（约合61万元人民币），是自动驾驶汽车发展历程中的一个重大进步。也就是说，虽然仍需有人坐在方向盘后，在适当的条件下，通过使用一组雷达、红外线和光学感应器来跟踪车道标记和前车，这辆车可以自动行驶在城市的车流、行人之中，也能在高速路上以超过120英里（约合193公里）的时速驾驶，甚至是在弯道上。
“It marks the beginning of autonomous driving,” said Dieter Zetsche, chief executive of Daimler, the parent company of Mercedes-Benz.
梅赛德斯-奔驰的母公司戴姆勒(Daimler)的总裁迪特·蔡澈(Dieter Zetsche)说，“它标志着自动驾驶的开始。”
There might be an element of hyperbole in that statement. But certainly the S-Class, which can also park itself, brake automatically to avoid hitting humans or other cars and sense when a driver is becoming fatigued, is a further evolution of systems intended to relieve some of the tedium of driving.
这一声明或许有些夸张。但S级轿车能够自动泊车、自动刹车以避免撞上人或其他车辆，并能在司机疲劳时有所感应，它是轿车系统的进一步改进，旨在减轻一些驾驶上的枯燥。
The optional system is analogous to the autopilots that enable airliners to carry out many of the routine tasks of flight and cruising but still require a human pilot to keep an eye on things. Future upgrades of the Mercedes S-Class will enable the car to automatically change lanes at autobahn speeds.
这一可选系统类似于自动驾驶系统，可以让飞机自主完成许多常规飞行和巡航任务，但仍旧需要一名飞行员进行监督。奔驰S级未来的升级版将能够以德国高速公路的速度行驶，并自动转换车道。
Along with BMW and Audi, its German luxury rivals, Mercedes is pushing technology ever closer toward a virtual chauffeur. BMW later this year plans to roll out a similar system in its i3 electric city car, and soon after in the 7 Series luxury sedan.
在梅赛德斯及其德国豪车竞争对手宝马(BMW)和奥迪(Audi)的共同推动下，我们的科技已经距离实现“虚拟司机”这一目标越来越近。宝马计划于今年晚些时候在其i3城市电动车上推出一个类似的系统，很快还将装到7系豪华轿车上。
Most major carmakers are working on some form of self-driving capability. The 2013 Lincoln MKZ has a system that keeps the car in lane and maintains a set distance from the vehicle ahead. But unlike the Lincoln, the Mercedes is programmed to hold the car in the middle of the lane, rather than just correcting if the driver drifts out of line.
大多数主要汽车制造商都在研制某种形式的自动驾驶功能。2013款林肯(Lincoln)MKZ拥有一个系统，能把车保持在一个车道内，并与前车保持固定距离。与林肯不同的是，梅赛德斯的程序是控制汽车在车道中间，而非仅仅在司机开出线时进行纠正。
Still, German engineers are skeptical of predictions by Google engineers and others that within five years vehicles will be driving themselves from the garage to the grocery store without human intervention. It will take a decade, maybe more, to solve all the technological and legal problems, they say.
不过，德国工程师怀疑谷歌(Google)工程师和其他人的预测，即五年之内，车辆就能够自动从车库开到杂货店而无需人工干预。他们说，这将需要10年或更久的时间来解决所有的技术和法律问题。
“We think we still have quite a bit of work ahead of us,” said Werner Huber, director of driver assistance technology at BMW.
“我们认为还有相当多的工作要做，”宝马的驾驶员辅助技术主任维尔纳·胡贝尔(Werner Huber)说。
It is logical that German carmakers would be leading the way toward vehicles that can drive themselves. Their well-heeled customers are willing and able to pay for a feature that would, for instance, let them check a stock portfolio online while stuck in traffic, rather than staring at the taillights ahead.
德国汽车制造商在开发自动驾驶汽车上占主导是合乎逻辑的。他们富有的顾客愿意、也有能力为这种功能花钱，举例来说，这能让他们在堵车时上网查看股票投资组合，而不是盯着前车的尾灯。
Self-driving cars might one day also offer mobility to people who are unable to drive because of age or handicaps. Google, applying its expertise in artificial intelligence, created a stir last year with a video that showed a blind man at the wheel of one of the company’s self-driving vehicles. The implication is that self-driving cars are close to reality.
或许在某一天，自动驾驶汽车还能让因年龄或残障而无法驾驶的人行动更自由。谷歌曾应用它在人工智能上的技术优势，在去年以一段视频引起轰动，其中展示了一名盲人驾驶该公司一辆自动驾驶汽车。这意味着自动驾驶汽车即将成为现实。
The more cautious assessment of self-driving technology by Mr. Huber and others partly reflects the classic Spock-vs.-Kirk difference in mentality between Germany’s rational, data-driven engineers and the visionaries of Silicon Valley.
胡贝尔和其他一些人对自动驾驶科技更为谨慎的看法，部分地体现出典型的史巴克(Spock)对寇克(Kirk)的不同心态，即理性的、看重数据的德国工程师和硅谷的幻想家之间的差别。（史巴克和寇克是影视作品《星际迷航》[Star Trek]中的主要角色，在这里代表理性和感性——译注）
German engineers point out they are a long way from having software that can do things that come natural to humans — for instance, judging whether a person standing at a street corner is about to cross the road or is just waiting for a bus.
德国工程师指出，要开发出软件来模拟人类与生俱来的一些能力，还有很长的路要走，例如判断一个站在街角的人是要穿过马路还只是在等公车。
There are also myriad legal issues which must be resolved by governments and insurance companies. If a car gets in an accident while on autopilot, who pays the damage, the car’s owner or the automaker? The questions about liability are one reason that the Mercedes system requires the driver to keep at least one hand on the steering wheel.
此外还有无数法律问题必须由政府和保险公司去解决。如果一辆汽车在自动驾驶时出现事故，由谁来为损失买单，是汽车拥有者还是汽车制造商呢？这类关于责任的问题，就是梅赛德斯系统要求司机至少把一只手放在方向盘上的一个原因。
Mr. Zetsche, the Daimler chief executive, said that experimental Mercedes technology already enables a car to drive itself from one place to another without human intervention, though more work remains to ensure the system is totally reliable.
戴姆勒的总裁蔡澈称，奔驰的实验技术已经可以使一辆车从一个地方自动驾驶到另一个地方，无需人工干预，不过仍需进行更多工作来确保该系统完全可靠。
It might take longer to perfect the legal framework than the software, he said.
他说，完善法律框架可能要比完善软件花更长时间。
“Certainly in a decade I would sit in a car — a Mercedes car — and let the car go from A to B,” Mr. Zetsche said in a brief interview at the S-Class introduction. “We might still struggle with the legal challenges.”
“在十年之内，我必定能够坐上一辆车，一辆奔驰车，让车自己从A地驾驶到B地，”蔡澈在S级轿车推介会上接受简短采访时说。“不过那时我们可能仍旧在与法律难题做斗争。”
A recent ride in a self-driving BMW research car illustrated some of the hurdles. The test vehicle, a 5 Series sedan loaded with a dozen unobtrusive sensors, handled heavy autobahn traffic outside Munich with aplomb. It slowed down to let a Ford Fiesta merge into traffic from an onramp, for example. It read traffic signs and adjusted the car’s speed automatically.
最近驾驶一辆自动驾驶的宝马研发用车说明了存在的一些问题。测试车是一辆装配了十几个不起眼传感器的5系轿车，在慕尼黑郊外拥挤的高速公路上显得泰然自若。例如，它能够减速让一辆从上闸道进入的福特嘉年华(Ford Fiesta)并入车流。它还能够读取交通标志，并且自动调整车行速度。
But the logic-driven BMW had trouble gauging the intentions of erratic humans. When a vehicle in the lane to the left slowed down for no apparent reason, the BMW slowed down too in order to avoid passing on the right. A human being might not have interpreted the rules of the road so literally.
但是靠逻辑驱动的宝马车在揣测飘忽不定的人类意图时遇到了障碍。当左边车道上的一辆车在没有明显原因的情况下放慢时，宝马轿车也慢了下来，目的是避免从右道超车。而人是不大可能以这种严格的方式来理解交通法规。
Autonomous driving will be easier to manage when car companies work out standards that would let cars share information like location and speed among themselves.
当汽车公司制定出标准，让汽车互相之间共享诸如位置和速度之类信息的时候，自动驾驶将更容易控制一些。
Cost is another obstacle. Self-driving technology will not be feasible until the equipment itself is affordable. The expense of research and development is one reason BMW formed a partnership with a German components supplier, Continental, to develop what it calls the Connected Drive systems.
成本是另一个障碍。在人们能够负担得起这种设备之前，自动驾驶科技无法可行。宝马与德国零部件供应商大陆集团(Continental)建立伙伴关系以开发所谓的“联网驾驶”系统(Connected Drive)，这其中研发费用也是一个考虑。
Alejandro Vukotich, head of development for driver assistance systems at Audi, pointed out that predictions of utopian technological progress are notoriously unreliable. He recalled reading an article in National Geographic as a boy that predicted cars would be driving themselves by the year 2000.
奥迪的驾驶员辅助系统开发部主任亚历杭德罗·沃库季奇(Alejandro Vukotich)指出，乌托邦式技术进步的预测极其不可靠。他回忆起童年时曾阅读过美国《国家地理》杂志(National Geographic)上的一篇文章，其中预测到2000年汽车就能实现自动驾驶。
“We have passed the year 2000,” Mr. Vukotich noted.
“我们已经过了2000年了，”沃库季奇说。
He and others see the path to fully automated driving as gradual. “It’s difficult to make a prognosis,” he said. “We will get closer step by step.”
他和其他一些人认为，完全自动驾驶需要逐步实现。“很难作出预测，”他说。“我们会一步一步地去实现。”

翻译：林蒙克
