貿易戰衝擊美國製造業，重塑全球經濟

Trade War Starts Changing Manufacturers in Hard-to-Reverse Ways

BEN CASSELMAN
2019年5月31日
PORTLAND, Ore. — When the Trump administration first imposed tariffs on $34 billion in Chinese imports in July, Andy LaFrazia figured it was just another curveball for his company.
奧勒岡州波特蘭——去年7月，川普政府首次對價值340億美元的中國進口商品加徵關稅時，安迪·拉法齊亞(Andy LaFrazia)認為，對他的公司來說，不過是又一番曲折而已。
“Everyone was saying: ‘Oh, it’s a negotiating tactic. It won’t last long,’” Mr. LaFrazia recalled.
「所有人都說：『哦，這是談判策略。不會持續太久，」拉法齊亞回憶道。
But nearly a year later, the trade war shows no sign of cooling off. So ControlTek, the electronics manufacturer that Mr. LaFrazia runs near Portland, is taking steps to protect itself, a strategic shift that has been repeated in boardrooms and executive suites around the world in recent weeks.
但近一年後，貿易戰仍沒有降溫的跡象。因此，拉法齊亞在波特蘭附近經營的電子產品製造商ControlTek正在採取自保措施，最近幾週，全球各地的董事會和高管層都在進行這種戰略轉變。
ControlTek is rewriting contract language to make it easier to pass the cost of tariffs on to its customers. It is shifting supply chains out of China where possible, and redesigning products to avoid Chinese components where it isn’t. And as a tiny player in an enormous global industry, it is discovering that there is only so much it can do.
ControlTek正在改寫合同措辭，方便將關稅成本轉嫁給客戶。它正在盡可能地將供應鏈轉移到中國以外的地方，並重新設計產品，避免使用中國製造的零部件。作為龐大全球產業中的一個小小參與者，它發現自己能做的只有這麼多。
廣告
“We’re very much at the end of the whip getting thrown around,” Mr. LaFrazia said.
「我們在很大程度上處於鞭子末端，不由自主地被甩來甩去，」拉法齊亞說。
Despite dire warnings from economists, Mr. Trump’s trade war has so far done little to derail the decade-long recovery from the Great Recession. Economic growth has remained strong, and the unemployment rate last month hit a 50-year low.
儘管經濟學家發出了可怕的警告，但到目前為止，川普的貿易戰幾乎沒有破壞大衰退後持續十年的復甦。經濟增長依然強勁，失業率在上個月降至50年來的最低點。
But evidence is mounting that the conflict has taken an economic toll. The Commerce Department said Thursday that trade — both imports and exports — slumped in April, and data released earlier this week showed a sharp slowdown in the manufacturing sector, amplifying a recent trend. The bond market in recent days has been sending signals that the trade war could be a threat to growth in the United States and globally.
但越來越多的證據表明，衝突已經造成了經濟損失。美國商務部週四表示，4月份進出口貿易均出現大幅下滑，本週早些時候公布的數據顯示，製造業大幅放緩，增強了近期的趨勢。最近幾天，債券市場一直在發出信號，表明貿易戰可能對美國乃至全球的增長構成威脅。
And as the conflict drags on, there are signs it is beginning to reshape the global economy in more fundamental ways.
隨著衝突的持續，有跡象表明，它正開始以更根本的方式重塑全球經濟。
“There’s definitely lasting damage that has been done,” said Mary Lovely, a Syracuse University economist and senior fellow at the Peterson Institute for International Economics in Washington. “It’s not going to mean the end of the world tomorrow, but it’s death by a thousand cuts. How competitive is America going to be in 10 or 15 years?”
「這肯定造成了持久的傷害，」雪城大學(Syracuse University)經濟學家、華盛頓彼得森國際經濟研究所(Peterson Institute for International Economics)高級研究員瑪麗·洛夫裡(Mary Lovely)說。「這並不意味著明天就是世界末日，而是一種凌遲的死法。10年或15年後，美國的競爭力會怎麼樣？」
Tariffs have not yet compelled businesses to return large-scale production to the United States, where labor and other costs tend to be much higher than in China and other overseas manufacturing hubs.
關稅還沒有迫使企業將大規模生產轉移回美國，美國的勞動力和其他成本往往比中國和其他海外製造業中心高得多。
廣告
But trade tensions are accelerating a corporate trend of shifting supply chains away from China. In a recent survey of more than 200 corporate executives by the consulting firm Bain, 42 percent said they expected to get materials from a different region in the next year, and 25 percent said they were redirecting investments out of China. More companies are likely to follow suit in coming weeks after the Trump administration moved to limit business with Huawei, the Chinese telecommunications giant, which the White House sees as a security threat.
但貿易緊張正在加速企業將供應鏈從中國轉移出去的趨勢。諮詢公司貝恩(Bain)最近對200多名企業高管進行的一項調查顯示，42%的高管表示，他們預計明年將從不同的地區獲得原材料，25%的高管表示，他們將把投資轉向中國以外。在川普政府採取行動限制與中國電信巨頭華為的業務往來之後，未來幾週可能會有更多公司照做。白宮認為，華為對美國構成了安全威脅。
Many companies were initially reluctant to abandon longstanding supplier relationships over a trade dispute that could be over in months, choosing instead to absorb the tariffs or find ways to share the costs with suppliers and customers. Now some are re-evaluating those decisions.
許多公司最初不願放棄長期的供應商關係，因為貿易爭端可能在幾個月內結束。他們選擇了承受關稅，或設法與供應商和客戶分擔成本。現在，一些人正在重新評估這些決定。
GoPro, the camera maker, said this month that it was shifting some production from China to Mexico. Universal Electronics, a manufacturer of remote controls, announced a similar move late last year. And Varex Imaging, a Utah-based maker of X-ray equipment, said this month that it was working to “redirect our supply chain away from China” in response to the tariffs.
相機製造商GoPro本月表示，將把部分生產從中國轉移到墨西哥。去年年底，遙控器製造商環球電子(Universal Electronics)也宣布了類似的舉措。總部位於猶他州的X射線設備製造商Varex Imaging本月表示，為應對關稅，該公司正努力「將我們的供應鏈從中國轉向別處」。
“Most companies took a wait-and-see attitude” at first, said Pete Guarraia, who leads Bain’s supply chain practice. “That was absolutely the mind-set. Now it’s: ‘I can’t wait any longer. I have to take some action.’”
「起初，多數公司採取觀望態度，」貝恩供應鏈業務主管彼特·瓜拉亞(Pete Guarraia)表示。「當時的心態絕對是那樣。現在則是『不能再等了，必須採取點行動』。」
Electronics manufacturers could be among the first to feel the full brunt of the trade war. The industry is perhaps uniquely global: Chips made in Oregon or Texas are shipped to a plant in Mexico to be attached to circuit boards made in China alongside capacitors made in Vietnam. It is not unheard-of for a product or its components to cross the Pacific three or even four times before showing up on retail shelves.
電子產品製造商可能是最先受到貿易戰衝擊的企業之一。該行業或許具有獨特的全球性：奧勒岡州或德克薩斯州製造的晶片被運往墨西哥的工廠，與中國製造的電路板以及越南製造的電容器連接在一起。出現在零售貨架之前，一件產品或其部件三次甚至四次跨越太平洋，算不上什麼新鮮事。
The crackdown on Huawei in recent weeks has opened a new front to the trade war. Huawei buys chips and other components from American manufacturers, and analysts warn that the fight could quickly spread to other companies.
最近幾週對華為的打壓又給貿易戰開闢了一條新戰線。華為要從美國製造商那裡購買晶片和其他零部件。分析人士警告稱，這場戰鬥可能很快蔓延到其他公司。
American companies sold more than $200 billion in computers and electronic goods to foreign buyers last year, including $18 billion to China. And while that was a small part of the United States’ $2.5 trillion in total exports last year, the broader tech sector has accounted for an outsize share of economic growth in recent years.
去年，美國公司向外國買家出售了價值2000多億美元的電腦和電子產品，其中180億美元銷往中國。雖然這只是美國去年2.5萬億美元出口總額的一小部分，但近年來，更廣泛的科技部門在經濟增長中所佔的份額已經非常大。
廣告
Anything that disrupts the global supply chains the industry relies on could threaten American economic growth, said Torsten Slok, chief economist for Deutsche Bank. Semiconductor sales, he said, have proved to be a reliable indicator of the direction of the broader economy — and sales have been falling this year.
德意志銀行(Deutsche Bank)首席經濟學家托斯滕·斯洛克(Torsten Slok)表示，任何擾亂該行業所依賴的全球供應鏈的行為，都可能威脅到美國的經濟增長。他表示，半導體銷售已被證明是反映整體經濟方向的可靠指標，今年該行業的銷售一直在下降。
“We will find out soon if the economy was strong enough to withstand this,” Mr. Slok said.
「我們很快就會知道，經濟是否足夠強勁，能夠承受這一切，」斯洛克說。
If the electronics industry sneezes, few places will catch a cold as quickly as Portland. The industry employs close to 40,000 people in Oregon, including 20,000 at Intel, the state’s largest private employer. Oregon exported $2.7 billion in electronics goods to China last year, more than any state other than California — a total that doesn’t include companies, like ControlTek, that are just across the Columbia River in Washington State.
如果電子行業有任何風吹草動，很少有地方會像波特蘭這樣最先受到影響。該行業在奧勒岡州僱傭了近四萬人，其中該州最大的私營企業英特爾(Intel)就佔兩萬人。奧勒岡州去年向中國出口了27億美元的電子產品，僅次於加州——這個數字還不包括像ControlTek這樣位於哥倫比亞河對岸、在華盛頓州的企業。
Founded in 1971, ControlTek has weathered the rise of Japan and then China. Today, the company and its 140 employees don’t try to compete directly with the high-volume factories in China.
ControlTek成立於1971年，經受住了日本和中國的崛起。如今，該公司及其140名員工不再試圖直接與中國的大批量工廠競爭。
Instead, ControlTek, like American manufacturers in other industries, has survived by carving out a niche based on quality and service. Its plant in Vancouver, Wash., has state-of-the-art machines that place components — integrated circuits, capacitors, resistors and other devices, some barely big enough to see with the naked eye — onto circuit boards destined for medical devices, aircraft and even pitching machines. But it also has employees hand-soldering parts beneath high-powered magnifying glasses, the kind of personalized attention that can look like an anachronism on today’s highly automated factory floors.
相反，像其他行業的美國製造商一樣，ControlTek通過質量和服務開拓出一個利基市場，得以生存下來。它在華盛頓州溫哥華的工廠擁有最先進的機器，可以將集成電路、電容器、電阻和其他設備的元件（有些元件幾乎無法用肉眼看到）安裝在為醫療設備、飛機甚至發球機設計的電路板上。但它也有使用高倍放大鏡手工焊接零件的員工，這種個性化的處理在如今高度自動化的工廠車間裡可能顯得不合時宜。
When the first round of tariffs went into effect last year, ControlTek executives had no idea how they would be affected, or even how to find out. The software for tracking inventory showed the company’s suppliers, but not where those suppliers got their material.
當第一輪關稅於去年生效時，ControlTek的高管們並不清楚他們將受到何種影響，甚至不知道要如何找到答案。庫存跟蹤軟體雖然列出了公司的供應商，但並沒有顯示這些供應商從哪裡獲得的原材料。
“I didn’t even know that was something you could find out,” Mr. LaFrazia said.
「我甚至不知道這些東西是可以弄清楚的，」拉法齊亞說。
It was also unclear which products were subject to tariffs. Mr. LaFrazia, a 54-year-old Air Force veteran, recalled spending hours poring over an online list of tariff codes to figure out which applied to his parts. When he called the manufacturers, they often didn’t know where they fell in the government’s taxonomy of tradable goods. Even the government’s experts can get confused — ControlTek recently had a shipment of parts delayed for 12 days because customs officials thought they contained aluminum that was subject to tariffs. (They didn’t.)
而且公司也不清楚究竟哪些產品需要徵收關稅。現年54歲的拉法齊亞曾在空軍服役。他回憶道，他用了好幾個小時的時間仔細研究一份關稅代碼的在線清單，找出哪些適用於他的零部件。當他打電話給製造商時，他們往往並不知道自己在政府的貿易商品中的分類。甚至連政府的專家也感到一頭霧水——ControlTek最近有一批零部件的交付延誤了12天，因為海關官員認為這些部件中含有需要繳稅的鋁。(其實並沒有。)
廣告
Over time, ControlTek has learned to navigate the system. Suppliers are sourcing components from Vietnam, Malaysia and other countries where possible, and ControlTek has begun factoring the tariffs into its product designs.
隨著時間的推移，ControlTek已經學會了駕馭這個體系。供應商正在盡可能地從越南、馬來西亞和其他國家採購零部件，而ControlTek已開始將關稅因素納入其產品設計。
“We’ll design China out,” Mr. LaFrazia said.
「我們將在設計上把中國排除出去，」拉法齊亞說。
The longer the trade war goes on, the more companies will have to make such decisions. Thomas Isaac, president and chief executive of Allied Technologies International, an Oregon manufacturer of parts for the telecommunications and aviation industries, said he had all but given up on selling to China. Sales there have slowed “almost to negligible” since tariffs took effect, he said. But Mr. Isaac has seen more business from customers looking to keep their suppliers closer to home.
貿易戰持續的時間越長，做出這樣決定的企業就會越多。奧勒岡州的電信和航空工業零部件製造商聯合技術國際(Allied Technologies International)的總裁兼首席執行官托馬斯·艾薩克(Thomas Isaac)表示，他幾乎已經放棄了中國這個銷售市場。他說，自關稅生效以來，那裡的銷售額已經「幾乎可以忽略不計」。但他發現，有一部分業務變多了，就是那些希望供應商離自己更近的客戶。
Mr. Isaac spent more than a decade at General Electric before acquiring Allied Technologies five year ago, and he said he remembered the enthusiasm for globe-spanning supply chains. Now that optimism is fading.
五年前買下聯合技術之前，艾薩克在通用電氣(General Electric)工作了十多年。他說，他還記得當時大家對全球供應鏈的熱情。現在這種樂觀情緒正在消退。
“The global supply chain sounded good for the last 20 years, but you’re already seeing companies pulling back and saying, ‘Let’s stay more local,’” he said.
他說：「過去20年，全球供應鏈聽起來很美，但你已經看到一些企業開始打起退堂鼓，說『讓我們更本地化一些吧』。」
While companies may be rethinking their supply chains, Mr. Isaac said he did not expect to see production shift back to the United States in meaningful volumes, the stated goal of Mr. Trump’s policies. So far, the data backs him up. Imports from China have fallen precipitously since the trade war began, both in electronics goods and over all. But that decline has been offset by increased imports from other countries. The nation’s trade deficit has been largely unchanged.
雖然企業可能會重新考慮它們的供應鏈，艾薩克表示，他預計並不會看到大量生產回到美國的現象，而那是川普政策的既定目標。到目前為止，數據支持他的觀點。自貿易戰爆發以來，從中國進口的電子產品及其他產品都大幅下降。但這種下降被從其他國家的進口增加所抵消。美國的貿易逆差狀況基本保持不變。
廣告
At ControlTek, tariffs have eaten into profit margins, although business as a whole hasn’t suffered. On one level, the trade war is no different from health care costs, labor issues or any of the other challenges that businesses encounter. But Stacey Smith, ControlTek’s vice president for human resources and marketing, said that for an American manufacturer, the tariffs were particularly hard to stomach.
在ControlTek，關稅侵蝕了利潤率，不過整體業務並沒有受到影響。從某種程度上說，貿易戰與醫療成本、勞工問題或企業面臨的其他任何挑戰並沒有什麼不同。但ControlTek負責人力資源和市場營銷的副總裁史泰西·史密斯(Stacey Smith)說，對一家美國製造商來說，關稅尤其難以承受。
“The tariffs are different because it’s your own government,” she said. “I understand the negotiating tactics. But it really is quite painful to be one of the pawns.”
「關稅是不同的，因為這是你自己的政府，」她說。「我明白這是談判策略。但成為其中的一枚棋子真是相當痛苦。」
