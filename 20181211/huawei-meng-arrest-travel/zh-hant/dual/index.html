孟晚舟案會讓中國科技精英疏遠美國嗎？

The Huawei Executive’s Arrest Is Igniting Fear. The U.S. Should Take Notice.

袁莉
2018年12月11日
Days after the Canadian authorities arrested one of China’s leading technology executives at the behest of Washington, Cisco warned some employees that China might return the favor.
自加拿大警方應華盛頓方面要求逮捕中國頂尖科技企業華為的一名高管數日後，矽谷網路巨頭思科警告部分員工，中國可能會進行報復。
In an email with the subject line “Travel Restriction to China,” the Silicon Valley networking giant cautioned employees against nonessential travel to China “due to recent events.” Cisco has since said that the email was sent in error and that there are no China travel restrictions on its employees.
在一封主題為「中國出差限制」的郵件中，思科警告員工，「由於近期的事件」，若不是非去不可，不要前往中國。思科此後稱，這封郵件發錯了，公司並沒有對員工出差前往中國進行限制。
Still, the fear is real.
但擔憂是真實存在的。
The arrest this month of Meng Wanzhou of Huawei, the Chinese telecom giant, appears to have put a certain kind of elite — tech-savvy, comfortable in both countries — square in the middle of the economic conflict between the United States and China. For many Chinese tech entrepreneurs, the United States suddenly doesn’t seem like the same place that welcomed them to study, work and raise money. That could be a big problem for both countries.
本月中國電信巨頭華為高管孟晚舟的被捕，似乎將某類精英——美中兩國生活優渥、精通科技的一個群體——夾在了美國和中國的經濟衝突之間。對很多中國科技企業家而言，美國似乎一下子不像那個曾歡迎他們前去留學、工作、掙錢的地方了。這對兩國都是個大問題。
廣告
Already, some tech types are reconsidering their travel plans, if not their connections, to the other country. Many see a bit of themselves in Ms. Meng. As Huawei’s chief financial officer and daughter of the company founder, she is a member of a tech elite that has long thought of itself as too valuable to be threatened. (American authorities say she was part of a plan to avoid American sanctions against Iran. Huawei says it is unaware of any wrongdoing on her part.)
一些科技業人士已經在重新考慮他們到其他國家的旅行計劃甚至是他們的交往圈子。很多人都在孟晚舟身上看到了一點自己的影子。作為華為的首席財務官和公司創始人任正非的女兒，孟晚舟是科技精英中的一員，而長期以來這一群體都認為，他們的價值意味著不會成為威脅的目標。（美國當局稱，她參與了躲避美國對伊朗制裁禁令的計劃。華為則表示，不知孟晚舟有任何不當行為。）
Over spicy hot pot last week after the announcement of Ms. Meng’s arrest, an American-educated Chinese tech entrepreneur told me that he wouldn’t travel to the United States unless necessary. Another Chinese tech figure, one who is backed by American money, discussed whether he should give up his beloved iPhone for a Huawei smartphone.
孟晚舟被捕消息傳出後，一位在美國讀過書的中國科技企業家上週一邊吃著熱辣的火鍋一邊說，除非萬不得已，他是不會去美國了。另一位得到美國資金支持的中國科技人士則說，他在想要不要放棄鍾愛的蘋果手機，轉而用華為智慧型手機。
That culture of fear and alienation has been building for a while, as the American government grows increasingly skeptical of China’s technology ambitions and its business ties in places like Silicon Valley.
隨著美國政府對中國的科技雄心及其與矽谷等地的商業聯繫越來越有戒心，這種恐懼與排外的文化氣氛已經積累一段時間了。
The founder of an artificial intelligence start-up said that when he visited the Bay Area in October, he was questioned extensively at immigration. One investor, a former Silicon Valley engineer who moved back to China a few years ago to set up a venture capital fund focused on A.I., left his laptop at home and deleted messages from the social media platform WeChat from his phone in case American security personnel decided to take a peek. They didn’t.
一家人工智慧初創企業創始人表示，他10月前往加州灣區，在入境時遭到了詳細盤問。一位曾在矽谷作工程師、幾年前回中國創辦專注人工智慧的風投基金的投資人，把筆記型電腦留在了家中，刪掉了手機上的微信聊天記錄，唯恐美國安全人員要看幾眼。他們並沒有。
(All the tech industry figures spoke on the condition of anonymity — to protect themselves not just from the Chinese government but from the American government as well.)
（所有這些業界人士都要求匿名以求自保，他們要防備的不僅是中國政府，還包括美國政府。）
The last time I saw so many liberal-minded Chinese elites so disappointed by the American government was after the bombing of the Chinese Embassy in Belgrade, Serbia, in 1999, which killed three Chinese nationals. The United States said it was a mistake, and President Bill Clinton apologized.
在我記憶裡，上一次有這麼多有自由主義傾向的中國精英對美國政府如此失望，還是1999年塞爾維亞貝爾格勒的中國大使館被轟炸之後，那起事件導致三名中國人死亡。美國當時稱那是一個錯誤，比爾·柯林頓(Bill Clinton)總統曾為此道歉。
廣告
“Out of national security consideration, we can start thinking about the possibility of ousting Apple from China,” Fang Xingdong, the founder of ChinaLabs, a technology think tank in Beijing, wrote on WeChat.
北京科技智庫互聯網實驗室創始人方興東在微信上寫道：「出於國家安全考慮，我們可以開始思考把蘋果逐出中國的可能性了。」
On Chinese social media, jingoism is running high. “A guilty conscience needs no accuser!” “Boycott Cisco!” “Let’s detain Tim Cook of Apple!”
在中國的社群媒體上瀰漫著濃烈的沙文主義氣息。「多行不義必自斃！」「抵制思科！」「逮捕蘋果的蒂姆·庫克！」
For China, this level of distrust could have some dire consequences. China and the United States remain tightly interconnected despite the clash over trade between Washington and Beijing. The Chinese economy is slowing down. Any pause in business relations between the two countries could make it worse.
對中國而言，如此高的不信任水平可能會導致可怕的後果。儘管在貿易上產生衝突，中國和美國仍然是密切關聯的。中國的經濟正在放緩。兩國的經貿關係發生任何中止都將是雪上加霜。
The United States, on the other hand, risks losing important potential allies in its tug of war with China’s authoritarian government. China’s tech elite are comfortable with the American system and open to many Western values. It would not be in Washington’s interest to push them closer to the Communist Party.
另一方面，在和中國專制政府的較量之中，美國則恐將損失重要的潛在盟友。中國的科技精英對美國的體制較為適應，對很多西方的價值觀也持較開放態度。將他們推到共產黨一邊將對華盛頓不利。
Many in the Chinese tech scene were already growing increasingly alienated from the party. President Xi Jinping’s order to enhance party building in the private sector has eroded the independence of private enterprises. The party has grown tougher in enforcing rules requiring companies to set up local party cells and to make executives answer to party secretaries.
中國科技界的許多人已經越來越疏遠共產黨。習近平主席加強民營企業黨建的命令，侵蝕了民營企業的獨立性。中國共產黨開始越來越強硬地實施企業建立黨支部、企管人員服從黨委書記指揮的規定。
“The party building in the past few years was a big present to the U.S. and Europe,” said Chen Zhiwu, an economist at Hong Kong University.
「近幾年強化黨建是送給美國和歐洲的大禮物，」香港大學經濟學家陳志武說。
廣告
China’s pro-market elites have been trying to use the pressure of a slowing economy and the trade war to push Mr. Xi to give power back to entrepreneurs, which would be in line with some of Washington’s goals.
中國親市場的精英一直試圖利用經濟放緩和貿易戰的壓力，推動習近平將權力交還給企業家，這將符合華盛頓的一些目標。
Now, they aren’t sure that the Trump administration views them any differently from the Chinese government.
現在，他們不確定川普政府對他們的看法是否與中國政府有任何不同。
Chinese executives have run into trouble before. Last week a jury in New York convicted Patrick Ho, a former Hong Kong official working for a troubled Chinese oil company, in a bribery case. American officials came close to outright killing the Chinese tech company ZTE this year for also violating sanctions against Iran, depriving it for a while of American-made technology that it needed to do business.
中國企業高管們以前也遇到過麻煩。上週，紐約的一個陪審團裁定何志平在一起賄賂案中有罪，他曾是一名香港官員，為一家陷入困境的中國石油公司工作。今年，中國科技公司中興通訊也違反了對伊朗的制裁，美國官員差點將其徹底封殺，在一段時間內禁止其獲取開展業務所需的美國技術。
This time is different. Huawei is among the most respected private enterprises in China. The world’s biggest telecom equipment maker and No. 2 smartphone maker, Huawei is frequently exalted in China’s business world for elbowing out its bigger Western rivals and developing cutting-edge technologies.
這次不一樣了。華為是中國最受尊敬的民營企業之一。作為全球最大的電信設備製造商和第二大智慧型手機製造商，華為因為經常可以擠走規模更大的西方競爭對手、開發尖端科技而在中國商界享有盛譽。
There are all kinds of tales about how hard Huawei engineers and salespeople work. Many entrepreneurs told me that they respected Ren Zhengfei, Huawei’s founder and Ms. Meng’s father, more than high-profile Chinese internet tycoons like Jack Ma of Alibaba and Pony Ma of Tencent because Huawei is the only Chinese corporation with a truly global footprint.
關於華為工程師和銷售人員如何努力工作，有各種各樣的說法。許多企業家告訴我，他們尊敬華為的創始人、孟晚舟的父親任正非，勝於阿里巴巴的馬雲和騰訊的馬化騰，因為華為是唯一一個真正在全球留下足跡的中國公司。
The United States accuses Ms. Meng of being directly involved in efforts to circumvent its sanctions against Iran. The authorities there may have compelling evidence. For people not familiar with China and China’s business world, it may be puzzling that some businesspeople would take Ms. Meng’s arrest so badly.
美國指責孟晚舟直接參与繞過美國對伊朗制裁的工作。美國當局可能有令人信服的證據。對於不熟悉中國和中國商界的人來說，一些商界人士對孟晚舟被捕反應如此強烈，可能令人費解。
廣告
Still, many people in China who have grown up under an authoritarian regime see only a blunt exercise of state power. Because many Chinese view Ms. Meng’s arrest as a tool to escalate the trade war, they see her as a victim of geopolitics rather than a potential lawbreaker.
然而，許多在威權體制下成長起來的中國人只覺得這是在赤裸裸地行使國家權力。許多中國人將孟晚舟被捕視為加劇貿易戰的工具，因此把她視為地緣政治的受害者，而不是潛在的違法者。
They also worry they could be seen as tainted simply because they are from China. Think of it as China’s “original sin” — it is hard to be a truly independent private business in China because the government controls most of the resources. The Chinese government uses bank loans, business licenses and regulations to make sure companies toe the party line. It is increasingly a major venture capitalist.
他們還擔心，只因為自己來自中國，就可能被視為是有問題的。可以把這當做中國的「原罪」——在中國，要想成為一個真正獨立的私營企業是很難的，因為政府控制著大部分資源。中國政府利用銀行貸款、營業執照和規章制度來確保企業遵守黨的路線。它正日益成為一個重要的風險投資者。
If the United States isn’t careful, it could alienate a group of people who may be instrumental if China is going to loosen up and give its people more freedom. Many admire the American political, judiciary and economic systems. If the United States puts more pressure on them, they may be forced to choose sides — and the Chinese government may seem like the only choice to make.
如果美國不小心，它可能會疏遠一個人群，如果中國要放鬆政策，給人民更多自由，這個群體可能會發揮重要作用。他們當中許多人敬仰美國的政治、司法和經濟體制。如果美國對他們施加更多壓力，他們可能會被迫選擇支持哪一方，而中國政府似乎是唯一的選擇。
“The whole world is taking sides,” Ren Yi, a senior investment banker in Beijing, wrote on the social media site Weibo. Huawei “suddenly finds itself on the front line of civilizational confrontation.”
「全球在站隊，」北京資深投資銀行家任毅（音）在社群媒體網站新浪微博上寫道。華為「突然發現自己置身於文明衝突的前線。」
