投資者種種擔憂令蘋果股價陰跌

Product Questions and Threats of Higher Tax Hit Apple Shares

NICK WINGFIELD
2012年11月10日
Autumn is traditionally the time of year when people start snapping up Apple products. It’s also when investors, in anticipation of another blockbuster holiday season from the company, do the same with Apple shares.
秋季向來是人們開始搶購蘋果產品的時節。而投資者們也向來會在秋季開始搶購蘋果股份，因為他們預計該公司會迎來又一個熱賣假日季。
But this year, even though Apple’s iPhones and iPads don’t seem to have lost any of their allure for holiday shoppers, its stock seems headed straight for the discount rack.
然而， 儘管今年蘋果的iPhone和iPad對假日消費者的吸引力好像並未減弱，蘋果公司的股價卻似乎朝着折扣區跌了下去。
On Wednesday, Apple’s shares slid 3.8 percent. They outpaced a broader decline in the stock market set off by investors’ uncertainty about how the outcome of the presidential election will affect taxes and consumer demand for the types of products Apple sells.
周三，股市出現大範圍下跌現象，蘋果股價更是下跌了3.8%。由於投資者不確定，總統選舉結果將對包含蘋果產品的商品類別的稅率及消費者需求產生何種影響，蘋果股票的表現弱於大市。
During the campaign, President Obama proposed increasing capital gains tax rates for people earning over $250,000 to 20 percent from the existing rate of 15 percent, and his re-election Tuesday night may have prompted some investors to unload shares in anticipation of a broader sell-off in stocks ahead of a tax increase, analysts said.
分析人士稱，奧巴馬總統在競選中提出，對收入超過25萬美元（約合150萬元人民幣）的人所徵收的資本收益稅，要由15%提高到20%。他於周二晚獲得連任，這促使一些投資者賣掉股份，因為他們預計股市會在加稅之前出現大範圍的拋售現象。
Owners of Apple shares would have good reason to fear higher taxes on capital gains. Apple shares have appreciated mightily since 2005 when they were about $35 apiece; they began this year at $411 and peaked at more than $700 in late September. The drop on Wednesday only added to what has been a grim few weeks for Apple shares, which have fallen over 20 percent from that peak, to $558 on Wednesday.
蘋果股票持有者害怕資本收益的稅率出現上升，對此，他們有着充分的理由。2005年，蘋果股票每股約為35美元，自此便出現巨幅上漲；今年年初，每股價格為411美元，9月底漲至峰值，達700多美元。蘋果股價在周三下跌之前，其實已連跌數周，由上述峰值跌至了周三的每股558美元，其下跌幅度逾20%。
The decline has followed a sequence of seemingly unrelated events, including a broader-than-normal overhaul of its product line that is expected to hurt profit margins in the near term and a rare shake-up in Apple’s senior ranks.
股價下跌之前，曾發生過一系列看似互不相關的事件，包括對產品線進行超過正常幅度的調整，而據預計，此舉在近期將影響公司利潤額。另外，蘋果高層還出現了罕見的人事大變動。
“It has just been wave after wave of bad news,” said Gene Munster, an analyst at Piper Jaffray.
派傑（PiperJaffray，美國著名投資銀行及資本管理公司——譯註） 的分析師基尼·蒙斯特(Gene Munster)稱：“一波又一波的壞消息接踵而至。”
The events also do little to diminish the questions reflecting longer-term concerns with which investors pepper analysts like Mr. Munster. How much bigger can Apple — with a $525 billion market value, the biggest of any corporation — get? Won’t Apple soon run out of people to sell iPhones, iPad and Macs to?
投資者有着種種長遠擔憂，他們用這些問題對蒙斯特等分析師進行狂轟濫炸，而這些事件絲毫不能減少他們的疑問。目前，蘋果公司的市值最大，達5250億美元，它最終能有多大呢？是不是該買iPhone，iPad和Mac的人都買完了，蘋果馬上就要沒有客戶了？
Those concerns have been one of the biggest reasons Apple’s stock has long traded at a discount, strange as that may sound, to its peers in the tech business. Apple’s forward price-to-earnings ratio — the value of the company’s stock divided by its expected earnings per share for the coming year — is just under 10.
這可能聽起來很奇怪，與科技產業的同儕相比，蘋果股票的市盈率長期以來處於較低位置，其中最大的原因之一就是上述投資者的種種擔憂。蘋果股票明年的動態市盈率不足10倍，這個比率是以公司的股價除以明年的每股預期收益得來的。
That figure is 14 for Google, 32 for Facebook and 131 for Amazon. Steve Dowling, a spokesman for Apple, said the company generally did not comment on its share price.
而谷歌的動態市盈率為14倍，Facebook為32倍，亞馬遜為131倍。蘋果公司的一位發言人史蒂夫·道林(Steve Dowling)稱，公司一般不對股價發表評論。
Apple executives have said they still see huge opportunities for the company and it is still growing at a remarkable clip for a company its size. During its last fiscal year, which ended Sept. 29, Apple’s revenue jumped 45 percent to $156.51 billion and its profits rose 70 percent to $41.73 billion.
蘋果的高管們表示，他們依然看到巨大的機遇，且對於一家像蘋果這樣規模的公司來說，增長速度仍然十分迅猛。在截止9月29日的上一財年中，蘋果的營業收入大幅增長45%至1565.1億美元，利潤上漲70%，達到417.3億美元。
While Apple’s most recent financial report was mostly in line with Wall Street forecasts, the company warned that its profit margin would most likely decline in the holiday quarter because of a sweeping refresh of the company’s iPad, iMac and MacBook laptop lines.
儘管蘋果最近一期的財務報告基本符合華爾街的預測，但公司警告稱，因為要對iPad、iMac和MacBook筆記本電腦的產品線進行全面更新，公司第四季度的利潤率極有可能下滑。
Apple products often cost more to manufacture during their first months on the market, but those costs come down as the process becomes more efficient and volumes increase. The company said its new iPad Mini would be among those products with low profit margins, raising concerns that Apple will make less money as it shifts to compete in lower-priced segments of the mobile market.
通常，在蘋果產品上市的頭幾個月，生產成本會較高，但隨着生產流程效率的提高以及產量的增加，這些成本會降下來。蘋果公司稱其最新的iPad Mini將是利潤率較低的產品，這讓外界擔心，隨着蘋果轉戰低端移動式設備市場，該公司的利潤也會隨之減少。
Apple’s worrisome financial report came just days before Apple announced the departure of Scott Forstall, the head of its mobile software development, in a move that it said was aimed at increasing collaboration between departments at the company. Apple divided the responsibilities of Mr. Forstall, who was a divisive figure at the company, among an array of other Apple executives.
就在發佈這份令人不安的財報的前幾天，蘋果公司宣布其手機軟件開發主管斯科特·福士特爾(Scott Forstall)離職。蘋果方面稱，此舉旨在增強公司不同部門之間的協作。斯科特·福士特爾是個頗具爭議性的人物。他的工作被分攤給了公司的其他幾位高管。
The surprise firing of Mr. Forstall was unusual for Apple, where turnover in its senior ranks is rare compared to other big technology companies. Apple also fired the head of its retail operations, John Browett.
解僱福士特爾的舉動令人震驚，也有些不同尋常，因為相比於其他科技巨頭，蘋果公司的高層極少發生變動。蘋果還解僱了負責其零售業務的主管約翰·布勞伊特(John Browett)。
Then late last week, the research firm IDC reported that 75 percent of smartphones shipped in the third quarter were Android phones, the main rival to Apple in mobile software. A year earlier, Google, the maker of Android software, had market share of 57.5 percent. The iPhone’s share rose at a much slower pace, jumping to 14.9 percent of shipments from 13.8 percent, IDC estimated.
上周晚些時候，調研企業國際數據公司(IDC)報告稱，第三季度出貨的智能手機中，Android手機佔了75%，Android是蘋果在手機軟件領域的主要對手。而一年前，Android軟件的生產商谷歌(Google)的市場佔有率只有57.5%。IDC估算，iPhone的市場佔有率的增速要慢得多，僅從13.8%上升到了14.9%。
But Apple is still making huge profits from the mobile market, far more than Google is. Even with the recent decline in its shares, Apple’s stock is still up 38 percent for the year.
但蘋果仍在手機市場獲利頗豐，遠遠領先於谷歌。即便其股價近期有所下滑，但蘋果股票今年以來還是上漲了38%。
David Rolfe, chief investment officer at Wedgewood Partners, a money management firm that counts Apple as its biggest stock holding, said he remained confident that Apple was one of the best investments around.
資金管理公司Wedgewood Partners的首席投資官戴維·羅爾夫(David Rolfe)稱自己仍然相信，蘋果是最好的投資對象之一。蘋果是該公司所持股票中最大的一支。
“The bears think Apple is in the eighth inning,” Mr. Rolfe said. “We think they’re still in the fourth or fifth inning.”
“賣空的人認為蘋果的好時光已是來日無多，”羅爾夫說，“但我們認為，它依然如日中天。”
