WTO終裁中國限制稀土出口案敗訴

China Export Curbs on Metals Again Found to Violate Trade Law

DIONNE SEARCEY, KEITH BRADSHER
2014年8月8日
China lost an appeal on Thursday of a World Trade Organization ruling that found it had broken international trade law by restricting exports of raw materials used in hybrid car batteries and other energy-efficiency products.
周四，中國在對世界貿易組織（World Trade Organization，簡稱WTO）的一份裁決提起的上訴中敗訴。此前，WTO裁決，中國限制出口用於混合動力汽車電池，以及其他一些節能產品的原料的做法，違反了國際貿易法。
The dispute, pursued by the United States in cooperation with the European Union and Japan, emerged after China significantly reduced its export quotas for rare earth elements in 2009, causing world prices to soar and disrupting the global market for the materials. The dispute also involved two metals used in steel alloys, tungsten and molybdenum.
2009年，中國大幅削減了稀土元素的出口配額，導致世界稀土價格飆升，擾亂了全球稀土市場。隨後，美國聯合歐洲和日本提起訴訟。這起糾紛還涉及鎢和鉬這兩種用於合金鋼的金屬。
The W.T.O. panel in Geneva that heard Beijing’s appeal issued a 177-page report that concluded China failed to demonstrate that its export quotas on the materials were justified.
在日內瓦，WTO的一個專家小組聽取了中國的上訴，發佈了一份長達177頁的報告。報告的結論是，中國未能證明其對稀土實施出口配額制是合理的。
Michael Froman, the United States trade representative, praised the ruling in a conference call with reporters, calling it “a clear signal that we will be proactive in standing up for the rights of American workers and American businesses.”
美國貿易代表邁克爾·費羅曼(Michael Froman)在同記者召開的電話會議中對該裁定表示讚揚，稱這「是一個明確的信號，我們將積極主動地維護美國工薪階層和美國企業的權利。」
“This report,” Mr. Froman added, “marks the end of a long back and forth on this issue and is the end of the line for this dispute.”
「這份報告，」費羅曼補充說，「標誌着圍繞這個問題的長期較量的結束，也是這起糾紛的結束。」
China produces most of the global supply of rare earth metals, which are used in crucial components for smartphones, light bulbs and other high-tech products. The Chinese Commerce Ministry did not respond immediately to Thursday’s ruling but had expressed “regret” at the W.T.O.’s initial ruling earlier this year.
全球稀土金屬供應中，大部分來自中國。智能手機、電燈泡和其他一些高科技產品的重要零部件，均會用到稀土金屬。中國商務部並未立即就周四的裁決做出回應，但今年早些時候，該機構曾對WTO的初步裁決表示「遺憾」。
Thursday’s ruling has broader implications for the often difficult balance between trade issues and the environment. Environmentalists have long criticized the W.T.O. for not making greater allowances for trade restrictions based on fighting pollution. China had sought in its appeal to use an old, seldom-cited international trade rule allowing countries to limit trade so as to avoid depletion of natural resources and protect the environment.
對於往往很難實現的貿易問題與環境之間的平衡，周四的裁決有着更廣泛的影響。長期以來，環保人士一直批評WTO未能更多地體諒基於防治污染而制定的貿易限制措施。上訴期間，中國試圖利用一項由來已久卻甚少被援引的國際貿易規則。該規則允許國家限制貿易，以避免耗盡自然資源，保護環境。
The United States countered that China had long allowed unrestricted production for the domestic market, helping Western manufacturers who shifted factories to China, even as China limited exports.
美國反駁稱，長期以來，中國允許不加限制地生產面向國內市場的產品，為把工廠遷至中國的西方廠商提供了幫助，同時卻又限制出口。
The rare earth industry has encountered a separate problem lately: plunging prices as many manufacturers found replacements for rare earths or at least ways to limit their use, after a vertiginous, yearlong spike in prices four years ago.
近來，稀土行業遇到了另一個問題：價格暴跌，因為在稀土價格四年前開始出現令人眩暈的持續暴漲後，許多廠商找到了稀土的替代品，或者至少是限制稀土使用的途徑。
Molycorp, the main American producer, announced heavy losses this week, while the share price of Lynas of Australia, the other main alternative to Chinese production, has plummeted over the last several years as its production costs from a mine in Western Australia and a processing facility in eastern Peninsular Malaysia proved high compared with Chinese producers.
本周，美國主要的稀土生產商莫利礦業(Molycorp)宣布嚴重虧損，而過去幾年，中國另一個主要競爭對手、澳大利亞萊納斯(Lynas)的股價暴跌，原因在於和中國的生產商相比，該公司在西澳大利亞的一處稀土礦，以及在馬來西亞半島東部的一座加工廠的生產成本太高。
On Thursday, Leo Gerard, the president of the United Steelworkers union, said the W.T.O. ruling would stop China’s manipulation and undercutting of the market, which had led to a temporary halt in activity in at least one rare earths mine in California with 300 workers.
周四，美國聯合鋼鐵工人工會(United Steelworkers union)主席利奧·傑拉德(Leo Gerard)表示，WTO的裁決將阻止中國操控和削弱稀土市場，中國的行為已導致至少一座稀土礦暫時停產。那座稀土礦位於加州，有300名工人。
“Once again we’ve caught China cheating,” said Mr. Gerard, who joined the conference call with Mr. Froman, the trade representative.
「我們再次抓到中國作弊，」傑拉德說。他與美國貿易代表費羅曼一同出席了那次電話會議。
The ruling could benefit the Obama administration by demonstrating the United States can win W.T.O. trade enforcement actions, as many politicians periodically favor unilateral action. China has a history of honoring at least the letter of W.T.O. rulings, by changing the regulations that Beijing has on the books.
裁決可能有益於奧巴馬政府，因為它表明，美國能爭取到WTO的貿易強制措施，而美國國內許多政界人士偶爾會贊成單邊行動。在過去，中國都會通過修改已出台的規定，至少遵守WTO裁決的字面意思。
But many critics of China suggest that the nation has not always followed the spirit of these rulings, and rare earths are a frequently cited example. As the W.T.O. case worked its way through the system, the Chinese government arranged the rapid consolidation of the Chinese industry, previously largely private, under a handful of state-owned enterprises.
但許多批評中國的人士表示，中國並不總是遵從這些裁決的精神，而稀土就是一個頻頻被提及的例子。在被提交給WTO的訴訟案進入相關程序期間，中國政府將該國稀土行業迅速整合為幾家國有企業。此前，中國稀土行業中大多為私有企業。
These enterprises now dominate world production of rare earths, and any decision by them to restrict exports could prove difficult to challenge at the W.T.O., though some trade lawyers contend that collective action by Chinese state-owned enterprises could be challenged as effectively as a government trade policy.
如今，這些企業主導着全世界的稀土生產。事實可能會證明，很難在WTO對這些企業限制出口的任何決定提出異議。但一些貿易律師稱，和面對政府的貿易政策時一樣，也可以有效地對中國國有企業的聯合行動提出異議。
Chinese internal security agencies have also conducted a crackdown over the last three years against illegal producers and smugglers of rare earths, making it harder for Japanese and Western producers to bypass the powerful state-owned enterprises.
過去三年，中國內部安全機構也展開了對非法生產稀土的廠商，以及走私販的打擊，導致日本和西方的生產廠商更難繞過強大的中國國有企業。
