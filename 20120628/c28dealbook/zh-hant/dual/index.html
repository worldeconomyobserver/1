新聞集團分拆有利可圖？

Valuing a News Corp. Breakup

MICHAEL J. DE LA MERCED
2012年6月28日
On Wall Street, it's called the "Murdoch discount."
在華爾街，這種現象被稱作“默多克折扣”。
That's the view that the shares of News Corporation trade lower than the intrinsic value of its many media assets because of the control exerted by Rupert Murdoch. Because of his hold, the argument goes, the company takes on expensive ventures like the $5 billion purchase of Dow Jones in 2007, an acquisition whose value was heavily written down soon after closing.
魯伯特·默多克 （Rupert Murdoch) 的新聞集團 (News Corporation)，其股票價值比集團下屬的很多媒體資產的內含價值還低，有觀點認為，這都要歸咎於默多克對集團施加的控制。這種觀點還認為，正是因為默多克的控制， 新聞集團收購了價格高昂的企業。比如2007年它以50億美金收購了道瓊斯公司（Dow Jones），收購剛剛達成，公司市值就大幅縮水。
With top executives now weighing a breakup, the media conglomerate has signaled it will focus on unlocking the value of its film and television properties, rather than on pursuing Mr. Murdoch's newspaper ambitions. Analysts and investors hailed the news, and shares of News Corporation surged 8.3 percent on Tuesday, to $21.76, its biggest one-day gain since August 2011.
目前，新聞集團的最高管理層正在考量分拆公司，而新聞集團也發出信號，表示將要集中精力釋放集團下屬電影電視資產的價值，而不是追隨默多克在報紙行業的雄心。分析家和投資者對此表示歡迎。新聞集團的股價周二上漲了8.3%，達到21.76美元，獲得自2011年8月以來的單日最大增幅。
The company, which has a market value of nearly $53 billion, is a conglomerate of entertainment businesses like the Fox movie and television operations and publishing businesses like The Wall Street Journal, The Sun and The Times of London and HarperCollins. Executives still have many details to iron out, including exactly how to divide the media conglomerate's properties. But the general outlines of a split are apparent. The movie studio, television channels and Internet assets would form one company. Newspapers and publishing would go into another.
這家市值將近530億美元的公司從事集團化的娛樂產業經營，如經營福克斯電影和電視業務。它也涉足出版行業，擁有像《華爾街日報》，《太陽報》，倫敦的《泰晤士報》和哈珀·柯林斯出版社在內的出版發行產業。管理層還將有細節問題要解決，包括究竟如何拆分這個傳媒集團的產業。但拆分的總體大綱很明確。電影製片廠，電視頻道和互聯網產業部分將組成一個公司。報紙和出版將劃歸到另一個公司。
The entertainment assets generated $6.2 billion in revenue and $1.3 billion in operating income in the quarter that ended March 31. By contrast, the publishing arm reaped about $2 billion in revenue and just $130 million in operating income.
截止到3月31日，第一季度娛樂產業帶給集團62億美元的收入和13億美的元營業收益。相比之下，出版行業只獲得20億美元收入和1.3億的營業收益。
So while entertainment companies tend to command relatively high trading multiples compared with their more traditional publishing counterparts, News Corporation doesn't reap that full benefit. After Tuesday's run-up in its stock price, the company trades at nearly 15 times earnings, while the Walt Disney Company trades at nearly 17 times earnings.
因此，雖然與傳統的發行行業相比，娛樂公司往往能獲得較高的市盈率，但新聞集團並未獲得那麼高的收益。經歷了本周二股票價格的急劇上漲之後，公司的市盈率是15倍，而沃爾特·迪斯尼公司 (Walt Disney Company) 的市盈率達到17倍。
Publishing companies trade at much lower levels. The Gannett Company trades at about 7.3 times earnings, for instance.
出版公司的市盈率水平更低一些。例如，甘尼特公司（Gannett Company）的市盈率大約在7.3倍。
Michael Nathanson, an analyst with Nomura, estimates that News Corporation's entertainment business will earn $3.1 billion for its 2012 fiscal year. Assuming the new, split company traded at Disney's multiple - analysts consider it a reasonable level, given the quality of the News Corporation operations - the entertainment business would have a market value of about $52.5 billion.
野村證劵 (Nomura) 的分析家邁克爾·內桑森（Michael Nathanson）預測新聞集團的娛樂產業在2012財年將贏得31億美元收入。分析家認為，考慮到新聞集團的經營質量，假設分拆後的公司能達到和迪斯尼公司一樣的市盈率水平是合理的。以此推測，公司娛樂產業的市值將達到525億美元。
In other words, its entertainment business would be worth nearly as much as all of News Corporation today. That raises the possibility that the market is ascribing no value to the publishing properties. That may be overly pessimistic, however.
換言之，新聞集團的娛樂產業板塊市值將和今日集團總體市值相仿。這提高了市場認為出版業板塊毫無價值的可能性。但這可能是過度悲觀的想法。
Nomura estimates the publishing business's 2012 profit at about $362 million. Using Gannett's trading multiple, the publishing arm could be worth $2.6 billion as a stand-alone entity. Using a higher multiple of nine times, which would reflect some analysts' belief that News Corporation owns more promising newspapers than Gannett, the business would be worth nearly $3.3 billion, or the same as its rival.
野村證券預測，出版行業板塊2012年的利潤大約在3.62億美元。以甘尼特公司的市盈率為例，出版行業作為單獨的經濟實體價值可望達到26億美元。如果像一些分析人士所認定的，新聞集團旗下的紙媒行業比甘尼特更具前景而以九倍市盈率計算，則集團的媒體出版行業將價值33億美元，和其競爭對手的價值大致相當。
Of course, there's a risk that the publishing arm could falter without the support of its corporate sibling's deeper pockets. Companies dependent on newspaper operations have struggled to replace dwindling print advertising dollars with digital ones, and Dow Jones doesn't possess the reach or resources that Thomson Reuters or Bloomberg L.P. do.
當然， 新聞出版公司沒有了集團下更賺錢的那一部分的支持，也可能會立足不穩，這種風險是存在的。依託報紙經營的公司已在竭盡所能用數字媒體廣告收入填補日益減少的印刷廣告收入的不足。道瓊斯並沒有湯森路透（Thomson Reuters）和彭博通訊社 (Bloomberg L.P.) 那樣的發行渠道和資源。
Some analysts, while praising the split discussions, cautioned that the Murdoch discount may be exaggerated. "To be fair, over the last couple years we believe the Murdoch discount has decreased as the company has instituted a buyback program and as president and C.O.O. Chase Carey has appeared to gain greater influence within the company," Anthony DiClemente, an analyst with Barclays, wrote in a note to clients.
一些分析人士在讚賞拆分討論的同時，也告誡說，“默多克的折扣”可能被誇大了。巴克萊銀行 (Barclays) 的分析師安東尼·迪克萊蒙特（Anthony DiClemente) 在給客戶的報告中寫道：“公平地說，我們認為在過去幾年裡，隨着公司建立回購計劃體系，以及總裁兼首席運營官蔡斯·凱里（Chase Carey）在公司內部獲得更大影響力，‘默多克折扣’效應已經在減退。”
