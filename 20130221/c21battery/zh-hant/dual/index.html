日劇反映日本電子製造商深陷窘境

TV Show Mirrors a Japanese Battery Maker’s Bind

HIROKO TABUCHI
2013年2月21日
TOKYO — For many viewers in Japan, the plot of the television series “Made in Japan” was all too real: a struggling manufacturer, Takumi Electronics, turns to advanced lithium-ion batteries to reverse its ailing fortunes.
東京——對於許多日本觀眾來說，電視劇《日本製造》(Made in Japan)的情節看上去太過真實：卓美機電(Takumi Electronics)是一家苦苦掙扎的製造商，通過轉向先進的鋰離子電池生產而扭轉了極其不佳的境況。
With the real-life manufacturer of Boeing’s Japan-made batteries under intense scrutiny, the three-part series, which was carried by the public broadcaster NHK, came at an angst-ridden time, and underscored the hopes that Japan had pinned on technologies like lithium-ion batteries.
由於在現實生活中，為波音公司(Boeing)生產電池的日本製造商正處在各方的密切關注之下，這部由公共電視廣播公司NHK播出的三集電視劇恰好出現在一個焦躁不安的時期，它也強調了日本寄托在鋰離子電池等各種新技術上的期望。
As portrayed in the series, Takumi Electronics might as well be Japan Inc. itself. Takumi’s lead in televisions and mobile phones has been eclipsed by more nimble upstarts in South Korea and China, echoing the fate of Japanese technology giants like Sony and Panasonic. The only hope the company has now lies in its advanced battery technology.
在電視劇中，卓美機電簡直就可以用來代表日本這個國家。該公司在電視和流動電話上的領先地位，在韓國和中國的那些更靈活的後起之秀面前已經蕩然無存，這反映出了索尼(Sony)和松下(Panasonic)等日本科技巨頭的命運。這家公司現在唯一的希望，就是其先進的電池技術。
“These lithium-ion batteries took us years to develop, and our technological advantage won’t waver so easily,” the Takumi Electronics president says defiantly to reporters after yet another disastrous earnings presentation. “We are betting on big growth in electric cars, and we are about to clinch some big deals.”
在又一次糟糕的業績發佈會之後，卓美機電的總裁對記者不服氣地說，「這些鋰離子電池耗費了我們多年時間來進行開發，因而我們的技術優勢不會被輕易撼動。我們把賭注壓在電動汽車上，並即將敲定一些大買賣。」
GS Yuasa, the real-life maker of Boeing’s lithium-ion batteries, made a similar bet, one that has not yet paid off.
現實生活中波音鋰離子電池的製造商傑士湯淺株式會社(GS Yuasa)也下了類似的賭注，但還未見回報。
Fire and smoke in batteries aboard Boeing’s next-generation Dreamliner have led to the grounding of all 50 of the planes while authorities around the world try to figure out the cause.
波音的新一代「夢想客機」(Dreamliner)上的電池冒出了火花和煙霧，令這個型號的所有50架飛機全部停飛，而世界各國有關當局都在試圖找出其中原因。
The recent troubles are hardly what GS Yuasa could have envisioned when it started making the batteries. In 2009, it started supplying the batteries for Mitsubishi Motors’ i-Miev electric vehicles, billed as the first mass-produced, fully electric car. Everything seemed to go well at the beginning. GS Yuasa executives said in interviews three years ago that the company was struggling to keep up with inquiries pouring in from automakers.
在傑士湯淺開始製造這些電池時，該公司應該絕對想不到會遇上現在的這些麻煩。2009年，該公司開始為三菱汽車(Mitsubishi Motors)的i-Miev電動汽車提供電池，這被稱為第一款批量生產的全電動汽車。開始時，一切看上去都很順利。傑士湯淺的高管在三年前的採訪中稱，來自汽車製造商的詢問請求不斷湧入，這讓該公司疲於應對。
But demand for the electric vehicles has not taken off. Last month, Mitsubishi Motors sold just 137 i-Mievs, and GS Yuasa’s factories have been running at less than capacity.
但對電動車的需求尚未起飛。到上個月為止，三菱汽車僅售出了137輛i-Miev，而傑士淺湯的工廠一直在低於產能的情況下運營。
That has marred plans at the company, which had hoped its lithium-ion technology would soon replace its older lead-acid batteries as its core business. GS Yuasa has still not turned a profit on its lithium-ion batteries, losing 3.26 billion yen, about $35 million, on the business, according to its annual report.
這影響到了該公司的計劃，即期待其鋰離子電池技術能夠很快取代其較老的鉛酸電池而成為其核心業務。傑士淺湯仍沒有在其鋰離子電池業務上盈利。根據其年度報告，該公司在這項業務上損失了32.6億日元，約合3500萬美元。
Enter Boeing and its next-generation 787 jet, which relies more than ever on electric systems and the batteries that power them. GS Yuasa won a contract in 2005 to supply Boeing with lithium-ion batteries through the French aviation electronics company Thales, and GS Yuasa has promoted aviation as a growth area that could make up for the disappointing electric vehicle market.
波音及其新一代787飛機，比以往任何時刻都更加依賴電力系統和為其提供電力的電池。2005年，傑士淺湯通過法國航電公司塔萊斯(Thales)贏得了一份為波音提供鋰離子電池的合同。傑士淺湯宣稱，航空業是一個增長領域，足以彌補電動汽車市場帶來的失望。
GS Yuasa’s president, Makoto Yoda, had high ambitions, telling Kyodo News in late 2011 that he wanted to double sales of lithium-ion batteries to clients other than automakers within five years.
傑士淺湯株式會社社長依田誠(Makoto Yoda)對此充滿信心，他在2011年末告訴日本共同社(Kyodo News)，他希望在5年內讓出售給汽車製造商之外客戶的鋰離子電池的銷量翻倍。
Advanced batteries are also crucial to wider Japanese industry at a time when the country remains stunned by how swiftly its once-dominant makers of semiconductors and flat-panel televisions have been overtaken by South Korean rivals. The culprit is commoditization, which made Japan’s prized technology indistinguishable from everyone else’s.
在這個時期，先進的電池對整個日本產業界的有着非凡意義，因為該國仍舊對自己在半導體和平板電視產業上的主導地位被韓國對手迅速奪走而感到震驚不已。這其中的罪魁禍首其實就是商品化，這讓日本最有價值的技術與任何其他廠商的產品別無二致。
There is already fear that Japan’s prized lithium-ion battery technology could go the same way.
現在已經有人擔心，日本最有價值的鋰離子電池技術可能也會重蹈覆轍。
Though Japan was long the world’s dominant supplier of lithium-ion technology, led by Sony’s first commercial lithium-ion batteries in 1991, rivals have largely usurped Japan’s lead. The catch-up has been especially swift in smaller lithium-ion batteries, which have become increasingly common.
長期以來，日本都在供應鋰離子科技產品上佔據主導地位，其最初的領軍產品便是1991年索尼製造的第一批商用鋰離子電池，然而，現在，它的領導地位卻已基本被競爭對手取代。而在已經變得越來越普遍的小型鋰離子電池領域，這一趕超發生地尤為迅速。
In 2011, Japan’s global share had tumbled to about 35 percent, from more than 90 percent a decade earlier, according to Techno Systems Research, a market research company based in Tokyo. Over the same period, South Korean manufacturers overtook Japan with an almost 40 percent global share.
據位於東京的市場研究公司Techno Systems Research稱，2011年，日本的全球市場份額從10年前的90%多跌至35%。而同一時期內，韓國生產商的全球市場份額幾乎達到40%，超越了日本。
Still, Japan is determined to maintain its lead on larger lithium-ion batteries for cars and planes, which manufacturers here stress are more difficult to design and manufacture, and require the intense attention to quality and safety on which Japanese engineers pride themselves.
不過，日本還是決心要在用於汽車和飛機的大型鋰離子電池生產上保持領先地位，該國的生產商強調稱，這些電池的設計和生產更為困難，並且對質量和安全的要求極高，而這兩者正是日本工程師引以為傲的資本。
“Helping to build the 787 was supposed to demonstrate how Japan still leads the world in advanced, reliable technology,” said Ichiro Takano, chief executive at TechnoAssociates, a consulting company based in Tokyo with expertise in batteries and renewable energy. “But that will backfire if we don’t get to the bottom of this quickly.” 
擁有電池和可再生能源諮詢業務的東京諮詢公司TechnoAssociates首席執行官高野一郎(Ichiro Takano)說，「幫助建造787飛機本來是為了展示，在先進、可靠的科技領域內，日本仍在世界上領先。但是如果我們不能迅速弄清楚問題發生的原因，就會出現適得其反的結果。」
GS Yuasa has long enjoyed a close relationship with the government. Genzo Shimadzu, the founder of the company that eventually became GS Yuasa, developed Japan’s first lead batteries in 1895 on behalf of Kyoto Imperial University.
長期以來，日本傑士湯淺公司都和政府關係密切。1895年，島津源藏(Genzo Shimadzu)代表京都帝國大學(Kyoto Imperial University)研發出日本第一批鉛蓄電池，他所創建的公司是傑士湯淺公司的前身。
Those batteries, called GS batteries after their inventor, were used on battleships in the Russo-Japanese War, helping to power transmitters that allowed the ships to stay in contact. The Japanese handily defeated the Russians at sea in 1905.
這些以其發明者命名的GS電池，曾被用在日俄戰爭中的戰艦上，為其發報機供電，有了這些發報機，船艦才能保持聯繫。1905年，日本在海上輕而易舉地打敗了俄國人。
GS Yuasa has received almost 17 billion yen (around $183 million) in Japanese government subsidies over the last three years. Through a program begun in 2009, Japan’s Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry has made 140 billion yen, or $1.5 billion, in direct subsidies to green technology companies, most of that to lithium-ion makers deemed to be capable of competing overseas.
過去三年內，傑士湯淺得到了將近170億日元（約合11億元人民幣）的政府補貼。通過一個始於2009年的項目，日本經濟產業省向綠色科技公司發放了1400億日元的直接補貼，其中大部分補貼流向了被認為具備國際競爭力的鋰離子電池製造商。
For now, GS Yuasa has stressed that it does not expect any immediate impact on its sales or reputation from the 787 grounding. According to company executives, its sales of the lithium-ion batteries to Boeing have still only come to several hundred million yen.
目前，傑士湯淺強調稱，787飛機被停飛並不會給公司的銷售額或聲譽造成直接影響。據公司高管稱，該公司銷往波音公司的電池僅價值數億日元。
“At this point, we have not sensed any damage to our reputation,” Toshiyuki Nakagawa, a GS Yuasa director, told reporters in Kyoto.
「現在，我們還沒有感覺到任何的名譽損失，」傑士湯淺的董事中川敏幸(Toshiyuki Nakagawa)在京都對記者說道。
A reputational blow is what Takumi Electronics, the fictional manufacturer in the drama series, also hopes to avoid as it teeters on losing its lithium-ion battery deal to a Chinese rival, a big embarrassment for any Japanese company.
在電視劇中，虛構的製造商卓美機電也想要避免名譽受損，該公司正面臨著將鋰離子電池交易輸給中國競爭對手的危險，而這對於任何一家日本公司來說都是奇恥大辱。
In the end, the Chinese manufacturer loses its deal after a top engineer — one it recruited from Takumi — reveals that the batteries, as they stand, pose a fire risk. And in a last-minute deal, the two companies decide to team up to develop safer batteries, combining Japanese technological expertise with Chinese manufacturing prowess.
最後，供職於該中國製造商的一名高級工程師揭露（該工程師是這家中國公司從卓美挖過去的），在未經改進的情況下，這些鋰離子電池有起火的危險，後來，中國公司輸掉了交易。最後時刻，兩家公司達成協議，決定將日本的技術和中國的製造能力相結合，共同開發更安全的電池。
“Once revered around the world, Made in Japan products are now being forced to fight grueling battles,” the narrator says in the final scene, set to booming orchestra music. “But we cannot falter. We must fight on.”
最後一集中，伴隨着低沉有力的管弦樂，一個旁白者說道，「日本製造的產品曾經享譽世界，現如今，它們卻要被迫進行艱苦的戰鬥。但是我們不能退縮，我們必須繼續戰鬥。」
