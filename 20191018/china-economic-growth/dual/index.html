中国经济进一步放缓，第三季度GDP增长6%

China’s Economic Growth Slows as Challenges Mount

KEITH BRADSHER
2019年10月18日
Sign up for NYT Chinese-language Morning Briefing.
[欢迎点击此处、或发送邮件至cn.letters@nytimes.com订阅《 世界经济观察报》中文简报。]
SHANGHAI — China’s economic slowdown worsened in the July-to-September period, according to data released on Friday, as the trade war with the United States and a host of other problems leave Beijing struggling to meet its goals.
上海——周五公布的数据显示，7月至9月，中国的经济放缓情况进一步恶化，与美国的贸易战以及一系列其他问题令北京难以实现其目标。
The figures show China continues to grow at its slowest pace in nearly three decades of modern record-keeping. While China is still expanding faster than any other major economy, Friday’s data suggest that the pace will come in at the low end of Beijing’s official target, which could add to worries about broader prospects for global growth.
数据显示，中国的增长速度继续处于近30年来的最低水平。尽管中国的经济增速仍高于其他主要经济体，但周五的数据表明，这一增速将处于北京官方目标的低端，这可能加剧人们对全球经济增长前景的担忧。
China's efforts to tame its addiction to lending and the deepening impact of the trade war have been major drags on the economy. Other problems are worsening, as the country’s vast automotive sector shrinks, its real estate sector levels off and as its pigs die in vast numbers from a swine fever epidemic.
中国抑制借贷成瘾的努力，以及贸易战日益加深的影响，一直是拖累经济的主要因素。随着中国庞大的汽车行业萎缩、房地产行业疲软，以及猪流感疫情导致大量生猪死亡，其他问题也正在恶化。
广告
China’s economic output grew 6 percent in the third quarter, according to official statistics, the low end of Beijing’s full-year target of 6 percent to 6.5 percent. In the first half of the year, its output grew 6.2 percent.
根据官方统计数据，中国第三季度经济产出增长6%，处于北京全年目标6%至6.5%的低点。今年上半年，其产出增长为6.2%。
China’s growth was set to slow down from the torrid pace of past years. Its economy is now twice the size it was about a decade ago. Its labor force is shrinking, and the country is already full of roads, rails and factories, limiting potential new investment.
中国的增长速度将从过去几年的迅猛增长变为放缓。它现在的经济规模是十年前的两倍。它的劳动力正在减少，而且这个国家已经到处是公路、铁路和工厂，限制了潜在的新投资。
The question is whether there are any sharp dips along the path to ever-slower growth.
问题在于，在通往更慢增长的道路上，是否会出现大幅下滑。
“What policymakers want is to make the process as long and as gradual as possible,” said Larry Hu, the head of China economics at the Macquarie Group, a big Australian financial services company. “The biggest challenge is to find new growth drivers from consumption and technology, as old ones such as property and globalization are fading out.”
“政策制定者希望它是一个尽可能长期、渐进的过程，”澳大利亚大型金融服务公司麦格理集团(Macquarie Group)首席中国经济学家胡伟俊(Larry Hu)说。“最大的挑战是，在房地产和全球化等老的增长动力逐渐消失之际，从消费和技术领域寻找新的增长动力。”
The trade war is adding to that uncertainty, though China’s problems go beyond that.
贸易战加剧了这种不确定性，尽管中国的问题远不止于此。
The conflict heated up in the third quarter of this year, when President Trump broadened his tariffs at the start of September to hit many consumer goods from China. Its exports to the United States plunged 22 percent in September compared with September 2018, when exporters were racing to ship their goods ahead of a previous set of tariffs. 
冲突在今年第三季度升温，特朗普总统在9月初扩大了关税，打击许多来自中国的消费品。与2018年9月相比，中国对美国的出口同比下降22%，当时出口商竞相赶在之前增加的关税前发货。
广告
Still, American tariffs account for only part of China’s overall economic slowdown. Exports to the United States are about 4 percent of the Chinese economy. Imported goods like semiconductors and oil amount to roughly half the value of those exports, said Nicholas Lardy, a longtime China specialist at the Peterson Institute for International Economics, so the net impact of exports to the United States is even less.
然而，美国关税只是中国经济整体放缓的部分原因。对美国的出口约占中国经济的4%。半导体和石油等进口商品约占这些出口商品价值的一半，彼得森国际经济研究所(Peterson Institute for International Economics)长期研究中国的专家尼古拉斯·R·拉迪(Nicholas R. Lardy)表示，因此对美国出口的净影响更小。
China also has ramped up exports to the developing world. As a result, its overall exports in September were down only 3.8 percent.
中国还增加了对发展中国家的出口。因此，9月份中国的整体出口仅下降了3.8%。
In fact, much of the slowdown has been domestic. China has been reluctant to plow more money into its economy in the form of lending, which has already saddled its financial system with burdensome debt.
事实上，中国经济放缓在很大程度上源于国内。中国一直不愿以放贷的形式向经济注入更多资金，这已经让其金融体系背上了沉重的债务负担。
Other areas have weakened, too, like the auto industry, as consumer confidence has weakened. Car sales are 5 percent of China’s economic output, according to official statistics, meaning they play a bigger role in China’s growth than exports to the United States. These cars contain nearly all Chinese-made parts; even foreign companies like General Motors assemble practically all their cars locally, with almost entirely Chinese-made parts.
随着消费者信心的减弱，其他领域也出现了疲软，比如汽车行业。根据官方统计数据，汽车销售占中国经济产出的5%，这意味着它在中国经济增长中的作用大于对美国的出口。这些汽车几乎全部使用中国制造的零部件；就连通用汽车(General Motors)这样的外国公司几乎所有的汽车都在当地组装，其零部件也几乎都是中国制造。
But these days, households in China just aren’t buying cars the way they used to.
但如今，中国的家庭不再像过去那样买车了。
The Chinese Passenger Car Association announced on Saturday that retail car sales fell 6.6 percent in September from a year earlier, a sharp drop for an industry once accustomed to double-digit annual percentage growth.
全国乘用车市场信息联席会上周六宣布，9月份汽车零售额同比下降6.6%，对于一个曾经习惯于两位数年增长率的行业来说，这是一个大幅下降的数字。
广告
The car sales slump started in the summer of 2018 and became severe by winter. While most auto industry experts expected sales to start looking better by this summer, that has not happened.
汽车销售从2018年夏季开始下滑，到冬季变得更加严重。虽然大多数汽车行业专家预计今年夏天销售情况会开始好转，但这并没有发生。
Cui Dongshu, the secretary general of the car association, said September at least was not as bad as some recent months. “The sales are recovering but very slowly, due to weak consumption,” he said. “The purchasing power of families is still not enough.”
该联席会秘书长崔东树表示，9月份的情况至少不像最近几个月那么糟糕。“由于消费疲软，销售恢复非常缓慢，”他表示。“家庭购买力仍然不足。”
Chinese automakers had planned to export a lot of cars to the United States starting this winter. But Mr. Trump’s tariffs have made that much harder and have prompted one carmaker, Guangzhou Auto, to suspend indefinitely its plans to enter the American market.
中国汽车制造商计划从今年冬天开始向美国出口大量汽车。但特朗普的关税让这一点变得更加困难，并促使广汽集团无限期暂停了进军美国市场的计划。
Property market weakness has also pinched growth, hurting consumer sentiment. A decades-long run-up in home prices gave hundreds of millions of Chinese the feeling that they were prosperous enough to afford their own home. But prices have mostly stabilized, and even fallen in some smaller cities, and many families are struggling with large mortgage payments.
房地产市场疲软也拖累了经济增长，打击了消费者信心。几十年的房价上涨让数亿中国人觉得他们足够富裕，买得起自己的房子。但房价基本上已经稳定下来，在一些较小的城市甚至还出现了下跌，许多家庭都在艰难地偿还巨额抵押贷款。
China's biggest challenge may be slower investment. The country’s roads, bridges, high-speed rail lines and ports are superb. Its factories are among the largest in the world. But having already spent so much, government agencies and companies alike are having more and more trouble finding opportunities to invest more.
中国面临的最大挑战可能是投资放缓。这个国家的道路、桥梁、高速铁路和港口都是一流的。它的工厂是世界上最大的。但由于已经投入了这么多资金，政府机构和企业越来越难以找到更多的投资机会。
Global firms are showing less interest in the Chinese market, and in some cases even starting to pull back.
跨国公司对中国市场的兴趣正在减弱，有的甚至开始撤出。
广告
“We have seen a significant divestment by multinationals in this part of the world in the past 18 months,” said Mark Webster, a partner in the Shanghai office of BDA Partners, a New York investment bank that specializes in Asia deals.
“过去18个月，我们看到跨国公司在亚洲大举撤资，”纽约投资银行BDA Partners上海办事处合伙人韦马柯(Mark Webster)表示。
The trade war is just part of the problem. Multinationals complain of much tighter regulatory actions in China, particularly antitrust cases. They also encounter extensive overcapacity in many industrial sectors, forcing companies to cut prices repeatedly to stay in business and destroying profit margins.
贸易战只是问题的一部分。跨国公司抱怨中国的监管行动要严格得多，尤其是反垄断案件。它们还在许多工业部门遭遇了严重的产能过剩，迫使企业不断降价以维持经营，并破坏了利润率。
The volume of mergers and acquisitions by foreign companies investing in China tumbled 89 percent in the first half of this year from the same period last year, to just $6.3 billion, Mr. Webster said.
韦马柯说，今年上半年，在华投资的外国公司的并购交易额较去年同期下降了89%，仅为63亿美元。
