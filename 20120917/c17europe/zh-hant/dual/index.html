歐債最困難的時刻已經過去

Dutch and Germans Give European Union Reasons to Cheer

STEVEN ERLANGER
2012年9月17日
PARIS — There was a general sigh of relief in the European Union this week. The cause was not better performance in the troubled and highly indebted southern countries of the euro zone, but crucial decisions made in the rich northern nations with perfect credit ratings, where skepticism about the common currency is running high.
巴黎——本周，歐盟國家普遍都鬆了口氣。其原因並非是深陷債務危機的歐元區南部國家的經濟好轉，而是因為擁有完美信用評級的北部富裕國家做出了至關重要的決定，儘管這些國家對共同貨幣持高度懷疑的態度。
On Wednesday, the German Constitutional Court found a way to declare that the permanent bailout fund, the European Stability Mechanism, is legal, clearing the way to use it in time to recapitalize troubled banks as well as governments. And the Dutch voted for mainstream parties in a parliamentary election, choosing not to be enticed by parties wanting to leave the euro.
上周三，德國憲法法院(German Constitutional Court)宣布永久性救援基金歐洲穩定機制（European Stability Mechanism，簡稱ESM）合法，為及時使用這筆基金為陷入困境的銀行和政府再融資掃清了道路。在荷蘭的議會選舉中，選民投票支持主流政黨，沒有受那些主張放棄歐元的政黨的蠱惑。
Combined with the European Central Bank’s decision to restart its bond-buying program in return for more budget discipline, immediately lowering interest rates on Italian and Spanish bonds, European leaders could begin to feel that perhaps the worst is over in the euro crisis, at least for now.
此外，歐洲中央銀行(European Central Bank)決定重啟其債券購買計劃，作為各國更嚴格預算紀律的交換，此舉立刻降低了意大利和西班牙主權債務的收益率。加上德國和荷蘭的結果，歐洲領導人總算可以鬆口氣，至少從現在來看，歐元危機最困難的時刻已經過去了。
“With the Dutch shying away from anti-European parties the same day the German Constitutional Court rules in favor of the E.S.M., Sept. 12 seems to have been a good day for the euro,” Dimitry Fleming of ING Groep NV said in an analysis via e-mail.
荷蘭國際集團(ING Groep NV)的迪米特里·弗萊明(Dimitry Fleming)在電子郵件中分析道，“在德國憲法法院的宣判贊成ESM的同一天，荷蘭選民迴避了反歐盟的黨派 。看來9月12日是歐元的好日子。”
Not all is well, of course. Greece remains a mess, and will probably need even more money. A decision keeps being postponed about when, and whether, to grant Athens another big portion of loan money it needs to stay afloat.
當然，並非一切都很好。希臘仍是一片混亂，很可能還需要更多的錢。是否，或何時為希臘政府再次提供大筆貸款來維持運轉，相關決定仍遲遲沒有做出。
The Germans remain deeply anxious about the Central Bank’s effectively printing money and building up larger stocks of shaky bonds. And there is reluctance among some nations, especially Germany, over the mechanism and scale of a Europe-wide banking regulator to try to prevent another crisis, particularly in Spain.
面對歐洲央行基本上是大量印鈔票的行為，以及越積越高、搖搖欲墜的債務，德國人仍深表憂慮。對於為了防止另一場危機爆發（特別是在西班牙）而建立的這個機制，以及歐洲銀行監管機構的規模， 許多國家也表現出消極態度，特別是德國。
Then there is significant division over just how far to take the idea of European fiscal and political integration, let alone union. In a speech on Wednesday, the European Commission’s president, José Manuel Barroso, pushed for “pooled sovereignty” and a European Union “federation of nation states that can tackle our common problems, through the sharing of sovereignty in a way that each country and each citizen are better equipped to control their own destiny.”
此外，對於究竟應當把歐盟的財政和政治一體化推進到何種程度，也有明顯的分歧，更不用說結成聯盟了。在本周三的一次演講中，歐盟委員會(European Commission)主席若澤·曼努埃爾·巴羅佐(José Manuel Barroso)呼籲建立“集合主權”，和歐盟“歐洲國家聯邦，應對我們共同的問題，通過主權分擔，讓每個國家，每個公民都能更好地掌握自己的命運。”
Any major changes would require another treaty, which would mean some difficult parliamentary votes or referendums. Germany has spoken of a treaty change to be ratified by referendum, but France, even under its Socialist president, François Hollande, is dubious about sacrificing too much sovereignty — let alone engaging in a vote.
任何重大的變化將需要另一個條約才能實現，這將需要進行一系列困難的議會投票或全民公投。德國政府曾說過，條約的修改應通過公投批准，但是法國政府，雖然是在社會黨(Socialist)總統弗朗索瓦·奧朗德(François Hollande)領導下，都對犧牲太多主權表示懷疑，更別談對此進行投票了。
In 2005, the French Socialists split over a new treaty for a European constitution; Mr. Hollande supported it, but Laurent Fabius, the current foreign minister, did not. And the proposal lost badly in a referendum, dooming the constitution. So there is considerable wariness in Paris about too explicit an integration.
2005年，法國社會黨內部因歐盟憲法的一個新條約發生分歧。奧朗德表示支持，但現任外交部長洛朗·法比尤斯(Laurent Fabius)反對。這個提案在公投中遭遇慘敗，註定了歐洲憲法的破產。所以法國政府對於太過直接的歐洲一體化持相當謹慎的態度。
That will be true in the Netherlands, too. Prime Minister Mark Rutte, one of the few European leaders to win re-election during this crisis, is hardly a fan of European integration. He has opposed any further loss of Dutch sovereignty, and he mocked the European flag during the campaign in a successful effort to take votes away from the far-right party led by Geert Wilders, who had been an ally in the previous government. More important, Mr. Rutte has vowed to refuse Greece any more loans.
荷蘭的情況也一樣。 作為這場危機中贏得連任的少數歐洲領導人之一，馬克·呂特首相(Mark Rutte)並不看好歐洲一體化。他反對荷蘭進一步放棄主權，曾在競選中取笑歐盟的旗幟，成功地從自己上屆政府的盟友、海爾特·維爾德斯(Geert Wilders)領導的極右翼政黨手中奪取了選票。更重要的是，呂特還曾發誓拒絕給希臘更多貸款。
Mr. Rutte firmly backs Chancellor Angela Merkel of Germany in her push for more fiscal discipline and budgetary oversight in the euro zone. But as the leader of a much smaller country, he appears to be far more reluctant than she is to contemplate more shared sovereignty or to give more power to Mr. Barroso and the bureaucrats in Brussels.
呂特堅決支持德國總理安格拉·默克爾(Angela Merkel)的努力，要求在歐元區推進更嚴格的財政紀律和預算監管。但作為一個小得多的國家的首腦，談到更大的共享主權或賦予巴羅佐和歐盟官僚更多權力的話，他似乎遠比默克爾消極。
The Dutch expressed considerable worry during the campaign about their money and how it would be used, and there is a widespread sense of fatigue and annoyance with the problems in Greece and other southern countries, which the richer north has repeatedly been asked to help.
競選期間，荷蘭人對他們的錢怎麼用表達了相當多的憂慮。由於希臘等南部國家反覆尋求北部富裕國家的幫助，在它們的問題上，荷蘭也存在一種廣泛的疲勞和厭倦情緒。
In vote totals that were nearly final on Thursday, Mr. Rutte’s center-right Liberal Party had won 41 out of 150 seats, 10 more than in 2010. The center-left Labor Party, led by Diederik Samsom, a nuclear physicist and former Greenpeace activist, came in second with 39 seats, an increase of nine. Mr. Wilders’s Freedom Party and the Socialist Party, both playing on skepticism about the euro, tied for third with 15 seats each.
投票統計周四幾近完成。結果顯示，呂特領導的中右翼自由黨(Liberal Party)贏得了150個議席中的41席，比2010年增加了10個席位。迪德里克·薩姆森(Diederik Samsom)領導的中左翼工黨(Labor Party)位居第二，贏得39席，增加了九席。薩姆森是一名核物理學家，曾是綠色和平組織(Green peace)的活動人士。社會黨(Socialist Party)和維爾德斯領導的新自由黨(Freedom Party)均對歐元持懷疑態度，兩者各獲15席，並列第三。
That was a loss of nine seats for Mr. Wilders, who was punished by voters for what many considered his irresponsible behavior in April, when he brought down the previous government because he refused to accept spending cuts to meet deficit targets. Mr. Wilders called for the Dutch to leave the European Union, not just to abandon the euro, and that appeared to be too extreme for many of his supporters, who turned to Mr. Rutte’s Liberals.
維爾德斯的政黨喪失了九個席位，因為4月被很多人認為是不負責的行為受到了選民的懲罰 。當時，由於拒絕接受為達到赤字目標所要求的開支削減，維爾德斯使上屆政府倒台。他呼籲荷蘭不僅放棄歐元，還要退出歐盟，對許多支持者而言，這看起來都太極端了，於是他們轉而支持呂特的自由黨。
“In Brussels they are having a party,” Mr. Wilders told his disappointed supporters. “That’s a pity.” He vowed to continue the fight against immigration, Islam and Brussels.
“布魯塞爾的官員正在開歡慶會，” 維爾德斯告訴他那些失望的支持者，“很遺憾。”他發誓將繼續反對移民、伊斯蘭教和歐盟。
The Christian Democrats, once the governing party, fell again, down to 8 seats from 13, and the pro-European centrist party, D66, won two more seats, for a total of 12.
曾執政又下台的基督教民主黨(Christian Democrats)從13席降至8席，親歐盟的中間黨派六六民主黨(D66)增加了兩個席位，共贏得12席。
The German foreign minister, Guido Westerwelle, said the election outcome “strengthens Europe and weakens populists and ‘re-nationalists.’ ” He added that “after the important decisions of the last few days, we must now use the momentum to develop Europe further and make it fit for the future in times of globalization.”
德國外交部長基多·韋斯特韋勒(Guido Westerwelle)稱，荷蘭選舉結果“鞏固了歐洲，削弱了民粹主義者和‘恢復民族主義論者’”。他還補充說，“經過過去幾天的一些重要決策，我們現在必須一鼓作氣深化歐洲的發展，使之適應全球化時代的未來。”
Mr. Rutte and Mr. Samsom are expected to form the heart of a new coalition, but negotiations could be lengthy. “There are two clear winners who are substantially different from each other,” Jozias van Aartsen, the mayor of The Hague, told Dutch radio.
呂特和薩姆森可能將組成新聯合政府的核心，但談判可能需要很長時間。海牙市長約齊亞斯·范阿爾特松(Jozias van Aartsen)告訴荷蘭電台，“選舉有兩位明顯的贏家，但他們本質上大不相同。”
Because the two parties together do not control a majority in the upper house, analysts expect that a final coalition could include both D66 on the left and the Christian Democrats on the right.
由於兩黨加起來不足以控制議會上院多數席位，分析人士預計，最終的聯合政府可能會同時包含左翼的六六民主黨和右翼的基督教民主黨。
The parties are fundamentally in favor of the Netherlands, which depends on trade, remaining in the euro zone and the European Union, which absorbs 75 percent of its exports. And with the Netherlands being one of the few AAA-rated countries remaining in the euro zone, the European Union needs the Dutch, too.
這些政黨均基本上支持荷蘭留在歐元區和歐盟。荷蘭經濟倚仗貿易，而歐盟消化了它75%的出口額。作為歐元區僅存的幾個信用評級為AAA的國家之一，歐盟也需要荷蘭。
