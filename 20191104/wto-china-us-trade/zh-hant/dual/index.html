WTO允許中國對美國商品實施貿易制裁

W.T.O. Allows China to Impose Trade Sanctions on U.S. Goods

ANA SWANSON
2019年11月4日
Sign up for NYT Chinese-language Morning Briefing.
[歡迎點擊此處、或發送郵件至cn.letters@nytimes.com訂閱《紐約時報》中文簡報。]
The World Trade Organization gave China the go-ahead on Friday to impose sanctions on up to $3.6 billion of American goods in a fight over unfairly cheap Chinese goods, a decision that is likely to further inflame the Trump administration’s antipathy toward the global trade body.
在一場圍繞中國商品傾銷展開的爭鬥中，世界貿易組織(WTO)上週五允許中國對價值達36億美元的美國商品實施制裁，這一決定可能將進一步激起川普政府對這家全球貿易組織的反感。
The ruling is the final decision in a case China brought against the United States nearly six years ago that stemmed from levies placed on more than 40 Chinese goods. The duties were imposed during the Obama administration, but the practice they were intended to curb remains one of the Trump administration’s biggest economic concerns about China.
這是對近六年前中國對美國發起的一項訴訟的最終裁決，該訴訟源於40多種中國商品被強征關稅。這些關稅是在歐巴馬執政期間實施的，但它們意欲遏制的行為仍是川普政府在經濟方面對中國最大的擔憂之一。
At issue are subsidies that the United States has accused China of providing to its companies so that they can sell goods more cheaply overseas. To stop China from flooding the American market with dozens of low-cost Chinese products — including solar panels, furniture, shrimp, steel pipe, tires and washing machines — the United States imposed “anti-dumping duties” on those goods.
爭論的焦點是，美國指責中國向本國企業提供補貼，以便它們能夠在海外以更低廉的價格銷售商品。為了阻止數十種低成本的中國產品充斥美國市場——包括太陽能電池板、傢具、蝦、鋼管、輪胎和洗衣機——美國對這些產品徵收了「反傾銷稅」。
廣告
A 2017 decision by the World Trade Organization found that the United States had not followed the global trade body’s rules in the way it imposed the duties. On Friday, the W.T.O. gave China the green light to try to recoup some of the losses it sustained by imposing enough tariffs on American goods to block $3.6 billion worth of American exports to China.
世界貿易組織2017年的一項決定顯示，美國的徵稅方式不符合該全球性貿易組織的規則。上週五，世界貿易組織給中國開了綠燈，允許它通過對美國商品徵收足額關稅來限制美國對中國價值36億美元商品的出口，從而彌補中國遭受的部分損失。
China has not yet indicated which American products it will hit with tariffs and it is unclear whether the United States would retaliate. Under the W.T.O. rules, China can keep the tariffs in place until the United States changes its behavior or the two countries agree to some type of resolution.
中國尚未表明將對哪些美國產品徵收關稅，也不清楚美國是否會採取報復措施。根據世界貿易組織的規定，在美國改變其行為或兩國同意某種解決方案之前，中國可以保持關稅不變。
A Trump administration official said that the United States was “disappointed” by the decision, saying that it overstated the financial effect on China and used an approach that “has no foundation in economic analysis.” He added that the United States did not plan to change the practices that the W.T.O. had objected to, including how it determines the level of duties it should impose on Chinese goods. That means any tariffs China imposes could remain in place indefinitely.
川普政府的一位官員說，美國對這一決定感到失望，稱它誇大了對中國的經濟影響，並稱其所使用的方法「沒有經濟分析依據」。他還說，美國不打算改變世界貿易組織所反對的做法，包括如何決定對中國商品的關稅水平。這意味著中國所徵收的任何關稅都可能無限期地保持不變。
The case is separate from President Trump’s trade war with China, which has taken place outside of World Trade Organization rules and resulted in the United States placing tariffs on $360 billion worth of Chinese goods.
該案並不屬於川普總統與中國之間的貿易戰的一部分，後者是在世界貿易組織規則之外進行的，已經導致美國對價值3600億美元的中國商品加徵關稅。
But it could further complicate relations between the world’s two largest economies as they try to reach an agreement to end their yearlong trade fight. China has already retaliated against the United States with tariffs on roughly $100 billion worth of American products and Mr. Trump has dangled the possibility of additional tariffs if Beijing does not agree to the administration’s demands. Both sides have already suffered economic pain from the tit-for-tat tariffs.
但在雙方試圖達成協議、結束一年之久的貿易戰時，這一結果可能會使世界最大的兩個經濟體之間的關係進一步複雜化。中國已經採取報復措施，對價值約1000億美元的美國商品徵收關稅。如果北京不同意美國政府的要求，川普先生也不排除進一步增加關稅的可能。雙方都因針鋒相對的關稅而遭受了經濟上的痛苦。
Beijing could use the threat of these tariffs as a source of leverage in trade negotiations. But China, which imports far fewer American goods than it exports to the United States, has been reluctant to increase the tariffs it has already imposed, because it could raise costs for essential products like medical devices and food.
中國政府可以利用這些關稅帶來的威脅，作為貿易談判的籌碼。但由於其進口的美國商品遠少於出口到美國的商品，中國一直不願提高已徵收的關稅，因為這可能會增加醫療器械和食品等必需品的成本。
廣告
“We do not believe the arbitrator’s decision will have any impact on continuing trade discussions between the United States and China,” the Trump administration official said.
「我們認為，仲裁決定不會對美中之間仍在進行的貿易談判產生任何影響，」前述川普政府官員說。
The ruling could have bigger consequences for the future of the W.T.O. The administration has already criticized the global trade body, including its inability to write rules that deter China from providing generous subsidies to its industries.
這一裁決對世界貿易組織的未來的影響可能要更大一些。美國政府已經對這一全球貿易組織提出了批評，包括它無法制定規則以阻止中國對其產業提供豐厚補貼。
In part through the use of state-owned enterprises, China has built giant industries to manufacture steel, aluminum, solar panels and other products that have flooded global markets with low-cost products and put American competitors out of business. The country threatens to do the same in emerging industries like new-energy vehicles and semiconductors, American officials say. But the W.T.O. defines subsidies narrowly, for example excluding cheap inputs from Chinese state-owned firms.
中國建起鋼鐵、鋁、太陽能電池板及其它產品的龐大工業，一定程度上就是通過利用國有企業。這些產業用低成本產品充斥了全球市場，並迫使美國競爭對手出局。美國官員說，該國威脅要在新能源汽車和半導體等新興產業採取同樣措施。但世界貿易組織對補貼的定義很狹隘，例如排除了來自中國國有企業的廉價投入品。
Clete Willems, a partner at Akin Gump who left the White House earlier this year, said the ruling exemplified some of the administration’s most significant concerns about the W.T.O., — its failure to come to terms with the role of the state in China’s economy.
今年早些時候從白宮離職的艾金·崗波律師事務所(Akin Gump)的合伙人克萊特·威廉斯(Clite Willems)說，這一裁決突顯出美國政府對世界貿易組織最主要的一些憂慮——它未能正視政府在中國經濟中扮演的角色。
“As a result, China is permitted to get away with behavior that wouldn’t be tolerated in any normal market economy,” Mr. Willems said. “The ruling defangs our ability to counteract subsidies provided by Chinese state-owned enterprises.”
「因此，中國的那些在任何正常市場經濟中都不被允許的行為得以逃脫懲罰」，威廉斯說。「這項裁決削弱了我們對抗中國國有企業補貼的能力。」
The Trump administration has embraced other W.T.O. rulings in the favor of the United States, including a decision earlier this week on a challenge to Indian export subsidies and a ruling in early October that allowed the United States to retaliate in response to European Union subsidies to plane maker Airbus.
川普政府此前對其他一些有利於美國的世界貿易組織裁決表示過歡迎，包括本週早些時候就一項對印度出口補貼的異議做出的裁決，以及10月初一項允許美國對歐盟補貼飛機製造商空中巴士(Airbus)的行為進行報復的裁決。
廣告
But it has simultaneously taken aim at other functions of the W.T.O., and in particular pushed part of the system that settles trade disputes to the breaking point.
但政府同時也在攻擊WTO的其他職能，尤其將這個系統中負責調解貿易爭端的那部分推向了臨界點。
The Trump administration has been blocking new appointments to the part of the W.T.O. that considers appeals on trade disputes, in what the United States says is an effort to persuade the body to make certain changes. As of Dec. 10, the so-called appellate body will no longer have enough members to hear cases, leaving some trade disputes without a final verdict.
川普政府一直在阻止世界貿易組織負責貿易爭端訴訟的機構做出新的任命，稱這是為了說服該機構做出某些改變。自12月10日起，所謂的上訴機構將不再有足夠的成員來審理案件，這將導致一些貿易爭端無法得到最終裁決。
Trump administration officials say privately that the shuttering of the appellate body will be no great loss. But others do not share that opinion. The concern, says Jennifer Hillman, a former W.T.O. appellate body member and an expert at the Council on Foreign Relations, is that countries may begin taking matters into their own hands.
川普政府官員私下表示，上訴機構的關閉不會造成太大損失。但其他人並不同意這種觀點。前世界貿易組織上訴機構成員、外交關係委員會(Council on Foreign Relations)專家珍妮佛·希爾曼(Jennifer Hillman)表示，人們擔心的是，各國可能會開始自行其是。
If countries turn away from the W.T.O. system and impose tariffs on each other outside of global trade rules, that increases the risk of damaging trade wars and protectionism that could weigh on the economy.
如果各國放棄世界貿易組織體系，並不顧全球貿易規則對彼此加徵關稅，這就增加了出現破壞性貿易戰和保護主義的風險，從而給經濟帶來壓力。
“Immediately you have the crisis of what to do about all the pending appeals that will no longer ever be completed, and whether the parties to those appeals are going to start taking unilateral action,” Ms. Hillman said.
「你會馬上面臨這樣的危機，如何處理所有這些再也無法了結的未決上訴，以及上訴各方是否將開始採取單邊行動，」希爾曼說。
