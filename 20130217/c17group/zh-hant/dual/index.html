G20會議向貨幣戰爭說不

Group of 20 Vows to Let Markets Set Currency Values

DAVID M. HERSZENHORN
2013年2月17日
MOSCOW — In a concerted move to quiet fears of a so-called currency war, finance officials from the world’s largest industrial and emerging economies expressed their commitment on Saturday to “market-determined exchange rate systems and exchange rate flexibility.”
莫斯科——為了緩和對所謂的貨幣戰爭的擔憂，來自世界最大工業國及新興經濟體的金融官員一致在周六做出承諾，表示將會致力於維持“市場決定匯率的體系和匯率彈性”。
In a statement issued at the conclusion of a conference here of the Group of 20, the finance ministers promised: “We will refrain from competitive devaluation. We will not target our exchange rates for competitive purposes.”
20國集團（Group of 20，簡稱G20）在莫斯科會議結束時發表了一份聲明，財長們承諾，“我們將會避免競爭性貶值，不會為競爭目的設定匯率。”
Speaking in introductory remarks on Friday, Ben S. Bernanke, the Federal Reserve chairman, had strongly indicated that the United States did not intend to censure Japan for weakening its currency over the last several months, something that has aided Japanese exporters and angered its competitors.
美聯儲(Federal Reserve)主席本·伯南克(Ben S. Bernanke)在周五致開幕詞時著重指出，美國不打算譴責日本在過去幾個月里弱化日元的政策，這些舉措促進了日本出口企業的發展，但卻激怒了其競爭對手。
At issue are stimulus programs backed by Prime Minister Shinzo Abe, who is also maintaining pressure on the Bank of Japan to keep interest rates near zero and flood the economy with money to support Japanese manufacturers. As a result, the yen has lost about 15 percent of its value against the dollar over the last three months, meaning products made in Japan, like some Sony electronics or models of Toyota cars, are relatively cheaper.
引發爭議的是日本首相安倍晉三(Shinzo Abe)支持的刺激計劃，安倍晉三還對日本銀行(Bank of Japan)施壓，敦促日本央行將利率維持在接近於零的水平、並注入大量資金支持日本製造商，促進經濟發展。因此，日元兌美元匯率在過去三個月里下跌了大約15%，這意味着日本產品的價格會相對便宜一些，比如一些索尼(Sony)電子產品和某些型號的豐田(Toyota)汽車。
Japan’s maneuver touched off fears that other countries and the European Union might follow suit in a so-called currency war, which has been the main topic of the Group of 20 meeting here, which runs through Saturday.
日本的策略使得人們擔憂，其他國家及歐盟可能會採取同樣措施，由此掀起所謂的貨幣戰爭。貨幣戰爭是周六G20莫斯科峰會的主要話題。
But Saturday's statement largely echoed one last week by seven top industrial nations pledging to let market exchange rates determine the value of their currencies. Currency devaluation can be used to gain competitive advantage because it makes a country’s exports cheaper.
但周六的聲明在很大程度上響應了七大工業強國在上周所做的聲明，當時他們承諾讓市場匯率來決定幣值。各國可以通過貨幣貶值來獲得競爭優勢，因為貨幣貶值會降低出口產品的價格。
“We all agreed on the fact that we refuse to enter any currency war,” the French finance minister, Pierre Moscovici, told reporters at the conference, which was held in a meeting center just a short walk from the Kremlin and Red Square.
法國財政部長皮埃爾·莫斯科維奇(Pierre Moscovici)在會議上告訴記者，“我們都同意這樣一個事實，我們拒絕加入任何貨幣戰爭。”此次會議在距離克里姆林宮和紅場只有幾步之遙的一個會議中心舉行。
In the statement, the Group of 20 pointedly avoided any criticism of Japan. The Japanese policies, which have reduced the cost of Japanese products around the world, were the primary cause of fears of a currency war.
G20在公報中刻意避免了任何針對日本的批評。日本的政策降低了世界各地的日本產品價格，該政策是引發對貨幣戰爭的擔憂的主要原因。
In essence, the Group of 20 expressed a view that loose monetary policy, including steps that weaken currency values, are acceptable when used to stimulate domestic growth but should not be used to benefit in global trade.
總之，G20表達了一種觀點，即包括貶值貨幣等舉措在內的寬鬆貨幣政策可以用來刺激國內經濟增長，但卻不能用來在國際貿易中獲利。
Critics of that view say that it amounts to a distinction without a difference because loose monetary policies stimulate growth and bolster exports at the same time.
對這個觀點持批評態度的人說，這等於是一種沒有區別的劃分方式，因為寬鬆的貨幣政策在刺激增長的同時，也能夠促進出口。
The United States has also used a loose monetary approach to aid in the economic recovery, in the form of “quantitative easing” by which the Federal Reserve buys tens of billions of dollars in bonds each month.
美國在恢復經濟的過程中也藉助了寬鬆貨幣政策，根據所謂的“量化寬鬆”(quantitative easing)政策，美聯儲每個月都要購買數以百億美元計的債券。
Faster-growing, developing countries like Brazil and China have expressed concerns about the loose monetary policies of more established economies like Japan and the United States. The money created by policies like the Fed’s quantitative easing can prove destabilizing as it enters faster-growing economies.
巴西和中國等增長迅速的發展中國家，對日本和美國等發達經濟體的寬鬆貨幣政策表示擔憂。美聯儲量化寬鬆等政策所創造的貨幣一旦進入增長迅速的經濟體，可能會帶來不穩定因素。
The Group of 20 acknowledged this concern in its statement, saying: “Monetary policy should be directed toward domestic price stability and continuing to support economic recovery according to the respective mandates. We commit to monitor and minimize the negative spillovers on other countries of policies implemented for domestic purposes.”
G20在聲明中對這種擔憂表示認可，並且說：“貨幣政策應該根據各國情況，以維護國內物價穩定及繼續支持經濟恢復為目的。我們承諾，我們將對為實現國內目的而實施的政策對他國所造成的負面影響進行監測，並盡量減少這種影響。
In its statement, the group also vowed to “take necessary collective actions” to discourage corporate tax evasion, particularly by preventing companies from shifting profits to avoid tax obligations. For instance, a number of big American companies, including Apple and Starbucks, have come under scrutiny recently for seeking out the friendliest tax jurisdictions.
G20還在聲明中承諾，“將採取必要的一致行動”來制止企業避稅行為，尤其要阻止企業通過轉移利潤來逃脫納稅義務的行為。例如，包括蘋果(Apple)和星巴克(Starbucks)在內的一些大型美國公司近期都因為尋求最有利的稅務管轄權而受到了審查。
As the three-day conference drew to a close, participants did not reach any new agreement on debt-cutting targets. Efforts to reach such a pact will continue at the annual Group of 20 summit meeting to be attended by President Obama and other world leaders in St. Petersburg in September.
為期三天的會議接近尾聲，與會者在債務削減目標方面沒有達成任何新的協議。將於今年9月在聖彼得堡舉行的G20年度峰會將繼續為達成此類協議而努力，屆時奧巴馬總統和其他一些世界領袖都將參會。
But while the debt agreement was elusive, the Group of 20 leaders reiterated efforts to work together, promising to “resist all forms of protections and keep our markets open.”
儘管債務協議很難達成，G20的領導人仍再次表示將共同努力，承諾要“排斥一切形式的保護主義，保持市場開放。”
