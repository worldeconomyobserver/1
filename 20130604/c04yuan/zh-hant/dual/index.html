中國5月製造業數據微漲

Manufacturing Growth in China Appears to Be Stabilizing

白天娜
2013年6月4日
Two surveys that take the temperature of China’s manufacturing sector showed an economy that appears to have stabilized for now, but that is struggling to recover some of the momentum it lost at the start of the year.
兩項衡量中國製造業繁榮程度的調查顯示，中國經濟似乎已暫時穩定下來，但卻還在為重拾今年初喪失的部分發展勢頭而拼搏。
The two purchasing managers’ indexes — one released by the British bank HSBC on Monday, the other published by the Chinese statistics bureau on Saturday — painted somewhat divergent pictures for May, though both underlined the fact that the factory sector was still struggling with lackluster demand.
\r兩項採購人經理指數都突顯了製造業依然因需求平平而苦苦掙扎的事實，儘管如此，兩者對五月經濟形勢的描繪卻存在幾分差異。這兩項指數中的一項是英國滙豐銀行(HSBC)於本周一發佈的，另一項是中國統計局於上周六發佈的。
The official survey, which is focused more on large and midsize state-owned companies of the kind that benefit from state-led investment, provided a positive surprise by edging up slightly, to 50.8 in May, from 50.6 in April. Economists polled by Reuters had expected the index to slip toward 50, with figures above indicating expansion and those below showing contraction.
\r官方的調查更關注那種從國家主導的投資中獲益的國有大中型企業，因此給出了一個正面驚喜，所提供的指數從4月份的50.6%輕微上升至5月份的50.8。此前，由路透社(Reuters)組織的經濟學家投票曾預計，採購經理人指數會滑向50%。高於50%的指數顯示製造業趨於擴張，而低於50%的指數則顯示製造業趨於萎縮。
On the other hand, the final version of the HSBC index for May, compiled by the research firm Markit and released Monday, was 49.2 points. The reading was a downward revision from the preliminary number of 49.6, which was published on May 23, and showed conditions at their weakest in eight months. The HSBC gauge is more focused on smaller companies, which have benefited less than their larger counterparts from the recent credit expansion, HSBC said in a research note.
\r另一方面，由調查公司Markit彙編、於本周一發佈的最終版滙豐銀行5月採購經理人指數卻是49.2%。相較於5月23日初步發佈的49.6%的指數，最終版有所下調，顯示了8個月以來最低迷的行業狀態。滙豐銀行的指數更關注較小的企業，滙豐銀行在一篇研究報告中說，與較大的同行企業相比，較小的企業從最近的信貸擴張中獲益較少。
“Overall conditions for China’s manufacturing sector were at best stabilizing at a relatively low level in May,” HSBC economists said, adding that a batch of official economic data for May, to be released this coming weekend, could provide more clarity on China’s growth outlook.
\r「中國製造業5月的總體情況，最多也只能說是穩定在了一個相對較低的水平。」滙豐銀行的經濟學家說。他們補充道，即將於本周末公布的一批官方5月經濟數據可能會讓中國經濟增長的前景更加明晰。
After years of double-digit expansion, China’s pace of growth has cooled in recent years — a slowdown that has in part been engineered by the authorities in Beijing as they try to ease a potentially worrisome flood of lending, forestall asset price bubbles and direct the economy toward higher-quality expansion.
\r經歷了多年的兩位數擴張之後，中國經濟的增長步伐已於近些年放緩，這种放緩部分是中國政府刻意為之的結果，因為他們試圖緩解可能造成不安的大量貸款、預先阻止資產價格泡沫、將經濟導向質量更高的擴張。
Beijing is expecting 7.5 percent growth this year, a rapid clip compared with the rates of expansion of other leading economies but a significantly slower pace than the 9.3 percent growth in 2011 and the 10.4 percent in 2010.
\r中國政府預計，今年的經濟增長率將會是7.5%，和其他主要經濟體的擴張速度相比可謂迅猛，不過，相比於2011年9.3%的增長率和2010年10.4%的增長率，這一增速已是大大地放緩了。
“While the data may ease some concerns of rapid deteriorating of the Chinese economy, the impact may be short-lived,” economists at the Australia and New Zealand Banking Group in Hong Kong said in a research note on the official purchasing managers’ index. They added that structural reforms were needed to help sustain growth prospects.
\r澳大利亞和新西蘭銀行集團(Australia and New Zealand Banking Group)駐香港的經濟學家們在一份論及官方採購經理人指數的報告中表示，「儘管這些數據可能緩解了人們對中國經濟快速惡化的部分擔憂，但它的影響可能持續不了多久。」他們補充說，必須要進行結構性改革，才能幫助維持經濟發展的前景。
