中國股市拋售潮蔓延至港股

Chinese Share Sell-Off Spills Over to Hong Kong

KEITH BRADSHER
2015年7月7日
 HONG KONG — Heavy government-coordinated purchases pushed up the value of large companies’ shares on mainland Chinese stock exchanges on Monday, interrupting at least temporarily a three-week downturn. But shares in smaller and midsize companies, which make up as much as two-thirds of the market by value, kept falling.
香港——周一，中國政府大力協調買入股票的舉措推高了內地股市裡大型企業的股價，至少暫時打破了長達三周的下行趨勢。不過，占市值三分之二的中小企業的股價繼續下跌。
A widening gap between the relative strength of large companies’ shares and the seemingly unending slide in the rest of the market threatens to widen social divisions in China. Millions of working- and middle-class families invested their life’s savings and borrowed heavily over the past year to buy shares in smaller companies, simply because the shares kept rising and almost regardless of the companies’ financial fundamentals.
大盤股相對穩健，而其他公司的股票似乎在永無休止地下跌，這種不斷擴大的鴻溝可能會加大中國的社會差距。過去一年中，成百上千萬工薪階層和中產階級家庭投入畢生積蓄並大量舉債購買中小盤股票，只因為它們的股價不斷上漲，幾乎不顧及這些公司的財務基本面。
By contrast, Chinese and foreign institutional investors heavily favored the larger companies. Many of the large companies are partially owned by the state, and their executives are senior Communist Party officials who wield considerable political influence. Some large companies also have close ties to some of the country’s leading political families.
相比之下，中國及境外的機構投資者極為青睞的是大盤股。很多大企業為部分國有性質，它們的管理人員是擁有一定政治影響力的共產黨高層官員。部分大企業還與中國的一些主要政治家庭關係密切。
With the Chinese government supporting the prices of many companies’ shares on mainland markets on Monday, particularly large companies, it was the turn of Hong Kong-listed shares to fall heavily.
隨着中國政府在周一採取措施推高內地股市很多企業的股價，特別是大企業，現在輪到香港市場上的股價大幅下跌了。
廣告
The Hong Kong stock market had previously been nearly immune to the sell-off on the mainland. It has tighter restrictions on trading with borrowed money and stronger corporate governance rules. So it did not rise nearly as fast as mainland indexes over the past year and until Monday had not fallen very far either.
香港股市之前幾乎沒有受到內地拋售現象的影響。香港對借錢開展交易的限制更為嚴格，公司治理條例也更為有力。因此，香港股市在過去一年中沒有像內地股市一樣快速攀升，直到本周一之前也沒隨之大幅下跌。
But the broad Hang Seng index of Hong Kong stocks slipped 3.2 percent on Monday, the Hang Seng index of 100 large mainland companies dropped 3.7 percent and the Growth Enterprise Market of mainly small mainland companies plummeted 14.4 percent.
不過，衡量香港股市整體狀況的恒生指數周一下挫了3.2%，恒生中國內地100指數下降了3.7%，衡量內地小企業狀況的創業板指數下跌了14.4%。
Andy Wong, a Hong Kong stockbroker, noted that it was much easier for foreigners to sell shares in Hong Kong in response to developments in mainland China than to sell shares on the Shanghai or Shenzhen stock markets.
香港股票經紀人安迪·黃（Andy Wong，音）指出，對於境外人士來說，為了應對中國內地股市的發展態勢，與在滬深股市賣出股票相比，在香港股市賣出要容易得多。
The falling Hong Kong market “is mainly the result of the overseas players’ offloading Hong Kong stocks in a big way — they appear angry at the Chinese government for coming out to interfere in the China stock markets,” he said. “Since they have no room to play up north, the overseas players have switched to playing the Hong Kong stock market today.”
香港股市的下跌「主要是海外投資者大規模拋售香港股票造成的。他們對中國政府出手干預股市的行為似乎相當憤怒，」他說。「由於在北面沒有操作空間，海外投資者如今轉到來香港股市。」
At a brokerage house in Hong Kong, investors were divided on how much to worry about the day’s setback.
在香港的一家證券公司，投資者對當天股價下挫值得擔心的程度存在不同看法。
Some, like Wong Chun, a 72-year-old investor who has been trading stocks since the 1980s, were clearly worried. “It is hard to say whether the current market-support measures taken by the Chinese government will work in the long term,” she said. “Should the public truly have fear in their heart, then nothing will stop the slide.”
一些人明顯憂心忡忡，比如從80年代開始炒股的72歲股民鍾宏（Wong Chun，音）。「中國政府現在做的那些支持市場的事情很難說會不會長期有效，」她說。「要是民眾內心真的擔心，那什麼也阻止不了下跌。」
But others were more blithe.
其他一些人則較為輕鬆。
“If the Chinese government has the will, it certainly has the ability to stop the slide in the Chinese stock market,” said Daisy Kong, a 52-year-old homemaker who was checking stock prices at the same brokerage house. “The Chinese government has so much money, they don’t need to borrow like Greece.”
「如果中國政府有決心，那肯定有這個能力來阻止中國股市下滑，」在同一家證券公司查看股價的52歲的家庭主婦黛西·江（Daisy Kong，音）說。「中國政府那麼有錢，不需要像希臘那樣去借。」
廣告
Acting at the Chinese government’s behest, 21 mainland brokerage firms set up a $19.4 billion fund over the weekend to buy large companies’ stocks. The central bank also provided money indirectly for the brokerage firms to provide to buyers who wanted to borrow money to invest — or who at least wanted to roll over their existing loans despite having lost heavily.
按照中國政府的要求，內地21家券商在周末期間設立了一個1200億元的基金，以購買大盤股。央行還間接為券商提供貸款，以便它們為那些希望借錢投資的人——或者那些儘管損失慘重，但至少希望延期償還現有借款的人——提供資金。
That support produced a sharp rally in large-company stocks at the opening in Shanghai and Shenzhen. These shares gave up their gains by late morning, stayed largely flat into the afternoon and then surged in the final half-hour, with the CSI 300 index of large-capitalization shares closing with a gain of 2.9 percent.
這種支持舉措促使大盤股在滬深股市開盤後大幅上漲。到接近中午的時候，這些股票再次回落，下午基本上保持平穩，然後在收市前的最後半小時猛漲，代表大盤股的滬深300指數收盤上揚2.9%。
By contrast, shares in small and midsize companies kept falling. The ChiNext price index of small companies lost 4.3 percent on Monday.
相比之下，中小盤持續下跌。 以小企業為主的創業板指數周一下挫4.3%。
At a branch of the China Securities brokerage firm near the Dongzhimen neighborhood in Beijing, investors gossiped in front of nine large LED screens flashing stock prices. By midafternoon, the nearly uniformly falling prices on the screens only further dispirited the investors in the trading hall, mostly gray-haired retirees. As the afternoon trading started, those who sat in the back rows fished out their lunchboxes and started eating.
在中信證券位於北京東直門附近的一家營業廳，投資者在九塊顯示股價的LED屏幕前閑聊。截至中午，顯示屏上幾乎全盤下跌的股價讓交易廳里的股民情緒進一步受挫，其中大部分是頭髮灰白的退休人員。隨着下午交易時段的開始，那些坐在後排的股民掏出午餐盒，開始吃飯。
Mr. Huang, with a receding hairline and wearing a faded yellow polo shirt and shorts, was the most talkative. “Sell off as soon as you can,” he exhorted his friends, women huddled silently in the last row, two of them with jade rosaries in their hands and another absent-mindedly knitting.
髮際線後退、身穿褪色黃色Polo衫和短褲的黃先生最健談。他勸默默擠在最後一排的幾名女性友人，「趕緊賣了。」其中兩名女性手裡捏着翡翠念珠，另一人則心不在焉地織着東西。
“Hurry up! Trust me, it’s really hopeless,” continued Mr. Huang, who declined to provide his given name. “Just stop trading. Let it drop.”
「快點吧！相信我。真的沒希望了，」黃先生繼續說道。「別買賣了。就讓它跌去吧。」黃先生拒絕提供全名。
廣告
“What kind of bailout is this?” complained a woman sitting behind Mr. Huang.
坐在黃先生身後的一名女性抱怨，「這是哪門子的救市？」
A voice came from the back of the hall: “This is strafing China’s middle class with a machine gun.” The agitated murmur in the room stopped momentarily at the comment, but soon resumed.
交易廳後方傳來一個聲音：「這是用機關槍掃射中國的中產階級。」大廳里嘈雜的聲音在那一瞬間突然停止，但很快，一切就又恢復了原樣。
