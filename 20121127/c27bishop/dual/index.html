中国未来经济增长取决于改革

The Confusing Outlooks for China's Growth

利明璋
2012年11月27日
The recent economic data out of China have been good, but as Caixin explains there is still no consensus on predictions for 2013 growth.
中国最近给出的经济数据还不错，不过，正如财新网解释的那样，预测中国2013年经济增长的时候，人们依然看法不一。
Researchers at Renmin University expect 2013 "real economic growth" to be 9.3 percent. A Chinese business newspaper reported on Monday that the Central Economic Work Conference would probably set the 2013 target gross domestic product growth rate at 7.5 percent in December, and the Conference Board predicted this month that the growth rate over the next six years was likely to average 5.5 percent.
人民大学的研究人员预测，中国在2013年“真实的经济增长率”将为9.3%。本周一，中国的一家财经报纸报道，于12月召开的中央经济工作会议可能会把2013年国内生产总值的增长目标设定在7.5%。世界大企业联合会(Conference Board)本月预测，中国在今后6年的平均经济增长率很可能为5.5%。
All but the most bearish analysts believe economic activity is picking up, though there are concerns that the growth is in response to real end-user demand as opposed to inventory restocking in anticipation of more stimulus. Looking past the high-frequency data, Gavyn Davies argues in The Financial Times that "China has faced greater economic challenges in the past three decades, and has succeeded in overcoming them. It can do so again."
除了最为悲观的分析师以外，几乎所有人都相信经济活动正在复苏，不过，也有人担忧经济增长反映的不是最终用户的真实需求，而是企业基于更多财政刺激措施即将出台的预期而补充库存的行为。回顾过去的高频数据，加文·戴维斯(Gavyn Davies)在《金融时报》(Financial Times)上撰文称，“过去30年里，中国曾经面临比现在还大的经济挑战，但却成功地克服了那些挑战。中国可以再来一次。”
Do not feel bad if you are confused by the differing views about China's economy. Even the usually bearish Ambrose Evans-Pritchard seems disoriented. In a remarkable turnabout for someone who has recently written alarmist columns like "China's Economic Destiny in Doubt After Leadership Shock" and "China's Revolution Risk," Mr. Evans-Pritchard sounds almost giddy in his description of Chengdu and the potential for China as a high-tech expansion drives a second boom in the hinterland.
如果关于中国经济的不同观点让你迷惑，不要感觉不是滋味。就连一向悲观的安布罗斯·埃文斯·普理查德(Ambrose Evans-Pritchard) 也似乎迷失了方向。埃文斯·普理查德最近曾写下一些危言耸听的专栏文章，比如《领导层发生震动，中国经济命运堪疑》(China's Economic Destiny in Doubt After Leadership Shock)和《中国的革命风险》(China's Revolution Risk)，有鉴于此，他 的转变引人注目。他认为高科技扩张将推动中国内陆地区的第二轮发展热潮，对成都和中国发展潜力的刻画几乎达到了令人眼花缭乱的地步。 
THE KEY FOR FUTURE GROWTH IS REFORM, as this column has discussed several times. Li Keqiang, No. 2 in the Communist Party and the premier-in-waiting, gave a talk last week in which he emphasized the need for continuing reforms. His comments were widely covered and praised in Chinese media but received only limited mention in most Western media, probably because of the Thanksgiving holiday in the United States. One early test of the apparent resolve for reform will be the income distribution reform plan that is expected by the end of the year, after several years of delays. Income distribution reform can happen only if the government is willing to take on special interests and state-owned enterprises.
就像本专栏多次讨论的那样，改革是未来经济增长的关键。中国的候任总理、共产党二号人物李克强上周发表讲话时强调了继续改革的必要性。他的评论得到了中国媒体的广泛报道和赞扬，不过，也许是因为当时正值美国的感恩节，大多数西方媒体只用有限的篇幅提及了此事。经历了几年的推迟之后，预计将于今年末出台的收入分配改革计划将是对表面上的改革决心所做的一个早期测试。只有当政府愿意挑战特殊利益集团和国有企业的时候，收入分配改革才有可能发生。  
On Sunday, The New York Times published "Lobbying, a Windfall and a Leader's Family," the second part of its investigation into the family wealth of Premier Wen Jiabao. Ping An Insurance, the company at the center of the report, said on Monday that it was considering legal action after the reports.
上周日，《世界经济观察报》发表了题为《温氏家族与平安崛起》(Lobbying, a Windfall and a Leader's Family)的报道，这是时报对温家宝总理家族财富所做调查的第二部分。处于报道核心的平安保险公司于本周一表示，看到报道之后，他们正在考虑提起法律诉讼。
Last week's column discussed the likelihood that Xi Jinping would begin a meaningful corruption crackdown. Rumors are going around that Beijing is planning an anti-corruption and rectification campaign in early 2013. The excessive role of the state in China's economy breeds graft, and without significant reform, any anti-corruption campaigns are likely to fail, even if they snare some high-level officials and impact the graft economy from which many firms profit, including global luxury goods companies.
上周的专栏文章对习近平开展一场意义深远的反腐行动的可能性进行了讨论。最近有传言称，北京正计划在2013年初开展一场反腐倡廉运动。中国政府对经济的过度干预滋生了腐败，如果不进行重大改革，任何反腐运动都可能会以失败告终，哪怕这些运动套住了一些高层官员，并对贪腐经济造成了一定打击。包括一些国际奢侈品公司在内的许多企业都受益于这种经济。
Both Mr. Xi and Li Keqiang have emphasized the need for rule of law in China, but there is still a yawning gap between rhetoric and reality that damages not only social order but also investor confidence and economic development.
习近平和李克强都曾强调在中国实行法治的必要性，然而，现实和言语之间仍存在巨大差距，这不仅破坏了社会秩序，还损害了投资者的信心和经济的发展。
On Saturday Fairfax Media published a disturbing report about Dr. Du Zuying, a Chinese Australian cardiac surgeon who co-founded a biotech venture. The company, China Biologic Products, is now listed on Nasdaq with a market capitalization of more than $300 million. Dr Du has been in custody in Shandong Province since February 2011 and his family claims that his former partners took his shares and colluded with the local police to have him arrested.
周六，费尔法克斯媒体集团(Fairfax Media)发表了一篇令人不安的报道，文章讲述了一家生物科技企业的联合创立者、华裔澳籍心外科医生杜祖鹰的故事。杜祖鹰创立的企业名为中国生物制品有限公司(China Biologic Products)，现已在纳斯达克(Nasdaq)上市，市值超过3亿美元(约合18.6亿元人民币)。而自2011年2月起，杜祖鹰医生就被拘押在山东省，其家人称，他的前合伙人吞没了他的股份，并和当地警方串通起来逮捕了他。 
A CHINESE INTERNET COMPANY successfully listed on the Nasdaq market last week. YY.com raised $82 million the day before Thanksgiving. Two existing investors bought 1.505 million of the 7.8 million shares offered and the deal priced at the bottom of the range, but it got done. And that is a positive sign for other Chinese firms considering a listing in the United States. One of the concerns about Chinese companies has been the frequent use of variable-interest entities to get around legal restrictions, as explained in a DealBook article. The Securities and Exchange Commission has raised a lot of questions about the variable interest entity structure, but its approval of the YY.com initial public offering is a sign the commission is still comfortable with it, as long as the risks are properly disclosed.
上周，一家中国网络公司在纳斯达克成功上市。在感恩节前一天，YY.com筹集到了8200万美元的资金。发行的股票总数为780万股，两位现有投资者购买了其中的150.5万股，虽然该公司IPO（initial public offering，首次公开募股）发行价只达到了此前确定的发行价区间下限，但还是成功上市。对于其他想在美国上市的中国公司来说，这是一个好兆头。对于中国公司，人们的担心之一便是它们频繁利用可变利益实体来规避法律约束，DealBook的一篇文章已对此进行了阐释。美国证券交易委员会(Securities and Exchange Commission)已经对可变利益实体结构提出了诸多质疑，但它对YY.com IPO的批准表明，只要相关风险得到了适当的披露，委员会仍可接受这种结构。
But without reforms to significantly strengthen the rule of law, the risks in China for any investor, foreign or Chinese, remain high.
但是，如果不通过改革大力加强法治，对于任何投资者来说，不论是中国还是外国投资者，投资中国的风险仍然会居高不下。
